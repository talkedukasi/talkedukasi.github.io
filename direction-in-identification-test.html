30. Electrical Diagrams and Symbols Test I. Identification: Directions: Identify the following symbols.​


✏Answer✏

Resistor: A symbol representing a passive electronic component that restricts the flow of electric current. It is typically depicted as a zigzag line.

Capacitor: A symbol representing a passive electronic component that stores and releases electric charge. It is typically depicted as two parallel lines with a gap between them.

Inductor: A symbol representing a passive electronic component that stores and releases energy in the form of a magnetic field. It is typically depicted as a coil or a series of loops.

Diode: A symbol representing an electronic component that allows electric current to flow in one direction and blocks it in the other. It is typically depicted as an arrow pointing in one direction with a line across it in the opposite direction.

Transistor: A symbol representing an electronic component that can be used for amplification or switching of electric signals. It is typically depicted as a combination of two diodes, with three terminals.

Battery: A symbol representing a power source that generates electric voltage. It is typically depicted as a series of stacked lines, with a longer line indicating the positive terminal and a shorter line indicating the negative terminal.

Ground: A symbol representing a connection to the Earth or a common reference point for electric circuits. It is typically depicted as a horizontal line with three vertical lines branching out from it.

Switch: A symbol representing a device that opens or closes an electric circuit to control the flow of current. It is typically depicted as a line with a gap, and a break or bridge across it to indicate its status (open or closed).

Fuse: A symbol representing a protective device that breaks the circuit in case of excessive current flow to prevent damage. It is typically depicted as a squiggly line with a diagonal line across it.

Light Emitting Diode (LED): A symbol representing a semiconductor device that emits light when electric current passes through it. It is typically depicted as an arrow pointing in one direction with two lines coming out of it, indicating the emission of light.
