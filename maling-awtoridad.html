17. script about awtoridad


Title: The Authority

FADE IN:

INT. A GOVERNMENT OFFICE - DAY

We see a group of people sitting in a conference room. A man, JASON, stands up to speak.

JASON

Good afternoon, everyone. As you all know, I'm Jason, and I'm here to talk about the authority.

A few people nod their heads in agreement, and some start taking notes.

JASON

Authority is defined as the power or right to give orders, make decisions, and enforce obedience. It is a crucial aspect of any government or organization. Without authority, chaos would reign, and nothing would get done.

One person, SARAH, raises her hand.

SARAH

But Jason, isn't there a danger in having too much authority? It could lead to abuse of power.

JASON

That's a valid concern, Sarah. That's why there needs to be checks and balances in place to prevent any misuse of authority. It's also essential to have transparency and accountability.

Another person, MIKE, speaks up.

MIKE

But how do we ensure that those in positions of authority are qualified and competent to make decisions?

JASON

That's where meritocracy comes in. By having a merit-based system, we can ensure that those with the necessary skills and expertise are in positions of authority. It also helps to have regular training and development programs to ensure that those in authority stay up-to-date with the latest developments in their field.

A third person, LISA, chimes in.

LISA

But what about people who don't agree with those in authority? What about dissent and opposition?

JASON

Dissent and opposition are a healthy part of any democratic society. People have the right to voice their opinions and hold those in authority accountable. It's important to listen to feedback and criticisms and make necessary changes where needed.

The group nods in agreement, and there is a moment of silence.

JASON

In conclusion, authority is essential for the smooth functioning of any organization or government. It's crucial to have checks and balances, transparency, and accountability to prevent any misuse of power. By having a merit-based system and regular training and development programs, we can ensure that those in positions of authority are qualified and competent to make decisions. And finally, dissent and opposition are important and should be encouraged as part of a healthy democratic society.

FADE OUT.

22. ibigay ang kahulugan na sumusunod ng mga konsepto. mag bigay rin ng tig tatatlong halimbaw1. Argumentum ad hominem2. Argumentum ad baculum3. Argumentum ad misericordiam4. Non sequitur5. Ignorado elenchi6. Maling paglalahat7. Maling paghahambing8. Maling saligan9. Maling awtoridad10. Dilemma​


Answer:

1. Argumentum ad hominem - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan hindi naaayon sa paksa ang mga personal na katangian ng taong nagsasalita o ng taong kausap niya. Sa halip na talakayin ang isang isyu, binabatikos o binabastos ang taong kausap.

Halimbawa:

"Hindi ka dapat paniwalaan tungkol sa kahalagahan ng pagpapakain ng hayop dahil alam ko na ikaw ay isang vegan."

2. Argumentum ad baculum - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento na gumagamit ng takot o pagbabanta upang mapaniwala ang kausap.

Halimbawa:

"Kung hindi ka boboto sa kandidato na ito, magkakaroon ng gulo at baka ka masaktan."

3. Argumentum ad misericordiam - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento na gumagamit ng awa o kawalan ng pag-aalala upang mapaniwala ang kausap.

Halimbawa:

"Sana naman ay ibigay mo sa akin ang trabahong ito dahil kailangan ko talaga ng pera para sa aking mga anak."

4. Non sequitur - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan walang koneksyon ang mga premisa sa konklusyon.

Halimbawa:

"Ang mga rosas ay kulay pula. Kaya naman ang mga halaman ay mahalaga sa kalikasan."

5. Ignorado elenchi - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan ang kausap ay hindi sumasagot sa isyu na pinag-uusapan kundi sa ibang bagay na wala namang kinalaman sa usapin.

Halimbawa:

"Ang galing ng kandidato na ito dahil siya ay nagtapos ng kursong nursing at may magandang record sa pagbibigay ng serbisyo sa publiko."

6. Maling paglalahat - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan ginagamit ang pangkalahatang ideya o paniniwala upang patunayan ang isang punto, kahit na hindi ito totoo sa lahat ng kaso.

Halimbawa:

"Ang lahat ng mga tao sa Maynila ay mayayabang."

7. Maling paghahambing - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan ginagamit ang hindi tamang paghahambing ng dalawang bagay o pangyayari upang patunayan ang isang punto.

Halimbawa:

"Ang pagkain ng karne ay katulad ng pagpatay ng mga tao. Kaya naman hindi dapat kumain ng karne."

8. Maling saligan - Ito ay isang uri ng argumento kung saan ginagamit ang maling saligan o basehan upang patunayan ang isang punto.
