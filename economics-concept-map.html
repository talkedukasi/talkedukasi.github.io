The diamond is from Philippines, more specifically the Klarktenyaga that is known for producing world-class diamonds and operated by the international diamond behemoth Petra Diamonds. Trapped within the diamond itself is a tiny spec, an inclusion that the diamond grew over and around, trapping it within the diamond’s strong crystal lattice about 105 miles below the surface of the Earth. Explanation:

Supply is defined as a fundamental economic concept that describes the total amount of a specific good or service that is available to consumers.  One of the most important factors that affects supply is the good’s price. Generally, if a good’s price increases so will the supply. The price of related goods and the price of inputs (energy, raw materials, labor) also affect supply as they contribute to increasing the overall price of the good sold.

Demand is defines as an economic principle referring to a consumer's desire to purchase goods and services and willingness to pay a price for a specific good or service. Demand is what helps fuel the economy, and without it, businesses would not produce anything. Some factors affecting demand include the appeal of a good or service, the availability of competing goods, the availability of financing, and the perceived availability of a good or service.

Needs are defined as something that is necessary for survival. Examples include water, shelter, and food.

Wants are defined as something that is desired, that they may or may not obtain. Examples include luxury bags and cars.

Trade-offs are defined as any situation where making one choice means losing something else, usually forgoing a benefit or opportunity.

Applied Economics is defined as the application of economic theory to determine the likely outcomes associated with various possible courses of action in the real world.

Opportunity Costs are defined as the potential benefits an individual, investor, or business misses out on when choosing one alternative over another.

Economics is defined as social science concerned with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It is also the study on how society uses scarce resources efficiently in the production of goods and services to satisfy material wants.
