Answer:

After Chaos and Gaia came Tartarus, which is deep inside the Earth, the lowest level of the Underworld. Then came Eros, the most beautiful of all immortals.

All else came into being after these four have emerged. Chaos give birth to Erebus (Darkness) and Nyx (Night). While sleeping, Gaia gave birth to Uranus (God of the Sky and Sky himself) and Pontus (God of the Sea and Sea). Uranus was the first son of Gaia and later became her husband.

After the emergence of Erebus, Nyx, Uranus and Pontus, all the rest of Creation came from mating. Uranus, the sky, grew as huge as Gaia and enveloped her. Fertile rain fell to the earth and Gaia then gave birth to the rest of the physical world, mountains, oceans, flowers and trees. 16. War of the Worlds By H.G. Wells Chapter 17- The Thunder Child. 1. In chapter 17, what do you think the author was describing when he said the Martians "were constraining. mankind".​


Answer:

In chapter 17 of War of the Worlds by H.G. Wells, the author describes the Martians as "constraining mankind". This likely refers to the Martians' superior power and their ability to easily decimate humans. The Martians are not interested in total extermination, but rather in the complete demoralization and destruction of any opposition. The Thunder Child's efforts against the Martians are little more than a suicide mission and show that humankind's best weapons can do little to stop the Martians. The Martians' attack has brought humanity together in a sense, as everybody suffers the same catastrophe, but it has also pitted humans against one another. Ultimately, the Martians' constraining of mankind refers to their ability to control and dominate humans through their superior technology and power
