6. Why is it necessary to learn how to formulate statements of opinion and assertion?​


Answer:

The assertion is where you make a claim and/or clearly define the side you want to argue.

1. Be knowledgeable

Before you start writing your assertions, make sure your facts are straight. Do some research on the subject, and collect any important information that you might need. Remember, every topic has two sides to it.

2. Back it all up

Your assertions needs to be a stable throughout. One of the best ways to hold up your assertions is to surround them with your research findings. I recommend following the assertion, evidence, commentary rubric.

3. Be clear and concise

Since each assertion lets you take a stand on your topic, it’s very important that you keep things clear and concise. Don’t beat around the bush. State your claim during the introduction, but don’t elaborate extensively yet. That’s for the latter parts of the essay. There’s also no need to use too many adjectives. Just keep everything short and to the point. Ideally, an assertion is only one sentence long, much like a thesis statement.

4. Be thematic

Once you’ve written your assertions down, you can proceed with the rest of your essay. You have to keep in mind that your essay’s structure has to be built around the assertions that you made in the first place. This means that most of the things you write afterwards should support and corroborate your assertions, and not contradict them.

29. what are the things to be considered in formulating an assertions?​


In formulating an assertions you should be knowledgeable, be clear and concise, be thematic, and back it all up with supporting details.

The assertion is one of the most crucial elements of an essay, especially an argumentative one, therefore knowing how to create them is critical. The assertion is where you make a claim and/or clearly clarify your side of the argument. When coming up with statements, it's simple to become sidetracked.

Be knowledgeble. Make sure your facts are correct before you begin composing your statements. Conduct some research on the issue and gather any relevant facts. Remember that every topic has two sides.

Your statements must be consistent throughout. Surrounding your arguments with research data is one of the finest methods to back them up. I advocate following the criteria of claim, evidence, and commentary.

Because each statement allows you to take a position on your issue, it's critical that you keep things clear and concise. Don't belabor the point. Declare your claim in the opening, but don't go into detail. That is for the last sections of the essay. Just keep it brief and to the point. An assertion, like a thesis statement, should ideally be one phrase long.

After you've typed down your statements, you may move on to the rest of your essay. Keep in mind that the framework of your essay must be developed around the statements that you stated in the first place. This implies that the majority of what you write after that should support and confirm your assumptions rather than dispute them.
