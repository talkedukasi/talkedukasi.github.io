17. 6. Science and poetry open complementary angles of vision onto human experience.​


Answer:

Science and poetry are two different modes of understanding and interpreting human experience, but they can be complementary in the sense that they offer different angles of vision that can deepen our understanding of the world around us.

Science offers a systematic and empirical approach to studying the natural world, with a focus on observable phenomena and the laws that govern them. It seeks to explain the world through the use of empirical evidence and the application of rational thinking. Science helps us to understand the physical and natural world in a precise and quantitative manner.

Poetry, on the other hand, offers a more subjective and imaginative approach to understanding human experience. It uses language and imagery to evoke emotions and sensations, and to explore the deeper meanings and complexities of our existence. Poetry can offer insights into the human condition that are not easily captured by scientific analysis, such as the emotions and experiences that shape our lives.

By combining the insights of science and poetry, we can gain a more comprehensive understanding of the world around us. Science can help us to understand the physical processes that underlie our experiences, while poetry can help us to explore the emotional and spiritual dimensions of those experiences. Together, science and poetry can offer us a more holistic and nuanced perspective on the complex tapestry of human experience.

19. What is the poetry in form ​


Form, in poetry, can be understood as the physical structure of the poem: the length of the lines, their rhythms, their system of rhymes and repetition. In this sense, it is normally reserved for the type of poem where these features have been shaped into a pattern, especially a familiar pattern.
