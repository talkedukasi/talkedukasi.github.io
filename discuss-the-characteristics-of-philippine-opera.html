3. Discuss the characteristics of Philippine Opera. Explain your answer


Answer:

The Philippine opera is a dramatic performance of musicians, singers and performers with the combination of text and musical score usually in a theatrical setting. It started in the 19th century featuring themes such as romance, deceit, murder and a lot more.

Traditional opera consists of two modes of singing and recitative -- the plot-driving passages often sung in a non-melodic style characteristic of opera, and in which the characters express their emotions in a more structured melodic style.

The other methods in which Philippine Opera is performed is through dancing. The most common is the Philippine ballet. The Philippine ballet are characterized by folktale presentations on local fables such as Lola Basyang - the ballet and others. The Philippine musical plays are in a way serve as fruits of theater and are highly influenced by broadway presentations. Its themes include adaptation from novels and the likes.

Difference between opera ballet and musical play :

The main difference between Ballet and Opera is that the Ballet is a type of performance dance (performing arts genre) and Opera is a artform combining sung text and musical score in a theatrical setting .

Similarities of musical play and opera :

They are both made up of several songs, or musical pieces, connected by lines of dialogue; this is usually spoken in a musical, whereas in an opera it is performed as a recitative in which the dialogue transpires in a melodic or musical pattern.

Examples of Philippine Opera :

Sandugong Panaginip - First Filipino opera by Pedro Paterno first performed in the Zorilla Theater on August 2, 1902.

Lakangbini - opera by Patricio Mariano staged at the Metropolitan Theater on December 19, 1933.

Laloba Negra - is a three-act Filipino opera.

10. discuss the characteristics of philippine opera ballet musical plays and other multi-media forms.Explain your answer


Philippine Opera, Ballet and Musical PlaysThe Philippine Opera often introduces elements in which people find themselves caught up in the character’s moments such as having conflicts through the use of romance or sudden life change (like tragedy). The performance mostly consists of singing and not spoken words. The Philippine Ballet is known to have presented mostly folktales and other mythical representations. Using dancers’ movements to create a lively flow on the stage and create a signature culture-related scenery. The Philippine Musical Plays were influenced by Broadway performances where there is a live audience, few spoken lines of actors and actresses and also includes timed singing as indicated in the script. From novels to the works of well-known artists were given the opportunity to display their art through musical plays.

20. discuss the characteristics of philippine opera ballet musical play and other multi media forms


The Philippine opera is a dramatic performance of musicians and singers with the combination of text and musical score usually in a theatrical setting. It started in the 19th century featuring themes such as romance, deceit, murder and a lot more. 
The Philippine ballet on the other hand is a drama performance using facial expressions and body movements conveying novels and stories. Local fables such as Lola Basyang are common subject of Philippine ballets.
The Philippine musical play is characterized by a lighter presentation compared to opera. The themes are usually contemporary and more manageable. It relies on novel adaptations and literary works such as Andres Bonifacio: Ang Dakilang Anak Pawis.

Good opera is important if it makes people feel something (other than indignation, or disappointment!). It is especially important if those feelings incline people to higher aspiration, greater sympathy, or more profound self-knowledge. It also has an historical voice and can be historically expressive.Opera, whose name comes from the Philipines' word for a work, realizes the Baroque ambition of integrating all the arts. Music and drama are the fundamental ingredients, as are the arts of staging and costume design; opera is therefore a visual as well as an audible art. Throughout its history, opera has reflected trends current in the several arts of which it is composed. Developments in architecture and painting have manifested themselves on the operatic stage in the design of sets and costumes for specific performances, and opera has also affected the visual arts beyond the stage in such domains as the design and decoration of opera houses and the portraiture of singers and composers. A feature unique to opera, however, is the power of music, particularly that written for the several registers of the human singing voice, which is arguably the artistic means best suited to the expression of emotion and the portrayal of character.
