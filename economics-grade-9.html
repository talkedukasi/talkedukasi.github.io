1. Microeconomics

What I've learned:

Microeconomics studies the allocation of resources between economic agents. It is the heir to the theory of prices and markets. Its ultimate goal is the simultaneous determination of prices and quantities produced, traded and consumed. Prices play a major role in microeconomic analysis.

Importance: The theory of individual and collective choices: the contributions of the theory of the consumer, of intertemporal choices and in uncertainty, the theory of the company. Economic equilibrium: a relative principle and restrictions.

2. Monopoly

What I've learned:

In a monopoly market, only one company sells a given product or delivers a given service, which gives it strong market power. The company that holds the monopoly, therefore the supplier, fr/33//ly sets and changes its selling prices since it is not subject to competition.

3. Circular flow of Economy

What I've learned:

the circular economy can be defined as an economic system of exchange and production which, at all stages of the life cycle of products (goods and services), aims to increase the efficiency of the use resources and reduce the impact on the environment while developing well-being

4. Supply and Demand

What I've learned:

Supply designates, within the framework of an economic approach, the quantity of goods or services offered on a market. It varies depending on the price. In a more commercial or marketing study context, the offer can also refer to all the players involved in bringing a product or service to market.

5. Economic Policies

What I've learned:

Economic policy is defined as all the means implemented by the State to achieve the objectives it has set itself with the aim of improving the general economic situation of the country.

The political systems are numerous, the main ones being the Republic, authoritarianism and totalitarianism. There is also monarchism or feudalism.
