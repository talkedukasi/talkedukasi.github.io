25. To whom does the poem “To a Lost One” was dedicated? Cite proof​


The Poem "To a Lost One" Definition

Angela Manalang Gloria wrote a poem about the sensation of being haunted. To achieve this effect from the poem's perspective, she made the ghost the persona rather than herself. This poem expresses the actual feelings of spirits who experience the agony and isolation of being separated from their loved ones. Furthermore, the verse emphasizes that the deceased do not wish to be forgotten and that they would do anything to be remembered by their surviving loved ones.

About Angela

Angela Caridad Legaspi Manalang was birthed on August 2, 1907, in Guagua, Pampanga, to parents Felipe Dizon Manalang (born in Mexico, Pampanga) married Tomasa Legaspi (whom she hardly talked about). Their family eventually settled in the Bicol region, namely in Albay. She received her elementary education from St. Agnes Academy in Legaspi, where she graduated as valedictorian. During her senior year, she transferred to St. Scholastica's College in Malate, Manila, where her writing began to attract attention.

Angela Manalang was among the University of the Philippines' first-generation female students. Angela initially enrolled in law school at her father's suggestion. However, she subsequently transferred to the literature on the recommendation of her professor C.V. Wickers, who also became her mentor.
