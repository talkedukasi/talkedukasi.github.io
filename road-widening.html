25. Give other effects of soil erosion to the Earth's surface and draw them. 1. Mountain 2. Rough roads 3. Widening of river 4. Volcano 5. Mounds​


Sure, here are some additional effects of soil erosion on the Earth's surface:

6. Loss of Fertile Soil: Soil erosion removes the top layer of soil, which is the most fertile and nutrient-rich layer. This can lead to a decline in agricultural productivity and food production.

7. Desertification: Soil erosion can contribute to the process of desertification, where formerly fertile land becomes a desert due to the loss of soil, vegetation, and water.

8. Landslides: Soil erosion can increase the risk of landslides, especially in areas with steep slopes and unstable soil. The removal of vegetation and topsoil can destabilize the land and make it more prone to sliding.

9. Coastal Erosion: Soil erosion can contribute to the loss of coastlines, as the removal of soil and vegetation can weaken the land and make it more vulnerable to erosion by waves and tides.

10. Water Pollution: Soil erosion can lead to water pollution, as sediment and other pollutants can be washed into rivers, lakes, and oceans. This can harm aquatic ecosystems and affect water quality for drinking, irrigation, and other uses.
