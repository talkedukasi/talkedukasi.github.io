3. Thesis title: Effectiveness of pricing towards the customer satisfaction 1.Significance of the Study 2.Related Literature and Studies


Answer:

Significance of the Study:

Pricing is a critical aspect that plays a significant role in determining the customer satisfaction level. The study on the effectiveness of pricing towards customer satisfaction is essential for businesses to understand the pricing strategies they should adopt to ensure customer satisfaction. This study aims to explore the connection between pricing and customer satisfaction and the role of different pricing strategies in shaping customers' perceptions of the value of the products and services they purchase. The findings of this study will help businesses to design effective pricing strategies that promote customer satisfaction, increase customer loyalty, and boost revenues.

Related Literature and Studies:

1. Grewal, D., Krishnan, R., Baker, J., & Borin, N. (1998). The effect of store name, brand name and price discounts on consumers' evaluations and purchase intentions. Journal of Retailing, 74(3), 331-352.

This study investigated the effect of store name, brand name, and price discounts on consumer evaluations and purchase intentions. The findings showed that price discounts were more effective in increasing purchase intentions than store name and brand name.

2. Dodds, W. B., Monroe, K. B., & Grewal, D. (1991). Effects of price, brand, and store information on buyers' product evaluations. Journal of Marketing Research, 28(3), 307-319.

This study examined the effects of price, brand, and store information on buyers' product evaluations. The findings indicated that price was the most critical factor influencing product evaluations, followed by brand and store information.

3. Grewal, D., Monroe, K. B., & Krishnan, R. (1998). The effects of price-comparison advertising on buyers' perceptions of acquisition value, transaction value, and behavioral intentions. Journal of Marketing, 62(2), 46-59.

This study investigated the effects of price-comparison advertising on buyers' perceptions of acquisition value, transaction value, and behavioral intentions. The findings showed that price-comparison advertising improved buyers' perceptions of acquisition value, transaction value, and behavioral intentions.

4. Phillips, L. W., & Nagle, T. T. (1999). Pricing and the psychology of consumption. Harvard Business Review, 77(5), 66-75.

This article examined the psychology of pricing and its impact on consumer behavior. The authors suggested that pricing is a crucial factor in shaping how consumers perceive and value products and services. They explored different personas of consumers and their response to various pricing strategies.

5. Simonson, I. (1992). The effect of product assortment on buyer preference. Journal of Retailing, 68(3), 359-369.

This study examined the effect of product assortment on buyer preference. The findings suggested that consumers' willingness to pay was positively correlated with the size of the product assortment. The study emphasized the importance of providing a broad assortment of products to satisfy customer preferences and increase customer satisfaction.
