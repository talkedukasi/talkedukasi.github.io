  • 6. MANILA, Philippines – Buying drinking water from refilling stations has become the most common way of obtaining clean and safe drinking water. There are now more than 20,000 water refilling stations in the country, where up to 40% of households nationwide and 60% of Metro Manila residents buy drinking water. Proactivity is encouraged for consumers to get the cleanest drinking water possible. As a research study from the Pediatric Infectious Disease Society of the Philippines puts it, “Characteristics of the [water refilling] stations such as the disinfection method, frequency of monitoring, personnel hygiene, attire and practices all affect the safety and potability of the product water.” Water refilling stations are required by law to adhere to the Department of Health (DOH) Implementing Rules and Regulations of the Sanitation Code of the Philippines, stipulated in Presidential Decree 856. Chapter 2 on water supply, as outlined in the DOH regulations, requires that all water refilling stations must be located at least 25 meters away from any direct source of pollution, and not in an area subject to flooding. Proprietors are required to have water samples tested by a DOHaccredited laboratory to comply with the Philippine National Standards for Drinking Water (PNSDW). In addition, the DOH has specific rules on how refilling stations should prepare, refill, handle, store and deliver water. Water containers must be properly sanitized before filling them with purified water, using DOH-approved sanitizing solution to disinfect the container from germs. Washing the container with merely water and soap is insufficient. The DOH also demands all personnel to wear protective gear such as gloves, face masks, hair nets, and scrub suits to prevent purified water from coming into contact with any part of the body. Refilled water for delivery is then transferred into sanitized transports that ensure water is maintained at room temperature (25C28C). Delivery vehicles that leave the containers exposed to dust, pollution, heat and direct sunlight are not acceptable. Alternatively, there are also in-home water purifiers, which have the advantage of eliminating the need for manual handling and transport of the water. In fact, the World Health Organization (WHO) and the United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF) have endorsed household treatment of water through point-of-use purifiers as an effective strategy to avoid water-borne diseases. 1. Different uses of freshwater:

Drinking and household use: Freshwater is essential for human consumption, including drinking, cooking, and personal hygiene.

Agriculture: Freshwater is used for irrigation to support crop growth and agriculture.

Industry: Many industries require freshwater for various processes, such as manufacturing, power generation, and cooling.

Hydropower: Freshwater is a vital resource for generating hydropower, which is a renewable energy source.

Ecosystem support: Freshwater ecosystems, such as rivers, lakes, and wetlands, provide habitats for numerous species and support biodiversity.

2. Source of drinking water:

The water we drink typically comes from various sources, including:

Surface water: This includes water from rivers, lakes, and reservoirs that undergo treatment processes to ensure its safety for drinking.

Groundwater: Water extracted from underground aquifers through wells and undergoes treatment before distribution.

Municipal water supply: Many urban areas have centralized water treatment facilities that treat and distribute water to households and businesses.

3. Water transportation and traffic situation in the Philippines:

Water transportation can help alleviate traffic congestion in the Philippines by providing an alternative mode of transportation, particularly in areas near bodies of water. For example:

Ferries and boats: Waterways can be utilized to transport people and goods, especially in coastal areas and regions with rivers and lakes.

Water taxis: In cities with accessible water channels, water taxis or similar services can offer an alternative means of transportation, reducing road congestion.

By diverting some traffic to water-based transportation options, it can help ease the burden on congested roads, reduce travel times, and provide additional mobility choices for commuters.

4. Effects of unregulated deep well use on groundwater in the Philippines:

Unregulated use of deep wells can lead to excessive groundwater extraction without proper management. This can have several negative effects:

Depletion of groundwater: Uncontrolled extraction can lower the water table, reducing the available groundwater and potentially leading to water scarcity.

Saltwater intrusion: In coastal areas, excessive pumping of groundwater can allow saltwater to intrude into freshwater aquifers, making the water unsuitable for consumption or agricultural use.

Land subsidence: Over-pumping of groundwater can lead to land subsidence or sinking, causing damage to infrastructure and ecosystems

The bodies of water surrounding the Philippines offer several opportunities for utilization by the countrymen, such as:
