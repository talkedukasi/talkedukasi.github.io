KABUKI AND PEKING OPERA

a.

Kabuki - Kabuki is a traditional Japanese dance-drama form. Kabuki theatre is recognized for its stylized performances, frequently glitzy clothes, and intricate kumadori make-up used by some of its actors.Historical plays, home dramas, and dance pieces are all examples of this type of traditional Japanese theater with highly stylized song, mime, and dance, which is now solely performed by male performers and uses exaggerated gestures and body movements to communicate emotions.The emphasis of kabuki performances began to shift away from dancing and toward drama.

b.

Peking Opera - Speech, singing, mime, and acrobatics are performed to an orchestral backdrop in this stylized Chinese type of opera going back to the late 18th century.The goal of Peking opera is not to precisely depict reality. Peking opera experts compare the fundamentals of the art form to the principle of Mo, or mimes or imitation, present in western plays. Peking opera should be evocative rather than imitative.

Where it is originated ?Kabuki is considered to have started in Kyoto during the early Edo era, when creator Izumo no Okuni organized a female dance troop to perform dances and light comedies. After women were forbidden from appearing in kabuki theatre in 1629, the art form evolved into its current all-male theatrical form. Kabuki grew in popularity in the late 17th century and peaked in the mid-18th century.Despite its name (Beijing theatrical style), Peking opera has its beginnings in southern Anhui and eastern Hubei, which share the same Xiajiang Mandarin dialect (Lower Yangtze Mandarin). After about 1750, Peking opera's two primary tunes, Xipi and Erhuang, were borrowed from Han opera. Beijing opera arose from various folk troupes. It caters to the general public. Western operas began in Italy in the late 16th century. Nobles were the only ones who got to see the performances.Significance of kabuki in Japanese culture and tradition Kabuki was not only a source of entertainment and spectacular performances, but it was also a source of the most up-to-date dress trends. During the Edo era, Kabuki was so popular that performances were held from morning to night. Love, moral issues, and historical events were all handled in Kabuki plays. The shamisen, biwa, and other traditional Japanese musical instruments are frequently used to accompany actors who talk in monotones. Kabuki is a showmanship-rich art form. A distinctive element of a kabuki performance is that what is on display is frequently simply a portion of a larger tale (usually the best part). As a result, it's a good idea to learn a bit about the plot before going to the performance to get the most out of it.Significance of peking opera in Chinese cultureis also called as "National Opera" and is a popular way to depict traditional Chinese culture. It's also the most popular type of Chinese opera, with a variety of aesthetic aspects serving as icons of traditional Chinese culture.

8. Musical characteristics of Kabuki,Peking Opera,and Wayang Kulit?​


Answer:

KABUKI

- Kabuki theatre (歌舞伎) features highly-stylized dancing, singing and elaborate make-up worn by a predominately all-male cast. Musically, it highlights the Nagauta form (often associated with shamisen).

PEKING OPERA

- The accompaniment for a Beijing opera performance usually consists of a small ensemble of traditional melodic and percussion instruments. The lead melodic instrument is the jinghu, a small high pitched two string spike fiddle. The jinghu is the primary accompaniment for performers during songs

WAYANG KULIT

- The three acts in wayang kulit performance parallel the mood progression of the three modes of music: Pathet Nem, Pathet Sanga, and Pathet Manyura. These three acts also symbolize stages and spiritual developments in the life of the story's hero.

21. kabuki makeup is for kesho what is for peking opera?


Peking opera make up

The actors in the 'Dan' and 'Sheng' roles wear relatively simple makeup, regarded as beautifying makeup, in a process that takes less than an hour. It consists of a layer of white oil-based makeup, followed by peach-red rouge from the eyebrows down to the eyes and cheeks, then water-based black ink is used to draw thick black lines around the eyes and eyebrows, and finally the lips are highlighted with color.

◾Red face paint indicates devotion, bravery and uprightness;

◾Black indicates either a rough and bold character, or an impartial and selfless personality;

◾Blue represents someone who is steadfast in principle, fierce and astute;

◾A character with a green face is impulsive and violent and acts with stubbornness and a lack of self-restraint;

◾A yellow face is a sign of fierceness, ambition and cool-headedness;

◾White face paint suggests treacherousness, suspiciousness, and craftiness;

◾Gold and silver colors usually indicate gods or spirits.

27. kabuki theater peking opera and wayang kulit​


Answer:

Kabuki is a Japanese traditional theatre art that is performed in a stylized manner which combines acting, singing and dancing. This rich blend of music, mime, dance, costume, and props and has been in existence for almost four centuries. The term Kabuki in modern Japanese means: ka, “song”; bu, “dance”; and ki, “skill.” It is a highly play that actors show their wide range of skills in visual and vocal performance.

In wayangkulit the dalang sings the mood songs (suluk) at regular intervals during performance. Suluk are never accompanied by the full gamelan ensemble. Often a single instrument accompanies the singer, never more than three or four. The major dramatic function of gamelan music is to accompany stage action. Entrances, exits, and fight scenes are executed in time to gamelan music.

Peking opera also known as the Beijing opera still follows traditional Chinese arts in stressing meaning, rather than precise actions. The opera artists’ goal is to make every performance exceptionally beautiful in every movement they will make. The performer’s vocal techniques as well as rhythms and melodies vary depending on his or her thoughts and feelings adhering to basic, strict conventions that are based on real life experiences presented in an artistic and symbolic manner.
