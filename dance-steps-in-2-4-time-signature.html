Answer:

Explanation:

Here is a step-by-step pattern for Horra Arabi dance:

(a) Start with your right foot and take three steps forward.

(b) On the fourth step, lift your left foot and hop on your right foot.

(c) Take three steps backward starting with your left foot.

(d) On the fourth step, lift your right foot and hop on your left foot.

(e) Repeat steps (a) to (d) continuously while moving in a circle formation.

It is recommended to perform Horra Arabi dance with a group of people, holding hands and moving in unison to the music. Have fun practicing and dancing together!

Answer:

Steps in 2/4 time signature:

1. The Two-Step: This is a basic dance step that involves taking two steps in one direction and then two steps in the opposite direction.

2. The Polka: This is a lively dance step that involves taking a quick step with one foot followed by a quick step with the other foot.

3. The Box Step: This is a simple dance step that involves taking four steps in the shape of a square.

4. The Charleston: This is a popular dance step from the 1920s that involves kicking one leg forward while swinging the other leg back.

5. The Jive: This is a fast-paced dance step that involves hopping and skipping while moving forward and backward.

Steps in 3/4 time signature:

1. The Waltz: This is a classic ballroom dance step that involves taking three steps in a box pattern.

2. The Minuet: This is a traditional dance step that originated in France and involves taking three small steps forward and then three small steps backward.

3. The Mazurka: This is a Polish folk dance step that involves taking three quick steps followed by one slower step.
