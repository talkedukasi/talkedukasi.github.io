26. How heat can be transformed into light energy, light energy into heat, efectrical energy into sound energy​


Heat into light energy: Heat can be transformed into light energy through a process called incandescence, where a material is heated to a high temperature, causing it to emit visible light. This is how traditional incandescent light bulbs work.

Light energy into heat: Light energy can be transformed into heat through a process called absorption. When light energy is absorbed by an object, the energy is converted into thermal energy, causing the object to heat up. This is why dark-colored objects absorb more light and heat up more than light-colored objects, which reflect more light.

Electrical energy into sound energy: Electrical energy can be transformed into sound energy through a process called transduction. This involves converting the electrical energy into mechanical energy, which causes a vibrating object, such as a speaker cone, to move back and forth, creating sound waves in the air. These sound waves carry the energy of the electrical signal as audible sound that we can hear. This is how speakers and headphones work.
