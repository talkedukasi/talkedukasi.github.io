Once upon a time, a country-man came into the town on market day, and brought a load of very special pears with him to sell. While he was crying up hi fruit, a poor old, ragged, hungry-looking priest stopped just in front of the barrow and very humbly begged him to give one of the pears. But the countryman refused. The countryman has hundreds of pears but the priest only asks for one and he didn’t give any. The constable of the market pulled some cash out of his purse, bought a pear and gave it to the priest. 

              For he was afraid that the noise would come to the ears of the mandarin. The old priest took the pear with a low bow. He has no home, no family, and no food because he gave up everything to become a priest. He can’t take it how people can be so selfish to refuse to five him one single pear. He ate the pear given by the constable, only leaving single pip. 

        Then he took a pick, dug a deep hole in the ground, and planted the pip. He then asked for hot water to pour on the pip. While he was pouring they saw first as tiny green sprout, then one leaf uncurled, and the shoots kept growing taller and taller; then there stood before them a young tree with a few branches and a few leaves; and ripe,, huge sweet-smelling pear. The priests face shone with pleasure, and the crowd roared with delight when he picked the pears.  

      Then the priest took the pick again, hacked at the tree until it fell with a crash .All the time has been going on, the countryman quite forgetting his barrow. When the old priest has gone and the crowd was getting thin, he turned to see his barrow and saw with horror that it was empty.

12. The Moral lesson of the Wonder tree


This folktale tries to impart the moral lesson of generosity and empathy. 
In this story, Zuleika grew up in the desert in a safe and a loving home environment. Upon learning that a highly respected man will visit their place, she wanted this man to have a good experience and perspective about their home. Even though she did not know this man personally, she is willing to extend her generosity. She did not think highly of herself. She thought of the man's welfare given that he will be travelling all the way from a very far place. From this behavior, we can sense that the main character knows how to consider the feelings of others. She can empathize with other people even if she is a young person.

The Wonder Tree:

Far out the dessert there was a tribe not so large in numbers, it was led by Ali Ben Ahmed. One moment the chief’s daughter ran towards him with anticipation and pointed out someone who rides in the dessert at that time of night.  

With their tradition to be very welcoming with strangers they called out to the him. They greeted the stranger and bowed very low as a show of high respect. The stranger returned their greetings and informed them that Ben Nedi will visit their tribe.

Excitement rose from every person in the tribe, they were preparing everything for the great Sheik Ben Nedi. They were also preparing valuable gifts to offer to him when he arrives. But, little Zuleika was crying in the corner of the tent thinking that she doesn’t have any to offer Ben Nedi. Her mother tried to cheer her up but she ran towards a well.

There she heard a soft voice saying she should stop crying as she will have something to offer the Sheik. The woman who Zuleika knew to be a fairy said to come back tomorrow where her tears fell. The next day, Zuleika was amazed on what sprung out of the sand it was a Wonder Tree and informed her father.
