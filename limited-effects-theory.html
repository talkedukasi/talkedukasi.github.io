2. why photoelectric effect is limitation of electromagnetic wave theory ​


➛Question:

why photoelectric effect is limitation of electromagnetic wave theory

Answer:

The photoelectric effect is a phenomenon in which electrons are emitted from a material when it is exposed to electromagnetic radiation, such as light. This effect was first observed by Heinrich Hertz in 1887, and it was later explained by Albert Einstein in 1905. According to classical electromagnetic wave theory, the energy of a wave is proportional to its amplitude ( the height of the wave). Therefore, if a wave with sufficient energy is directed at a material, it should be able to eject electrons from the material. However, experiments showed that the photoelectric effect only occurred when the frequency (color) of the incident radiation was above a certain threshold, regardless of its amplitude. This was a challenge to the classical wave theory, which predicted that the intensity of the radiation (which is proportional to the amplitude) should be the determining factor for the photoelectric effect.
