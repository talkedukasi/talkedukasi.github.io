3. 5. A jaguar travels a distance of 50 miles in 1.5 hours. If the jaguar runs with an acceleration of 2 miles/h?, and reaches a final velocity of 60 mi/h, what is the jaguar's initial velocity?


Explanation:

We can solve this problem using the following kinematic equation:

distance = (initial velocity × time) + (0.5 × acceleration × time^2)

We are given that the distance traveled by the jaguar is 50 miles, the time taken is 1.5 hours, the acceleration is 2 miles/h^2, and the final velocity is 60 mi/h. We are asked to find the initial velocity.

First, we can use the final velocity and the acceleration to find the time it took for the jaguar to accelerate from its initial velocity to its final velocity:

final velocity = initial velocity + acceleration × time

60 mi/h = initial velocity + 2 mi/h^2 × t

t = (60 mi/h - initial velocity) / 2 mi/h^2

Next, we can substitute this expression for time into the kinematic equation:

50 miles = (initial velocity × 1.5 hours) + (0.5 × 2 miles/h^2 × ((60 mi/h - initial velocity) / 2 mi/h^2)^2)

Simplifying and solving for the initial velocity:

50 miles - 1.5 hours × 60 mi/h + 0.5 × ((60 mi/h)^2 - 2 × initial velocity × 60 mi/h + initial velocity^2) = 0

Simplifying further and rearranging:

0.5 × initial velocity^2 - 30 × initial velocity - 20 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, we get:

initial velocity = (-(-30) ± sqrt((-30)^2 - 4 × 0.5 × (-20))) / (2 × 0.5)

initial velocity = (30 ± sqrt(900 + 40)) / 1

initial velocity = 25.92 mi/h or -5.92 mi/h

Since the jaguar is accelerating and its final velocity is greater than its initial velocity, we reject the negative solution. Therefore, the jaguar's initial velocity is approximately 25.92 mi/h.

6. Three gorillas and two jaguars are exposed side by side in a zoo. The number of ways they can be presented so that the jaguars are next to each other is:a. 2 b. 4 c. 24 d. 48 e. 120​


Answer:

The answer is d. 48.

Step-by-step explanation:

There are 5 animals total and the two jaguars must be next to each other, so we can think of them as one unit. Now we have 4 units (3 gorillas and 1 unit of 2 jaguars) that we need to arrange.

To find the number of ways these 4 units can be arranged, we can use the formula for permutations of n distinct items: n! (n factorial). In this case, n = 4, so 4! = 4 x 3 x 2 x 1 = 24.

However, since the 2 jaguars are the same animal and are next to each other, we have to divide by 2! (2 factorial) to correct for overcounting.

So the final answer is: 24 / 2! = 24 / 2 = 12.

So the number of ways the jaguars can be next to each other is 12*2 = 48

COMBINATION

This is a question that we will answer using the concepts of combination. We conclude that it is possible to organize the gorillas and the jaguars in 4 different ways in which the jaguars are together.

So there are 4! = 24 possible combinations.

To better understand the answer, consider the following explanation:

Combining gorillas and jaguars;

Let's call the gorillas G and the jaguars O, to represent the combinations. To arrange them so that the two ounces are together, the possibilities are:

O O G G G

G O O G G

G G O O G

G G G O O

There are 4 ways to organize them, therefore:

[tex]4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24[/tex]

Therefore, the correct answer is c. 24 combinations.

20. A jaguar jumps 290m indistance. Determine the time it take for the Jaguar to jump 20 seconds with a velovity of meters persecond formula: t=d/v​


Answer:

The given formula is:

t = d/v

where,

t = time taken (in seconds)

d = distance jumped (in meters)

v = velocity or speed (in meters per second)

Using the given formula, we can calculate the time it takes for the jaguar to jump 290 meters:

t = d/v

t = 290 m / (20 m/s)

t = 14.5 seconds

Therefore, it takes 14.5 seconds for the jaguar to jump a distance of 290 meters with a velocity of 20 meters per second.
