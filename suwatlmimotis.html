2. How is suwatlmimotis made?


Answer

The comb is carved from wood. It is then decorated with glass beads and pieces of mirrors which are glued onto the comb by using a sticky, rubbery sap from a Sicoco Tree. The Suwatlmimotis has 18 bead strings of different length hanging downwards from the comb. On top of the headpiece is a tail made of horsehair which is glued by using Sicoco sap.

Other Info

The Suwatlmimotis is a wooden comb with glass beads that hangs down from the comb when worn at the back of the head. It is a headpiece accessory of the T'boli tribe along with the Hilot brass belt. The Suwatlmimotis is worn by the women of the T'boli tribe.

The sacred scripture of Islam, Qurn (Arabic: "Recitation"), is sometimes written Quran and Koran. Kulintang

Kulintang is a modern title for an old instrumental type of music consists of a row of small, horizontally arranged melodic gongs accompanied by bigger, suspended gongs and drums.

Suling

The suling is an end-blown flute that is employed in Indonesian gamelan ensembles. Giant gamelans, which may contain over fifty instruments and include flutes, drums, stringed instruments, large hanging gongs, and ranks of potlike gongs, are used for palace and other major ceremonies.

Sarimanok

The mythological bird known as the Sarimanok has become an icon of Maranao art. It is portrayed as a colorful fowl with a feathered tail and a fish in its mouth or talons. Scroll, leaf, and spiral designs adorn the head in abundance. It's thought to be a sign of good fortune.

Cariñosa

The cariosa, which means "lovely" or "affectionate," is a colonial-era Philippine folk dance from the Maria Clara suite, in which the fan or handkerchief plays an important part in putting the couple in a romantic situation. The cariosa originated on Panay Island and was introduced to the Philippines by the Spaniards during their colonization. It is related to various Spanish dances, such as the bolero, as well as the Mexican jarabe tapatio, or Mexican hat dance.

Eustress

Eustress is a psychological, physical, or biochemical/radiological stress that is helpful. Endocrinologist Hans Selye invented the word, which combines the Greek prefix eu-, which means "good," with stress, which literally means "good stress." Eustress can be defined as the thrill of a roller coaster ride, a scary movie, or a fun task.

Bipolar disorder, formerly known as manic depression, is a mental health illness that involves emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression) (depression). You may feel gloomy or hopeless when you are depressed, and you may lose interest or pleasure in most activities. 15. BRIEF ANSWER OFT'BOLI ACCESSORIES1.HILOT2.SUWATLMIMOTISBRASS MAKING3.KULINTANG4.GANDANG5.DUAHANAGONG​


SPIRIT OF THE T’NALAK

The belief system around the creation of textiles is a world where mortals and spirits intermingle as dramatized in rituals of appeasement and penance, and in the belief in life after death. Weavers use materials coming from the natural environment and these materials are grass, pineapple fiber, pandan leaves, banana fiber, sinamay, and abaca. All these are regarded as sacred because they contain a spirit. When a weaver tries to get inspiration from nature, the inspiration is from a spiritual world.

The T’boli, the indigenous people of Lake Sebu in the highlands of South Cotabato, Mindanao, design the abaca tie-dyed t’nalak textile. The t’nalak are woven dreams. It’s tie-dye patterns are handed from mother to daughter, or bestowed on the weaver in dreams by Fu Dalu, the female spirit of the abaca. Like the t’nalak, the artist tries to preserve the original nature of the material as much as possible, as shown by the undyed threads and the feel of the finished product.

This devotion to the natural world is a reminder of the sacredness that it possesses. This is true to many other art forms of the Philippines, being able to distinguish where all created art is derived from is the continuous honoring of the gifts of life.
