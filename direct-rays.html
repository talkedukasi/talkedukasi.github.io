4. opposite rays are two rays that share the same endpoint that take off in opposite directions​


Answer:

An angle is formed by two rays with a common endpoint. Each ray is called an arm of the angle. The common endpoint is called the vertex of the angle.

Step-by-step explanation:

A pair of opposite rays are two rays that have the same endpoint and extend in opposite directions. Rays are always named with two points and the first point in the name must be the endpoint. So, when you name opposite rays, the first letter in the name of both rays must be the same.

The direction of light ray is always in a straight line. You can't change that straight line.

Explanation:

Answer:

Light is an electromagnetic wave and the straight line paths followed by narrow beams of light, along which light energy travels, are called rays.
