Simple Past or Past Perfect?

Both the past simple and past perfect, as well as past perfect simple, express finished past activities. The simple past is used to describe past events, frequently in chronological sequence. The past perfect is used to describe things that happened before another past action (usually expressed in the simple past). In spoken English, the simple past is frequently used instead of the perfect tense.

Simple Past

Sequential past events and past actions

Example:

Jessica flew to London yesterday.First, she checked in, and then she went to the gate.Finally, the plane was ready for boarding, and Jessica got on the plane.

Signal words:

firstthen

Past Perfect

Activities and events that took place in the past before a particular point or event

Example:

As he had never traveled by plane before, he was a little nervous.He had already fastened her seatbelt when the flight attendants gave the safety demonstration.After the flight attendants had completed the safety demonstration, the plane took off.

Signal words:

alreadyup to thenbefore (that day)afteronce
