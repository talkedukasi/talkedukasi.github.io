1. My meaningful Exit from Senior High School


Senior High School

Senior high school would be easy if we make it easy and it would also be hard at the same time if we make it challenging.

For me, it was a mix of fun, sad, easy and challenging all at the same time. This is the time wherein I am about to fully decide the path that I would want to go as I am about to begin the next chapter of my life. I had so much fun during my senior year because of the experiences that I have gained and the accomplishments I achieved. I also felt sad as I am about to go to a different chapter in my life and there would be chances that the people that I have been with would also go to different places and I might not see them anymore like I normally do every day in school. I even cried during the graduation day as some will be studying outside the country and we need to bid our goodbyes.  

I also find it easy compared to the years when I first started. I have great teachers that helped me become better and understand the importance of education. I was also challenged at the same time because I will finally be more independent and have to choose the course that I will be taking up in college. It was hard at first if you really do not know what you want.  

What I did, I ask my parents if they have any suggestions of the perfect course to take in college and even asked them how it is to be a college student. The reason why I did that is to prepare myself and somehow get ideas from their experience.  

Senior year was my favorite year above all – I experienced how great prom is, even get the chance to dance with my friends. Also, that was the first time that my parents allowed me to go out with my friends and had fun with the after party.

Senior year made me become more confident with myself. I can say that I was able to develop and discovered my strengths and my weaknesses. It helped me more to know what I want to be in life and to know my purpose. Our school even provided us an event and invited different successful people to share their knowledge and give us tips on how to become successful in life. These individuals really helped us to be more determined and to strive hard to reach our goals.  

Life indeed is not that easy. The moment we get out and face the real world, we need to be strong and determined to be able to survive. There would be a lot of challenges that we never get to understand yet until we get to experience it ourselves. So, I am really grateful enough that I was able to gain all the knowledge and strength during my senior year and finally decided the path that I want to take in college.

7. Struggles of senior high high school student on the transition from online classes to face-to-face learning?​


[tex] \small \tt{ \green{Senior \: High \: Struggles}}[/tex]

The transition from online classes to face-to-face learning can be challenging for senior high school students in several ways. Here are some of the struggles they may face:

Social anxiety: After being isolated and working independently for so long, students may feel anxious about going back to a social environment. They may have difficulty connecting with their classmates and teachers, especially if they are new to the school or class.

Learning in a new environment: Students may feel disoriented in a new physical setting and may need time to adjust to the new learning environment. They may need to get used to new classrooms, schedules, and routines.

Distractions: In a traditional classroom setting, there may be more distractions such as noise, other students, or even the physical classroom itself. Students may find it difficult to focus and concentrate on their studies.

Time management: Students may need to adjust their time management skills to accommodate the new schedule and workload. They may need to prioritize their tasks and assignments differently from what they were used to during online classes.

Increased workload: In some cases, students may be given more work to do in a face-to-face setting than they were during online classes. This can be overwhelming for some students, especially if they are also dealing with the other challenges mentioned above.

It's important for teachers and parents to support students during this transition period by providing guidance and resources to help them adjust to the new learning environment. It may also be helpful to provide opportunities for students to connect with their classmates and teachers in a safe and comfortable setting, such as through extracurricular activities or one-on-one meetings.

Lack of social skills - Senior high school students who have been attending online classes for a long time may feel awkward when they return to face-to-face learning. They may struggle to engage in conversations or interact with their classmates and teachers.

Transportation issues - Students who live far from school may face transportation issues, such as limited public transportation, increased travel time, or the need to spend more money on transportation expenses.

Academic pressure - The transition from online classes to face-to-face learning may add to the stress of senior high school students, especially if they are struggling academically. They may also feel overwhelmed by the sudden change in the learning environment and the added workload.

Sabotaged routines - Senior high school students may find it a challenge to readjust their daily routines, such as waking up earlier, traveling to school, and engaging in physical activities. This can result in fatigue and decreased productivity.

Fear of infection - The current pandemic remains a legitimate concern for students and their families. Some may experience anxiety about the risk of contracting the virus, especially if they have not yet been vaccinated.
