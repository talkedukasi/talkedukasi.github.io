11. differentiate contemporary arts practices to contemporary art techniques​


Answer:

Contemporary art is often looked at with a skeptical eye. The truth about contemporary art is that it uses techniques that most people aren’t quite used to, causing that skepticism to come into play. However, these techniques are actually quite difficult and all deserve our credit. It’s an incredible feat to create a piece of contemporary art, especially using these techniques. They challenge the mind and offer unique perspectives in a way that art has never been able to do before. For that reason, they’re some of the most valuable techniques that a person can experience through artwork. Here are three techniques that are among the most relevant in contemporary art today.

1. MINIMALISM

One of the artistic movements that has been most relevant throughout the contemporary art movement is minimalism. Minimalism seeks to take away what’s unnecessary and leaving only what’s essential. Minimalism is incredibly difficult and has even made its way into being a huge part of branding and design for companies all over the world. This is probably the most important part of contemporary art, as it has informed so much of society. Have you noticed that branding has gotten more minimalist over the past ten years or so? You can thank the contemporary art movement for doing that.

2. FOUND OBJECTS

Taking something that people view as useless, and then using it in a unique way in order to make artwork is one of the most important movements that currently exist in contemporary artwork. The idea is that things that we often view as junk are actually more valuable than we think that they are. Everything you find can be used to tell a story, or to create new diverse perspectives that we’re looking for when we attend an art exhibitions. The found objects movement is one of the most important movements in all of contemporary art currently.

3. LARGE-SCALE ART

Walter De Maria created an art piece in 1977 that included a four hundred steel poles over one mile by one kilometer. That’s incredible. Creating something huge to express diverse perspectives in artwork is incredibly popular, even today. Over the past forty years, large scale art, and art that includes the environment has come into its own as one of the most important movements within contemporary art. That’s why you often see modern art pieces alongside buildings, or as huge structures out in the open.

CONTEMPORARY ARTWORK RELIES ON CERTAIN TECHNIQUES

With every new age of art, there are new unique techniques that define how the movement develops. For contemporary art, among many techniques, you can find techniques such as minimalism, found objects, and large scale paintings. Each of these bring their own unique perspective into the artistic community, and each is valued as a pick for a technique that is most influential in contemporary art. Look out for these when you’re out at an exhibition next, you’ll find that they’re much more relevant than you perhaps think that they are.

19. Contemporary art as reflection of contemporary culture and society


Answer:

Contemporary art as a reflection of contemporary culture and society.

Artists are historians, and their work serves as a diary. Without a doubt, art is a mirror of the times as well as an expression of beauty and ingenuity. This is also one of the primary reasons why art is a dynamic medium. Art's evolution may be seen in all of its forms. From the worlds of music, dance, and stage performances to the visual arts, such as sculptures and paintings, there has been a shift in standards and culture.

A work of art is a reflection of the artist's personality. It depicts his thoughts, feelings, and passions. Religion, battle, and power were all major subjects for medieval artists, and they can be seen in almost every piece of medieval art. As it ushered in the twentieth century, Impressionism provided us with the calm, vibrancy, and color of the world, long before it was confronted with the terrible reality of inequality and conflict.

Many people consider the arts to be trivial and unimportant in comparison to the technologies that are revolutionizing the world. Artists aren't revered in the same way that engineers and doctors are.

Art is a social reflection. Because the arts encompass a wide range of disciplines such as painting, music, literature, dance, and other forms of creative expression, they have a substantial impact on society, religion, and education, and hence deserve the same respect accorded to the sciences.

Art evolves to represent society's new advances as it expands and grows. Art is a reflection of our history and a record of a significant aspect of our life.

If you've ever studied literature, you'll notice that it reflects the time period in which it was written. When writers write, they are impacted by whatever is going on in their culture at the time. For instance, Jeffrey Eugenides' novel "The Marriage Plot" was a meditation on how the concept of marriage as a driving storyline, which was popularized by authors such as Jane Austen, is no longer the same driving force today.

His writing reflects a society where divorce is all too common, and his personal perspective reflects our culture's marital values. People in the future will read Eugenides' story and gain an understanding of what society valued prior to the future generation.

28. How does tradition become contemporary, and the contemporary traditional?​


Answer:

1.

Anything traditional can become contemporary by reinterpreting its function, use, pragmatism, or even ethics and morality. Sometimes traditions outwear their longevity because the original reason for their being no longer applies. Example: it used to be traditional that people used outhouses to go to the toilet; not a good tradition if it was sub-zero temperatures in the night! Now people have WCs (water closets) installed in the interior of their homes.

2.

However, folk lore and folk wisdom can be embedded in folk traditions - which can reappear on a cyclic basis, such as the re-acceptance of eating garlic on a daily basis, or the re-emergent herbalism.

3.

I have heard of one family’s tradition that they always had to use a square roasting pan for their Sunday roast. After a few generations of the family had done it, someone thought to ask why? It appears there was no good reason for doing it now - it was initially done because the great grandmother had an oven that couldn’t accept a rectangular pan. The contemporary version of that tradition was to go out and purchase a more accommodating roasting pan.
