Answer:

Restructuring is the process of re-organizing something to make it more efficient and effective. It involves changing the structure of something to make it more suitable for a particular purpose. Restructuring can involve changing the way something works, such as introducing new policies, procedures, or systems. It can also involve changing the way an organization is structured, such as re-organizing departments and changing reporting lines. Restructuring can also involve introducing new roles, changing job titles, and altering the way people work together. Restructuring can be used to make an organization more efficient, to create new opportunities for growth, and to reduce costs. When it comes to education, restructuring could involve changing the curriculum, introducing new technologies, or altering the way teachers and students interact. It could also involve introducing new policies and procedures, such as assessment and reporting methods, or it could involve changing the way schools are managed and governed. Whatever the changes, the goal of restructuring is to make the educational system more effective and efficient.

15. changes of curriculum during the period of revolution


Answer:

During the period of revolution in the Philippines, there were significant changes made to the educational system and curriculum. Here are some of the changes that were made:

1. Introduction of nationalistic curriculum - The curriculum during the revolution period shifted towards a more nationalistic approach, emphasizing the importance of Philippine culture and history. This was in response to the desire of Filipinos to break free from the oppressive colonial rule of Spain.

2. Establishment of schools and universities - During the revolution, many schools and universities were established in the Philippines, with the aim of educating Filipinos and providing them with the skills necessary to contribute to the growth and development of the country.

3. Integration of vocational training - The curriculum during the revolution period also included vocational training, aimed at providing Filipinos with the skills necessary to work in various industries and to become self-sufficient.

4. Use of the vernacular language - The use of the vernacular language, such as Tagalog, was promoted in the curriculum in order to make education more accessible to Filipinos who were not proficient in Spanish, which was the language of instruction during the Spanish colonial period.

5. Shift towards secular education - The curriculum during the revolution period also shifted towards secular education, with less emphasis on religious instruction and more focus on practical skills that could be applied in everyday life.

These changes in the curriculum during the period of revolution were aimed at empowering Filipinos and giving them the tools necessary to create a strong and independent nation. The focus on nationalistic and practical education helped to instill a sense of pride and self-reliance among Filipinos, which continues to influence the educational system in the Philippines today.
