12. ProjectCompilation05, Short2) Write downa) Character.englishc Plot&. Conflict& themeofstoriesHS6) setting place.timeShortstorieselements​


a) Character(s):
In short stories, there can be one or more characters. The main character is typically the protagonist, and there may be other supporting characters. The characters may be developed in varying levels of detail, depending on the length of the story.

b) Language:
The language used in a short story is typically English or the language of the writer's choice.

c) Plot:
The plot of a short story is the sequence of events that make up the story. It typically includes an introduction, rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution.

d) Conflict:
The conflict is the central problem or challenge faced by the protagonist in the story. There can be internal or external conflicts, and they can be resolved in different ways.

e) Theme:
The theme is the underlying message or idea that the story conveys. It can be a universal or timeless idea that resonates with readers.

f) Setting:
The setting is the time and place where the story takes place. It can be a specific location or a more general one. The setting can have an impact on the characters and their actions.

g) Time:
The time setting can refer to the time of day, the historical period, or the time period in which the story takes place. It can affect the language, attitudes, and behaviors of the characters, as well as the events of the story.

Overall, these are some of the key elements that are commonly found in short stories. However, it is important to note that the specific details and structure of a short story can vary depending on the writer's style and the specific story being told.

This capstone project focuses on promoting community service among students for the betterment of our environment. • To encourage students to participate in community service activities.

• To promote eco-friendly and sustainable practices among students.

• To foster a sense of social responsibility among students.
