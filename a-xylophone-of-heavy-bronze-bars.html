1. Saron - A xylophone made of heavy bronze bars.

2. Gender - A bronze xylophone with resonance chambers beneath it.

3. Bonang - A set of bronze bowls played by striking them with mallets.

4. Gong and Kempul - Hanging gongs that are struck with mallets.

5. Kenong and Ketuk - Single inverted bronze bowls played by striking them with mallets.

6. Gambang - A wooden xylophone with bamboo resonators.

7. Rebab - A two-stringed fiddle.

8. Suling - A bamboo flute.

9. Kendang - A horizontal drum beat with the fingers on both ends.

10.Tjelempung - A zither with thirteen double strings.

Answer:

1. Saron: A xylophone made of heavy bronze bars with a distinctive sound. It is played with mallets and produces a bright, metallic tone.

2. Gender: A bronze xylophone with resonance chambers beneath the bars that create a buzzing effect. It is played with mallets and produces a sweet, bell-like sound.

3. Bonang: A set of bronze bowls that are struck with mallets. They produce a deep, resonant sound and are often used to provide a rhythmic foundation for the ensemble.

4. Gong and Kempul: Hanging gongs that are struck with mallets. They produce a deep, booming sound and are used to punctuate the music.

5. Kenong and Ketuk: A pair of inverted bronze bowls that are struck with mallets. They produce a sharp, ringing sound and are used to keep the beat.

6. Gambang: A wooden xylophone that is played with mallets. It produces a warm, mellow sound and is often used to play melodies.

7. Rebab: A two-stringed fiddle that is played with a bow. It produces a mournful, haunting sound and is often used to play solo melodies.

8. Suling: A bamboo flute that is played with the fingers. It produces a high, piercing sound and is often used to play solo melodies.

9. Kendang: A horizontal drum that is played with the fingers on both ends. It provides the rhythmic backbone of the ensemble and is often used to signal changes in the music.

10. Tjelempung: A zither with thirteen double strings that is played with a plectrum. It produces a bright, ringing sound and is often used to play melodies.

27. a bronze xylophone consist of 10-14 tuned metal bars is called​


Answer:

Gendèr

Explanation:

A gendèr is a type of metallophone used in Balinese and Javanese gamelan music. It consists of 10 to 14 tuned metal bars suspended over a tuned resonator of bamboo or metal, which are tapped with a mallet made of wooden disks (Bali) or a padded wooden disk (Java).


28. Finger cymbals that are made of thick and heavy bronze


Answer: Chhing finger cymbals

Explanation:

Made of thick bronze, Chhing finger cymbals are bowl-shaped, and about two inches in diameter. The player strikes them together, one in each hand. For the closed sound, chhepp, the player damps them. For the open sound, chhing, the player lets them ring.
