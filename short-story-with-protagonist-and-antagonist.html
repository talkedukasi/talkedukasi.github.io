Agata (Protagonist) 2. Elena (Antagonist) 3. Valeriya 4. Ernest 5. Vlad Setting: Modern time (Year 2020- 2023) -Rural area​


Answer:

Title: The Farmhouse Confrontation

Agata had always dreamt of owning her own farm, a place where she could grow her own food and live a peaceful life away from the city. She finally found the perfect farmhouse in a rural area and moved in, ready to start her new life.

However, she soon found out that her neighbor Elena was not pleased with her arrival. Elena had always been possessive of the land around her and didn't want anyone encroaching on it. She made it clear to Agata that she was not welcome in the area and that she would make her life difficult.

Agata tried to ignore Elena's hostility, but it soon became unbearable. Elena would constantly make noise at night, scare her animals and damage her crops. Agata decided to confront Elena and try to reason with her.

As Agata entered Elena's property, she was met by her three hired thugs - Valeriya, Ernest and Vlad. They were ready to attack her, but Agata stood her ground and refused to back down. With her quick thinking and resourcefulness, Agata managed to outsmart and overpower the three thugs.

In the end, Elena realized that she was in the wrong and apologized to Agata for her behavior. They agreed to coexist peacefully and even became friends.

Elements of Plot:

Exposition: Agata moves into her farmhouse in a rural area

Rising Action: Elena expresses her hostility towards Agata and makes her life difficult

Climax: Agata confronts Elena and her hired thugs

Falling Action: Agata outsmarts the thugs and convinces Elena to apologize

Resolution: Agata and Elena reconcile and become friends.

26. what is protagonist and antagonist in the story of the tale of woodcutter and the tiger


I think it is not quite easy to pin point who the protagonist and antagonist in the story is.

For one, the wood cutter only lied to protect himself from the tiger, so as not to be eaten.

In this particular scene, the tiger is the antagonist while the wood cutter is the protagonist.

As the story develops however, readers may feel sympathy for the tiger because he was greatly deceived by the wood cutter. So for some, because of this concept, the tiger is the protagonist while the wood cutter is the antagonist.
