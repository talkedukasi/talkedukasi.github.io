6. helen finished knitting a blanket. she wanted the length of the blanket to be 9cm longer than twice the width of the blanket. if the area of the blanket is 200cm^2, find the dimensions of the blanket? ​


Answer:

L = 25

W = 8

Solution:[tex]L = 9 +2W[/tex][tex](9+2W)(W)=200[/tex][tex]9W+2W^2=200[/tex]

Subtract 200 to both sides

[tex]9W + 2W^2 - 200 = 0[/tex]

Change the places of [tex]2W^2[/tex] and [tex]9W[/tex] to form it like [tex]ax^2 + bx + c = 0[/tex] that can be solved using quadratic formula: [tex]x = \frac{-b±\sqrt{b^2-4ac} }{2a}[/tex]

[tex]2W^2 + 9W - 200 = 0[/tex]

Thus, [tex]w = \frac{-9±\sqrt{9^2-4(2)(-200)} }{2(2)}[/tex]

[tex]w =\frac{-9±\sqrt{1681} }{2(2)}[/tex]

[tex]w =\frac{-9±41 }{4}[/tex]

We will ignore the negative answer because there are no dimensions of any figure that is negative.

Thus, [tex]w = \frac{32}{4} = 8[/tex]

Since we already found the width, we can now find the length.

[tex]L = 9 + 2W[/tex]

[tex]L = 9 + 2(8) = 25[/tex]

To check if the answer is correct, lets multiply it

25 × 8 = 200, which is the area of the blanket.

30. Which is used to transport an unconscious victim who should not be lifted due to serious injuries? a. blanket drag b. chair drag c. hammock carry d. loverâ€™s carry


The correct answer is A. Blanket Drag. A blanket drag is used to transport an otherwise unconscious victim who should not be lifted because of serious injuries. This task usually accomplished by placing the victim in a supine position (lying on his or her back) on a blanket and pulling the blanket from the floor. A chair drag, hammock or lover's carry are not appropriate because these would entail lifting the patient from the ground, which could further increase the severity of the injuries.  

When performing first aid, it is best that we know what we are doing so as to prevent further damage. One tenet of first aid is to not increase the injury further and help the patient become comfortable.
