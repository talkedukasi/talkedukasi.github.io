Anglo-Saxon law (Old English ǣ, later lagu "law"; dōm "decree, judgment") is a body of written rules and customs that were in place during the Anglo-Saxon period in England, before the Norman conquest. This body of law, along with early Scandinavian law and Germanic law, descended from a family of ancient Germanic custom and legal thought. 18. what is the social stratification system during spanish era?


The social stratification system during the Spanish colonial era in the Philippines was hierarchical and divided people into different groups based on their ancestry, race, and wealth. The system was led by the Peninsulares, followed by the Insulares, and then the Mestizos, who were further divided based on their ancestry. The system favored those with Spanish blood and placed them in positions of power and wealth.

Answer:

The caste system was hierarchical and organized individuals based on their race, ethnicity, and social status.

1. The Peninsulares, or Spaniards born in Spain, were at the top of the caste system.

2. The Insulares, who were Spaniards born in the Philippines, made up the second tier.

3. Mestizos, or people of mixed Spanish and indigenous ancestry, made up the third tier.

4. The Indios, or indigenous peoples of the Philippines, made up the fourth tier.

19. Agricultural system during the pre-colonial era, spanish era and american era


Answer:

•Pre-colonial Philippine societies relied more on swidden agriculture than intensive permanent agriculture. For example, in pre-colonial Visayas, the staple crops such as rice, millet, bananas and root crops were grown in swiddens (kaingin).

•The colonial government at this period introduced a pueblo agriculture, a system wherein native rural communities were organized into pueblo and each Christianized native family is given a four to five hectares of land to cultivate. The pueblo agriculture practiced no share cropper class or landless class.

•Realizing that being landless was the main cause of social unrest and revolt at that time, the Americans sought to put an end to the miserable conditions of the tenant tillers and small farmers by passing several land policies to widen the base of small landholdings and distribute land ownership among the greater number of Filipino tenants and farmers.
