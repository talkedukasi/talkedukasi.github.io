sketching-give a brief account or general outline of something

technical sketching-A technical sketch, which can also be referred to as a CAD, Fashion Flat, or Technical Flat, is a detailed and accurate representation of your garment. It is most often used as a part of the Tech Pack to communicate the design, construction, and stitch details of your garment to the factory

orthographic sketching- An orthographic drawing represents a three-dimensional object using several two-dimensional views of the object. It is also known as an orthographic projection

oblique sketching-The oblique sketching is a pictorial representation of an object, in which the diagram is intended to depict the perspective of objects in three dimensions

isometric sketching-Isometric Sketch In engineering education, Isometric Sketching or isometric drawing is a popular term. It is the pictorial representation of an object in which all the three-dimensions are drawn at the full scale of it. It is similar to the isometric projection. In such a case, all the lines parallel to the major axes are measurable.

lettering-the letters written or printed on something

10. Disscuss and illustrate the two types of sketch namely: A. Rough Sketch B. Final Sketch​


Answer:

A. Rough Sketch- is the first full visual prototype. They're produced quickly and without much concern for composition or even aesthetics. It's far easier in this early stage to move stuff around and switch things out, than it is later on down the line when composition and details have begun to tighten out.

B. Final Sketch- Finished product of the rough sketch. • Prepared for courtroom presentation and. often will not show all measurements and distances originally recorded on the rough sketch.

28. 1) Sketch an acute angle. Justify your work. 2) Sketch two adjacent angles. 3)Sketch two equal adjacent angles. 4)Sketch two unequal complementary adjacent angles. 5)Sketch two unequal supplementary adjacent angles. 6)Sketch two equal angles and their supplements.7) Sketch an acute angle and an obtuse angle;their compliments; their supplements. ​


Answer:

1) An acute angle is any angle that measures less than 90 degrees. For example, a triangle with angles measuring 30°, 60°, and 90° has an acute angle of 30 degrees.

2) Two adjacent angles are angles that share a common vertex and a common side. For example, two angles that share a leg on a straight line can be adjacent angles.

3) Two equal adjacent angles are angles that measure the same number of degrees and share a common vertex and a common side. For example, two angles measuring 45 degrees and sharing a leg can be equal adjacent angles.

4) Two unequal complementary adjacent angles are angles that add up to 90 degrees but measure different amounts. For example, one angle could measure 30 degrees and the other 60 degrees.

5) Two unequal supplementary adjacent angles are angles that add up to 180 degrees but measure different amounts. For example, one angle could measure 60 degrees and the other 120 degrees.

6) Two equal angles and their supplements are angles that measure the same number of degrees as each other and add up to 180 degrees. For example, two 90-degree angles and their supplements of 90 degrees would work.

7) An acute angle could measure 30 degrees and an obtuse angle could measure 120 degrees. Their complements would be 60 degrees and 70 degrees, respectively. Their supplements would be 150 degrees and 60 degrees, respectively.
