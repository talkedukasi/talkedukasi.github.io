5. construct a simple essay by comparing and contrasting how the issue (Political dynasty) was presented in different media plat forms (print, broadcast, online).​


The story of the political dynasty is a familiar one, with stories told across print, broadcast and online media channels. The means by which these channels have chosen to present this issue has been varied, however. Print media for instance has favored the straight narrative and was largely analytical in nature in order to provide background information on generational changes within the party. Broadcast media has opted for a more impartial approach. Programs have generally avoided commenting on the dynasty and have instead focused on the development of competing political dynasties within the party. Online media however has taken an interesting approach to the story, with a solid majority of articles presented as opinion pieces rather than as straight narrative. This is in line with the general trend that online media has been exhibiting, making an effort to present controversial issues from a variety of viewpoints rather than just picking one single point of view that would best suit the needs of the media outlet.

Answer:

Media bias is the bias of journalists and news producers within the mass media in the selection of many events and stories that are reported and how they are covered. The term "media bias" implies a pervasive or widespread bias contravening the standards of journalism, rather than the perspective of an individual journalist or article. The direction and degree of media bias in various countries is widely disputed.[1]

Practical limitations to media neutrality include the inability of journalists to report all available stories and facts, and the requirement that selected facts be linked into a coherent narrative.[2] Government influence, including overt and covert censorship, biases the media in some countries, for example China, North Korea and Myanmar.[3][4] Market forces that result in a biased presentation include the ownership of the news source, concentration of media ownership, the subjective selection of staff, or the preferences of an intended audience.

There are a number of national and international watchdog groups that report on bias of the media.
