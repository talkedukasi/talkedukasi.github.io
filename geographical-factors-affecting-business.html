3. Explain each external forces that affect the business environment. 1. Demografic factors . 2. Economic factors 3. Competitive Forces 4. Geographical and Ecological Environment 5. Social and Cultural Environment


Answer:

1. Demografic factors

Demographics can include any statistical factors that influence population growth or decline, but several parameters are particularly important: population size, density, age structure, fecundity (birth rates), mortality (death rates), and sex ratio (Dodge 2006). We introduce each of these in turn.

2. Economic Factors

An economic factor is a factor that can affect and influence an individuals' financial status. They include education, employment status and income.

3. Competitive Forces

Competitive forces are the factors and variables that threaten a company's profitability and prevent its growth. They are generally grouped into two categories: Direct forces that determine how low the floor can go for price competition.

4. Geographical and Ecological Environment

Environmental Geography is a branch of geography. It deals with the spatial distribution of various ecosystems, habitat, plants, animal and human life. In environmental Geography, emphasis is given to analyze the relationship between people and nature.

5. Social and Cultural Environment

The sociocultural environment refers to trends and developments in changes in attitudes, behavior, and values in society. It is closely related to population, lifestyle, culture, tastes, customs, and traditions. These factors are created by the community and often are passed down from one generation to another.

The classification of contemporary “world religions” into the two categories of missionary religions (Buddhism, Christianity, and Islam) and religions tied to specific lands (Hinduism, Judaism, Shinto, African Religions), however strained it may be, allows one to underscore the importance of geography in understanding Hinduism. To begin to “map” and “explore” the geography of Hinduism is to reckon with a vast “subcontinent.” For this, interdisciplinary tools are available, beginning with those of the geographer and the archaeologist; soon taking in the findings of linguists, historians, historians of science, and of scholars of literature, religion, and the arts; and then those of anthropologists and sociologists. As these endeavors make clear, however, the mapping of South Asia is not just a modern scholarly venture; it has also been done in some Hindu texts. We thus can expect to find a reiterative quality to the geography of Hinduism, not only across both disciplines and texts but across our efforts to table its contents. Moreover, mappings of South Asia extend beyond the subcontinent: cosmologically to the universe, and historically to periods of a “greater India” and of a Hindu diaspora.

Criminologists have long studied the role of geographical factors in the development and existence of criminal behavior. Some of the key geographical factors that have been found to contribute to criminal activity include population density, urbanization, and access to resources.

For example, research has shown that higher population density can lead to increased competition for resources, which can in turn lead to a higher prevalence of criminal activity. Urbanization, or the process of people moving from rural areas to urban centers, can also lead to increased competition for resources and higher levels of crime.

In addition, access to resources such as education, employment opportunities, and social services can play a role in the development of criminal behavior. Those who have limited access to these resources may be more likely to turn to criminal activity as a means of survival or to gain access to the resources they need.

SOCIAL AND ECONOMIC FACTORS

In addition to geographical factors, social and economic factors can also play a role in the development and existence of criminal behavior. For example, poverty and social inequality can create conditions that foster criminal activity. Those who live in poverty may be more likely to engage in criminal behavior as a means of survival or to gain access to the resources they need to improve their lives.

Furthermore, social and economic disparities can create feelings of frustration and resentment among individuals, leading some to engage in criminal behavior as a way to express their discontent. In addition, the lack of access to education and employment opportunities can lead to a lack of positive outlets for individuals to channel their energy and talents, making them more likely to engage in criminal activity.

Overall, the development and existence of criminal behavior is influenced by a complex interplay of factors, including geographical, social, and economic factors. By understanding these factors, criminologists can better understand the causes of criminal behavior and work to develop effective strategies for preventing and reducing crime.
