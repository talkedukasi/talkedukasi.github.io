2. Express one's beliefs/convictions based on the material viewed


One's beliefs based on the material viewed

Conviction demonstrates that one is utterly sure of what they say or think. It is the outcome of internalizing knowledge and belief and is significant and superior to both of them. For any useful and healthy result or consequence to be achieved, all three variables must be consistent with one another and work in harmony.

If you have solid facts to support your claims, then boldly and forcefully make your points. Your audience is more likely to agree with you when you write or speak with conviction and as though you are convinced of your point. But a conviction differs slightly from a belief. A conviction is anything that a person firmly believes in. The person must gather all the facts in order to be persuaded. A belief, as opposed to an opinion, is a conviction based on morals, values, or a personal or cultural religion. It's common to refer to assertions like "Capital punishment is legalized murder" as "opinions" because they express ideas without being supported by any facts or other proof.

The person must gather all the facts in order to be persuaded. This information forms the foundation of the conviction. Therefore, a conviction does not alter over time or in response to new information, unlike a belief. Let's look at the distinction between a belief and a conviction through this essay.
