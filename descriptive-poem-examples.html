What is poetry?

The rhythm and emphasis of a line can be based on syllabic beats, and poems can be arranged using rhyming lines and meters. Freeform poetry, which has no set framework, is another type of poem. Rhyme and meter, which are principles guiding the quantity and placement of syllables in each line, are frequently used. In poetry, sounds, pictures, and concepts that may be too complicated or abstract to properly convey are created through the combination of words.

Poetry used to be produced in accordance with rather rigorous meter and rhyme standards, with each culture having its own set of guidelines. For instance, Greek and Arabic poets used different rhyme systems and meter than Anglo-Saxon writers. Despite the continued usage of these traditional forms, contemporary poets routinely ignore all restrictions; their poems typically do not rhyme and do not follow any particular meter. These poems still aim to generate beauty via their words and have a rhythmic aspect.

A mineral

1. (Geology) A naturally occurring solid formed through geological processes. Any given mineral has a characteristic chemical composition and a specific set of physical properties.

2. (Dietary) An inorganic compound needed for proper body function and maintenance of health, for example, iron in the form of haeme present in red meat.

is an element or chemical compound that is normally crystalline and that has been formed as a result of geological processes. Examples include quartz, feldspar minerals, calcite, sulfur and the clay minerals such as kaolinite and smectite.

Talc and quartz

Talc and quartz are common minerals, distinguished by different physical properties. Talc is soft, easily scratched and has a greasy feel. Quartz is hard and has a vitreous or glass-like appearance.

Minerals are often used in the production of ceramics.

Properties of minerals

Minerals have a characteristic chemical composition and a highly ordered atomic structure.

Minerals can be readily identified by several physical properties such as hardness, lustre, streak and cleavage. For example, the mineral talc is very soft and easily scratched whereas the mineral quartz is quite hard and not so easily scratched.
