5. What is the accounting treatment when the financial statements of an associate are not prepared as of the same date as the financial statements of the investor


EQUITY METHOD

An investment in an associate is recorded using the equity method as of the day it meets the criteria for being considered an associate.

When an associate's financial statements are prepared as of the end of a reporting period different from the investor's, the gap cannot be more than three months when using the equity method. reporting comparable transactions and additional occurrences.

In any instance, there cannot be a more than three-month gap between the end of the reporting period of the associate or joint venture and that of the business. The reporting periods' lengths and any discrepancies in their endings must remain constant from one reporting period to the next.

When utilizing the equity technique, the investor consults the associate's most current financial accounts that are readily available. When the reporting date of the investor differs from that of the associate, the investor's reporting date is used to generate the associate's financial statements.

Financial forecast is the prediction of the flow of the business, whether the business will be successful or not.
