29. differentiate real estate as profession from real estate as business


Real estate as a profession typically refers to the work of licensed professionals such as real estate agents, brokers, appraisers, property managers, and other related professions. These professionals are involved in various aspects of the real estate industry, such as buying and selling properties, managing properties, conducting property appraisals, and providing advice to clients.

Real estate as a business, on the other hand, refers to the activity of buying, selling, renting, or investing in real estate with the goal of making a profit. This can involve various activities such as real estate development, property flipping, real estate investing, and property management. In this context, the focus is on generating income and building wealth through real estate transactions.

While there is some overlap between the two, the main difference lies in the primary motivation behind the activity. Real estate as a profession is primarily focused on providing professional services to clients and earning a commission or fee for those services. Real estate as a business, on the other hand, is focused on generating profits through real estate transactions.

It's worth noting that many professionals in the real estate industry also engage in real estate as a business. For example, a real estate agent may also invest in rental properties or flip houses on the side. However, the two should still be considered distinct activities with different goals and motivations.
