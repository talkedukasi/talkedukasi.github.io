Slogan about math theme Adapting, Engaging, and Applying Mathematics in the New Normal:

Even if it's hard, stay optimistic.

EXPLANATION:

The challenge faced by some applying mathematics is the lack of mastery of technology in operating various applications supporting the learning process. Therefore, there is a need for training for teachers as a form of readiness in dealing with changes in the learning process which was initially carried out conventionally face-to-face in class. But now it is done virtually using a variety of applications. Various online trainings through virtual meetings have been widely attended so as to help math teachers apply learning methods so they are not boring, especially during a pandemic.

Not only limited to providing material through virtual classrooms or social media groups, teachers and students can still interact through the virtual application. Even though during the virtual meeting we still encounter many obstacles, such as weak internet network conditions, electricity that has not yet entered certain areas and other conditions. However, this at least gives color that online learning can be carried out even though it is not optimal but still optimistic that this can encourage and arouse students' enthusiasm.

However, we need to give appreciation that teachers are still trying to provide the best service, namely educating and teaching amid the pandemic that we are now facing. We all must remain optimistic that various difficulties and obstacles in online learning will hopefully trigger the enthusiasm of teachers and education stakeholders to continue to innovate together for the advancement of education in our beloved homeland and hopefully the Covid-19 pandemic will end soon so that all aspects our life returns to normal.

Answer:

The Covid-19 pandemic interrupted Bangsaen Beach activities and disturbed livelihoods of small business operators. Before the pandemic, Bangsaen tourism experienced issues of beach quality degradation due to overcrowding, competitive vending, and lack of diversification. After the pandemic, the municipality imposed new regulations, reset zones to safeguard the public health, and jump-started the economy. The changing regulations created conflicts with vendors on zoning rearrangements and reduced sales. The author conducted a survey to investigate the beach activities, the local small business operations, and the local authority’s regulation changes. The survey results indicated that Bangsaen needs alternative attractions to complement its beach activities and to help cope with traffic congestion. The results also find out the social disparity in the demography of the vendors, which calls for attention to gender aspects and inclusive facets in the social infrastructure development strategy. This study suggests that vendors collaborate collectively with the local government to challenge the appropriation of beach spaces and to create innovative tactics. In addition, destination management organizations need to strive for better collaboration with small business operators to help them adapt to the change and enter the formal economy.
