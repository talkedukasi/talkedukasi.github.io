18. what are the advantage and disadvantage of homogenous and heterogeneous culture​


Answer:

There are others, but the main advantage of a homogeneous culture that I can think of is this: Simply, people trust one another implicitly and easily due to a basic day-to-day understanding of each other’s humanity.

When a group has all grown up with the same media, and in roughly the same conditions, and with roughly the same manners and understanding of the way the culture’s systems work, it is easy for them to empathize with each other. People tend to be kinder to those whom they understand well, and in a homogeneous culture, you understand most people pretty well.

When a slight is committed, others tend to be more charitable to the perpetrator. In these cultures, there is a general belief that people are largely “good” with only a few “bad apples” to blame for most of society’s problems.

The disadvantage to this is that since most people are brought up similarly, they think similarly. There is little room for innovation or free thought, and in some cases those who don’t toe the line will find themselves ostracized.

This leads to a general stagnation of the society and possibly an amplification of humanity’s natural xenophobia and racism.

What do you think are the advantages and disadvantages of homogenization of culture? how about heterogenization?

There are others, but the main advantage of a homogeneous culture that I can think of is this: simply, people trust each other instinctively and conveniently because of a simple everyday awareness of each other's humanity.  When a population has all grown up in the same media, and under nearly the same conditions, and with roughly the same etiquette and knowledge of how the institutions of culture operate, it is simpler for them to empathize with each other. People seem to be kinder to those they understand, and in a homogeneous society, most people understand you fairly well. When a slight is committed, people appear to be more sympathetic to the perpetrator. There is a common misconception in these cultures that people are mostly "healthy" with just a few "poor apples" to blame for much of society's problems. Advantages

1. Smoother and potentially improved communication efficiency (though it is possible the distance forces one to evaluate one's own culture more closely, and to ultimately improve how well you express its values).

2. More global collaboration on the basis of mutual vision of the environment, shared goals, shared methodologies for achieving them. Global collaboration is likely to involve a degree of similarity, ideals are expressed in common societies.

3. A wider demand for entertainment (music, media, etc.) that responds to common desires and fads. Achieved economies of scale. Many choices for customers.

4. Greater predictability of the behavior of others.

Disadvantages

1. Less variety of proposals. Different societies are powerful tests to see how a society might function and offer examples to test current values and the status quo. They offer inspiration for changes within a given society.  

2. Less freedom to leave your own society if you don't like it.

3. Flameness of uniformity. Many of them are curious to learn how a radically new method operates.

4. There may be an aspect of "specialization" that arises from possessing diverse cultures. There may then be a net economic loss from this aspect (in isolation)
