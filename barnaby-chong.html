13. Mr Chong deposited 1 000 into his savings account that gives an interest rate of 5% per annum and compounded once every half year. How much is Mr Chong's savings at the end of the third year?​


To solve this problem, we can use the formula for compound interest:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:
A = the amount of money at the end of the investment period
P = the principal amount (the initial deposit)
r = the annual interest rate (as a decimal)
n = the number of times the interest is compounded per year
t = the number of years

In this case, Mr Chong deposited $1,000 into an account that has an annual interest rate of 5%, compounded once every half year. This means that the interest rate per compounding period is 5%/2 = 2.5%, and the number of compounding periods per year is 2 (since the interest is compounded twice a year).

Using the formula above, we can calculate Mr Chong's savings at the end of the third year as follows:

A = $1,000(1 + 0.025/2)^(2*3)
= $1,000(1.0125)^6
≈ $1,159.27

Therefore, Mr Chong's savings at the end of the third year is approximately $1,159.27.

29. Case analysis Us vs. ah chong (15 Phil 488)


The case of "US vs. Ah Chong" is a landmark decision made by the Philippine Supreme Court in 1910. The case involved a Chinese citizen named Ah Chong who was working in the Philippines as a peddler of Chinese goods. Ah Chong was charged with violating a law that required all aliens to obtain a certificate of residence in order to live and work in the Philippines.

Ah Chong argued that the law was unconstitutional, as it violated his rights to due process and equal protection under the law. The Philippine Supreme Court agreed, holding that the law violated these constitutional rights.

The Court held that everyone had the right to equal treatment under the law, regardless of their citizenship. As such, the law requiring aliens to obtain a certificate of residence imposed a burden on aliens that was not imposed on citizens. Furthermore, the Court held that the law lacked a clear standard for determining who was an alien and what constituted a certificate of residence.

As such, the Court declared the law to be unconstitutional and void, and Ah Chong's conviction was overturned.

The case of "US vs. Ah Chong" set an important precedent for the protection of the rights of aliens in the Philippines. It established that all individuals, regardless of their citizenship, are entitled to the same rights and protections under the law. This ruling reflects the importance of upholding fairness and equal treatment in a just society.
