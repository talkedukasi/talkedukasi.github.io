1. what is Globalization or globalisation​


Answer:

Globalization, or globalisation

(Commonwealth English; see spelling differences), is the process of interaction and integration among people, companies, and governments worldwide. Globalization has accelerated since the 18th century due to advances in transportation and communication technology. This increase in global interactions has caused a growth in international trade and the exchange of ideas and culture. Globalization is primarily an economic process of interaction and integration that is associated with social and cultural aspects. However, disputes and diplomacy are also large parts of the history of globalization, and of modern globalization.

Economically, globalization involves goods, services, data, technology, and the economic resources of capital.[1] The expansion of global markets liberalizes the economic activities of the exchange of goods and funds. Removal of cross-border trade barriers has made the formation of global markets more feasible.[2] Advances in transportation, like the steam locomotive, steamship, jet engine, and container ships, and developments in telecommunication infrastructure, like the telegraph, Internet, and mobile phones, have been major factors in globalization and have generated further interdependence of economic and cultural activities around the globe.[3][4][5]

Though many scholars place the origins of globalization in modern times, others trace its history to long before the European Age of Discovery and voyages to the New World, and some even to the third millennium BC.[6] The term globalization first appeared in the early 20th century (supplanting an earlier French term mondialization), developed its current meaning some time in the second half of the 20th century, and came into popular use in the 1990s.[7] Large-scale globalization began in the 1820s, and in the late 19th century and early 20th century drove a rapid expansion in the connectivity of the world's economies and cultures.[8]

In 2000, the International Monetary Fund (IMF) identified four basic aspects of globalization: trade and transactions, capital and investment movements, migration and movement of people, and the dissemination of knowledge.[9] Environmental challenges such as global warming, cross-boundary water and air pollution, and over-fishing have been linked to globalization.[10] Globalizing processes affect and are affected by business and work organization, economics, sociocultural resources, and the natural environment. Academic literature commonly divides globalization into three major areas: economic globalization, cultural globalization, and political globalization.[11]

27. compare and contrast of internationalism, globalisation and globalism​


Comparison and contrast of internationalism, globalization and globalism​:

Internationalism is a movement that promotes greater economic and political cooperation between nations for the common good.Globalization is a process of international integration that occurs due to the exchange of world views, products, ideas, and other aspects of culture.Globalism is a concept that is often used to describe various globalization ideologies.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

INTERNATIONALISM

Internationalism is a movement that promotes greater economic and political cooperation between nations for the common good. Proponents of this movement, such as those of the World Federalist Movement, claim that all nations must work together because their common interests in the long term are far more important than individual needs in the short term. Internationalism is another reaction to the same situation. Like nationalism, internationalism is much more ambiguous and complex than its theoretical framework. Capitalist internationality creates nationalism and internationalism. Since Napoleon's French Revolutionary Empire rose and fell, nationalism and internationalism have been at odds with each other.

GLOBALIZATION

Globalization is a process of international integration that occurs due to the exchange of world views, products, ideas, and other aspects of culture. The progress of transportation and telecommunications infrastructure, including the emergence of the telegraph and the Internet, is a major factor in globalization which increasingly encourages the interdependence of economic and cultural activities. In the 20th century, land vehicles, intermodal transport, and airlines made transportation even faster. The invention of electronic telecommunications, such as mobile phones and the Internet, allowed billions of people to be connected in various ways in 2010.

GLOBALISM

Globalism consists of several types such as justice globalism, holy globalism, and market globalism. Market globalization includes the ideology of neoliberalism. The shrinking of the meaning of globalism into a single ideology of market globalism and neoliberalism sometimes triggers conflict. For example, in the book The Collapse of Globalism and the Reinvention of the World (2005), Canadian philosopher John Ralston Saul considers globalism to be compatible with neoliberalism and neoliberal globalization. He argues that although it has not yet had a big impact, globalization has already broken up into opposing parts and the people involved in globalization are reaffirming their national interests in both positive and destructive ways.

Answer:

1. Globalization is the process of integration and interconnectedness among people, businesses, and governments across the world. It involves the exchange of goods, services, ideas, and cultures between different countries and regions.

2. Globalization can be defined as the increasing interconnectedness and interdependence of the world's economies, cultures, and societies. It is driven by advancements in technology, transportation, and communication, and has led to greater economic growth, cultural exchange, and political cooperation among nations.

3. Metaphors that can be used to improve the definition of globalization are:

- The global village: This metaphor suggests that the world has become a single community, where people are connected and interdependent. It emphasizes the idea that people from different parts of the world are linked by common experiences, values, and aspirations.

- The butterfly effect: This metaphor suggests that small changes in one part of the world can have significant effects on other parts of the world. It emphasizes the idea that globalization has made the world more interconnected and that events in one country can have ripple effects across the globe.

- The melting pot: This metaphor suggests that globalization has led to the blending of cultures and traditions from different parts of the world. It emphasizes the idea that globalization has created a more diverse and multicultural world, where people can learn from and appreciate different perspectives and ways of life.
