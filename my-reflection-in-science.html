20. my reflection about science subject


My reflection about science subject:

Initially there was a question that came out of me, “Why should we study science? This knowledge has never been used!" then my teacher or my parents said “You are wrong son, science is very important. You'll realize it when you graduate from school." So, why are students not invited to see the attractiveness and usefulness of science at school? This is reinforced by the statement from the NSSE (Natural Study of Student Engagement) that to develop a curriculum in elementary schools, the most basic question is "What is the function of science lessons in everyday life?"

Learning activities that are adapted to everyday behavior will make learning applicable and relevant. The subject matter studied must be "relevant" by relating new information to relevant concepts contained in a person's cognitive structure. Cognitive structures are facts, concepts, and generalizations that students have learned and remembered. Relevance learning initiated by Ausubel is a learning process where students are easier to understand and learn, because teachers are able to make it easy for students so that they can easily relate experiences or knowledge that already exists in their minds. So that learning by simply receiving and memorizing (rote learning) is no longer relevant to students.

Introduction

The Career Planning for Social Science unit has been an eye-opening experience for me. I have gained a deeper understanding of myself, my strengths, and my areas of development. Through the use of career development theory, I have been able to identify my intended career goals and the steps I need to take to achieve them. In this essay, I will reflect on my learning and development in this unit and discuss how it has helped me plan for my future.

What I Have Learned About Myself

One of the key things I have learned about myself is my personality type. Using the Holland's career development theory, I have discovered that I have a social personality type. This means that I enjoy working with people and helping them to solve problems. I also value social relationships and prefer jobs that involve interpersonal communication and collaboration. This has helped me to narrow down my career options and focus on jobs that align with my personality type.

Another thing I have learned about myself is my strengths and areas of development. Through the use of self-assessment tools such as SWOT analysis and skills inventory, I have been able to identify my strengths such as problem-solving, communication, and leadership skills. I have also identified areas that need improvement such as time management and networking skills. This has helped me to develop a plan to enhance my skills and address my development needs.

My Intended Career Goals

My intended career goal is to become a social worker. The inspiration for this career choice comes from my passion for helping people, particularly those who are vulnerable and marginalized in society. I believe that as a social worker, I can make a positive impact on people's lives by providing support, advocacy, and empowerment. I have also been inspired by the stories of social workers who have made a difference in their communities and have helped to bring about social change.

What I Need to Do to Achieve My Career Goals

To achieve my career goals, I need to acquire the necessary experience, skills, attributes, and qualifications. One of the things I need to do is to gain practical experience through internships and volunteering opportunities. This will provide me with an opportunity to apply my theoretical knowledge and develop my skills in a real-world setting. I also need to develop my communication, networking, and time management skills to be an effective social worker.

In terms of qualifications, I need to complete a degree in social work and obtain a license to practice as a social worker. I also need to continue my professional development by attending workshops, seminars, and training programs to enhance my knowledge and skills. Additionally, I need to develop personal attributes such as empathy, compassion, and resilience to be able to work effectively with clients and manage the emotional demands of the job.

My Current Skills

Currently, I possess a range of skills that are relevant to my intended career as a social worker. I have strong communication skills, both verbal and written, which will be essential in working with clients, colleagues, and stakeholders. I also have good problem-solving skills, which will enable me to identify and address the needs of my clients. Additionally, I have leadership skills, which will be useful in managing teams and advocating for social change.

My PDP

My personal development plan (PDP) involves a range of activities that will help me to achieve my career goals. These include gaining practical experience through internships and volunteering, attending workshops and training programs, and developing personal attributes such as empathy and resilience. I also plan to improve my networking skills by attending networking events and building relationships with professionals in the field. Additionally, I plan to continue my education by pursuing a master's degree in social work to enhance my knowledge and skills.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Career Planning for Social Science unit has been a valuable learning experience for me. Through the use of career development theory, I have gained a deeper understanding of myself and my intended career goals. I have also developed a plan to achieve my career goals by acquiring the necessary experience, skills, attributes, and qualifications. I am confident that with the skills and knowledge I have gained in this unit, I will be able to make a positive impact on people's lives as a social worker.
