1. Setting of sorry wrong number


It is a radio act/show  in a radio station...(see https://brainly.ph/question/260951)

It is a 1940's thriller story that believed happened in U.S.

Lucille Fletcher's play originally aired on the Suspense radio program on May 25, 1943, essentially a one-woman show with Agnes Moorehead as Mrs. Stevenson.

In 2015, the May 25, 1943 broadcast was deemed "culturally, historically, or aesthetically significant" by the Library of Congress and selected for inclusion in the National Recording Registry.


The following places are the specific settings of the radio act:


New York

New York is the city in which the play is set. It is the location of the Queensboro Bridge, Mrs. Stevenson's bedroom, Murray Hill, and Second Avenue. New York is a massive city full of murders, which Duffy says the police do their best to stop.



Queensboro Bridge

Located in New York, the Queensboro Bridge is a viaduct for a railroad. Mrs. Stevenson lives near the Queensboro Bridge, and it is over the Queensboro Bridge that a train passes, drowning out her screams as she is murdered.



Murray Hill

Murray Hill is an area in New York. It is where Mr. Stevenson works. Murray Hill work number is the one that Mrs. Stevenson consistently tries, without success, to reach throughout the play.



Second Avenue

Second Avenue is located just around the corner from Mrs. Stevenson's home.

Answer:

Explanation:

Sorry, Wrong Number

1. Yes, the story is a one-act play because it only has one scene and it all happened in Mrs. Stevenson's house while she was near the telephone. (You may read more about it here: https://brainly.ph/question/1050345 )

2. Elements of Sorry, Wrong Number

a. Setting

The story is set in Mrs. Stevenson's bedroom inside her house which is located in Queensboro Bridge, New York City. But overall, the story took place in New York City as it is also where Murray Hill and Second Avenue are located. The story happened at night. (For a detailed description, you may refer here: https://brainly.ph/question/1043885)

b. Plot and plot structure

Sorry, Wrong Number is about a bedridden woman who one night kept on calling his husband's office. During the process of her call, she hears a murder plan on the phone. She then tried to call the police station was able to speak to an officer who just dismissed her report not until the actual murder happened.

c. Character and characterization

Mainly, there are two main characters in this story: Mrs. Stevenson and Sgt. Duffy.

Mrs. Stevenson can be described as fearful, panicked and troubled throughout while Sgt. Duffy is an apathetic policeman who just ignored Mrs. Stevenson's report. (You can read more on Sgt. Duffy's characterization here: https://brainly.ph/question/1987754?source=aid1370937)

d. Conflict

The story's conflict is when Mrs. Stevenson overheard the murder plan over the phone.

e. Theme

The theme of the story can be fear. It was highly built-up in the beginning of the story when Mrs. Stevenson knew about the plan to kill the woman. She grew fear for the woman's life and the fear continued when she started fearing for her own life. (Other possible themes here: https://brainly.ph/question/1056431)

f. Suspense and Atmosphere

The story's suspense is when the killer got inside of Mrs. Stevenson's house and the mystery atmosphere was completed when a train passed by and Mrs. Stevenson's scream was not heard.

3. Mrs Stevenson feels conflicted when she is connected to her husband's phone and overhears a conversation between 2 men who are plotting to kill another person. Though she makes many attempts to disclose this murder plot, no one believes her and attributes this story to her hysteria.
