8. why Islam, Christianity and Islam are regarded as abrahamic religions?​


The term "Abrahamic Religion", or the religion of Abraham, is a theoretical designation for Judaism, Christianity and Islam, where Abraham (Abraham) is seen as the ancestor of the three religions. Therefore, the discussion on Abrahamic Religion will discuss the views of the Jewish, Christian and Islamic traditions regarding Abraham, as well as the views of the three religions regarding their "founders", namely Moses, Jesus (Isa), and Muhammad.

About Abrahamic Religion

In comparative religion, the Abrahamic religions -- also often referred to as divine religions or Abrahamic religions -- are any religions that arose from a shared ancient Semitic tradition and whose adherents trace back to Abraham ("Father/Leader of many peoples" Hebrew אַבְרָהָם ("Avraham") Arabic ابراهيم ("Ibrahim"), a patriarch whose life story is told in the Hebrew Bible/Old Testament, and as a prophet in the Qur'an and also called a prophet in Genesis 20: 7.

This religion is a large group of monotheistic religions, including Christianity, Islam. Abrahamic religions represent more than half[1] of all religions in the world. However, many adherents of these religions reject this kind of grouping of their religion or belief with the notion that their religion contains ideas that differ or even contradict other religious ideas about Abraham and God or Allah.

According to Jewish tradition, Abraham was the first person from the post-flood era to reject idol worship through rational analysis (Shem and Eber continued the tradition from Noah), and because of this he appears symbolically as the fundamental figure for monotheistic religion. In this sense, Abrahamic religions can simply be called monotheistic religions, but not all monotheistic religions are Abrahamic religions. In Islam he is considered to be the first monotheist in the world, when monotheism has disappeared (Abraham is a prophet who is in a series of prophets, starting from Adam) and is therefore often referred to as Ibrahim al-Hanif or Abraham the Monotheist.

23. using the venn diagram, compare and contrast judaism and christianity.​


Answer:
Both Judaism and Christianity share some similarities and differences. Let's break it down:

Similarities:

Both religions are monotheistic, believing in one God.Both religions share the Old Testament as a sacred text.Both religions have a strong emphasis on ethics and morality, including the Ten Commandments.Both religions have a strong sense of community, with shared traditions and practices.

Differences:

Christianity believes in the Holy Trinity (Father, Son, and Holy Spirit), while Judaism believes in one God without any trinity.Christianity believes that Jesus Christ is the son of God and the savior of humanity, while Judaism does not believe that Jesus is the Messiah.Christianity observes Sunday as a day of worship, while Judaism observes Saturday (the Sabbath) as a day of rest and worship.Christianity has many different denominations and branches, while Judaism is more unified in its beliefs and practices.

Overall, while there are some similarities between Judaism and Christianity, there are also some key differences that distinguish the two religions.
