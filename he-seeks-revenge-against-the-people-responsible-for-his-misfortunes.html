18. In The Odyssey - Amphimedon, what motivates Odysseus to dress as a beggar? He is trying to be humble. He wants to surprise Penelope. He is angry at Amphimedon. He wants revenge against the suitors.


In The Odyssey - Amphimedon, what motivated Odysseus to dress as a beggar was that he was trying to surprise Penelope. The element of surprise was critical. He was trying to assess the situation at home because things could have changed. Hence, the disguise was needed. Odysseus has always acted with shrewdness and always strategic in his actions.  

But why act as a beggar?

A beggar would be inconspicuous, and he would be able to make assessments based on how a beggar is treated among other things. As a beggar, he is deemed invisible--when was the last time you remember a dealing with a beggar? When was the last time you took note of a beggar while you are walking around? This is not something that we usually take into mind. He made use of that information and tried to evaluate what had happened as he was away for 20 years. Penelope could have been with a new husband or the whole island could have turned against him at that point. This was also  a way to take care of the suitors of his wife and the suitors as they were taking advantage of his home in his absence.  

What can we learn from this?

Being strategic in our actions. We may not know what the situation is but if we take a viewpoint where we can make analysis and evaluation, we can have a good picture of the situation and act accordingly.
