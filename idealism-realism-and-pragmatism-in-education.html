6. One famous philosopher stated that "The universe exists whether the mind perceives it or not." Evaluate the classical philosophy being associated in the quotation. A. utilitarianism B. metaphysics C. realism D. idealism


Answer:

The statement "The universe exists whether the mind perceives it or not" is associated with the philosophical concept of realism. Realism is a philosophical approach that asserts the existence of an objective reality that is independent of human perception and interpretation.

Realism holds that objects and events have an independent existence outside of the mind. In other words, the world exists as it is, regardless of whether humans perceive it or not. This view stands in contrast to idealism, which suggests that the mind and its perceptions are the only reality.

Utilitarianism, on the other hand, is a normative ethical theory that suggests that the best action is the one that maximizes overall well-being or happiness. Metaphysics is a branch of philosophy that deals with the nature of reality, including the relationship between mind and matter.

Therefore, the statement "The universe exists whether the mind perceives it or not" is most closely associated with the philosophical approach of realism.

14. Briefly discuss the difference between idealism and realism as applied to education


Answer:

idealism:

Idealism is the school of educational thought promoted by Plato in 400 B.C. Plato thought that humans could be improved from within, by correcting their thoughts and discovering knowledge already there since birth. Idealism focuses on reasoning and how a person can bring knowledge up from inside of himself. In this view, the world exists solely in the minds of people and that ultimate truth relies on a consistency of ideas. The more perfect our ideas become, therefore, the better we can serve the world. In Emmanuel Kant's idealism, the world exists, but our minds are separate from it.

realism

Realism is the school of educational thought promoted by Plato's student, Aristotle. Realism holds that the only reality is the material world, that study of the outer world is the only reliable way to find truth; the world is an objective phenomenon that our minds must adhere to. We achieve greater and greater knowledge through proper study of the world. In Realism, a person is an empty vessel for knowledge, which can only come from outside of the self, through observation. This philosophy was the progenitor of the scientific method, a system of inquiry relying on objective facts.

22. Describe the ideal 21st Century graduate; and describe the education delivery mode. ​


Answer: Ideal 21st Century graduate:

Explanation:

The ideal 21st century graduate is someone who is well-rounded and adaptable, with a broad range of skills and knowledge that will enable them to thrive in an ever-changing world. They are not just experts in their field but are also skilled in critical thinking, problem-solving, collaboration, and communication.

In terms of specific skills, the ideal 21st century graduate should be proficient in technology and digital literacy, as well as possess a strong understanding of global issues and cultural awareness. They should be able to work effectively in diverse teams and have a strong sense of empathy and social responsibility.

In terms of education delivery mode, the ideal approach would be a combination of traditional classroom learning and technology-enabled learning. This could include online lectures and interactive modules that allow students to learn at their own pace, as well as in-person seminars and workshops that encourage collaboration and hands-on learning.

The education delivery mode should also prioritize experiential learning, where students have the opportunity to apply their knowledge in real-world settings through internships, service learning, or research projects. This approach helps students develop practical skills and gain valuable experience that will prepare them for the workforce.

Overall, the ideal 21st century graduate is someone who is not only well-educated but also adaptable, innovative, and able to navigate the complexities of our rapidly changing world. By embracing technology, experiential learning, and a holistic approach to education, we can help students develop the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the 21st century and beyond.

29. compare idealism and realism​


Answer:

Both idealism and realism, as philosophical terms, deal with the relationship between our minds and the world. Idealism is the view that things exist only as ideas, with no reality as material objects outside of the mind. Realism is the view that objects exist in themselves, independently of our consciousness of them.
