22. What is the summary of values education for human solidarity?


Lourdes Quisumbing started the article saying human solidarity is founded on mutual respect of each other’s uniqueness, and a deep sense of appreciation of our common humanity.

It is about globalization and its effects and inevitable links to our culture. Education is a vital system and part of an individual's life to hone his/her knowledge, appreciation, wisdom and values regarding culture, race, genderand creed; of social class, economic status, or political persuasions.
She said "Young people across cultures are in search for the deeper meanings of their existence and for enduring values and roots that provide them with stability and strength, security and hope, happiness and peace to their lives, in these rapidly-changing and confusing times. They look up to their leaders and they feel lost. A crisis of confidence is spreading throughout the world."

Finally, Ma'am Quisumbing ended the article enumerating the principles to form the core of a new global ethics which are human rights and responsibilities, democracy based on H.R. and fundamental freedoms, protection of minorities (respect for diversity), commitment to peaceful conflict-resolution and fair negotiation, and equity within and between generations.

The dog, with much discomfort and limping in one leg, tries to find the way back to her house, but she can’t. After several weeks of hunger, thirst, and cold, she lays down on old cartons to cry, remembering the happy moments she had spent with her old family.

Before falling asleep, she makes a wish to find a truly compassionate and supportive family that could help her in her difficult moments. When Sendy wakes up, she notices that she was in a very different place.

The little dog had been rescued by a noble lady who was walking down the street and noticed Sendy was sad and ill. Then, with all her love, she took Sendy to her house, where she welcomed the little dog as her new pet.
