3. it is the evolutionary process leading to the appearance of modern day humas​


Answer:

Human evolution is the evolutionary process that led to the emergence of anatomically modern humans, beginning with the evolutionary history of primates—in particular genus Homo—and leading to the emergence of Homo sapiens as a distinct species of the hominid family, which includes the great apes.

What are the evolutionary stages of human being?

The evolution of modern humans from our hominid ancestor is commonly considered as having involved four major steps: evolving terrestriality, bipedalism, a large brain (encephalization) and civilization.

What species did modern humans evolve from?

Although we did not evolve from any of the apes living today, we share characteristics with chimpanzees, gorillas, and orangutans (the great apes), as well as other apes. We most likely evolved from Homo heidelbergensis, the common ancestor we share with Neanderthals, who are our closest extinct relatives.

4. Adaptation can be defined as the evolutionary process whereby an organism becomes better able to live in its habitat. Write an essay on the importance of adaptation as a mechanism for the survival of a species.​


Adaptation is a vital mechanism for the survival of a species, allowing organisms to better cope with changes in their environment and promoting the evolution of new species. Although adaptation is not a panacea for environmental challenges, it is an essential component of the natural world and underscores the importance of biodiversity and ecological resilience.


5. WHY SPECIES IN DIFFERENT GEOGRAPHIC LOCATIONS AFFECYS ITS EVOLUTIONARY PROCESS?​


Answer:

A species occurs in a given place and time because members of this species (or its ancestors) evolved in this location or dispersed to it at some point in the past.For example, Antarctica has fewer species than a temperate deciduous forest, which in turn has fewer species a tropical rainforest.
