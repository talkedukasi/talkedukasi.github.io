3. What are the arts and crafts of Leyte, Antique, Siquijor, and Capiz?


The following are the arts and crafts of Leyte, Antique, Siquijor and Capiz.   Leyte  

Leyte is located in Eastern part of Visayas.  

They are known for their handicrafts that are made of Abacca, Coconut Shells, Pandan Leaves, Bamboo and Malave Woods. Examples of their handicrafts are Abacca Bags and Coco Shell Placemats. In Tacloban, they have Tikog Crafts. I've attached the photo samples of tikog bags made from Tacloban, Leyte.

Antique  

Antique is located in the Western part of Visayas.  

They are most famous for their weaving, particularly Piña. Piña cloths are made from the fibers of the leaves of pineapples. The piña clothing is popularly used for barongs which is the traditional menswear here in the Philippines. Attached are the example of Piña clothes and embroideries.

Siquijor  

Siquijor belongs to the Central Visayas Region.  

Like with the other Central Visayan provinces, they are known for their hand-woven baskets, woodcarvings and painting.  

Capiz  

The province of Capiz is located in the region of Western Visayas.  

They are well known for their Capiz shell crafts. Capiz Shell is found in the coastal waters of capiz and is the outer shell of a windowpane oyster. They make the following handicrafts made out of the shells: wind chimes, garlands, lanterns and other things.   Examples of the capiz shell crafts are attached.

27. Give me 5 folk crafts of Siquijor and leyte. ​


Answer:

Siquijor:

Bolo Making - Handmade knives with intricately designed wooden handles are a popular craft in Siquijor.Banig Weaving - Banig mats are woven from indigenous materials such as pandan leaves and are used for sleeping, sitting, and decoration.Coconut Shell Craft - Coconut shells are transformed into bowls, plates, and various household items through intricate carving and design.Tuba Basketry - Baskets made from woven bamboo strips are used for storing and transporting Tuba (coconut wine) in Siquijor.Folk Dance Crafts - Traditional dances such as the Kuratsa and Tinikling are popular in Siquijor and the costumes, props, and decorations used for these dances are handmade.

Leyte:

Banig Weaving - Similar to Siquijor, Leyte is also known for its banig mats woven from pandan leaves.Tinalak Weaving - Tinalak is a traditional fabric woven by the T’boli people of Mindanao and is also produced by some artisans in Leyte.Coconut Shell Craft - Leyte's artisans also use coconut shells to create various handicrafts including lamps, bowls, and vases.Talisay Weaving - Talisay leaves are used to make hats, bags, and other woven items.Beadwork - Beadwork is a popular folk craft in Leyte, and local artisans create intricate designs on clothing, jewelry, and home décor using colorful beads.
