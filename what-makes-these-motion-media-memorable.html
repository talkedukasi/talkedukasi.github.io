16. what makes extemporaneous speech different to manuscript or memorized speeches​


Answer:

Extemporaneous speaking consists of delivering a speech in a conversational fashion using notes. Manuscript speaking consists of reading a fully scripted speech. It is useful when a message needs to be delivered in precise words. Memorized speaking consists of reciting a scripted speech from memory.
