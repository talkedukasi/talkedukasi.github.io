4. What is the difference between ethical marketing and green marketing.​


ANSWER:

The major difference between green and sustainable is that "green" is only concerned with a single aspect, environmental health, while sustainability is concerned with the whole system, involving economic, social, and environmental benefits

EXPLANATION:

Green marketing refers to the practice of developing and advertising products based on their real or perceived environmental sustainability. ... When a company's green marketing activities are not substantiated by significant investments or operational changes, they may be criticized for false or misleading advertising.

Practicing ethics in marketing means deliberately applying standards of fairness, or moral rights and wrongs, to marketing decision making, behavior, and practice in the organization. In a market economy, a business may be expected to act in what it believes to be its own best interest.

Answer:

Green marketing refers to the practice of developing and advertising products based on their real or perceived environmental sustainability. 

Examples of green marketing include advertising the reduced emissions associated with a product’s manufacturing process, or the use of post-consumer recycled materials for a product's packaging. Some companies also may market themselves as being environmentally-conscious companies by donating a portion of their sales proceeds to environmental initiatives, such as tree planting.

The Use of Deceptive Practices

The ease with which a company can access and gather data about its customers can lead to deceptive practices and dishonesty in the company's research methods. This type of ethical problem can run the gamut — from not telling customers that information is being collected when they visit a website to misrepresenting research results by using faulty data.

17. Why is misrepresenting the benefits of a product an ethical issue?.


Answer:

The question of product benefit misrepresentation being an ethical issue has an obvious answer.

A producer misrepresenting the benefits of their product lacks transparency and integrity both vital to the survival of their business, hence making them unreliable and dishonest. To put it in perspective, let's say that a certain company claims that their medicine reduces the risk of heart attack by 36% through false manipulation of statistics when in reality their product is the same as any other product there is. This false manipulation of statistics can encourage consumers to buy their products without a doctor's consultation, which can lead to hidden health issues. When the time comes, there will always be the possibility of consumers reporting negative health symptoms and possibly death due to the effects of the medicine.

28. What are the ethical considerations that companies must take into account when implementing a global marketing strategy?​


Answer:

When implementing a global marketing strategy, companies must take into account the following ethical considerations:

1. Cultural differences: Companies must understand and respect the cultural differences of the countries they are marketing in, and ensure that their marketing strategies and messages are appropriate and sensitive to those cultural norms.

2. Language barriers: Companies must take into account the language barriers and ensure that their marketing messages are translated accurately and effectively, without any unintentional misunderstandings or misinterpretations.

3. Local laws and regulations: Companies must be aware of the local laws and regulations in the countries they are marketing in, and ensure that their marketing strategies are compliant with those laws and regulations.

4. Environmental impact: Companies must consider the environmental impact of their marketing strategies and ensure that they are not contributing to environmental degradation or pollution.

5. Social responsibility: Companies must act in a socially responsible manner and ensure that their marketing strategies do not exploit vulnerable populations or contribute to societal problems, such as inequality or discrimination.

6. Respect for human rights: Companies must respect human rights, including labor rights, and ensure that their marketing strategies do not involve any practices that violate those rights.

By taking these ethical considerations into account, companies can ensure that their global marketing strategies are not only effective but also responsible and sustainable.
