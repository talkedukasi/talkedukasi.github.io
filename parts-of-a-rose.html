Euphorbia is a very large and diverse genus of flowering plants, commonly called spurge, in the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae). "Euphorbia" is sometimes used in ordinary English to collectively refer to all members of Euphorbiaceae (in deference to the type genus), not just to members of the genus.[2] Some euphorbias are commercially widely available, such as poinsettias at Christmas. 14. What is the common parts of oregano,lipang aso,rose,grafted calamansi, rose and what are the specialized structures​


Answer:

The common parts of oregano, lipang aso, rose, grafted calamansi, and rose are the stem, leaves, flowers, and roots. The specialized structures are the root hairs, which are responsible for the absorption of water and nutrients; the stomata, which allow for gas exchange; the sepals, which protect the flower bud; the petals, which attract pollinators; and the reproductive organs, which are responsible for the production of seeds.
