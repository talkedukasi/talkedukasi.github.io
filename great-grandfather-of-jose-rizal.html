Explanation:

To create a family tree, you can start by talking to your parents, grandparents, aunts, uncles, and other relatives to gather information about your ancestors. You can ask them about their names, birth and death dates, occupation, nationality, and any notable events or accomplishments in their lives.

Once you have gathered this information, you can start creating your family tree by drawing a diagram with yourself at the bottom and your parents and grandparents above you, and so on for each generation. You can use symbols and colors to represent each family member's gender, relationship to you, and other information. As you add names to your family tree, you can also include historical events that occurred during each person's lifetime. For example, you can note major wars, political events, technological advancements, and other milestones that impacted your family and the world around them. Depending on your family's history, it may be possible to trace your ancestors back to the time of Rizal before 1898. You can consult historical records, such as birth, marriage, and death certificates, census records, and immigration documents, to learn more about your family's roots. Once you have created your family tree, you can post it on the walls of your classroom and share it with your classmates. This can be a fun way to learn about each other's backgrounds and discover similarities and differences in your family histories.
