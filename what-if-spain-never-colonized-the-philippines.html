7. how did Spain colonized the Philippines ​


Answer:

Spain arrived in the Philippines in 1521 and gradually established control over the archipelago through a combination of military conquest and political alliances with local leaders. In 1571, the Spanish founded the city of Manila, which became the capital of the colony. From 1821 to 1898, the Philippines was under direct royal governance by Spain.
