4. how does the story's structure it's use of flash back and memories impact of overall impact of messages of the "Small Key".​


Answer:

The story’s structure, its use of flashback and memories, has a significant impact on the overall message of “The Small Key”. The structure of the story creates a sense of nostalgia that sets the tone for the narrator’s reflections on the past and his longing for a time gone by. The flash backs and memories that are interspersed throughout offer further insight into the narrator’s feelings, as well as provide the audience with a greater understanding of the characters and their relationship. By showing the deep bond between the narrator and his grandfather, the story conveys the importance of love and connection between family members, as well as the significance of treasuring such a bond when it is broken. The small key is itself a symbol of the connection between them, and by the end of the story it acts as a symbol of how important it is to hold onto the memories of loved ones who are no longer with us.

22. how does the protogonist desire for her mother's approval and affection shape her decisions and her Sense of self of the story of the small key?​


Answer:

n the story of "The Small Key" by Paz Latorena, the protagonist's desire for her mother's approval and affection greatly affects her decisions and her sense of self.

Throughout the story, the protagonist struggles with her identity and her place in her marriage. She longs for her mother's approval of her marriage to her husband, Pedro, who is from a lower social class than her. This desire stems from her mother's disapproval of their relationship and her fear of being ostracized from her family and society.

The protagonist's desperation for her mother's acceptance leads her to make decisions that compromise her values and beliefs. She goes against her own instincts and allows her husband to search through her deceased mother's belongings, including her private diary, in search of a small key that unlocks a hidden drawer. This action not only violates her mother's privacy, but also goes against her own moral compass.
