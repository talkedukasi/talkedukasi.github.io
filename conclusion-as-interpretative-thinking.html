1. conclusion as interpretative thinking​


Answer:

Drawing conclusion is a type of interpretative thinking.

Before coming up with a conclusion, you have to gather as much information as you can. And not just "any information," because you need to only get "relevant information." Without gathering relevant information, you will have difficulty doing inferences, because conclusion is based on that.

It is like connecting dots based on your gathered information. After this, you can now dig deeper and discover new insights. You use facts and logic in order to come up with interpretation.

Interpretation is a logical analytical conclusion. Your interpretation will depend on the implications, impacts, and/or significance of the issue at hand. When you're done gathering information, it's now time to ask: What now? What can we draw from this? What can we infer from this?

Answering these will make you arrive to drawing conclusion.
