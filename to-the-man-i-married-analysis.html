12. what made the poem titled To The Man I Married different from a petrarchan and shakespearean sonnet?​


Italian poet Francesco Petrarch gave the sonnet form to English writers. It typically consists of fourteen lines of iambic pentameter connected by a complex rhyme pattern.

ContextTo The Man I Married

Petrarchan and Shakespearean Sonnets

The first two quatrains mostly discuss various ways in which the physical beauty of the material world inevitably dims, fades, and/or falls short of ideal beauty at some point. The lover, whose beauty Shakespeare idolizes here, is given the gift of immortality by the poet. The poet gives his sweetheart the gift of immortality in the third quatrain of his poetry. The alternating pattern maintains continuity while the changing rhymes highlight the dualist aspect of beauty (how things that are beautiful in their prime inevitably get old, fade, and die).

StructureTo The Man I Married

Manalang, Angela The sonnet "To the Man I Married" by Gloria is a blend of English and Italian; it is composed of an octave with the rhyme scheme "ABABCDCD" and "EFEFGG" in the sestet. The English sonnet's basic rhyme structure is used, but instead of three quatrains and a couplet, it uses an octave and a sestet. Two rimed quatrains with the rhyme structure ABAB,ACDC make up Part II.

In the second section of Gloria's "To the Man I Married," there are only two quatrains, doing away with the couplet as it makes each line shorter and seems to concentrate the meaning.

Petrarchan and Shakespearean Sonnets

Shakespeare himself used the sonnet form in his own love poetry, following the guidelines established by English poets Wyatt and Surrey. Three quatrains and a couplet at the end make up this form, often known as the English or Shakespearean sonnet. The rhyming pattern follows a straightforward ABAB CDCD EFEF GG structure. The effect is similar to taking a short drive with a very fast driver: the first lines, including the first quatrain, are in low gear; the second and third quatrains accelerate sharply, and ideas and metaphors flash past; and then there is a sudden throttling-back, and one glides to a stop in the couplet. , and Shakespeare, like Petrarch, made use of structure to delve deeply into a theme in a brief work.

28. how does figurative language portray the qualities of two objects on To the man I married ?​


Answer:

Using figurative language is an effective way of communicating an idea that is not easily understood because of its abstract nature or complexity. Although figurative language does not offer a literal explanation, it can be used to compare one idea to a second idea to make the first idea easier to visualize. Figurative language also is used to link two ideas with the goal of influencing an audience to see a connection even if one does not actually exist. Writers of prose and poetry use figurative language to elicit emotion, help readers form mental images and draw readers into the work.
