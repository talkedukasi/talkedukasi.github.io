5. what is the effect of this Pandemic to our Cultural Heritage?​


Answer:

A lesser-known effect of the pandemic though has been the heavy strain it has placed on efforts to preserve and protect cultural heritage. In response to the pandemic, governments have issued strict guidelines and restrictions to shut down theaters, museums, cultural heritage sites, libraries, airports, malls, schools, and restaurants. With no cure or vaccine on the horizon, governments are working to mitigate the damage of the lockdowns on cultural heritage sites.

19. explain the line "our heritage of greatness, for the national heritage is - lets face it - a heritage of smalness.​


Answer:

The line "our heritage of greatness, for the national heritage is - let's face it - a heritage of smallness" suggests that the speaker believes that the common understanding of national heritage is limited and does not adequately capture the greatness of the country or its people. Instead, the speaker believes that true greatness lies in something beyond what is traditionally associated with national heritage.

By using the phrase "heritage of smallness," the speaker implies that the traditional understanding of national heritage is focused on minor accomplishments or details that are not truly indicative of the country's true potential or achievements. The phrase "let's face it" is used to acknowledge that this idea may be controversial or uncomfortable, but the speaker believes it is important to recognize in order to move towards a more accurate understanding of the country's history and potential.

Overall, the line suggests that the speaker believes that there is more to be celebrated and recognized about the country than what is commonly acknowledged through its national heritage.

26. Interview: What is the future of our Cultural Heritage? ​


Answer:

What is the future of taking care of our Cultural Heritage? Cultural Heritage has grown over the past decades to become one of the most pending questions to mankind. Individuals continue more than ever before to look into the very roots of their existence, which is a good thing to do. However, this root-treatment cannot be established by oral traditions only. One needs bio- and material cultural objects that reflect how our forefathers were living and left behind. Only since the mid 19th century, many of these artifacts are assembled in hundreds of museums where they are treated with good care. However, with the accumulation of artifacts and the restoration and necessary conservation of cultural objects, it will become a burden to pay for this type of work and as the assemblage will only grow, there will come a point in time that there will not be enough budget to pay for all that work. So, how do you see the future of Cultural Heritage?
