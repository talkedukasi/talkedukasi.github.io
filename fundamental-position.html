3. At the end of this module, the learners are expected to:Distinguish the fundamental dance positions of the arms and feet; andPerform the fundamental dance position.​


Answer:

Learning Content:

Here are the five fundamental arms and feet position in dance.

(ARMS POSITIONS)

1st position

-Both arms are raised forward in a circle in front of the chest with the fingertips about an inch apart.

2nd position

-Both arms are raised sideward with graceful curve at shoulder level.

3rd position

-Left arm is raised sideward as in 2nd position; right arm raise upward.

4th position

-Left arm is raised in front as in first position; right arm raised overhead.

5th position

-Both arms are raised overhead.

(FEET POSITIONS)

1st position

-Heels close together; toes apart within an angle of around 45 degrees.

2nd position

-Point the right toe sideward; bring down the heel of the right foot.

3rd position

-Heel of one foot is close to in step of other foot.

4th position

-Point the right toe forward; bring down the heel of the right foot.

5th position

-Close the right foot to the left by circling and return to the first position.

9. Execute the fundamental position of arms​


Answer:

The fundamental position of arms is a basic stance used in many disciplines, including dance and martial arts. It involves standing up straight with your arms by your sides, palms facing your body, and fingers closed.

To execute the fundamental position of arms, follow these steps:

1. Stand up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart and your weight evenly distributed between both feet.

2. Keep your shoulders relaxed and your spine aligned.

3. Let your arms hang down by your sides, with your palms facing your body and your fingers closed.

4. Make sure your elbows are close to your body and not sticking out.

5. Keep your head and eyes facing forward, and your chin parallel to the ground.

6. Hold this position for as long as necessary.

The fundamental position of arms is often used as a starting point for more complex movements and techniques in various disciplines, so it's essential to master this stance before moving on to more advanced techniques.
