“Senakulo”, “Princesa Miramar at Principe Leandro”, and “Paglipas ng Dilim” are just some of the Philippine theatre art plays. Meanwhile, “Sangdugong Panaguinip”, “La Loba Negra”, and “Noli Me Tangere – Opera”  are examples of Philippine opera. Most Philippine theatre art has western influences due to the colonization of western countries like Spain and America.

Theater Art Plays

Philippine theatre art plays are greatly influenced by the Spaniards. Themes of Christianity were widely adapted, either shows through stories of conversion or struggles of lovers against faith.  

Senakulo. Remarks: This Philippine theatre art is a description of the Passion of Christ. Princesa Miramar at Principe Leandro. Remarks: This Philippine theatre art is a komedya about a romance between two royals with battle scenes between Moros and Christians. Paglipas ng Dilim. Remarks: This Philippine theatre art is a sarswela that depicts social issues of reputation and morality.

Operas

Philippine operas are generally influenced by Italian opera. It started in the Philippines when zarzuelas were introduced since it includes operas.  

Sangdugong Panaguinip. Remarks: This Philippine opera is the first to be done in the native language. The story follows Lapu who dreamt of rescuing maidens that were meant to be claimed as tributes by pirates. La Lobo Negra. Remarks: This Philippine opera is based on the story of a governor-general’s assassination and his wife’s revenge plan. Noli Me Tangere. Remarks: This Philippine opera is originally a novel by Jose Rizal but was composed into opera by Felipe Padilla De Leon.
