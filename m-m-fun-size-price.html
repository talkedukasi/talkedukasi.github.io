Question: The trapezoidal lot has the following measurement The shorter side is 18m, the longer side is 22m, and the distance between the shorter side to the longer side is 10m. what is the area of the lot?

A. 150 m²B. 200 m²C. 180 m²

Answer: [tex] \Large\underline{\rm\green{\:B.\:200m^{2}\:}}[/tex]

Solution: Using the Formula to find the area of the trapezoid with the bases are 18m and 22m while the height is 10m.

[tex] \begin{aligned}& \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\& \boxed{Area = \frac{(b_1 + b_2)h}{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}{Area = \frac{(18m + 22m)10m}{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}{Area = \frac{(40m)10m}{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{aligned}{Area = \frac{400 {m}^{2} }{2} } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]Area = 200 {m}^{2} [/tex]===============================

18. 1. A four-sided garden has a perimeter of 1 490 m. If the three sides have lengths of 468 m, 370 m, and 392 m, how long is the remaining side? ​


Answer:

The perimeter of a four-sided garden is the sum of the lengths of all four sides. Therefore, we can find the length of the remaining side by subtracting the sum of the lengths of the three given sides from the total perimeter.

Perimeter of the garden = 1,490 m Length of the first side = 468 m Length of the second side = 370 m Length of the third side = 392 m

Total length of the three given sides = 468 m + 370 m + 392 m = 1,230 m

Length of the remaining side = Perimeter of the garden - Total length of the three given sides Length of the remaining side = 1,490 m - 1,230 m Length of the remaining side = 260 m

Therefore, the length of the remaining side is 260 meters.
