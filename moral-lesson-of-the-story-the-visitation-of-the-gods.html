1. moral lesson in the god stealer


The moral lesson in the story 'The God Stealer' by Francisco Sionil Jose is not to trust someone easily. The initial assessment should be done as always. We should always get to know the person really well whom we share our stories, pain, happiness, and life. Because trust is very important in any kind of relationship. We shouldn't trust someone so easily just because they showed us kindness and acceptance. Sometimes, it takes one to know a real one.

The Moral lesson of the story "God Stealer" is to always appreciate and value where you came from. The main character named Phillip has become less sentimental of his past origins and his cultural identity as a price of living in the city. From that day on, the little boy took care of his toys and never broke them again. He also learned to fix things on his own and became more responsible.

The moral lesson of the story is that we should always try our best to fix our mistakes and take responsibility for our actions. We should also take care of our belongings and appreciate what we have.

26. moral lesson of the story the necklace​


THE NECKLACE

The story The Necklace revolves around a middle class family, the family of Madame Mathilde Loisel who strives to become or to appear rich or wealthy. And such action of Madame Mathilde Loisel brought her to sufferings.

MORAL LESSON OF THE STORY THE NECKLACE

WEALTH AND HAPPINESS DO NOT CORRELATE

It is very evident in the story The Necklace hat the main character is very much into becoming or appearing wealthy, in fact she equates wealth to happiness. That is, one lesson to get from the story is that do not equate wealth to happiness. A lot of people who have everything but are not happy.

DO NOT BE GREEDY

In the story The Necklace, clearly Madame Mathilde Loisel was not satisfied of the life she has. She was clearly the opposite of her husband who was satisfied in his post.

BEAUTY IS ONLY SKIN DEEP

This proverbial expression is the story's main lesson, meaning that a pleasing appearance is no guide to character.

Explanation:

1.) One story from the Old Testament that demonstrates the Jewish belief in one God is the story of Moses and the Burning Bush (Exodus 3:1-22). In this story, God speaks to Moses through a bush that burns but is not consumed. God instructs Moses to lead the Israelites out of Egypt, and Moses asks for God's name. God responds, "I am who I am" or "I will be what I will be," signifying his eternal nature and absolute sovereignty.

2.) The story of the Burning Bush is a significant example of Jewish monotheism, as it demonstrates that there is only one true God who has revealed himself to his people. The story also emphasizes the importance of obeying God's commands and trusting in his plan, even when it may seem difficult or impossible.

3.) The moral lesson of the story is that God is always present and always in control, and that we should trust in him even when we face challenges or uncertainty. It also teaches the importance of being obedient to God's commands and following his plan for our lives, even when it may involve sacrifice or risk.
