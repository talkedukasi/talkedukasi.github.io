18. theoretical framework Effectiveness of self-directed learning modules as perceived by senior high school students in academia system global colleges cebu city campus​


The theoretical framework for the study on the effectiveness of self-directed learning modules as perceived by senior high school students in the Academia System Global Colleges Cebu City Campus can be based on several theoretical perspectives, including the self-directed learning theory, constructivism, and adult learning theory.

Self-directed learning theory posits that learners should be given the freedom and autonomy to take control of their own learning process. This theory emphasizes the importance of learners' motivation, self-regulation, and metacognition in the learning process. As such, the study can examine the impact of self-directed learning modules on students' motivation, self-regulation, and metacognition.

Constructivism, on the other hand, asserts that learners construct their own knowledge through active engagement with the learning materials. This theory emphasizes the importance of learners' prior knowledge, beliefs, and experiences in shaping their learning process. The study can examine how self-directed learning modules can facilitate students' construction of knowledge and how their prior knowledge, beliefs, and experiences can influence their learning outcomes.

Finally, adult learning theory emphasizes the unique characteristics of adult learners, including their motivation, experience, and self-direction. The study can examine how the design and implementation of self-directed learning modules can take into account these unique characteristics and how these modules can effectively engage adult learners.

Overall, the theoretical framework for the study on the effectiveness of self-directed learning modules can draw from several theoretical perspectives, including the self-directed learning theory, constructivism, and adult learning theory. By considering these theoretical perspectives, the study can provide a more comprehensive understanding of how self-directed learning modules can impact students' learning outcomes in the context of senior high school education.
