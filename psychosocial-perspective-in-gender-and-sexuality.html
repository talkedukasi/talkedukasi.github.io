1. explain the gender and sexuality as a psychosocial issue​


Answer:

Gender and sexuality are psychosocial issues because they are related to the psychological development and socialization of individuals. Gender and sexuality, as psychosocial issues, can influence the way individuals interact with their environment and with other people. They can also influence an individual's sense of self-worth, identity, and sense of belonging. Gender and sexuality can shape an individual's behavior, values, and beliefs, and can even shape their decisions about relationships, careers, and other life choices. Gender and sexuality can also be affected by societal norms, expectations, and cultural influences. Ultimately, gender and sexuality are psychosocial issues that can have a profound impact on an individual's life.
