2. TRIAD DIAGRAM Objective: Compare and contrast the characteristics of the different types of soil. Direction: Write the differences and the similarities of the three types of soil. ​


Answer:

There are three types of soil: sand, silt, and clay. Each type of soil has its own unique characteristics. Sand soils are often dry, nutrient deficient and fast-draining. Silt soils differ from sand soils by having a greater tendency to form a crust, which is often very hard. Clay soils are heavy and sticky when wet but can become hard when dry. ¹

Here are some similarities and differences between the three types of soil:

Similarities:

- All three types of soil contain minerals and organic matter.

- All three types of soil can be improved with the addition of organic matter such as compost or manure.

Differences:

- Sand soils are made up of larger particles than silt or clay soils.

- Silt soils have a greater tendency to form a crust than sand or clay soils.

- Clay soils are made up of smaller particles than sand or silt soils and can become hard when dry.

3. Direction: Compare and contrast the different kinds of soil Write the needed information below. Main Topic1.Soil type:Characteristics:2.Soil type:Characteristics:3.Soil type:Characteristics:​


Answer:

Main Topic: Different Kinds of Soil

1. Clay Soil:

Characteristics:

- Fine-grained soil with high plasticity when wet and hardness when dry

- Retains moisture well but can become waterlogged

- Difficult for air and water to penetrate

- Suitable for agriculture but requires additional inputs to improve drainage and aeration

2. Sandy Soil:

Characteristics:

- Coarse-grained soil with low plasticity

- Drains quickly due to large spaces between particles

- Low in nutrients and organic matter

- Often acidic in nature

- Good for building foundations but requires irrigation and fertilization for agriculture

3. Loam Soil:

Characteristics:

- Mixture of clay, sand, and silt

- Has a balanced texture and structure that allows for good drainage, aeration, and moisture retention

- Contains organic matter and nutrients that support plant growth

- Highly suitable for agriculture and gardening

Note: There are other types of soil such as peat soil, chalky soil, etc. but the given information only includes the three most common soil types.

Sand:
