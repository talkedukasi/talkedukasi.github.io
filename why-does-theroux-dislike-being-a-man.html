Being a Man by: Paul Theroux

Theroux dislike being a man because according to him the whole idea of manhood in America is pitiful.

Theroux purpose in writing this essay is to express his feelings towards being a man and to explain that the view of manliness is as controversial as feminism. When women are perceived to be less than men due to their weaknesses and incapacities, then men should somehow be perceived less than women when their weaknesses and incapacities were apparent.

Some of the underlying issues in Theroux definition of manhood include misconceptions regarding masculinity, behavior exhibited by men, the transition from being boys to men, and being literal - minded.

The article Being a Man is an eye opener that to be a man is also to be human. The expectations of the world towards men are so high that even men themselves cannot live within these expectations. Being a man is being human, being natural, and being free from the expectations and conceptualization of manhood and manliness.
