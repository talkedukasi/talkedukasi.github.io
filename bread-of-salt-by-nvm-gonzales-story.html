5. Could you help me find the plot or summary of seven hills away by NVM gonzales


Answer:

"Seven Hills Away" by NVM Gonzales is a short story about a young boy named Victor who lives in a small rural village in the Philippines. Victor is in love with a girl named Paula, who lives in a neighboring village that is separated from his own by seven hills.

One day, Victor decides to visit Paula, but he must cross the seven hills to get there. He sets out early in the morning and begins his journey, facing many obstacles along the way. He encounters a group of boys who mock him, a woman who tries to steal his money, and a group of soldiers who threaten him.

Despite these challenges, Victor perseveres and eventually reaches Paula's village. He spends a beautiful day with her, enjoying her company and the beautiful scenery of the village. However, as the day comes to a close, Victor realizes that he must return home before dark.

Victor sets out on his journey back, but he is met with even more challenges than before. He becomes lost in the dark and stumbles upon a group of men who are stealing coconuts. The men threaten him and he becomes afraid, but he manages to escape and continues on his journey.

Finally, after a long and difficult journey, Victor makes it back to his village. He is exhausted and battered, but he is also filled with a sense of accomplishment for having made the journey to see Paula. The story ends with Victor reflecting on his journey and the love that he has for Paula.

11. what is the Denoument of the story of "The bread of salt"​


Answer:

"The Bread of Salt" is a short story written by N.V.M. Gonzalez. The denouement of a story is the part where the conflict is resolved, and the loose ends are tied up. In the case of "The Bread of Salt," the denouement occurs near the end of the story when the protagonist, a young boy named Alfredo, finally realizes that he has been infatuated with a young girl named Aida, who is the niece of the homeowners where he delivers bread.

In the story's climax, Alfredo attends a party at the house of the girl he loves, hoping to see her. However, he is disappointed when he discovers that Aida has no interest in him and is in fact in love with another man. This realization leads to the denouement of the story, as Alfredo's illusions are shattered, and he is forced to confront the harsh reality of his situation.

The final scene of the story shows Alfredo returning home from the party, disillusioned and disappointed. As he reflects on his experiences, he realizes that his infatuation with Aida was merely a fantasy and that he must accept the reality of his life as a poor baker's boy. This realization represents the denouement of the story, as Alfredo's internal conflict is resolved, and he learns an important lesson about the nature of love and life.

16. what is the whole summary of the story Bread of Salt


Answer:

The Bread of Salt Summary

As an author, N.V.M. Gonzalez usually wrote about the Filipino life and Filipinos in general. One of his most remarkable short stories is “The Bread of Salt”. The story is about a young boy who was very much in love with a girl named Aida but he was turned down mainly because of the difference in class that he later realized. However, this story is unlike any other typical love story. In fact, story is talks more about the painful realization of the young boy who was blinded from reality. In opening scene, we are told about the great Spanish house where Aida lived and from the line “I often wondered whether I was being depended upon to spend the years ahead in the service of this great house”, it clearly shows the difference of their classes. As we all know, in the real world, classes are a hindrance to love and marriage which the boy failed to realize from the very start because of his young age. Young boys naturally tend to think.

one of his dreams was becoming a rich and famous violinist. As we can see, the boy's experience to find out the truth is really painful, that even at the last part; it was quoted "the bread is not ready", which suggests that the boy is not quite ready to accept the real world. The pan de sal symbolizes the boy and by giving the task of buying the the pan de sal every morning to the girl, it shows how he is trying to escape from the reality so he could concentrate more on his dreams. He had wrongly regarded Aida as "his world" and thus it was painful to finally realize that he has no chance. He also had so many plans for Aida, including writing love letters and buying her a brooch. Unfortunately, how could one concentrate on his dreams if he is only at the aged of fourteen and has not even finished studying? Finally in the last part, the turning point of the story, where he hid the "egg yolks dip in honey and peppermint" in his pocket, he was caught by Aida. He has even thought of marrying Aida in the future. In the middle part of the story, the boy was relieved and was happy when he found out that her aunt brought a maid with her and that, his task of buying pan de sal every morning is now given to the "poor girl". This just shows that no matter how hard he tries to hide his "true" self, it will eventually, one day, come out. These things clearly show that the boy was very much in love with Aida so much so that he forgot that in this world not all dreams really come true. revolves around them and this is why the young boy had a lot of dreams and plans. He had already felt like he was part of the "higher class" since he was invited to the party but the truth is, his not. Some can be full of joy and happiness others not but no matter what, the realities of this world often dictates what will and must happen. If from the very start, the boy had accepted the realities of this world, maybe he would not have been to "hurt" at the last part.


17. What is the climax of the story the bread of salt


"The Bread of Salt" is a short story written by Filipino author N.V.M. Gonzalez. The story follows the journey of a young boy named Alfredo, who falls in love with a wealthy girl he meets during his daily bread deliveries.

The climax of the story occurs when Alfredo attends the party of the girl he has been infatuated with, realizing the stark contrast between his own social status and that of the guests. He becomes aware of the unbridgeable gap that exists between them and realizes that his dreams of being with her are just that, dreams. He then becomes disillusioned with his own ambitions and is left to confront the reality of his position in society.

The story's climax represents a turning point for Alfredo, as he comes to realize the limitations of his own social and economic circumstances, and the impossibility of his romantic aspirations. It marks a moment of realization and maturation for the protagonist, as he begins to understand the harsh realities of life and the challenges that come with pursuing one's dreams.

25. what are the results or outcome of the story the bread of salt ​


Answer:

The outcome of the story "The Bread of Salt" by N.V.M. Gonzalez is that the main character, a young boy named Salvador, learns a valuable lesson about the reality of his dreams and aspirations.

Throughout the story, Salvador has a crush on a young girl from a wealthy family who he meets while delivering bread to her house. He dreams of impressing her with his cooking skills and winning her affection.

However, when he enters a baking competition with his homemade bread and fails to win, he realizes that his dream of impressing the girl may never come true. He also learns that he cannot rely solely on his talent and must work hard to achieve success in life.

The story ends with Salvador realizing that his dreams may not come true, but he must continue to strive for success and make the most of his talents and opportunities.
