28. 2. How would you describe the works of art in the Spanish and American era?​


Answer:

In general, the term “American Colonial art” describes the art and architecture of the 17th and 18th century settlers who came to America from Europe. It is so Eurocentric that it has no contact with the tribal art traditions of American Indian art, either on the east coast, plains or west coast.

Although ethnic art forms such as pottery, weaving and metalworking were preserved, the Spanish friars and the Chinese, the main partners in the colony, slowly introduced newer art forms. The icons carried by the friars were used as models for the sculpture.
