8. English: write the exposition rising action climax falling action resolution (plot structure) of the story Dead Star and little prince​


Answer:

Here's the plot structure for "Dead Star" by Paz Marquez Benitez and "The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry:

"Dead Star" by Paz Marquez Benitez:

Exposition: The story is set in a town where a young lawyer named Alfredo Salazar returns for a homecoming. He is attending the town's festivities and reminiscing about his past, including his love affair with a woman named Esperanza, who is now married to another man.

Rising Action: Alfredo sees Esperanza again at the festivities and starts to relive the old feelings he had for her. He discovers that Esperanza's husband is a man he knows, and this causes him to become more obsessed with her.

Climax: Alfredo and Esperanza have a conversation where Alfredo confesses his feelings for her, but Esperanza tells him that she is already married and cannot reciprocate his feelings.

Falling Action: Alfredo realizes that he has been living in a fantasy world and that Esperanza is not the same person he fell in love with. He finally accepts the fact that he needs to move on from her and stop dwelling in the past.

Resolution: Alfredo decides to leave the town and move on with his life, accepting the reality of the situation.

"The Little Prince" by Antoine de Saint-Exupéry:

Exposition: The narrator, a pilot, crashes his plane in the desert and meets a young prince who has come from another planet.

Rising Action: The little prince tells the narrator about his journey to Earth and the various planets he visited, where he encountered strange and interesting characters.

Climax: The little prince meets a fox who teaches him about love and friendship.

Falling Action: The little prince realizes that he loves the rose he left behind on his planet and decides to return home.

Resolution: The little prince says goodbye to the narrator and allows himself to be bitten by a snake, returning to his planet and reuniting with his beloved rose. The narrator returns to civilization, changed by his encounter with the little prince.

11. Bounty in our hands title character main character secondary character setting place mood begging rising action climax falling action outcome Bounty in our hands please answer


Answer:

Title: Bounty in Our Hands

Main Character: John, a bounty hunter

Secondary Character: Sarah, a farmer's daughter

Setting Place: The Wild West, circa 1870s

Mood: Suspenseful, adventurous

Begging: John is a skilled and renowned bounty hunter who has been on the trail of a notorious outlaw named Jack for months. Jack has a hefty bounty on his head, and John is determined to capture him and collect the reward. One day, John hears that Jack has been spotted in a small town called Millfield. John sets out to Millfield, but when he arrives, he finds that Jack has already fled.

Rising Action: John starts to search the surrounding areas for Jack, and during his search, he comes across a small farm where he meets Sarah, a beautiful and feisty farmer's daughter. Sarah tells John that she saw Jack a few days ago, and she offers to help him track him down. John agrees, and the two set out on horseback to follow Jack's trail.

Climax: After several days of tracking, John and Sarah finally catch up to Jack in a remote canyon. Jack is heavily armed and is not willing to surrender without a fight. John and Jack engage in a thrilling shootout, and in the end, John manages to shoot Jack and capture him alive.

Falling Action: John takes Jack back to Millfield, where he collects the bounty and hands Jack over to the authorities. Sarah is impressed by John's bravery and skill and starts to develop feelings for him. John, however, is a loner and is not interested in settling down.

Outcome: John leaves Millfield, but he can't stop thinking about Sarah. He eventually decides to return to the farm and tell Sarah how he feels. Sarah is overjoyed and the two start a romantic relationship. John continues to work as a bounty hunter, but he now has someone to come home to.

Don Julian's Residence House of Judge Del Valle Don Julian's House in Tanda, has a beach and coconut trees Real Street, Santa Cruz. Because they are commemorating Holy Week as seen by the procession they made with Our Lady of Sorrows, the story takes place during Lent.

4. THE PLOT

INTRODUCTION

Carmen was inquiring about Alfredo and Esperanza at Don Julian's home.

RISING ACTION

Along with Don Julian, he had visited Judge Del Valle's home as neighbors. He met Julia Salas. He called her Mrs. Del Valle the entire time, which made him feel awkward. Visits to the judge's home increased in frequency. Then he understood that despite his engagement to Esperanza, he was in love with Julia.

CLIMAX

Julia and Alfredo were reunited following the procession honoring the Lady of Sorrows. When Julia learned about Alfredo's wedding, she wished him well. Alfredo must handle a really challenging circumstance. Would he decide on what he WOULD like to do? Or would he decide to do what he MUST?

FALLING ACTION

Julia did not want Alfredo to violate their agreement with Esperanza. She said farewell. Alfredo left for Esperanza to go home. The last word has been said, therefore.

DENOUEMENT

Esperanza and Alfredo got married. After eight years, he was looking for Brigida Samuy, a woman who was crucial to his defense in Julia's hometown of Sta. Cruz. He visited Julia's home and discovered that she is still single. Then he understood that his love for Julia was nonexistent, just like a DEAD STAR.

5. POINT OF VIEW

The third-person omniscient point of view is used to tell the story. Only if he permits it, can we see or feel what the characters are thinking and experiencing. We are only aware of what the character is aware of and what the author gives us access to.

6. CONFLICT BETWEEN PERSON VS. FATE

In the narrative, Alfredo battles against his destiny as well as the challenges that come with life and love. He will have to deal with challenges when making major life decisions.

7. THEME

The idea of forbidden love is conveyed in Paz Marquez Benitez's short story "Dead Stars." According to this, forbidden love is only apparent, and its negative effects follow the person until he recognizes his shortcomings.

8. MORAL OR HUMANISTIC APPROACH

When Alfredo must choose between Esperanza and Julia Salas, Esperanza is the one he MUST choose. In the end, he decided on Esparanza and understood that his love for Julia Salas was like a DEAD STAR, whose brightness gradually fades and disappears.

13. is there an active faultis there an active fault identified in eur ​


Answer:

Active fault – An active fault is a fault that is likely to have another earthquake sometime in the future. Faults are commonly considered to be active if there has been movement observed or evidence of seismic activity during the last 10,000 years. Active faulting is considered to be a geologic hazard and related to earthquakes as a cause. Effects of movement on an active fault include strong ground motion, surface faulting, tectonic deformation, landslides and rockfalls, liquefaction, tsunamis, and seiches. Quaternary faults are those active faults that have been recognized at the surface and which have evidence of movement in the past 1.6 million years. That is the duration of the Quaternary Period. Related geological disciplines for active fault studies are geomorphology, seismology, reflection seismology, plate tectonics, geodetics and remote sensing, risk analysis, as well as other disciplines.

In relation to fracking, there are theories that the fracking process in the crude oil industry can disrupt an active fault or possibly activate an inactive or dormant fault. Seismologists all over the world are actively studying areas in the world where fracking takes place to either validate or dispute these theories.

18. Which of the following geologic features is an example of a reverse fault?A. Anatolian faultB. San Andreas faultC. Himalayas mountainD. East African rift zone​


An example of a reverse fault is San Andreas fault.

Understanding faults

Faults are places where earthquakes occur. Reporting from the Energy and Mineral Resources Office of Lampung Province, faults are faults or areas of fracture accompanied by a relative shift to other rock blocks.

Fault fields vary greatly in size, from a few centimeters to tens of kilometers. This field is formed due to the movement of the plates and produces a force on the rock.

Types of faults

Anderson in 1951 divided the types of faults based on the stress principle. The principle stress is the force acting perpendicular to the plane so that the shear stress in that plane is zero. Anderson's classification divides the types of faults into three types.

1. Normal faults

The first type of cesarean is a normal cesarean. This fault experiences a shift that is influenced by the force of gravity. This fault occurs when the compressive force reaches the maximum force vertically.

2. Shortness of rise (reverse fault or thrust fault)

This type of fault is characterized by one of the rock blocks shifting upwards and the other rock blocks shifting downwards. This fault usually forms a tilt angle of less than 45 degrees.

3. Horizontal fault (strike-slip fault)

Horizontal faults are faults whose direction of movement is parallel.

These faults are further divided into two types, namely the dextral faults and the sinistral faults.

Dextral horizontal faults are faults that move to the right, while sinistral faults move to the left. The Semangko Fault under the Bukit Barisan Mountains has a right shear direction, so it is a horizontal fault.

25. describe the normal fault,reserve fault,strike-slip faulth​


Answer:

Normal fault - a dip-slip fault in which the block above the fault has moved downward relative to the block below. This type of faulting occurs in response to extension and is often observed in the Western United States Basin and Range Province and along oceanic ridge systems.

Strike-slip fault - a fault on which the two blocks slide past one another. The San Andreas Fault is an example of a right lateral fault.

Reverse faults - are exactly the opposite of normal faults. If the hanging wall rises relative to the footwall, you have a reverse fault. Reverse faults occur in areas undergoing compression
