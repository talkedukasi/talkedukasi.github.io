14. Two adjacent students stand on stationary skateboards, face each other, and push apart. The skateboarder on the left is 65 kg, the one on the right is 85 kg. If the lighter skateboarder moves at 3.2 m/s, determine the velocity of the heavier skateboarder.​


Answer:

Conservation of linear momentum accounts for the direction of motion participating objects. The heavy skateboarder will move off with a speed of 2.45 m/s in the opposite direction.

What is the conservation of linear momentum?

As per the conservation of linear momentum, "When two objects approach each other, then the momentum before the collision is equal to the momentum after the collision.

The mass of the left skateboarder is m = 65 kg.

The mass of the right skateboarder is m' = 85 kg.

The speed of lighter skateboarder is, v = 3.2 m/s.

Applying the conservation of linear momentum as,

mu + m'u' = mv + m'v'

u and u' are the initial velocities of each skateboarder. Similarly, v and v' are their final velocities.

Solving as,

Since skateboarders are at rest initial, u = u' = 0.

m(0) + m'(0) = 65 × 3.2 + (85 × v)

v = -2.45 m/s

Thus, we can conclude that the heavy skateboarder will move off with a speed of 2.45 m/s in the opposite direction.

18. a skateboarder starts from rest and accelerate at a rate of 3m/s for 10s.what is the final velocity of the skateboarder?​


Using the equation:

final velocity = initial velocity + acceleration x time

We know that the skateboarder started from rest (initial velocity = 0), the acceleration is 3 m/s², and the time is 10 seconds. Therefore:

final velocity = 0 + 3 m/s² x 10 s

final velocity = 30 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the skateboarder is 30 m/s.

To find the final velocity of the skateboarder, we can use the formula for uniformly accelerated motion:

Final velocity (v) = Initial velocity (u) + (Acceleration (a) × Time (t))

Given:

Initial velocity (u) = 0 m/s (since the skateboarder starts from rest)

Acceleration (a) = 3 m/s²

Time (t) = 10 s

Plugging these values into the formula:

Final velocity (v) = 0 m/s + (3 m/s² × 10 s)

= 0 m/s + 30 m/s

= 30 m/s

Therefore, the final velocity of the skateboarder after 10 seconds of acceleration is 30 m/s.
