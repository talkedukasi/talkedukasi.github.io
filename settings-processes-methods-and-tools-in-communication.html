Answer:

Sure, here are three communication settings and how the process of communication takes place with the help of tools, methods, and channels of communication:

1.SCHOOL: Sender: Teachers, school administrators Receiver: Students, parents

Tools/Methods: In-person conversations, phone calls, emails, newsletters, notice boards, online learning platforms, social media groups.

Channels of communication: Teachers communicate with students through in-person conversations, online learning platforms, and email. They may also send newsletters or post notices on a notice board to keep parents informed about upcoming events or important information. Additionally, schools may have social media groups for parents to stay connected and informed.

2.GOVERNMENT: Sender: Government officials, elected representatives Receiver: Citizens, media outlets

Tools/Methods: Press releases, news conferences, speeches, public meetings, town halls, social media posts.

Channels of communication: Government officials and elected representatives communicate with citizens through press releases, news conferences, speeches, public meetings, and town halls. They may also post updates on social media platforms to keep citizens informed about government activities and policies. Media outlets play a significant role in amplifying government messages to a wider audience.

3.CIVIL SOCIETY: Sender: Non-profit organizations, volunteers Receiver: Donors, beneficiaries, supporters

Tools/Methods: Campaigns, fundraisers, volunteer training, newsletters, social media posts, email marketing.

Channels of communication: Non-profit organizations and volunteers communicate with donors, beneficiaries, and supporters through various channels such as campaigns, fundraisers, volunteer training, newsletters, social media posts, and email marketing. These organizations may also collaborate with the media to promote their cause and raise awareness about their activities and initiatives.

Overall, effective communication is crucial for building and maintaining relationships and achieving organizational goals in all of these settings. Choosing the appropriate tools, methods, and channels of communication is essential to ensure that messages are delivered clearly and effectively to the intended audience.
