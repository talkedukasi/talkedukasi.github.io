2. Short essay: In 5 sentences, provide a brief analysis of the Philippine education system using the three major perspectives in education. 1. Functionalist Perspective2.cnflict perspective3.symbolic interactionist perspective​


Answer:

The functionalist perspective sees the Philippine education system as a means to prepare individuals for the workforce and contribute to the economy. The conflict perspective views the unequal access to quality education among different socioeconomic classes as a perpetuation of social inequality. The symbolic interactionist perspective highlights the importance of teacher-student relationships and how it shapes the students' experiences and outcomes in education. Overall, the Philippine education system faces challenges such as underfunding, teacher shortages, and outdated curricula, which affect the quality of education and hinder its effectiveness in producing well-rounded individuals who can contribute to society.

18. Historical perspective in 18th century​


Answer:

The Eighteenth Century (1701-1800) was a period marked by significant progress in science, commerce and trade. It was also a century of political upheaval with the new political ideas of the Enlightenment culminating in the American and French Revolutions.

The 18th century lasted from January 1, 1701 (MDCCI) to December 31, 1800 (MDCCC). During the 18th century, elements of Enlightenment thinking culminated in the American, French, and Haitian revolutions. During the century, slave trading and human trafficking expanded on a global scale. Revolutions began to challenge the legitimacy of monarchical and aristocratic power structures, including the structures and beliefs that supported the slave trade. The British Industrial Revolution began, leading to radical changes in human society and the environment.
