10. compare and contrast the charactereristics of romantic music to romantic art​


1. Emphasis on emotion: Both romantic music and art placed a strong emphasis on emotions, but music was better suited for conveying intense emotional experiences. In contrast, romantic art often depicted more subtle emotions, using visual cues and symbolism to evoke complex feelings.

2. Individualism: Both romantic music and art celebrated individualism and the unique experiences of the artist. However, in music, this often manifested as a focus on the composer's personal life and experiences, while in art, it was more about expressing the individual perspective of the artist.

3. Nature and the past: Both forms of art and music glorified nature and the past, but in different ways. Romantic art often depicted the beauty of natural landscapes and the sublime power of nature, while romantic music often used nature as a metaphor for human emotion. Similarly, romantic art often looked to the past for inspiration, while romantic music often looked to the music of the past for inspiration.

4. Structure and form: Romantic music and art both experimented with structure and form, but in different ways. In music, romantic composers often pushed the boundaries of traditional musical structures, using unconventional forms and structures to express their ideas. In contrast, romantic art often used traditional techniques and forms, but with a new emphasis on individual expression.

5. Symbolism and metaphor: Both romantic music and art used symbolism and metaphor, but in different ways. In art, symbolism was often used to evoke complex emotions and ideas, while in music, metaphor was often used to express emotions and ideas that were difficult to put into words.

1. At its core, composers of the Romantic Era saw music as a means of individual and emotional expression. Indeed, they considered music the art form most capable of expressing the full range of human emotion. As a result, romantic composers broadened the scope of emotional content. Music was expected to communicate to the audience, often by using a narrative form that told distinct stories.

Romantic composers prioritized the emotional or narrative content of the music above its form, which is why they broke so many of the classical composers' rules. Romantic composers didn't reject or break with the musical language developed during the Classical Period. They used its forms as a foundation for their work but felt unconstrained by them.

Beethoven is the originator of this approach. He lived and worked during the transition from the Classical to the Romantic Period, and was an inspiration to the Romantic composers who came after him.

Beethoven's symphonies "shift[ed] the terrain" for what a symphony could be. He also demonstrated coming Romantic Era characteristics, such as composing auto-biographical works and naming movements, such as the third movement of his String Quartet No. 15 in A minor, Op. 132 (Song of Thanksgiving to the Deity from a convalescent in the Lydian mode).

26. lucio san pedro was known for being a. romantic. ballader b. romantic artist c. romantic composer d. romantic nationalist​


Answer:

Romantic Nationalist

Explanation:

Lucio San Pedro is a master composer, conductor, and teacher whose music evokes the folk elements of the Filipino heritage. Cousin to “Botong” Francisco, San Pedro produced a wide-ranging body of works that includes band music, concertos for violin and orchestra, choral works, cantatas, chamber music, music for violin and piano, and songs for solo voice. He was the conductor of the much acclaimed Peng Kong Grand Mason Concert Band, the San Pedro Band of Angono, his father’s former band, and the Banda Angono Numero Uno. His civic commitment and work with town bands have significantly contributed to the development of a civic culture among Filipino communities and opened a creative outlet for young Filipinos.

His orchestral music include The Devil’s Bridge, Malakas at Maganda Overture,Prelude and Fugue in D minor, Hope and Ambition; choral music Easter Cantata, Sa Mahal Kong Bayan, Rizal’s Valedictory Poem; vocal music Lulay,Sa Ugoy ng Duyan, In the Silence of the Night; and band music Dance of the Fairies, Triumphal March, Lahing Kayumanggi, Angononian March among others.
