1. What is communication for various purposes?


Communication with a purpose is the type of communication carried out with different purposes.

Types of communication with the purpose:

1. Communication to inform something.

This type of communication focuses on conveying the message that the speaker has to the recipient of the message.

2. Communication to invite or influence.

In this type of communication, the speaker will focus on communicating using words that can influence the other person to do what the speaker wants. This type of communication is often used by sellers to consumers to buy products.

3. Communication to express feelings.

In this type of communication, the speaker will focus on conveying and expressing his feelings to the other person.

4. Communication to reach an agreement.

This type of communication focuses on discussions between people who are communicating. The communicating parties will convey their respective opinions and then seek mutually agreed agreements.

5. Communication to meet needs.

In this type of communication, the speaker will focus on conveying his needs to the other person so that his needs or desires can be fulfilled.

3. identify the speaker's purpose in various communication situations​


Answer:

Purpose

Before one begins to think about delivering a speech, one must determine why he or she is giving the speech. Speeches serve a variety of purposes. The immediate audience helps determine the purposes of a speech. People assemble for a speech because they expect to hear or learn something they did not already know. A speaker must satisfy these expectations.

Establishing one's purpose in giving a speech demands explicit attention. It is not enough to believe that the speech is expected or that speaking is somehow a routine act. Such assumptions will quickly be discerned by an audience; and if the audience suspects that the speaker is there unwillingly or unenthusiastically, such an audience will be far less receptive. If a speaker does not have a clear reason to give the speech, then the speech should not be given.

The Central Purpose of a Speech

There is really only one purpose of a speech: a speaker must wish to engage his or her audience with a central idea or proposition. The act of engagement is crucial. A speech is a dynamic relationship between a speaker and the audience. A speaker who views an audience as nothing more than the passive receptacles of his or her insights will lose that audience.
