17. Jefferson declares that a person’s “opinion in matters of religion shall in no wise” do which of the following?


Thomas Jefferson declares that a person's opinion in matters of religion shall in no wise.

His main point was to tell the people that religion may not be an excuse to have a better life than other people when it comes to civil rights.

He means that people cannot tempt others to deny their religion. This is especially predominant in countries that have different religions.


Thomas Jefferson in politics and governance declares that a person’s “opinion in matters of religion shall in no wise" corrupt civic principles and give that person greater civil rights.

The actual line was:  

"No man shall be compelled to frequent or support any religious worship, place, or ministry whatsoever, nor shall be enforced, restrained, molested, or burthened in his body or goods, nor... otherwise suffer on account of his religious opinions or belief... All men shall be free to profess and by argument to maintain their opinions in matters of religion, and... the same shall in no wise diminish, enlarge, or affect their civil capacities."

22. Make a slogan or personal declaration on how you can be committed to your self-development.


THE SLOGAN FOR SELF-DEVELOPMENT:

"You have to have a level of dissatisfaction to feel the drive to want to grow."

EXPLANATION:

Self-development can only occur when a person still has dissatisfaction or thirst and realizes that there is still a part of him/her that can be developed. Self-development requires an expensive investment in oneself. To yourself? Is this selfish? Of course not. By developing ourselves, our capacity and potential to help others will also increase.

Self-development is a proactive attitude to always look for ideas and ways to achieve self-improvement. As we should have realized, formal education is not the end of learning towards greatness and contribution. Moreover, formal education usually does not teach us the competencies of success, such as how to build relationships, build a career, be a good parent, achieve happiness, and so on. Well, usually the success of any self-development methodology is seen from the two attitudes formed in the process, namely acceptance and dissatisfaction.

Any self-improvement technique will surely teach you to accept yourself as you are. That there are many things to be grateful for and we should just accept it as it is. If there is a model of self-development that doesn't teach us to behave that way, then something is wrong there. But then this is it; when we want to develop for the better, shouldn't we automatically have dissatisfaction with our current condition? And indeed: if we are serious about self-improvement, we will always detect a disturbing gap: between who we are and what we want to be, between our current and future levels of self-competence.

First you are asked to accept yourself as you are, then why are you then asked to instill anxiety and not be complacent? In fact, to be successful in self-improvement, we do have to throw away our present and former selves. But why is there such a serious logical conflict here? This is where it gets interesting. What does self really mean? Which part to be grateful for? Which part do we deserve not to be complacent? As we already know, humans are made up of spirit and body entities. While what they can do, one of them is to build competence in the form of knowledge, skills, and mentality.

Criteria

The criteria for selecting a national artist include Filipino citizenship, the contribution of their works to nation-building, impact of their distinctive and pioneering works or styles on succeeding generations of artists, excellence in the practice of their art form which enriches artistic expression or style, and prestigious national and international recognitions.

The National Artist Award secretariat (the NCCA and CCP) announces the opening of nominations through media releases and letters to qualified organizations, such as government and nongovernment cultural organizations, educational institutions, and private foundations and councils.

The National Artist Award Panel of Experts and Jury of Experts, with the living national artists as automatic members, screen and deliberate on the nominees, after which a final list of recommendees is sent to the President for the issuance of proclamation.
