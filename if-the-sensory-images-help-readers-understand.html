Answer:

1. sensory image is a strategy readers use to think more deeply about a text. It is when a reader combines their schema and the information in the text to create an image in their mind. This image can represent all of the five senses (visual, smell, taste, sound, touch, or feeling).

2. Imagery can make something abstract, like an emotion or theory, seem more concrete and tangible to the reader. By using imagery, writers can evoke the feeling they want to talk about in their readers...and by making their readers feel, writers can also help readers connect to the messages in their work.

3. sensory images also help a reader draw on specific details in the text (e.g. a character's thoughts, words, or actions; elements of tone, meaning, or beauty of a text), creating an interaction between the reader and the text.

4. Sensory language enhances your writing and immerses your reader in the scene. It helps the reader to visualize, hear, and imagine the scenario, so they can experience it rather than just digest the information you're trying to convey.

5. Language that connects with tangible and sensory (taste, smell, touch, sight, and sound) is easier for readers to understand and relate to.

29. 1. What are the 6 sensory images, define each, give its usage and importance. 2. Give at least 2 examples of the usage of each sensory images. 3. How does the Sensory images help you with your day to day activities? Elaborate your answers.​


Answer:

The 6 sensory images are sight, sound, taste, smell, touch, and hearing.

Sight: Sight, or visual imagery, refers to the sense of seeing and the ability to perceive visual stimuli. Sight is used to interpret and make sense of the world around us, and it is often the most dominant sense used in communication.

Sound: Sound, or auditory imagery, refers to the sense of hearing and the ability to perceive auditory stimuli. Sound is used to communicate and convey meaning, and it plays a key role in how we perceive and interpret our surroundings.

Taste: Taste, or gustatory imagery, refers to the sense of taste and the ability to perceive flavors and other taste sensations. Taste is used to identify and distinguish different foods and beverages, and it plays a key role in how we perceive and enjoy the flavors of the world around us.

Smell: Smell, or olfactory imagery, refers to the sense of smell and the ability to perceive odors and other smells. Smell is used to identify and distinguish different scents and fragrances, and it plays a key role in how we perceive and enjoy the smells of the world around us.

Touch: Touch, or tactile imagery, refers to the sense of touch and the ability to perceive physical sensations through contact with the skin. Touch is used to interpret and make sense of the physical world around us, and it plays a key role in how we interact with and manipulate objects.

Hearing: Hearing, or auditory imagery, refers to the sense of hearing and the ability to perceive auditory stimuli. Hearing is used to communicate and convey meaning, and it plays a key role in how we perceive and interpret our surroundings.

2. Examples of the usage of each sensory image:

Sight: Watching a movie, reading a book

Sound: Listening to music, attending a live performance

Taste: Eating a delicious meal, trying a new type of food

Smell: Smelling a bouquet of flowers, identifying a familiar scent

Touch: Feeling the texture of a soft blanket, sensing the warmth of a fire

Hearing: Listening to a conversation, hearing a bird singing

3. Sensory images are important for our day-to-day activities because they allow us to interpret and make sense of the world around us. They provide us with the means to perceive and experience the sights, sounds, tastes, smells, and sensations of the world, and they play a key role in how we communicate and interact with others. Sensory images help us to navigate our environment and make informed decisions, and they can also enrich our lives by providing us with the means to appreciate and enjoy the beauty and diversity of the world around us.
