Answer:

Energy can be transformed from one form to another through various processes. Here are some examples of energy transformations:

Flashlight: In a flashlight, chemical energy from the battery is transformed into electrical energy, which is then transformed into light energy and heat energy when the bulb is turned on.Washing machine: In a washing machine, electrical energy from the power source is transformed into mechanical energy, which is used to turn the drum and agitate the clothes. Friction between the clothes and the drum generates heat energy.Food: When food is eaten, the body transforms the chemical energy stored in the food into various forms of energy, such as mechanical energy for movement, heat energy for maintaining body temperature, and electrical energy for nerve and muscle function.Electric bulb: In an electric bulb, electrical energy is transformed into light energy and heat energy when the filament inside the bulb is heated to a high temperature by the flow of electric current.Pupil: When light enters the eye, it is transformed into electrical energy by the photoreceptor cells in the retina. This electrical energy is then transformed into chemical energy and then back into electrical energy as the signal is transmitted to the brain. In the brain, the electrical energy is transformed into other forms of energy, such as sound energy or mechanical energy for movement.

Overall, energy can be transformed from one form to another through various processes, and the type of energy that is produced depends on the specific materials and conditions involved in the transformation.
