3. What is the transactional theory of reading?


The transactional theory signifies that both the reader and the text play important roles in the formation of meaning. Meaning is produced by continuous transaction between the reader and the text, employing the meaning potential of the text and the reader's experiential reservoir.

1964 book by American linguist Noam Chomsky. 16. Who provide this definition on reading? "There is a transaction between the reader and the text which involves the reader's act on interpreting the text. The interpretation is influenced by the reader's experiences, language and cultural background as well as the reader's purpose for reading." Select one: a. William Gray b. Hudelson c. Gambrell d. Bernhardt


Answer: B. Hudelson


Sarah Hudelson wrote about Literacy Development of Second Language Children in F. Genesee’s Educating Second Language Children: The whole child, the whole curriculum, the whole community.


So to sum up, Hudelson defined reading on her "Literacy Development of Second Language Children" during 1994.


She offered a definition of reading - a language process in which an individual constructs meaning through a transaction with written text that has been created by symbols that represent language. The transaction involves the reader’s acting upon or interpreting the text and the interpretation is influenced by the reader’s past experiences, language background, and cultural framework, as well as the reader’s purpose for reading.


This definition actually emphasizes the reader’s role in constructing meaning through the texts and the importance of the language skills as well as the experience that the reader brings to the act.


It is a definition of comprehension that corresponds to the interaction between the texts and the readers. For Sarah Hudelson, reading truly implies comprehension. Furthermore, literacy development and acquisition is essentially a cognitive process and the readers or the students’ role is to functionally and actively use the written languages to make, create, and construct meanings through transactions with written texts that have been produced by the symbols. She explains that this mental transaction involves the student or reader in interpreting and acting the texts, as well as the interpretations are influenced by the past experiences of the students or readers, the language background, and of course, of the cultural framework as well as the purpose for reading of the students or readers.

    The Interpersonal Theory of Nursing was created by Henry Stack-Sullivan, who is regarded as the founding of personal psychiatry. This theory clarified the significance of life transitions for schizophrenia as well as the part played by social interactions and experiences in the development of characters. The six development models that Stack-Sullivan refers to as "periods" or "methodology phases in formation" are explained by the interpersonal theory.

     Infanthood is the preliminary step, which lasts between birth to 18 months. The satisfaction of wants is this event's primary attribute. The second step lasts from the age of 18 months to 6 years. Children are distinguished by a need for deferred pleasure. The establishment of a friendship group is a hallmark of the adolescent period, which lasts from ages six to nine. Between the ages of nine to twelve is the fourth stage, called preadolescence. The growth of connections between individuals of the same gender defines it. Between the ages of twelve and fourteen is early puberty. The teenager is developing a personality at this time. The developmental period, the sixth stage, lasts from fourteen until twenty-one years old. The development of long-lasting, personal relations characterizes this stage of Stack-nursing Sullivan's paradigm.

     Identity, conscience, and fear are all explained by Stack-thesis. Sullivan claims that security management is the steps taken by the person to lessen fear and maintain safety. All of the security precautions a person takes to stave off stress and maintain their self-esteem collectively make up their self-care system. To precisely treat people who are depressed and insane, this nursing theory offers the theoretical framework for individual therapy. 24. Why is there a need in mastering the theory of debit and credit to analyze a business transaction?​


Answer:

For every value received, there is a value given; or for every debit, there is a credit. This is the concept of double-entry accounting.

Explanation:

The accounting equation (Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity) must remain in balance after every transaction is recorded, so accountants must analyze each transaction to determine how it affects owner's equity and the different types of assets and liabilities before recording the transaction.
