Non-print sources are media that use electronics or electromechanical energy for the end user (audience) to access the content. This is in contrast to static media (mainly print media), which today are most often created electronically, but do not require electronics to be accessed by the end user in the printed form.

29. What is print and non-print materials? Give some examples.​


Anything that is printed on paper or another physical medium, such as books, newspapers, magazines, brochures, flyers, posters, and pamphlets, is considered a print material. The written word and images are used to convey information to the reader or viewer in these materials.

On the other hand, any materials that do not rely on printed text or images to convey information are considered non-print materials. Videos, films, television shows, audio recordings, podcasts, websites, blogs, and social media are all examples of digital or audiovisual media. In order to convey information to the audience, they make use of a variety of multimedia elements like sound, images, graphics, animation, and interactivity.

Books, textbooks, newspapers, and magazines are examples of print materials, while online news articles, YouTube videos, podcasts, and e-books are examples of non-print materials.
