8. Why do estuaries have different organisms participating in its food chain?​.


Explanation:

Estuaries connect land and sea. They are partly enclosed bodies of water situated at the edge of the land – a mixture of freshwater from streams and rivers and saltwater from the sea. Estuaries come in all shapes and sizes and can be called harbours, inlets, bays, lagoons, sounds, wetlands and swamps. They are the nurseries of oceans. Many fish and shellfish are spawned in estuaries. Full of nutrients and home to resilient organisms, estuaries provide rich feeding grounds for fish and birds.

23. define food web&food chain, estuary&intertidal zone pls answer i need it now. ​


Answer:

Food Web: A food web is a complex interconnected network of food chains. It shows the feeding relationships between different species in an ecosystem. The species are grouped into different trophic levels, with each level being fed upon by the level above it and feeding the level below it.

Food Chain: A food chain is a single directional path of energy flow in an ecosystem. It starts with a producer, like a plant, and ends with a consumer, like a human or a fish. Every organism in the food chain takes energy from the organism below it and passes it on to the organism above.

Estuary: An estuary is a partially enclosed coastal body of water that is affected by the tides and has freshwater and saltwater mixing together. Estuaries are important habitats for many species of birds, fish and other wildlife.

Intertidal Zone: The intertidal zone is the area between the high and low tide marks on a shoreline. This zone is exposed to air during low tide and is underwater during high tide. It is one of the most productive ecosystems on the planet and is home to a wide variety of marine life.
