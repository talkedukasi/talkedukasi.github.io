1. trace the transformation of energy of radio​


Explanation:

The transformation of energy in radio waves can be traced as follows:

1. Electrical energy: The radio signal starts as an electrical current that flows through a transmitting antenna.

2. Electromagnetic energy: As the electrical current moves back and forth along the antenna, it creates an electromagnetic field that stretches out into space around the antenna.

3. Radio wave energy: The electromagnetic field generated by the electrical current in the antenna propagates through space as radio waves. These waves are a form of electromagnetic radiation that travels at the speed of light.

4. Signal transmission: The radio waves travel through the atmosphere, where they may encounter various obstacles, such as buildings, trees, or mountains. The waves can reflect off these obstacles or diffract around them to reach their intended destination.

5. Reception: When the radio waves arrive at the receiving antenna, they induce an electrical current that is proportional to the strength of the incoming waves.

6. Amplification: The electrical signal received by the antenna is typically weak and needs to be amplified before it can be processed further. This is done by a radio receiver or amplifier, which boosts the signal to a level that can be detected and decoded.

7. Audio energy: Once the signal is amplified, it can be converted into audible sound waves by a loudspeaker or headphones. The electrical energy of the radio signal has now been transformed into sound energy that can be heard by humans.
