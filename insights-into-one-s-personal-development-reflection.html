The Philippines has a language problem. Among its more than 7,000 islands, nearly 200 languages have sprung up—each with speakers numbering from a few hundred to millions across the world. With so many disparate languages, which one ought to serve as the common denominator? Spanish, English, and Filipino have each been proposed as solutions, but questions of inclusivity, representation, and effectiveness have hounded their statuses as official national languages.

This gap is what mother tongue-based multilingual education (MTB-MLE) intends to address. Signed into law as part of Republic Act 10533 or the Enhanced Basic Education Act of 2013, MTB-MLE is implemented as part of the Department of Education’s (DepEd) K-12 Basic Education Program. DepEd describes it as early education in which lessons are taught in the learner’s mother tongue or first language. The program currently translates material and lesson plans into eight languages for different regions in the country.

Less than a decade after MTB-MLE’s inception, educators continue to anticipate its results, weighing in on its educational benefits, costs, and inclusivity issues.

4. why is it important to have an insight about your personal development?​


IMPORTANCE OF HAVING INSIGHT ABOUT YOUR PERSONAL DEVELOPMENT

Having insight into your personal development is important because it will help you get out of your comfort zone. It will also boost your confidence and help you know your priorities. Most importantly, being aware of your own personal development will help your work and balance life when you grow up.

Things to consider why they're important?It Inspires You to Move Away From Your Comfort Zone

Interrogating your areas of weakness by asking yourself or the people around you will push you out of your comfort zone. It is one aspect of personal development. In the workplace, this will almost certainly mean that you don't like or excel at certain elements of your job. Your confidence and experience will grow as a result of facing these issues head-on.

It will boost your  self-esteem

Learning a new skill, taking up a new interest, or reading more broadly are all examples of personal growth. Self-assigned goals are a great way to increase your self-esteem. Your work performance will be better if you are confident in yourself and your talents.

It Aids You in Identifying Your Priorities

By exploring your underlying beliefs, motives, and life goals, taking the time to focus on your personal growth will force you to become more self-aware. You'll gain fresh insights about yourself and possibly re-evaluate your priorities, which could have an impact on your professional performance. It's possible that this period of reflection will lead to a major career move. By learning how you develop yourself, you will determine your passion along the way.

It Helps You Have a Better Work-Life Balance

If you don't already have one, maybe focusing on your own personal development can motivate you to find one if you don't already have one. Take better care of your mental health by spending time for yourself and for those who you love. Learn to break away from toxic people and focus on developing yourself.
