13. what are the example of state and non-state institution ​


Answer:

NON-STATE INSTITUTION

Non-state institution is an institution who participates in international affairs and relations but are not affiliated with any state or nation.

EXAMPLES OF NON-STATE INSTITUTION

Banks

Corporations

Cooperatives

Trade Unions

Transnational Advocacy

Development Agencies

International Organizations

BANKS

Banks are examples of a non-state institution. Banks are very important since banks regulate the flow of the people's flow of money.

CORPORATIONS

Corporations are established by group of shareholders who have ownership in the corporation. A corporation is usually set up to make profit and provide return to its shareholders.

COOPERATIVES

Cooperatives are non-state institutions which are owned by people who voluntarily share or cooperate with each other under the influence of their social, economic and cultural benefits. Cooperatives are mainly set up in order for humans to create mutual benefit with each other.

TRADE UNIONS

Trade unions are organized by workers and laborers for members to share mutual benefit. One of the major goal of labor unions is to achieve an improved working conditions of a certain country's labor force.

TRANSNATIONAL ADVOCACY

Transnational advocacy which aims to influence a political, economic and social decision, is an idea from an individual or group. Actors working together internationally on an issue is what transnational advocacy network includes.

DEVELOPMENT AGENCIES

Development agencies are agencies which simply aims or wish to improve the current standpoint of a society.

INTERNATIONAL ORGANIZATIONS

International organizations are organizations that promote, enhance and ensure its members through advocacies and state actions. There are two main types of international organization:

1. International nongovernmental organizations( INGOs)

2. Intergovernmental organizations (IGOs).
