1. Timeline of community library ​


Answer:

Here is a possible timeline of the development of community libraries:

Early 19th century: The concept of community libraries begins to take shape in the United States, as groups of citizens pool their resources to purchase books and establish lending libraries.

1876: The American Library Association (ALA) is founded, with the mission of promoting the development of libraries and improving the profession of librarianship.

Late 19th century: The idea of free public libraries gains traction in the United States, with the establishment of the first free public library in Peterborough, New Hampshire in 1833. The movement spreads rapidly, with many communities establishing their own public libraries in the decades that follow.

Early 20th century: Andrew Carnegie, a Scottish-American businessman and philanthropist, donates millions of dollars to fund the construction of public libraries throughout the United States and other countries.

Mid-20th century: Libraries continue to evolve, with the development of bookmobiles and the expansion of library services to include audio and video materials.

Late 20th century: Libraries embrace new technologies, including computerized catalogs and internet access for patrons.

21st century: Community libraries continue to thrive, with many libraries offering a wide range of programs and services to meet the needs of their communities, including job training, technology classes, and cultural events.

Throughout its history, the community library has played a vital role in providing access to information and promoting literacy and education. While the technology and resources available to libraries have changed over time, the fundamental mission of the community library remains the same: to serve as a free and open resource for all members of the community.

20. ACTIVITY TIMELINE Directions: Write the history timeline of origami, from the start to present. Timeline


Answer:

Origami, which is the art of paper folding, is one of the most iconic cultural contributions of Japan. When did it get its start? Where did it get its start? How did it get its start? These are all questions answered in this lesson.

Historians are unable to pinpoint an exact date for the beginnings of origami, but it most likely began soon after paper was invented by the Chinese nearly 2,000 years ago, sometime around the year 100 CE. Approximately 500 years later, Buddhist monks brought paper to Japan. Historians speculate that the practice of paper folding is most likely as old as paper itself, so the Chinese probably practiced some form of paper folding before the Japanese.

The name ''origami'' is fairly new compared to the art form's long history. In 1880, the art of Japanese paper folding was changed from orikata, which means ''folded shapes,'' to origami, from the Japanese oru (meaning ''to fold'') and kami (meaning ''paper'').
