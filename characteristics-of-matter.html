4. how are characteristic properties of matter used to differentiate matter?​


Explanation:

Characteristic properties of matter are used to differentiate matter by allowing scientists to distinguish between different types of substances. Some common characteristic properties include:

Density: The density of a substance is a measure of its mass per unit volume and is a unique property of that substance. This can be used to differentiate between substances with different densities.

Melting Point: The temperature at which a solid turns into a liquid is called the melting point. Different substances have different melting points, so this property can be used to identify them.

Boiling Point: The temperature at which a liquid turns into a gas is called the boiling point. Different substances have different boiling points, which can be used to differentiate them.

Solubility: The solubility of a substance is the maximum amount of a substance that can dissolve in a solvent at a given temperature and pressure. This can be used to differentiate between substances with different solubility.

Conductivity: The conductivity of a substance refers to its ability to conduct electricity. Different substances have different conductivity, which can be used to differentiate them.

Magnetic Properties: The magnetic properties of a substance refer to its ability to be attracted to or repelled by a magnetic field. Different substances have different magnetic properties, which can be used to differentiate them.

By using these and other characteristic properties, scientists can differentiate between different types of matter and identify unknown substances.
