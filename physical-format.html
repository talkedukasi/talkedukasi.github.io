15. explain how physical,chemical and biotic factor takes place in the formation of the ped


PEDON SOIL

I think the last word in the question pertains to a pedon. What is a pedon?

The pedon is known as the tiniest possible three dimensional volume of soil (usually 1 m2 at the surface and extends to the bottom) that is large enough to provide the classification of natural soil.

Factors for its formation

1. Physical Change - Some of the factors that allow physical changes to take place in the pedon soil for its formation are:

• Water (bodies of water and precipitation)

• Wind

• Temperature (Atmospheric conditions)

• Frost wedging

2. Chemical Change - Several factors affect the pedon soil's chemical components and structure, some of these are:

• Dissolution

• Hydration

• Hydrolysis

• Oxidation

3. Biological - Factors that allow biological formation in the pedon soil are lichen and root-wedging.
