The Sacrament of Reconciliation is one of the treasures of the Catholic Tradition. Yet it is not well understood. 16. reflection about sacrament of reconciliation


SACRAMENT OF RECONCILIATION

The Sacrament of Penance (also known as the Sacrament of Reconciliation or Confession) is one of the Catholic Church's seven sacraments (also known as sacred mysteries in Eastern Christianity) in which the faithful are cleansed of their sins and reconciled with the Christian community. Currently, reconciliation rituals are utilized to highlight the social nature of the sacraments, but both grave and venial sins must be confessed for the sake of devotion. Only individuals who have been ordained as priests have the authority to administer absolution, according to current church doctrine and practice.

The sacraments of Penance, Reconciliation, Forgiveness, Confession, and Conversion are all names for the sacraments of Penance, Reconciliation, Forgiveness, Confession, and Conversion.

Christ ordained this sacrament for the conversion of the baptized who have been alienated from him by sin since the new life of grace gained in Baptism does not erase the weakness of human nature nor the inclination to sin (that is, concupiscence).
