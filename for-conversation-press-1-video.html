2. Summary of the story "for conversation, press #1 "


The text For Conversation, Press # 1, expresses the writers thoughts on the effects of online connectivity and the advent of technology in general.

In the text, the writer recounted his own experience of being ignored when a friend he was jogging with suddenly received a call. He mentioned that he also saw that most people in the park were not talking to each other but instead were engrossed in their gadgets.

In the subsequent paragraphs, he stated the reasons why this is not right. He also provided solutions to his dilemma.

3. reflection about for conversation press #1


I was happy to find someone who has the same thoughts as mine about 24-7 online connectivity.

Being a person who enjoys talking to random people whenever I get the chance, I feel sad because whenever I am in a queue waiting in line, everyone seems to be avoiding real conversations but instead focus too much or at least pretend to watch movies, listen to music, and browse the internet.

Technology is has revolutionized the way people communicate but it should be a reason for developing real and not virtual relations.

Text-to-self connections relate ideas learned in a text with a student's own experiences or ideas. Text-to-text connections are recurringwords or ideas within a book. Finally, text-to-world connections are links between ideas in a text and other domains of knowledge.

What is a text to self connection?

Text-to-self connections are highly personal connections that a reader makes between a piece of reading material and the reader's own experiences or life. An example of a text-to-self connection might be, "This story reminds me of a vacation we took to my grandfather's farm."

29. these are a series of animated images or soundless video in the smallest file size, that loop continuously without anyone pressing play?​


A series of animated images or soundless video in the smallest file size, that loop continuously without anyone pressing play

GIF (Graphical Interchange Format) is an image format invented in 1987 by Steve Wilhite, a US software writer looking for a way to animate images with the smallest file size possible. In a nutshell, GIFs are a series of images or soundless video that will loop indefinitely without requiring anyone to press play. GIFs become instantly familiar as a result of this repetition, much like the beat of a song.

Is it "GIF" or "Jif"? This is our generation's "Tomato, tomahto" – regardless of how you say it, we know GIFs are here to stay. Fortunately, people nowadays are less concerned with grammar and more concerned with the image itself. Along with memes and viral videos, millennials' favorite file format has recently become the default tool of web humor. They've become so popular that the term "GIF" was named the Oxford Dictionary Word of the Year in 2012.
