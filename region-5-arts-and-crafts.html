The Bicolano people or the Bikolanos (Bikol: Mga Bikolnon) are the fourth-largest Filipino ethnolinguistic group.[2] Male are usually called as Bicolano and Bicolana for female. Their native region is commonly referred to as Bicolandia, which comprises the entirety of the Bicol Peninsula and neighbouring minor islands, all in the southeast portion of Luzon.

They are largely an agricultural and rural people, producing rice, coconuts, hemp, they are rich in spices. That's why Bicolanos are said to be great when it comes to eating chili food or foods which are high in spice. Nearly all of them are Christians, predominantly Roman Catholics, but with some Protestant minorities. Proves that they celebrate Saints, and other patriots. Their languages are divided based from their cities, which is actually a collection of closely related varieties, is closely related to other languages of the central Philippines, all of which belong to the Austronesian (specifically Malayo-Polynesian) super-family of languages.[3]
