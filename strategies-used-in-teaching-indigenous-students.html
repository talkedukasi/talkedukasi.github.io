2. Part II. Situational Analysis (Items 31-60) You are a newly-hired permanent teacher in Division X and assigned to GVC Integrated School. The school is situated in a far-flung area and 95% of the students belong to the indigenous people. With the students in mind, apply what you have learned in Educ 223 course based on the specific questions below. 1. What should you consider in teaching students in this community? (10 Points) 2. What teaching strategies should you employ to ensure inclusive education in this group? Explain 2 strategies from your answer. (10 points) 3. Should you consider as well the assessment, learning resources, and instructional processes? Why? (10 Points)​


Answer:

1. When teaching students in this community, it is important to consider the unique backgrounds and experiences of the students, as well as their needs and the conditions of the community. Moreover, it is essential to create a safe and supportive environment that is inclusive and respectful of the different cultures, languages, and beliefs. Furthermore, teachers should also consider the availability and access of resources, such as books and instructional materials, as this is often a challenge in far-flung areas.

2. To ensure inclusive education in the group, teachers should employ teaching strategies that support learning for all students. Firstly, teachers should use differentiated instruction and ensure that instructional materials are tailored to the students' unique backgrounds and experiences. In this way, teachers can meet the specific needs of each student. Secondly, teachers should also promote peer-to-peer learning, in which students work together to complete tasks and help one another understand challenging content. This encourages social cohesion and respect for diversity among students.

3. Yes, teachers should consider the assessment, learning resources, and instructional processes when teaching students in this community. The assessment should be developmentally appropriate and fair for all students and should be adjusted to students’ diverse backgrounds and experiences. Furthermore, learning resources should be carefully selected to meet the individual needs of the students and allow them to learn effectively. Lastly, instructional processes should be engaging and interactive, allowing students to be actively involved in the learning process.
