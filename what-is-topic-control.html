3. What is Topic Control?


Topic control -  Sticking to the topic throughout the discussion.

 

When one nominates a topic and the parties begin to talk about the topic and the one, usually the one who nominates the topic, reminds anyone who goes off topic to stick to the agreed one, topic control takes place. In a way topic control is topic restriction.

 

Examples:

May I remind everyone that the topic is ___?

We are talking about ____ here, right?

Let’s go back to the topic.

5. What is Topic Control?


Topic control is a communicative strategy used to control and prevent unnecessary interruptions and topic shifts in a certain conversation. Topic control is considered a procedural formality or informality that affects the development of certain topics in particular discussion or conversation.

 

Moreover, topic control works along the concept of turn-taking, where everyone can express related ideas to the ongoing topic without the need to interrupt others.

 


6. what is Topic control? give an example


Topic control is a technique of exerting one's influence in a conversation or discussion to keep the topic on a desired subject. An example of this in real life is continued mentions of the desired topic in a conversation, not allowing other parties to begin a discussion on any other subject.
