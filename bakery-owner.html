5. Bakery A used 12kg of flour less than bakery B. Bakery C used twice as much flour as bakery A and bakery B combined. If the three bakeries used a total of 936kg of flour, how much flour did bakery C use?​


Answer:

576kg of flour

Step-by-step explanation:

Bakery B:

x kg

Bakery A:

(x-12) kg

Bakery C:

2(x-12+x) kg = 2(2x-12) kg

Solve for x:

936=x+x-12+2(2x-12)

936=2x-12+4x-24

936=6x-36

936=6(x-6)

156=x-6

150=x

Substitute the value of x to each term:

Bakery B:

x kg = 150kg

Bakery A:

(x-12) kg = 138kg

Bakery C:

2(2x-12) kg = 576kg

Bakery C used 576kg of flour.
