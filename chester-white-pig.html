1. 1. Chester has 45 red chips, 12 blue chips and 24 white chips. What is the probability that Chester randomly selects a red chip or white chip?​


Answer:

0.85 or 85%

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the probability that Chester randomly selects a red chip or white chip, we need to find the total number of red and white chips and divide it by the total number of chips.

The total number of red and white chips is 45 + 24 = 69.

The total number of chips is 45 + 12 + 24 = 81.

Therefore, the probability of selecting a red chip or white chip is:

69/81 = 0.85

Or, we can also express it as a percentage:

85%

Therefore, the probability that Chester randomly selects a red chip or white chip is 0.85 or 85%.

Chester Irving Barnard (November 7, 1886 – June 7, 1961) was an American business executive, public administrator, and the author of pioneering work in management theory and organizational studies. His landmark 1938 book, The Functions of the Executive, sets out a theory of organization and of the functions of executives in organizations. The book has been widely assigned in university courses in management theory and organizational sociology. Barnard viewed organizations as systems of cooperation of human activity, and noted that they are typically short-lived. According to Barnard, organizations are generally not long-lived because they do not meet the two criteria necessary for survival: effectiveness and efficiency.

29. Give the Penotypic Ratio and the Genotypic ratio of the offspring that will result from a cross between two pigs which are homozygous black dominant and homozygous white?


Let's first start with the alleles.

B - black (dominant)

b - white (recessive)

Now, let's move to the genes of the parents.

Father - BB (homozygous black pig)

Mother - bb (homozygous white pig)

REMEMBER: This is an example of Mendelian Genetics: Law of Complete Dominance as hinted by the problem. The law of complete dominance states that the dominant trait for a certain allele always masks the recessive allele. The revealed phenotype will always be the dominant gene but when checked for the genotype it will be revealed that the recessive gene is still there, only that it is not expressed.

Test cross using a Punnett square will yield the following:

Female \ Male       B              B

b                             Bb            Bb

b                            Bb            Bb

Phenotypic ratio: 4:0 (black:white)

Genotypic ratio: 4 Bb: 0 (all heterozygous black)
