22. 1.what is the poem all about? 2.what symbolisms are present? 3.what historical event in the country is being talked about in the poem? 4.what is the moral of the story? MANGO POEM BY REGIE CEBICO​


The poem "Mango" by Regie Cebico is about the speaker's memories of their childhood and the significance of mangoes in their life. It describes the different stages of mangoes, from the young green fruit to the ripe golden one, and how they evoke feelings of nostalgia and longing for the past.

The mango is a powerful symbol in the poem, representing the speaker's connection to their childhood and the simplicity of rural life. The stages of the mango, from the unripe green fruit to the ripe golden one, also symbolize the different stages of life and the fleeting nature of time.

The poem does not explicitly mention any historical event in the country, but it highlights the speaker's longing for the simplicity and beauty of rural life, which may be seen as a contrast to the fast-paced urban lifestyle in modern times.

The moral of the story is that despite the passing of time and the changes that come with it, the memories and experiences of our childhood can continue to hold a special place in our hearts and bring us comfort and joy. The poem also emphasizes the importance of connecting with nature and preserving our cultural heritage.
