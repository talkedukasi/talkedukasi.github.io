22. a national artist awardee in dance and the mother/founder of the Philippine folkdance?​


The national artist awardee in dance who is also considered as the mother/founder of the Philippine folk dance is Francisca Reyes-Aquino. She is recognized for her extensive research, documentation, and promotion of traditional Philippine folk dances, as well as her contributions to the development of Philippine dance as a formal art form. Her work has helped to preserve and promote the rich cultural heritage of the Philippines through dance.

Answer:

Fondly called the “Mother of Philippine Dancing, Francisca Reyes-Aquino lived a life dedicated to preserving Filipino heritage through studying and teaching folk dances. Reyes-Aquino is the first National Artist for Dance.
