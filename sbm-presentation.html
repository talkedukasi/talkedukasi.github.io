3. which countries apply SBM? did sbm implementation help improve their schools? Share your findings​


SCHOOL-BASED MANAGEMENT (SBM)

School-based management (SBM) is a strategy to improve education by transferring significant decision-making authority from state and district offices to individual schools.

School-based management provides principals, teachers, students and parents control over the education process by giving them the responsibility for decisions about the programs/projects. SBM can create a learning environment for the students.

El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, and Nicaragua introduced education decentralization reforms, including school-based management (SBM) in some schools.

School-based Management (SBM) has been implemented in Indonesia since 2000, but after 16 years of implementation, there are still many schools that have not succeeded in implementing it. Implementation of school-based Management so far has met with limited success. Although most principals reported that they had the autonomy to make school decisions, they also said that they did not take advantage of it by making significant programmatic and instructional changes.

17. 1. what are the potential disadvantages of school -based management ( SBM) in terms of decision making and accountability? 2.what are some best practices for implementing school-based management in a way that is legally sound effective?3.How can school -based management empower stakeholders in the school community to take an active role in decision?1.As a student,How does school -based management create a more effective learning environment?​


Answer:

1. Potential disadvantages of school-based management (SBM):

- SBM can create unequal distribution of resources and opportunities among schools.

- SBM can potentially widen the existing gap between high-performing and low-performing schools.

- SBM may create conflicts between stakeholders, such as parents, teachers, and administrators, which can lead to delayed decision-making and implementation.

2. Best practices for implementing school-based management:

- Ensure that SBM policies are aligned with national and local laws and regulations.

- Involve all stakeholders in the decision-making process and establish clear lines of accountability.

- Provide adequate training and support to school leaders and teachers.

- Establish a system for monitoring and evaluating the effectiveness of SBM policies and practices.

3. How school-based management can empower stakeholders:

- SBM can give stakeholders more control over decision-making, allowing them to tailor policies and practices to their specific needs and contexts.

- SBM can encourage greater participation and collaboration among stakeholders, fostering a sense of ownership and accountability.

- SBM can promote transparency and open communication, creating a more transparent and responsive system.

4. The potential advantages of school-based management for students include:

- Improved academic achievement as a result of greater stakeholder involvement and investment in the school's success.

- More effective allocation of resources and opportunities based on the specific needs of the student population.

- A greater focus on teaching and learning, as stakeholders work together to create a supportive and effective learning environment.
