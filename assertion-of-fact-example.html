23. explain what is assertion of fact, assertion of convention, assertion of opinion,and assertion of preference ​


Answer:

Assertion of Fact: An assertion of fact is a statement that can be proven to be true or false. It is based on evidence and can be verified. Examples include: The sky is blue, Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.

Assertion of Convention: An assertion of convention is a statement that reflects the accepted behavior or practice in a particular society or culture. Examples include: It is polite to say please and thank you, In the United States, people drive on the right side of the road.

Assertion of Opinion: An assertion of opinion is a statement that expresses an individual's beliefs or feelings about something. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: Chocolate ice cream is the best flavor, I think rap music is underrated.

Assertion of Preference: An assertion of preference is a statement that expresses an individual's preference for something over another option. It cannot be proven to be true or false and may vary from person to person. Examples include: I prefer coffee over tea, I like apples more than oranges.

28. Write 5 example for each Type of Assertion. • Statement of Convention • Statement of Fact • Statement of Opinion • Statement of Preference​


Answer:

There are many types of assertions in logic and critical thinking, but here are five examples each of three common types:

1. Categorical Assertions:

- All dogs are mammals.

- No humans are invincible.

- Some birds can fly long distances without stopping.

- All trees have roots.

- No cars can fly.

2. Hypothetical Assertions:

- If it rains, the ground will be wet.

- If I study hard, I will pass the exam.

- If I save enough money, I will buy a new car.

- If a plant gets enough sunlight, it will grow.

- If I eat too much junk food, I will gain weight.

3. Disjunctive Assertions:

- Either I will go to the gym or I will go for a run.

- The cat is either on the couch or in the kitchen.

- The concert will be either indoors or outdoors.

- We can travel by car or by train.

- The restaurant serves either vegan or vegetarian food.
