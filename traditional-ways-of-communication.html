9. Lecture is the most traditional method associated with teaching in which the teacher simply conveys the knowledge to the student sin a one way channel of communication


The lecture method is the most convenient and cost-effective way to teach any topic. Except for the blackboard, it practically necessitates the use of scientific gear, experiments, and supplementary materials. The lecture method is a teacher-controlled and information-centered strategy in which the instructor serves as the sole resource in the classroom. The sole instructor speaks in this manner, while the learners passively listen. This causes boredom in the classrooms since contact between the student and teacher decreases.

The lecture method is widely utilized in the field of education. This strategy is utilized to gain knowledge and concepts. The lecture technique is primarily concerned with cognitive objectives. The presentation of the material is the central objective of this technique. Teachers plan and direct the whole teaching-learning process in this approach. The teacher can use audio-visual aids to make the lesson more engaging.

Advantages of Lecture Method

Through their excitement, effective lecturers may express the intrinsic attraction of a subject.Lectures can provide students with information that do not have access to.Lectures can be tailored to fit the needs of distinct audiences.Lectures may convey a lot of information.Lectures may be delivered to huge groups of people.Lectures provide the lecturer with complete control over the learning experience.

Disadvantages of the Lecture Method

Lectures do not provide an opportunity to give feedback on the amount of student learning.Students are frequently inert during lectures since there is no system in place to guarantee that they are intellectually engaged with the topic.When pupils are passive, they tend to forget information rapidly.Lectures assume that all students are learning at the same rate and at the same level of comprehension.Lectures are not appropriate for teaching higher-order thinking abilities such as application, analysis, synthesis, or assessment; teaching motor skills; or influencing attitudes or values.Lectures are not ideal for teaching difficult, abstract concepts.
