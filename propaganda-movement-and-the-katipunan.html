1. The propaganda movement and the katipunan


A group of Filipinos engaged in the Propaganda Movement, which lasted roughly from 1880 to 1898 with the peak of activity between 1880 and 1895, as a series of communication actions through books, leaflets, and newspaper articles calling for political reforms. Given that the propagandists, many of whom had some Spanish ancestry, perceived the campaign as an assimilationist one, themselves as the inheritors of Spanish civilization, advocated for the full incorporation of the Philippines into Spain as a Spanish province rather than just as a colony, with Filipinos being granted the same citizenship rights as Spanish citizens. Second, it aimed to give native Filipino clergy more influence and drive out Spanish friars from the Philippines. Finally, as a cultural movement, it displayed the young Filipino elite's writing and artistic output to prove that they were on par with their Spanish colleagues in terms of intellectual sophistication.

Although the Propaganda Movement was an elite movement, it failed to reach out to the larger Filipino community and instead concentrated on the Spanish government and populace. Given the intense political polarization in the Spanish political landscape, the Spanish government showed little interest in the circumstances in the Philippines, and as a result, the movement received little backing and made little progress there. In the Philippines, the propagandists themselves were viewed as rebels, and many were sent into exile. Despite its ultimate failure, the movement helped shape the political consciousness that fueled the 1896 nationalist revolt and the subsequent struggle for independence.

14. How did the failure of the Propaganda Movement lead to the formation of Katipunan?​


The failure of the peaceful Propaganda Movement led to the formation of the Katipunan, a revolutionary society that aimed to liberate the Philippines from Spanish colonial rule through armed struggle. The leaders of the Propaganda Movement realized that peaceful means were not enough to achieve their goals, and the Katipunan became their alternative solution.

19. a Punan ang tsart tungkol sa Kilusang Propaganda at Katipunan. Mga Nagtatag ng Kilusang Propaganda Pahayagan ng Propaganda Mga Nagtatag ng Katipunan Pahayagan ng Katipunan​


Answer:

Ang Kilusang Propaganda ay isang samahang itinatag ng mga liberal na Pilipino upang matamo ang pagbabago sa mapayapang pamamaraan. Samantala, ang Katipunan naman ay ay isang lihim na samahan naitinatag ni Bonifacio upang itaguyod ang kaisipang maka-bayan at rebolusyonaryo sa mga Pilipino.

Ang pangunahing adhikain ng Kilusang Propaganda ay isulong ang pantay na karapatan ng mga Pilipino at kastila sa pamamagitan ng paghiling ng asimilasyon sa pamahalaang Kastila. Kung susuriin, ang hinihiling lamang ng mga propagandista ay pagbabago at hindi ganap na pagsasarili ng bansa. Sa kabilang banda, ang Katipunan naman ay naglalayong sugpuin ang patuloy na pagmamalabis at di makataong pamumuno ng mga Espanyol sa Pilipinas tungo sa ganap na pagsasarili ng bansa.

Binubuo ang Kilusang Propaganda ng mga ilustrado o mga Pilipinong nakapag-aral sa ibang bansa at Maynila katulad ni Rizal, Jaena at Del Pilar. Lahat sila ay nagmula sa mayayamang pamilya sa Pilipinas. Samantala, ang Katipunan naman ay binubuo ng masa.

Ginamit ng Kilusang Propaganda ang kanilang pluma upang upang maipahayag ang kanilang mga kahilingan sa pamahalaang Kastila. Nariyang sumulat sila ng mga nobela, magasin, aklat, at iba pang babasahin. Samantala, ginamit naman ng Katipunan ang dahas at madugong rebolusyon upang makamit ang tunay na kalayaan.

30. How did the propaganda movement and the katipunan led to the start of nationalistic endeavors?


QUESTION

How did the propaganda movement and the katipunan led to the start of nationalistic endeavors?

ANSWER

The propaganda movement mulled after Rizal's capture and the collapse of the Liga Filipina. La Solidaridad went out of trade in November 1895, and in 1896 both del Pilar and Lopez Jaena passed on in Barcelona, worn down by destitution and dissatisfaction.
