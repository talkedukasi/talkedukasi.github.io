9. what is the social stratification system during spanish era?


The social stratification system during the Spanish colonial era in the Philippines was hierarchical and divided people into different groups based on their ancestry, race, and wealth. The system was led by the Peninsulares, followed by the Insulares, and then the Mestizos, who were further divided based on their ancestry. The system favored those with Spanish blood and placed them in positions of power and wealth.

11. What is the Social Stratification system in the Philippines during Pre-Spanish era?​


Answer:

Before the Spanish colonization of the Philippines, the society was organized into social classes or strata based on several factors such as wealth, occupation, and ancestry. At the top of the social hierarchy were the maginoo or nobles, who were wealthy and powerful, and held political and religious positions. The timawa or freemen were the next in rank, followed by the maharlika or warrior class, who were given land grants in exchange for their military service. The alipin or slaves were at the bottom of the social hierarchy, and were either debt slaves or war captives who were forced to work for their masters. Social mobility was limited and usually only occurred through marriage or the acquisition of wealth and power. This social stratification system persisted even after the arrival of the Spanish colonizers and was only gradually dismantled over time.

20. How does people from the Pre-historic Era, Ancient Egypt, Greek, Roman, and Gothic Era express their beliefs and art​


Answer:

othic art was a style of medieval art that developed in Northern France out of Romanesque art in the 12th century AD, led by the concurrent development of Gothic architecture. It spread to all of Western Europe, and much of Northern, Southern and Central Europe, never quite effacing more classical styles in Italy. In the late 14th century, the sophisticated court style of International Gothic developed, which continued to evolve until the late 15th century. In many areas, especially Germany, Late Gothic art continued well into the 16th century, before being subsumed into Renaissance art. Primary media in the Gothic period included sculpture, panel painting, stained glass, fresco and illuminated manuscripts. The easily recognizable shifts in architecture from Romanesque to Gothic, and Gothic to Renaissance styles, are typically used to define the periods in art in all media, although in many ways figurative art developed at a different pace.

22. what is the justice system during the old English era vs today?


Anglo-Saxon law (Old English ǣ, later lagu "law"; dōm "decree, judgment") is a body of written rules and customs that were in place during the Anglo-Saxon period in England, before the Norman conquest. This body of law, along with early Scandinavian law and Germanic law, descended from a family of ancient Germanic custom and legal thought. However, Anglo-Saxon law codes are distinct from other early Germanic legal statements – known as the leges barbarorum, in part because they were written in Old English instead of in Latin. The laws of the Anglo-Saxons were the second in medieval Western Europe after those of the Irish to be expressed in a language other than Latin.
