PROBLEMS OF BARANGAY LUOP

Numerous problems barangay were brought up throughout the event, however, after carefully examining them, the top 5 difficulties were identified:

1.  An inefficient system for delivering healthcare

The barangay's health care delivery system's accessibility is at the top of the list. Understanding what "inaccessibility" means in this situation is crucial because it could lead to misunderstandings. The fact that Barangay Luop is one of the select few in the Municipality to have a Health Center is important information about the barangay that could help us dispel their perception of "inaccessibility". The midwife overseeing Barangay Luop, however, also has the same duties with other barangays. Therefore, the lack of a health facility is not the main issue facing the population of Barangay Luop; rather, it is the lack of a permanent medical staff who could take care of their medical needs as they arise.

2. Lack of alternate means of support

The requirement for a different means of subsistence is another issue that the Brgy people constantly worry about. Farming is the main source of income in the barrio, and farmers are typically men. As a result, women are typically left at home to care for family requirements like doing laundry. The majority of the time, however, housekeeping is finished early, giving the women—usually mothers—more leisure time. They want to use their spare time to be productive and assist their spouses in providing for the family.

3. Sanitation

Global sanitization is a problem. In fact, the World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that 2.6 billion people worldwide lack access to proper sanitation. If the current pattern holds, 2.7 billion people won't have access to even the most basic sanitation by 2015. Brgy Luop is not an exception to this widespread problem. The lack of implementation of the Solid Solid Waste Management Act (RA 9003) as required by law and inadequate knowledge of waste segregation and disposal are underlying problems that make this challenge even more difficult.

4. High Incidence of Malnutrition

Malnutrition is a significant health issue, particularly in developing nations, according to WHO (including the Philippines). In its simplest form, malnutrition is "poor nourishment." It concerns the body's response to a variety of infections that cause nutrient malabsorption or the inability to utilise nutrients efficiently to sustain health, as well as not enough, too much, and the wrong kinds of diet. Malnutrition is a problem in Brgy Luop for a number of reasons, but the main one cited by the locals is poverty. People are unable to consume the proper types of food because of poverty. Lack of money equates to a lack of food, and a lack of food can frequently result in malnutrition, especially in young children.

5. High Incidence of Hypertension

The rising number of citizens with hypertension diagnoses is the last but certainly not the least issue that the locals observe. The situation is made worse by the lack of proper understanding regarding how to control and treat hypertension. Therefore, the residents of the town decided to address this issue. These were the issues that the locals in the area identified.
