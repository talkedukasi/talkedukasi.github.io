Answer:

Sooner or later, affirmative action will die a natural death. Its achievements have been stupendous, but if we look at the premises that underlie it, we find assumptions and priorities that look increasingly shopworn. Thirty years ago, affirmative action was invented on the basis of these five appropriate premises:

1. Adult, white males make up something called the U.S. business mainstream.

2. The U.S. economic edifice is a solid, unchanging institution with more than enough space for everyone.

3. Women, blacks, immigrants, and other minorities should be allowed in as a matter of public policy and common decency.

4. Widespread racial, ethnic, and sexual prejudice keeps them out.

5. Legal and social coercion are necessary to bring about the change.

Today all five of these premises need revising. Over the past six years, I have tried to help some 15 companies learn how to achieve and manage diversity, and I have seen that the realities facing us are no longer the realities affirmative action was designed to fix.

To begin with, more than half the U.S. work force now consists of minorities, immigrants, and women, so white, native-born males, though undoubtedly still dominant, are themselves a statistical minority. In addition, white males will make up only 15% of the increase in the work force over the next ten years. The so-called mainstream is now almost as diverse as the society at large.

Answer:

1. How are women's lives portrayed in the work?

In "Dead Stars," women's lives are portrayed primarily in domestic roles and as objects of men's affections. The story depicts women as taking care of children and managing household tasks, while men are depicted in more professional roles.

2. Is the form and content of the work influenced by the writer's gender?

This is not specified in the critique but one can approach it as that as a woman writer Paz Marquez Benitez could have chosen to represent the women characters differently.

3. How do male and female characters relate to one another? Are these relationships sources of conflict? Are these conflicts resolved?

In "Dead Stars," the male characters often relate to the female characters in a patriarchal and possessive manner. The central relationship in the story, between Alfredo and Julia, is a source of conflict and ultimately unresolved. Alfredo's continuing affection for Julia creates distance in his marriage and prevents him from fully committing to his wife.

4. Does the work challenge or affirm traditional views of women?

The critique suggests that "Dead Stars" reinforces traditional views of women as being relegated to domestic roles and as objects of men's affections.

5. How do the images of women in the story reflect patriarchal social forces that have impeded women's efforts to achieve full equality with men?

The images of women in the story, who are primarily depicted in domestic roles and as objects of men's affections, reflect patriarchal social forces that have limited women's opportunities and autonomy.

6. What marital expectations are imposed on the characters? What effect do these expectations have?

The characters are expected to conform to traditional gender roles within their marriage, with men as the head of the household and women as subservient. These expectations create distance and dissatisfaction in the marriage between Alfredo and Esperanza.

7. What behavioral expectations are imposed on the characters? What effect do these expectations have?

Characters are expected to conform to traditional gender norms in terms of their behavior and emotions. Alfredo's emotions and behavior are constrained by societal expectations that men should not show vulnerability or be emotionally attached to women other than their wives.

8. If a female character were male, how would the story be different (and vice versa)?

If Julia were a male character, the story would likely focus on a different kind of power imbalance, as a man in a relationship with a woman would not be constrained by the same societal expectations. It's possible that the story would have a different outcome as well, with the relationship between Julia and Esperanza being more of a rivalry.
