THROWING EVENT IN ATHLETICS

The four throwing events that were held today are listed below. Shot put, discus, hammer, and javelin are the four most common throwing events, and they all entail throwing tools of varied weights and forms at a distance.   Benefit of throwing heavy objects is that they make the movement in the circle feel more natural.

Let's discuss the throwing events

Discus

The goal of discus is to throw a circular biconvex disc as far as it can from a circular throwing area that has been set up. During an able-bodied throw, the athlete faces away from the throwing field, spins 1.5 times, then sidearms the discus, allowing it to spin off the fingers before releasing it.

Javelin

The goal of a javelin is to hurl a spear-like javelin as far as it can from a target area. Before launching the javelin, able-bodied participants will run up a runway that goes up to the throwing area. The javelin must be hurled over the shoulder or upper part of the throwing arm and gripped by a single hand at the grip point at the center of the javelin. The tip of the javelin must be the first part of the javelin to land on the ground within the defined landing area for a throw to be counted.

Shot put

The goal of shot put is to throw a metal shot in a spherical shape as far as it can from a circular throwing area. During the throw, the shot is held in close proximity to the neck/chin region and cannot be lowered below this level. Similar to discus, an able-bodied throw, or put, begins with the competitor facing away from the throwing field. At this point, the athlete can use one of two force-generating techniques: "spin" or "glide." The "spin" approach is similar to the discus technique in that it involves the athlete spinning 1.5 times before releasing the shot. Before firing the shot, the "glide" consists of a strong spin from the rear-facing stance, pushing off the dominant leg. The put is pushed with one hand, with the point of release being above the shoulder level, with the point of release being above the shoulder level. The shot must land within a designated landing zone for a putt to be counted.
