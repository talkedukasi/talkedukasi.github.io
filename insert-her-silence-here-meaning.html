2. SYNOPSIS OF INSERT HER SILENCE HERE BY SHAKIRA ANDREA SISON.


Answer:

.

Explanation:Inside my mother was the sea. I used to lie on her belly as a child and press my ear against her soft, warm, and Chinese-white skin. When asked, I would say that I was listening to the baby, although I was the youngest of four and I knew there wouldn’t be any more. I was the last.

Mama would watch TV or read a book, and I would close my eyes to what I thought was the sound of water in her gut, trying to make out its tiny grumbles, imagining the passage of rice grains after the evening’s meal. More often than not I would end up falling asleep to the sound of its waves as her stomach churned and intestines moved, creating the borborygmi that became my lullaby.

She was a woman of few words. Like my father, she grew up during the war, and being a child of two survivors, one simply learned to let stories that are untold remain so. But unlike my father, she didn’t overcompensate with stories of Northern games and ghosts, beached whales, pretty classmates, and the right shoes to wear to reflect their undergarments. While my father told tales of his past, my mother said nothing. It was as if she carved out the space that created a venue for his words.

THE MEANING OF SOUND OF SILENCE IS PARADOX. Figurative Language These are languages portrays words that does not directly show literal meaning. It is used to make words more colorful and intriguing. Many writers use figurative languages to awe a reader or to create a colorful atmosphere while reading their texts. Paradox is a figurative language that tells the opposite of what it says. It is something that makes you question more like, “fighting is loving”. These two words do not agree with each other but portrays an agreeable meaning. A sentence may say, “April is fighting and loving her daughter Snow.” This may be interpreted that April as a mother always fight against her daughter's wrong doings because she loves her and she does not want her to go the wrong the direction. THE SOUND OF SILENCE  An example of paradox that is commonly heard by many. You may think why a silent  room may have a sound in your ears.  Or why the silence of the forest is heard by everyone. Let us say, James loves Kara very much in silence. He may not speak it out to Kara maybe because of several reasons but he simply shows his love through taking care of her through bringing free lunch or offering a ride home. Kara may not verbally listen to his desires for he is in silence but James’ sound of silence is surely heard by her heart.

The paradox SOUND OF SILENCE is a way of telling someone that sound are not just produce verbally by our mouth but is heard or felt by the heart through our actions. We always agree on the quote, “Actions speak louder than words.”
