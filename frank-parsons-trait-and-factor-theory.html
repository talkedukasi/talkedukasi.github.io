4. 1. Which of these factors do not enhance listening skills?a) Attentionb) Clear perceptionc) Fakenessd) Frankness​


A person's listening skills are developed based on a number of factors, but from the given choices, we can definitely say that fakeness is not one of those.

Here, we are faced with a question that seeks to weed out a factor that does not contribute to the enhancement of one's ability to listen to another effectively. Let us tackle each choice to get a better understanding of them:

(a) Attention... is an apparent skill-enhancing factor and is probably the most important one. To be able to actually listen, you must pay attention!

(b) Clear perception... or a good understanding of whatever you are listening to is also essential. You will know how to respond properly if you have a clear perception.

(d) Frankness... can, in a way, indeed improve your listening skills. By being honest with your responses, you are letting the other party know that you have heard them well and you would like to react genuinely.

(c) Fakeness... does NOT enhance your listening skills. How can you show your sincerity in listening if you cannot state the facts?

17. TALCOTT PARSONS your own understanding ​


Answer:

Talcott Parsons was an American sociologist of the classical tradition, best known for his social action theory and structural functionalism. Parsons is considered one of the most influential figures in sociology in the 20th century.

–The majority of Talcott Parsons' writings were centered on the Structural Functionalism thesis, which holds that social roles that people choose to embrace are formed by how those positions assist the society as a whole.

–Parsons spent the majority of his professional career at Harvard University, where he was associated with the many iterations of the sociology department for 32 years. He made contributions to the field of sociological theory, primarily through the formulation of a "general theory of action."

20. what is are the contributions of talcott parson in the structural functionalism?


Answer:

Talcott Parsons viewed society as a system. He argued that any social system has four basic functional prerequisites: adaptation, goal attainment, integration and pattern maintenance. These can be seen as problems that society must solve if it is to survive.
