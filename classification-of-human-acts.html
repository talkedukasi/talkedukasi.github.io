3. Discuss the two Classifications of human act.?​


Answer:

Elicited Acts - simply will-acts - acts begun and completed in the will. An example of this is INTENTION.  An elicited act is an act that you were "persuaded" to do. The person has a choice whether they want to do it or not.

A commanded act is an act that someone is "ordered" to do. The person doesn't have a choice, they must do it or face consequences. Commanded acts - actions that are carried out by the mind and body that are ordered by the will.
