9. Provide situational examples on The Historical Foundation of Education in The Philippines On New Society And The 5th Republic Era


Answer:

The historical foundation of education in the Philippines during the New Society and the 5th Republic era (1972-1986) was marked by significant changes in the educational system. Here are some situational examples:

The implementation of the New Society Education Program: In 1973, President Ferdinand Marcos introduced the New Society Education Program (NSEP), which aimed to provide a more relevant and responsive education system that is geared towards national development. The NSEP emphasized the integration of vocational and technical skills in the curriculum to address the needs of the workforce.

The establishment of the Ministry of Education, Culture and Sports (MECS): In 1978, the Ministry of Education, Culture and Sports was established to oversee the implementation of education policies and programs. This move centralized the management of education in the country and provided a more streamlined approach to education.

The introduction of the Integrated Reorganization Plan: In 1972, the Integrated Reorganization Plan was introduced to restructure the government bureaucracy. Under this plan, the Department of Education, Culture, and Sports was created, and it was tasked to manage the educational system in the country.

The adoption of the 1973 Constitution: The 1973 Constitution provided for free and compulsory elementary education for all Filipinos. This constitutional provision paved the way for the government to prioritize the education sector and provide access to basic education for all.

The implementation of the Bilingual Education Policy: In 1974, the Bilingual Education Policy was introduced to promote the use of Filipino as the medium of instruction in schools. This policy aimed to foster a sense of national identity and pride among Filipinos by promoting the use of the national language

Art history is the study of aesthetic objects and visual expression in historical and stylistic context.[1] Traditionally, the discipline of art history emphasized painting, drawing, sculpture, architecture, ceramics and decorative arts, yet today, art history examines broader aspects of visual culture, including the various visual and conceptual outcomes related to an ever-evolving definition of art.[2][3] Art history encompasses the study of objects created by different cultures around the world and throughout history that convey meaning, importance or serve usefulness primarily through visual representations. As a discipline, art history is distinguished from art criticism, which is concerned with establishing a relative artistic value upon individual works with respect to others of comparable style or sanctioning an entire style or movement; and art theory or "philosophy of art", which is concerned with the fundamental nature of art. One branch of this area of study is aesthetics, which includes investigating the enigma of the sublime and determining the essence of beauty. It is, however, questionable whether many questions of this kind can be answered satisfactorily without also considering basic questions about the nature of art. The current disciplinary gap between art history and the philosophy of art (aesthetics) often hinders this inquiry.[4]

27. Historical event curriculum development in the philippines


The historical event curriculum development in the Philippines :

Education before the coming of Spaniards.Education during the Spanish Occupation.Education during the American Occupation.Education during the Japanese Occupation.Education of the Martial Law.Education under the Philippine Republic.

Further Explanation :

Curriculum development refers to the process of selecting, organizing, executing about the learning experiences based on the needs, abilities of the learners. Based on the researches of the historical foundation of the Philippine curriculum, the development of the curriculum in Philippines history is based on the five motives which are religion, political, utilitarian, mass education and excellence in education.

The historical events of curriculum development in the Philippines :

Education before the coming of Spaniards : This period is also known as the pre Hispanic period, in the pre Hispanic period there were no formal schools. The education given was done by oral practical and hands-on, in this period the education was no formal parents mostly taught their children how to live and hunt for food, the sons are taught how to hunt, and the daughters were taught how to do household chores.Education during the Spanish Occupation : In the Spanish era the schools were managed by religious organizations as the main objective is to spread Christianity through teaching and education, the curriculum that was created was mostly religion based which were reading, writing, and religion. The subjects were; Languages (Latin, Spanish grammar and literature), History (Universal, Spanish), Mathematics, Philosophy, and Geography.Education during the American Occupation : During the American era, English became the main language in school, the soldiers became the first teachers and taught in school but later on the government hired proper teachers to teach and the American mostly focus on political education. The subjects were mathematics, language, science, history and government, social sciences and Physical Education.Education during the Japanese Occupation : In the Japanese era there were principles of the Japanese education which were; Diffusion of the Japanese language in the Philippines community, the promotion of vocational course, inspiration for people to love their neighbour, and foster a new Filipino culture based.Education of the Martial Law : The education system use of language were changed in school requiring the Filipino and English language to be used in schools. Science, math, and literature classes were taught in English while the rest were taught in Filipino.Education under the Philippine Republic : The curriculum that is established by the Philippine republic is K - 12 reform, which means 1 year of kindergarten education, six years of elementary education and six of secondary education, four years of junior high school and two years of senior high school.
