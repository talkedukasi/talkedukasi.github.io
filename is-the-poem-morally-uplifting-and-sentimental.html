Answer:

Our thoughts and impulses, according to religious sentimentalists, play a major role in the anatomy of morality. Some people believe that moral thoughts are inherently sentimental, while others believe that moral facts must make reference to our sentimental reactions, and still others believe that feelings are the primary source of moral awareness.

26. How did the movie with the same title show the poem’s sentiment? (Invictus Poem)


Invictus, the Morgan Freeman and Matt Damon-led film, strikes a chord with William Ernest Henley's poem in terms of the resilience portrayed by the central characters.

Nelson Mandela refused to bend his beliefs and fought for a unified South Africa. Francois Pienaar was drawn to the influential president's belief and channelled his faith through rugby.

Despite the obvious racial tension enveloping the entire nation, the two leaders didn't buckle. South Africa's two dominant races built a slow but sure friendship as the Springboks won the Rugby World Cup.

The Springboks' fairy-tale run to the top repaired a painful wound in South Africa's rough history. It validated the convictions of Mandela and Pienaar. It resonates all too well with William Ernest Henley's poem about personal strength and one's desire to own his destiny.
