Stan pays Mathabane to compete in contests and talks with his University of Southern California coach about Mathabane to attend college in the United States. 16. Excerpt from kaffir boy theme?


Answer:

Themes :

racismclassismperseveranceoppressionindividual choice

Explanation:

These are the major themes in the excerpt from kaffir boy or the autobiography of Mark Mathabane:

Racism - this is the most relevant theme of this autobiography since the author is an African and during his time, Africans are not treated well. They are not given the same rights as the white people. He wanted to emphasize the need to quit racism. Classism - the usual struggle of the society when there is always a high wall that separates the high and low class family.Oppression - when there is racism and classism, oppression is always present. In the story of the Kaffir boy, he was oppressed by the people who are against him and his family. Perseverance - the story showed the readers that life is hard and difficult but it is important to not give up. Life struggles must not defeat you but rather fight and win the battle. Individual choice - the life that you'll have always depend upon the choices you make. In the story of Kaffir boy, he chose to be strong and fight for his future even when it's a very hard thing to do. He made the choice, the right choice.
