2. Interpret the fable of the winged ants in search of light


Answer:

The fable of the winged ants in search of light discusses to things:

1. Individuality

2. Goodness

Explanation:

The fable is about the two types of ants: winged and wingless ants. These ants are inside an air-conditioned room. The ants felt cold, so they thought that the light on the ceiling could help give them warmth. However, the wingless ant cannot fly to the light. The winged ant belittled the wingless ant because it cannot reach the light.

The story ended when the ants saw the reflection of the light in the water. The ants dived into the water because they thought there is also a light there. The winged ants died.

The fable shows that we are all unique and equal individuals. Some people may have traits that others do not have. Our individuality is not meant for us to feel superior just because we have a certain trait. It is not right for us to discriminate against others.

The fable also discusses that when we do harmful things to others, it will come back to us. Sow seeds of goodness and kindness and we will also reap goodness and kindness. If we sow bad seeds, we will also reap bad seeds.
