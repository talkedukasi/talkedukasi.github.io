Answer:

1. When someone thinks of revenge, various areas of the brain are activated. The amygdala, which is responsible for processing emotions such as fear and anger, is particularly active during thoughts of revenge. The prefrontal cortex, which is involved in decision-making and impulse control, may also become more active as the person considers the potential consequences of seeking revenge

2. The opposite effect of revenge is forgiveness. When a person forgives someone who has wronged them, it can lead to a number of positive effects on the brain and body. Research has shown that forgiveness can reduce stress, lower blood pressure, and improve mental health. Forgiveness can also increase feelings of empathy and compassion towards others, and can promote healthier relationships

3. , Bacon argues against the idea of revenge and suggests that it is a primitive and barbaric response to harm.

Bacon asserts that revenge is often fueled by emotions such as anger and hatred, which can cloud a person's judgment and lead them to commit acts of violence or cruelty. He also notes that seeking revenge can lead to a never-ending cycle of retaliation, as each act of revenge may prompt another act in response.

4. Frank Sinatra was once quoted as saying, "The best revenge is massive success." This quote suggests that instead of seeking revenge, one should focus on achieving personal success and accomplishing their goals.

While this quote may be interpreted in different ways, I personally believe that it contains some truth. Seeking revenge can be a destructive and counterproductive response to harm, as it often leads to more negative emotions and can perpetuate a cycle of harm and retaliation. Focusing on personal success, on the other hand, can provide a sense of purpose and fulfillment, and can help individuals move forward from past hurts

5. Vanessa Van Edwards explains that revenge is a natural human emotion and discusses the reasons why people seek revenge. She points out that revenge can provide a sense of justice or closure, but it can also have negative consequences such as perpetuating a cycle of revenge and harming one's own mental health.

Van Edwards also discusses the concept of forgiveness as an alternative to revenge and highlights the benefits of forgiveness for both the victim and the perpetrator.
