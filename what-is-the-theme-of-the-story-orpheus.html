11. what is the theme of orpheus?


Orpheus - the greatest musician of the ancient world - loses his wife Eurydice when she steps on a snake. 
Orpheus finds his way down to Hell where his music charms Hades and Persephone (the king and queen of the dead) so that they agree that Eurydice can follow Orpheus as he returns to the land of the living. But Orpheus must not look back on Eurydice while she follows him back into the light. 
Just before they arrive at the exit from the underworld Orpheus forgets and looks back at Eurydice, and loses her forever. 

The myth has many lessons. Love persists beyond death (Orpheus tries to rescue Eurydice from the underworld). Music can give the illusion of happiness (Orpheus ransoms Eurydice with his songs) but not the reality (the ransom fails). Life is short and you should take your pleasures while you can, the day will come when it is too late

18. what is the resolution of the story orpheus?


Answer:

Resolutions of the Story of Orpheus

The Story of Orpheus has different versions of the resolution. In one of the known versions, it tells about how Orpheus failed to bring Eurydice back to life because he was not able to follow the only condition of not looking back upon walking towards the world of the living.

Due of his overwhelming excitement, he looked back to Eurydice, which caused Eurydice to disappear. Her disappearance left Orpheus in so much pain and misery.

In another version, it said that he was killed and torn into pieces by the order of Dionysus because he allegedly preferred worshiping the rival god, Apollo. There was a different version however, where a happy ending was the resolution of the story. It was said that he was brought back to life by a mysterious religion after being inspired by his music.
