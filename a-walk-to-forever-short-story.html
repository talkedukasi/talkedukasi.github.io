1. What is the tone and mood of the story "Long walk to forever?" why you did so?


The tone of the story "Long walk to forever" by Kurt Vonnegut is nostalgic, romantic, and introspective. The mood of the story is bittersweet, gentle, and reflective.

The author uses a tender and romantic tone to convey the feelings of the characters, Newt and Catherine, as they reconnect after a long separation. The nostalgia in the tone is reflected in the characters' reflections on their past and the memories they share. The introspective tone is evident in the characters' thoughts and internal monologues about their feelings and relationship.

The mood of the story is bittersweet, as the characters are happy to reconnect but also face the reality that they will soon part ways. The gentle and reflective mood is evoked by the serene setting and the lyrical descriptions of nature. Overall, the tone and mood contribute to the story's themes of love, memory, and the passage of time.

20. In your own idea Is history An agreed upon set of facts or a forever-fixed story that is never subjected to changes and updates?​


Answer:

History is not an agreed upon set of facts nor is it a forever-fixed story that is never subjected to changes and updates. Instead, history is a dynamic field that constantly evolves as new evidence is discovered and new interpretations are made. Historians use evidence, such as primary sources like diaries and letters, as well as secondary sources like scholarly articles and books, to construct narratives and make arguments about what happened in the past. However, these interpretations are often open to debate and can be revised as new information is uncovered. History is a process of inquiry, investigation, and interpretation, and as such, it is constantly evolving and changing over time.
