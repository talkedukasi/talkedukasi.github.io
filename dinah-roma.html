1. The Lucky Plaza Poem by Dinah Roma


Answer:1. Dinah Roma’s “The Lucky Plaza” dramatizes a common scene in the famous Lucky Plaza of Singapore where migrant Filipino workers usually congregate on weekends. Characterize the speaker of the poem and his/her attitude towards the place and even the people in Lucky Plaza. Cite details from the poem to support your answer.

The speaker seems to know the stories of struggles, loneliness even the happiness and success of the migrant workers who always go on the Lucky Plaza. The speaker saw the Plaza as like a house that are open to everyone to come stay and visit. From the lines “To reach for her from my table’s end; to let her know she could leave the bowl, its cool indifference behind…”

2. Describe the way the poem depicts a migrant worker in Singapore, the subject of the poem. Cite details to support your answer.

The migrant workers are patient in all aspects in life, the way how they feel and how they work. They have also a big heart that can handle every situation for the sake of their loved ones, whether it is good or bad. From the phrase “That light unwilling to surrender…”

3. What could be the distinct comparative differences between the speaker of the poem and the migrant worker?

The distinct comparative of the speaker is that the speaker is just wondering around the Plaza and has some thoughts about the people there the way she saw and heard it, while the migrant workers are the reality of everything she says to her poem.

4. The word “hand” in the lines, “and touch her hand trained/in the acquiescence of service” functions as a metonym? What does it signify about the subject of persona’s reflection?

The word “hand” signifies as the heart of the migrant workers, of how he manages all the circumstances in life of being a migrant worker.

5. The lines “Wanting her to end/the telling of stories whose origins I know so much// and wouldn’t want to recall”, and “She to the house polished to her children’s/ absence, strangers in the company of kin” function as dramatizing contradictions. Explain.

The speaker wanted not to hear all those loneliness of the migrant workers, she knows it already and it hurts her when she heard something like that.

6. The persona was able to establish some similarities and differences between her and the migrant worker. By citing relevant parts of the poem, explain the persona’s rumination on this.

It is all about the whole poem that the persona’s rumination why she knew everything about migrant workers is that maybe once in her life she is one of them, who found herself going to the Plaza to, where migrant workers find it is the best place to ease their tired body and soul, ease the loneliness and meet some friends to talk to, and by her luck she escaped herself of being a migrant worker that she saw on the Plaza when she wrote the poem.

7. Why are the following lines showing an instance of irony: “singing of beauty cage her pain / within places of the imaginary”?

They seem so happy but deep inside their heart is crying because of loneliness.

8. What is the general perspective of the speaker on migrant domestic work?

The general perspective of the speaker towards the migrant domestic work is that it is the most hard work in every kind of work because being use to do a migrant domestic work, one person must be physically, emotionally, spiritually, mentally fit to work whatever the circumstances you will face.

What is the theme of the poem Lucky Plaza by Dinah Roma?

The theme of the poem The Lucky Plaza by Dinah Roma is the somewhat sad and lonely situation of many Overseas Filipino Workers. The Lucky Plaza is the name of a shopping center in Singapore where Filipinos meet on weekends. Her time with them opened her eyes to the reality behind nearly everyone working there.
