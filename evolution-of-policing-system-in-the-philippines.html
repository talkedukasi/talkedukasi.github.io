12. in your own words discussed the evolution of police service in the Philippine setting?​


Answer:

The Philippine National Police traces its roots back to the late 19th century when the Philippine Constabulary was established. The Philippine Constabulary was a gendarmerie-type police force that was tasked with maintaining law and order in the country. In the early 20th century, the Constabulary was absorbed into the newly-established Philippine Army.

After the Philippines gained independence from the United States in 1946, the Philippine Constabulary was re-established as a separate and civilian entity. In the 1950s, the Constabulary was once again merged with the Philippine Army.

The Philippines became a republic in 1973 and the Philippine Constabulary was again made into a separate and independent agency, now known as the Integrated National Police. The Integrated National Police was absorbed into the Philippine National Police in 1991.

The Philippine National Police is the country's primary law enforcement agency. It is tasked with maintaining peace and order, as well as investigating and preventing crimes. The National Police has a wide range of duties, from responding to emergency calls to investigating complex crimes.
