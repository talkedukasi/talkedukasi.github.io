21. Make your OWN word problem involving functions/piecewise functions or rational functions.​


Step-by-step explanation:

Here is a word problem involving functions:

Bob is a delivery driver and he charges different prices for different distances. For distances up to 10 miles, he charges $3 per mile. For distances greater than 10 miles but less than or equal to 20 miles, he charges $2.50 per mile. For distances greater than 20 miles, he charges $2 per mile.

Bob was asked to deliver a package that was 15 miles away. How much will Bob charge for this delivery?

To solve this problem, we can define three piecewise functions: one for distances up to 10 miles, one for distances greater than 10 miles but less than or equal to 20 miles, and one for distances greater than 20 miles.

For distances up to 10 miles, the function is:

f(x) = 3x, where x is the distance in miles

For distances greater than 10 miles but less than or equal to 20 miles, the function is:

f(x) = 2.5x, where x is the distance in miles

For distances greater than 20 miles, the function is:

f(x) = 2x, where x is the distance in miles

Since the distance of the delivery is 15 miles, we can use the second piecewise function to calculate the cost:

f(15) = 2.5 * 15 = $37.50

Therefore, Bob will charge $37.50 for the delivery.
