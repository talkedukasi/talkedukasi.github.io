ARTS AND CRAFTS OF MIMAROPA AND VISAYAS REGION

ARTS AND CRAFTS IN MIMAROPA

MORION - Dapdap, dita, and santol trees are plentiful in Marinduque, and their wood is typically used. Each mask is carved over a period of months to make Morion.

TINGKOP - Tingkop is a blackened and natural bamboo harvest basket in the shape of a cone. It is a demonstration of their weaving skills. The design stands out because of the incredibly slight change in the under-over pattern of the bamboo strips.

AMBAHAN - Ambahan is a seven-syllable meter that is rhythmic and lyrical. Without a specific musical pitch or accompaniment, this is given through recitation and chanting. Its goal is for the person reading the poem to represent a scenario or a set of traits in a symbolic fashion.

ARTS AND CRAFTS IN VISAYAS REGION

KIMONA - Kimona may also be utilized as a shade to keep out the sun's rays or the rain's wrath. COTTON and ABACA are used to make it.

SINAMAY - Sinamay's industry is world-class. It is prepared from abaca and TWINE and banana-like plants that are native to the area.

BANIG - Banig is a woven product that includes mats, wall hangings, and Samar's famed colorful sleeping mat.
