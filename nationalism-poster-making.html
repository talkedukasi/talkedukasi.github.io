the National Museum of the Philippines is a complex of museums and galleries that showcase the country's rich cultural and natural heritage. It is composed of several branches, including the National Museum of Fine Arts, National Museum of Anthropology, National Museum of Natural History, and National Planetarium. Each branch offers a unique set of exhibits and collections, ranging from paintings, sculptures, and artifacts to botanical and zoological specimens, geological and archaeological finds, and astronomy-related displays. The National Museum of the Philippines also conducts various educational and research programs to promote awareness, appreciation, and preservation of Philippine culture and history. It is open to the public and offers free admission to all visitors.
