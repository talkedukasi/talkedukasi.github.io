16. Components of Research Process In writing a research, it is very important that a researcher knows where to start and to end. Below is a schematic diagram showing the components/parts of research process. This schematic diagram will serve as your guide as you go along writing your research. The Schematic Diagram of the Research Process Problem/Objective Theoretical/ Conceptual Framework Assumptions Hypotheses Review of Related Literature Research Design Data Collection Data Processing and Statistical Treatment Analysis and Interpretation of Data Summary, Conclusion and Recommendations​


Answer:

1. Problem/Objective: This component identifies the research problem and sets the overall goal of the research.

2. Theoretical/Conceptual Framework: This component provides a theoretical or conceptual basis for the research. This framework explains the important concepts and theories that will be used in the research.

3. Assumptions: This component identifies the assumptions that will be used in the research.

4. Hypotheses: This component presents the expected outcomes of the research. Hypotheses are educated guesses that will be tested during the research process.

5. Review of Related Literature: This component provides a review of previous research done in the area of interest. It presents the state of knowledge about the research problem and also identifies gaps in knowledge that the research will address.

6. Research Design: This component explains the methodology that will be used to conduct the research. It outlines the plan for data collection and analysis.

7. Data Collection: This component involves the collection of data based on the research design.

8. Data Processing and Statistical Treatment: This component involves the processing of raw data obtained from data collection. Data are organized, classified, coded, and analyzed using statistical tools.

9. Analysis and Interpretation of Data: This component involves the interpretation of statistical results obtained from data analysis. It presents the findings of the research in a clear and concise manner.

10. Summary, Conclusion, and Recommendations: This component summarizes the findings of the research and answers the research questions. Conclusions are drawn based on the findings, and recommendations for future research are provided.
