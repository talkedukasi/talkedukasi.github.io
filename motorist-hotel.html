[tex]\tt{total \: time \: elapsed} \\ = t_{1} + t_{2} \\ = \frac{ s_{1} }{ v_{1} } +\frac{ s_{2} }{ v_{2} } [/tex]

[tex]\tt{total \: time \: elapsed} \\ \frac{160km}{80 \frac{km}{h} } + \frac{160km}{100 \frac{km}{h} } =3.6h. [/tex]

14. why are motorist warned to slow when it is raining?​


During rainy days, the road becomes wet and causes substances like a layer of tar, rubber, and oil on the surface to mix with the water that causes the pavement to become unsafe. When the surface becomes slippery, it causes less friction to the vehicles, and motorcycles contain only two wheels alone, unlike other vehicles. Take note that the higher the number of wheels, the stronger the friction it can contain to the road. When the surface is wet, it creates a greasy layer and will lead the tires to have less connection to the road the reason why it is important to slow down when the roadways are soapy. If the friction decreases and the velocity of the motorcycle increases, the higher the possibility of an accident that probably will cause severe injury or worst case, death. That is why, it is vital to slow down when driving during rainy days, especially the motorist.
