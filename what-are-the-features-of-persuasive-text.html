Answer:

a statement of position at the beginning

a logical sequence

the argument is put forward in a series of points with back up evidence

a good argument shows cause and effect. This is the connection between an action and what leads to it, eg.The fish died as a result of pollution in the water: Violence in movies contributes to violence in society

a summing up or restating of position at the end

24. What are the 6 features of Persuasive text/essay. Enumerate them and give brief explanation of each feature. ​


Answer:

6 Features of Persuasive Text/Essay

Repeated Words - Repeated words is a literary device that involves intentionally using a word or phrase for effect, two or more times in a speech or written work.Alliterative Words - Alliterative words is an adjective used to describe things that use or are examples of alliteration, the repetition of the same or a similar sound at or near the beginning of each word in a series.Emotional Language - By emotional word, we refer to any word characterized by emotional connotations (e.g., “lonely,” “poverty,” “neglect,” “bless,” “reward,” “elegant”) or denoting a specific emotional reaction (e.g., “anger,” “happy,” “sadness“).Strong Argument - Strong argument is a non-deductive argument that succeeds in providing probable, but not conclusive, logical support for its conclusion. Rhetorical Questions - Rhetorical questions is a question asked in order to create a dramatic effect or to make a point rather than to get an answer.Humour - Humour or humor is the tendency of experiences to provoke laughter and provide amusement.
