29. 2. Give one situation for each where you have to show your personne and your moi.


Answer: Showing one's "personne" refers to presenting oneself as a social identity or a role that is expected in a given situation, while showing one's "moi" refers to expressing one's personal values, desires, and preferences. Here are some examples of situations where you might have to show your "personne" or your "moi":

Job interview: In a job interview, you may need to show your "personne" by presenting yourself as a competent and qualified candidate who is a good fit for the position and the company culture. At the same time, you may also need to show your "moi" by expressing your career goals, your values, and your work style, and how they align with the job requirements and the company's mission.

Family gathering: In a family gathering, you may need to show your "personne" by playing a role that is expected of you, such as a responsible adult, a supportive sibling, or a loving parent. At the same time, you may also need to show your "moi" by expressing your feelings, opinions, and experiences, and how they shape your relationships with your family members.

Social activism: In a social activism context, you may need to show your "personne" by representing a collective identity or a shared cause, such as gender equality, environmental justice, or racial equity. At the same time, you may also need to show your "moi" by speaking up for your own experiences and perspectives, and how they relate to the broader social issues you are advocating for.

Creative project: In a creative project, such as writing, painting, or composing, you may need to show your "personne" by following the conventions and standards of the genre or medium you are working in, and appealing to your audience's expectations and tastes. At the same time, you may also need to show your "moi" by expressing your own unique vision, voice, and style, and how they differentiate your work from others and reflect your personal creativity.
