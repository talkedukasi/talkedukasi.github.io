12. write the description of its origin, music, costume, and movement.Cordillera dancesMindanao dancesSpanish influence dancesRural Dances (need answer now please)​


Answer:

Cordillera Dances:

Origin: Cordillera Dances are traditional dances performed by the people of the Cordillera Region of the Philippines. These dances are deeply rooted in their culture and reflect their way of life, beliefs, and rituals.

Music: The music for Cordillera Dances is typically played on native instruments like bamboo flutes, gangsa (gongs), and drums. The musical rhythm and tempo vary depending on the type of dance being performed.

Costume: The dancers wear traditional Cordillera costumes which are colorful and vibrant. Men wear traditional loincloths while women wear tapis (wrap-around skirts) and blouses decorated with beads and embroidery. They also wear ornate headgear and accessories made of natural materials like feathers, shells, and seeds.

Movement: Cordillera Dances are characterized by slow, rhythmic movements that mimic the swaying of trees and the flowing of water. The dance movements are graceful and fluid, often accompanied by intricate hand and footwork.

Mindanao Dances:

Origin: Mindanao Dances are traditional dances performed on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. These dances reflect the diverse cultural influences in Mindanao, including Malay, Muslim, and indigenous traditions.

Music: The music for Mindanao Dances is often played on native instruments such as gongs, flutes, and drums. The musical style and tempo vary depending on the type of dance being performed.

Costume: The dancers wear colorful costumes that reflect the cultural heritage of Mindanao. Men typically wear traditional shirts and pants while women wear tapis (wrap-around skirts) and blouses decorated with embroidery and beadwork. They also wear ornate headgear and accessories made of natural materials like feathers, shells, and seeds.

Movement: Mindanao Dances are characterized by fast, energetic movements that showcase the dancers' agility and flexibility. The dance movements often incorporate intricate footwork and hand gestures, making the dances visually stunning and captivating.

Spanish Influence Dances:

Origin: Spanish Influence Dances are traditional dances that were influenced by Spanish colonialism in the Philippines. These dances reflect the blend of Spanish and Philippine cultures, which created a unique dance style.

Music: The music for Spanish Influence Dances is often played on Western instruments such as guitars, violins, and pianos. The musical style and tempo vary depending on the type of dance being performed.

Costume: The dancers wear traditional Spanish-influenced costumes, which are elegant and elaborate. Men typically wear suits or barongs, while women wear bell-shaped skirts and blouses adorned with lace, embroidery, and flowers.

Movement: Spanish Influence Dances are characterized by graceful movements and elegant poses. The dance movements often incorporate elements of footwork, turns, and hand gestures, making them visually stunning and romantic.

Rural Dances:

Origin: Rural Dances are traditional dances performed by the people in rural areas of the Philippines. These dances reflect their way of life, work, and celebration of their culture.

Music: The music for Rural Dances is often played on bamboo instruments such as flutes, percussions, and stringed instruments. The musical style and tempo vary, depending on the type of dance being performed.

Costume: The dancers wear simple yet colorful costumes that reflect their rural heritage. Men typically wear barongs, while women wear blouses and skirts made of cotton or silk. They also wear native hats and accessories, made of natural materials like feathers, leaves and seeds.

Movement: Rural Dances are characterized by lively movements and energetic footwork. The dance movements often imitate movements of daily life activities like planting, harvesting, and hunting, showing their connection to their environment. The dance is often colorful and merry, reflecting the happy and carefree nature of rural life.

25. what is the similarities of spanish influence dance and rural dance​


Answer:

Nearly 400 years of Spanish rule left an unremovable mark on the Philippines. Spain brought with them all aspects of their culture to the Islands. This includes the Catholic faith, clothing, and dance. The barong tagalog and the terno are Philippine interpretations of Spanish dress made to fit the humid climate of the Philippines. Aside from creating their own versions of European fashion, Philippine aristocrats created Filipino adaptations of European dance as well. These include jotas, fandanggos, mazurkas and waltzes that were danced by young socialites to the stringed music of the rondalla.
