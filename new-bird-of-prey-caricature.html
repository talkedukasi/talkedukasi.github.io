15. which of the following birds have strong sharp claws and brake for catching other animals?a. birds of preyb. flightless birdsc. perching birds d. water birds​


Answer:

a. birds of prey

Explanation:

Birds of prey, also known as raptors, are known for their sharp claws, called talons, which they use to catch and kill their prey. These birds typically have large, powerful feet and sharp talons that are well-suited for grasping and holding onto their prey. Some examples of birds of prey include hawks, eagles, falcons, and owls. In contrast, flightless birds, perching birds, and water birds do not typically have strong, sharp claws and do not use them for catching other animals.

As in humans, the point of sharpest vision is the fovea. Foveae are funnel-like areas of the retina packed with color-perceiving cone cells. Humans have one fovea per eye, while diurnal birds of prey have two.

Linear motion consists of 2: constant velocity motion with constant velocity and uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration

vₓ = 9.6 m/s

h = 14 m

The magnitude of the prey's velocity

constant velocity motion ⇒horizontal velocity vx = vox(initial horizontal velocity) = 9.6 m/s

vy² = 2gh
