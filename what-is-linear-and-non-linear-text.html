6. what is linear and non linear text?​


Answer:

The linear text refers to traditional texts which are those texts that use words to form sentences and paragraphs. This type of text needs to be read from the beginning up to the end to understand the message of the writer. Some examples of linear text are Novels, poems, shorts stories, letters, news articles, educational texts, etc... While Non-linear text uses words combined with symbols, photos, or graphs to convey and give information to the readers. In Non-linear text, the reader doesn't have to go through the text sequentially to make sense of the text. The reader decides where to start reading or what parts to read.

Examples of Non-linear text:

Timeline - presents relevant information and to be understood by the readers in chronological order. This helps the readers understand the order of certain events and how a particular event leads to another Flowchart - shows the sequence of events from start to finish to document a process. Graph - illustrates the relationship between two or more variables using points, bars, lines, or differentiated parts of a whole. Bar graph - uses bars to present a comparison of numerical information. Line graph - uses lines to present a trend or change observed over a while. Pie graph - uses a circle which is divided into slices to show how a whole is divided into different Table - summarizes and organizes information (maybe in words or numbers) using rows and column Diagram - illustrations of an object or a scene in which specific, individual parts are labeled Organizational Chart - represents an organization's structure, highlighting the different jobs, departments, and responsibilities Venn Diagram - highlights the similarities and differences of two objects being comparedMap - shows a scale representation of a whole or part of a specific area

9. Answer the following questions in 2-3 sentences. 1. What is the difference of linear texts from non-linear texts? 2. What is the reading path for linear and non-linear texts? 3. As a student, what will be your advantage if you are familiar with the use of non-linear texts?​


Answer:

1.)The main difference between linear and non-linear texts is the way in which the information is presented and organized. Linear texts are organized in a straightforward, chronological manner, with each piece of information building on the previous one. Non-linear texts, on the other hand, are organized in a more complex, interconnected manner, with information presented in multiple different formats (e.g. text, images, videos) and allowing the reader to explore the information in various different ways.

2.)The reading path for linear texts is typically a straightforward, chronological sequence from beginning to end. Non-linear texts, on the other hand, often allow the reader to choose their own reading path, jumping between different sections or formats of the text and exploring the information in a more flexible and interactive way.

3.)As a student, being familiar with the use of non-linear texts can be an advantage because it allows you to explore information in a more flexible and interactive way, and can help you to better understand and retain complex ideas and information. It can also help you to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, as you are required to make connections and draw conclusions based on the information you are presented with.

13. What is a linear and non-linear text


Answer:

Linear text refers to traditional text that needs to be read from beginning to the end while nonlinear text refers to text that does not need to be read from beginning to the end. As their names imply, linear texts are linear and sequential while non-linear and non-sequential. Thus, there is only one reading path in linear texts, which is determined by the author. However, nonlinear texts can have multiple reading paths depending on the readers.
