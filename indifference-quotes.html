Explanation:

The quote "Writing was a way of escaping my real life, a way to cope with cruelty and indifference" suggests that writing can be a powerful tool for individuals to escape their real-life problems and cope with the harsh realities of life. In this analysis, we will explore the central idea, context, impact, and message of this quote.

Central Idea:

The central idea of this quote is that writing can serve as a means of escapism and coping mechanism for individuals who are faced with cruelty and indifference in their real lives. The author suggests that writing allows them to escape the harsh realities of their life, even if only temporarily, and find a sense of solace and comfort in their writing.

Context:

The context of this quote is likely based on the personal experience of the author or speaker. It could be that they have faced cruelty and indifference in their life, and writing has served as a means of coping with these experiences. Alternatively, the quote could be more broadly applied to those who face similar challenges in their own lives.

Impact:

The impact of this quote is that it sheds light on the power of writing as a therapeutic tool for individuals. It highlights the ability of writing to provide a sense of escape from reality and offer a form of emotional release for those who may be struggling with difficult experiences. Additionally, the quote may inspire others to turn to writing as a means of coping with their own struggles and difficulties.

Message:

The message of this quote is that writing can be a powerful tool for individuals to cope with the harsh realities of life. It encourages individuals to use writing as a means of escape and emotional release, and to embrace the therapeutic benefits of writing. Additionally, the quote suggests that writing can serve as a form of self-expression and personal growth, allowing individuals to explore their own thoughts and emotions in a safe and supportive environment.

In conclusion, the quote "Writing was a way of escaping my real life, a way to cope with cruelty and indifference" highlights the powerful therapeutic benefits of writing as a means of coping with difficult experiences. It encourages individuals to embrace the power of writing to provide a sense of escape, emotional release, and personal growth.

The case of Reynaldo Carcillar, a Taft-based pedicab driver is another example of the bystander effect. The bystander effect is a psychological phenomenon, in which, people are most likely to ignore aid when there are a lot of people around. The greater the number of people around, the lesser the chances of someone stepping up to help. With a lot of bystanders around, people would likely think that someone from the crowd would come to help which is not likely the case, since, they all think the same.

One case of the bystander effect and the most popular one was about the case of Kitty Genovese.  Kitty Genovese was a New Yorker, from Queens to be exact. She was murdered in 1964, in which, she was stabbed by a male assailant. Her neighbors heard her cries for help and still chose to ignore her. Another case of the bystander effect happened in China, where a 2-year old girl was struck by a vehicle. Several bystanders passed by her and never even bothered to check on her. Sadly, several minutes later after she laid there on the road a much larger vehicle struck her again, she survived but died 8 days later.
