17. A ladder is 3m long. It leans in the window of a building. If the foot of the ladder is 2m from the foot of the building, what is the height of the window above the ground? ​


Answer:

Use the Pythagorean theorem.

c^2 = a^2 + b^2

c is the hypotenuse, in this case the length of the ladder.

Let a be the distance the ladder is placed from the wall.

Rearrange the equation to solve for b:

b = √(c^2 — a^2)

b = √(7^2 — 2^2)

b = √(49 — 4)

b = 6.71m
