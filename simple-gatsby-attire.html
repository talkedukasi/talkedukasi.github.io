3. Make simple descriptions about Thailand and Cambodia. You may include their attire, arts/crafts, cultures and traditions.​


Answer:

Thailand

The culture of Thailand has evolved greatly over time, from its relative isolation during the Sukhothai era, to its more contemporary Ayutthaya era, which absorbed influences from all over Asia. And in the reign of King Chulalongkorn in the late 19th century, the European trend of nationalism began to insert greater influence into Thai culture.  

Traditional Thai clothing is called chut thai  which literally means "Thai outfit". It can be worn by men, women, and children. Chut thai for women usually consists of a pha nung or a chong kraben, a blouse, and a sabai. Northern and northeastern women may wear a sinh instead of a pha nung and a chong kraben with either a blouse or a suea pat. Chut thai for men includes a chong kraben or pants, a Raj pattern shirt, with optional knee-length white socks, and a sabai.

Traditional principles concerning pregnancy and childbirth are largely influenced by folk beliefs, especially in rural areas of central and north Thailand. Modern practices follow the Western medical model.

Cambodia

Throughout Cambodia's long history, religion has been a major source of cultural inspiration. Over nearly three millennia, Cambodians have developed a unique Cambodian culture and belief system from the syncreticism of indigenous animistic beliefs and the Indian religions of Buddhism and Hinduism. Indian culture and civilization, including its languages and arts reached mainland Southeast Asia around the 1st century AD.

It is generally believed that seafaring merchants brought Indian customs and culture to ports along the Gulf of Thailand and the Pacific en route to trade with China. The Kingdom of Funan was most probably the first Cambodian state to benefit from this influx of Indian ideas. There is also French colonial influence as well.

10. what is the cause and effect of the novel great gatsby?​


"The Great Gatsby" is a novel written by F. Scott Fitzgerald that was first published in 1925. The novel explores themes such as the American Dream, wealth, love, and the corrupting influence of power.

One of the main causes in the novel is the pursuit of the American Dream, which drives many of the characters' actions. For example, Jay Gatsby becomes wealthy through illegal means in order to win back his lost love, Daisy Buchanan, and live the luxurious life he believes she desires. Additionally, the rampant consumerism and materialism of the 1920s create a desire for wealth and status that motivates characters like Tom and Daisy Buchanan to engage in extramarital affairs and other immoral actions.

The effects of these actions are complex and varied. One major effect is the corruption of the characters, as they become increasingly willing to betray others and engage in morally dubious behavior in order to achieve their goals. For example, Gatsby's pursuit of Daisy ultimately leads to his downfall, as his illegal activities and involvement with criminal elements catch up with him. Similarly, the Buchanans' affairs and recklessness lead to tragedy and heartbreak for themselves and others.

Overall, "The Great Gatsby" portrays the dangers of unchecked ambition and the moral decay that can result from the pursuit of wealth and power.

12. compare the ballroom attire in the 16th century and the attire in the 20th century. Describe each


Answer:

The ballroom attire in the 16th century was characterized by grand and elaborate styles. Women would wear large, flowing gowns made of expensive materials such as silk and adorned with intricate embellishments such as lace and jewels. Men would wear high-collared shirts and doublets paired with breeches, hose, and shoes with large buckles.

In contrast, ballroom attire in the 20th century became more streamlined and simple. Women would often wear sleek, form-fitting dresses in solid colors or with simple patterns, while men would wear tuxedos or suits in classic styles. The focus shifted from elaborate embellishments and accessories to clean lines and understated elegance.

Overall, the evolution of ballroom attire reflects changes in fashion and cultural attitudes over time, with a move towards simplicity and practicality in the 20th century.


13. In the novel Great Gatsby, how the social classes affect the conflict?​


Answer:

In "The Great Gatsby" by F. Scott Fitzgerald, social class plays a significant role in the conflicts that arise between the characters. The novel portrays the social hierarchy of the 1920s in America, where the wealthy and powerful hold most of the privileges and opportunities, while those in lower classes struggle to make ends meet.

Jay Gatsby, the protagonist, is an example of someone who is not born into the upper class but strives to become part of it. He accumulates his wealth through illegal means, such as bootlegging, in order to impress Daisy Buchanan, his love interest, who belongs to the wealthy and elite class. The conflict arises when Gatsby's desire to win Daisy's love and acceptance is thwarted by the societal norms and expectations that come with her social status.

Tom Buchanan, Daisy's husband, represents the old money and social elite, and his arrogance and sense of entitlement create a contrast with Gatsby's humble origins and aspirations. The tension between Gatsby and Tom, who represent different social classes, escalates into a conflict over Daisy's affection and loyalty.

The novel also highlights the emptiness and corruption that often accompany wealth and power. The wealthy characters in the novel, such as Tom and Daisy, are portrayed as shallow, selfish, and morally bankrupt, while the poorer characters, such as George Wilson and Myrtle Wilson, are depicted as being more genuine and sympathetic.

In summary, the social classes in "The Great Gatsby" play a major role in the conflicts between the characters, highlighting the tension and disparity that existed during the 1920s in America.
