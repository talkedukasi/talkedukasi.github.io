2. Lesson: Relating Triangles to Quadrilaterals and Relating One Quadrilateral to Another Quadrilateral Direction: Using the table below, find the similarities and differences of triangles and quadrilaterals. Triangle Quadrilateral Similarities Differences​


Triangle Quadrilateral

Similarities Both are polygonal shapes with straight sides.

Both have interior angles that add up to specific values: 180 degrees for triangles and 360 degrees for quadrilaterals.

Both have sides that can be classified as congruent or non-congruent.

Both have diagonals that can be drawn within the shape.

Differences Triangles have three sides and three angles, while quadrilaterals have four sides and four angles.

Triangles are classified based on the length of their sides and the measure of their angles, while quadrilaterals are classified based on their shape.

Quadrilaterals have more possible classifications than triangles (square, rectangle, parallelogram, trapezoid, etc.).

Not all quadrilaterals have congruent sides or angles (unlike some triangles, such as equilateral triangles).

Some quadrilaterals have diagonals that bisect each other, while others do not.
