Lilibeth left her house two hours earlier than Shirley and walked at a rate 6 km/h slower than Shirley's rate of Biking. If after 1.5 hours of Shirley's biking, she reached Lilibeth's walking, what is the rate of Shirley's biking?HI PO PA ANS PO ASAP NEED KO NA PO KASI NOWW THANKSS!!​


Let's call the rate of Shirley's biking "B" and the rate of Lilibeth's walking "W".

We know that:

W = B - 6 (because Lilibeth's walking rate is 6 km/h slower than Shirley's biking rate)

We also know that after 1.5 hours, Shirley reached Lilibeth.

So, using the rate and time formula:

distance = rate x time

we can set up two equations:

distance traveled by Shirley = B x 1.5

distance traveled by Lilibeth = W x (1.5 + 2) (because Lilibeth left 2 hours earlier)

Since they met after 1.5 hours of Shirley's biking, we know that the distance traveled by Shirley is equal to the distance traveled by Lilibeth.

So we can set the two equations equal to each other:

B x 1.5 = W x (1.5 + 2)

We know that W = B - 6, so we can substitute that into the equation:

B x 1.5 = (B - 6) x (1.5 + 2)

Solving for B, we can find that the rate of Shirley's biking is B = 15 km/h.

12. moral from lottery by shirley jackson


The moral of Shirley Jackson's Lottery seems connected to society's awareness of the tradition's danger. We need to remind ourselves that just there is a norm, something that has been done in traditional ways, it doesn't mean we should close our door for new ways, new systems, especially if this something new is much better, more humane and an outstandingly effective way to deal with our predicaments.

Shirley Jackson wrote this story during the era when communism  and socialism were feared by most society and the historical context in which dropping of bombs against other nation and having some concentration camp were being appreciated. Her story targeted those people who will only speak up if and only if they are being directly affected by social issues.
