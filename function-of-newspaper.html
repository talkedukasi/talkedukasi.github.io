21. •What is media? Media definition INTRODUCTION: REVIEW MEDIA INTERNET RADIO MAGAZINES TELEVISION NEWSPAPERS Photo Credit: http: //communicationtheory. org/functions-of-mass- communication/​


Answer:

Media refers to the channels or means of communication used to convey information, news, entertainment, and other forms of content to a wide audience. It encompasses various forms of communication, both traditional and digital, that facilitate the transmission and dissemination of information.

Here are the definitions of the different types of media you mentioned:

Internet: Internet media refers to the digital content and communication channels accessible through the internet. It includes websites, social media platforms, online news portals, blogs, online videos, and other digital platforms that allow users to access and share information.

Radio: Radio media involves the broadcasting of audio content over radio frequencies. It includes traditional terrestrial radio stations as well as online radio platforms that deliver music, news, talk shows, and other audio programs to listeners.

Magazines: Magazines are print publications that are issued periodically, typically weekly, monthly, or quarterly. They cover a wide range of topics such as lifestyle, fashion, sports, current affairs, and specialized interests. Magazines often include articles, photographs, and illustrations.

Television: Television media refers to the broadcasting of audiovisual content through television networks or digital platforms. It involves the transmission of programs, shows, documentaries, news, and advertisements to viewers via television sets or online streaming services.

Newspapers: Newspapers are print publications that provide news, current events, and feature articles. They are typically published daily or weekly and contain a variety of sections covering different topics like politics, business, sports, culture, and more. Newspapers may also have online editions or digital versions accessible through websites.

Media plays a crucial role in society by providing information, entertainment, and a platform for public discourse. It influences public opinion, shapes cultural narratives, and serves as a watchdog by holding institutions accountable. With the rise of digital media, the boundaries between different types of media have become more blurred, and there is often convergence and integration of various media forms in the digital landscape

27. VE THE FOLLOWING PROBLEM BY FILLING IN THE NECESSARY INFORMATION AND GRAPHING THE FUNCTION. Larry earns extra money by selling newspapers. He gets P5 for every newspaper sold plus P25 daily salary. How much will he earn if he sells 20 newspapers? a. What is the equation of the linear function? b. What is the independent variable in the situation? c. What is the dependent variable in the situation? d. Complete the following table of values to identify the elements of the domain and range of the function.need now pls fix ur answers​


Answer:

a. The equation of the linear function is:

Earnings = (P5 x number of newspapers sold) + P25

Earnings = (P5 x 20) + P25

Earnings = P100 + P25

Earnings = P125

Therefore, Larry will earn P125 if he sells 20 newspapers.

b. The independent variable in the situation is the number of newspapers sold.

c. The dependent variable in the situation is the amount of earnings.

d.Number of newspapers sold Earnings

0 P25

5 P50

10 P75

15 P100

20 P125

The domain of the function is the set of all possible values of the independent variable, which in this case is the number of newspapers sold. The range of the function is the set of all possible values of the dependent variable, which in this case is the amount of earnings.

The domain of the function is [0, ∞) and the range of the function is [P25, ∞). The earnings increase linearly with the number of newspapers sold, and there is a minimum earning of P25 even if no newspapers are sold.
