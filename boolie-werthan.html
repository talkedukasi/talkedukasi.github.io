Answer:

Driving Miss Daisy is about a Jewish old woman who encountered a struggle driving her own car. Due to that her son Boolie hired a African-American driver to accompany her to whenever she wants to go. At first Miss Daisy refuses such thing because she doesn’t want people thinking that she is acting rich and is very prideful of her wealth.

The Characters in Driving Miss Daisy:

Miss Daisy- a woman in her seventies opposed to the idea of having her personal chauffeur  Hoke- the driver Boolie hired for his mother Boolie- the son of Miss Daisy

Traits of the son Boolie:

Kind Understanding Wants what’s best for his mother Respectful to his mother’s decisions

One example of Boolie being respectful to his mother is that when Miss Daisy found out that Hoke had stolen a can of salmon and ate it without asking for any permission. She wanted to fire him here and then. Boolie respected his mother’s decision and was ready to talk to Hoke about it.

But as Hoke appeared bringing the same can of salmon he ate and says that he replaced it and apologized for eating it without Miss Daisy knowing.
