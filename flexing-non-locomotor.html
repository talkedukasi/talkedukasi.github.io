24. What is locomotor and non locomotor​


Answer:

Locomotor movements are those that incorporate traveling from one point to another. ... Nonlocomotor movements are body movements without travel, such as bending, swaying, or wiggling. Manipulative movements involve both the body and an object.

Answer:

Locomotor and Non Locomotor

Explanation:

Locomotor- These are movements where the body travels through space from one location to another. Locomotor movements primarity use the feet  for support however, the body can travel on other parts such as the hands and feet.

Non-Locomotor- These are movements that occur in the body parts or the whole body and do not cause the body to travel to another space.  However, non locomotor movements can be combined with locomotor movements such as a walk and arm swing.

29. Differences between locomotor skills and non-locomotor skills Differences between locomotor skills and non-locomotor skills


Answer:

Locomotor Skills and Non-locomotor Skills

Locomotor Skills

The movements that incorporate traveling from one point to another.

Body moving from one place to another.

Develop bodily control.

Build upon to create routines and games.

The skills involve different pathways, different direction and different speed.

Examples of Locomotor Skills

Here are some examples of movements that require moving from a place to a place.

walking

running

jumping

hopping

skipping

sliding

galloping

leaping

Non-locomotor Skills

These are movements that do not require body to move from place to place.

Perform in stable position. These are movements that can be done while sitting or standing.

Mostly used in gymnastics.

Examples of Non-locomotor Skills

stretching

bending

pulling

pushing

swaying

twisting

log rolling

balancing
