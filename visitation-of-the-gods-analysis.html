8. define the job analysis and function of the job analyst.​


Answer:

Job analysis is the process of understanding the details of a specific job. This means gathering information through various methods to understand the functions employees perform, the tools and skills employees need, and the results they achieve. The process of job analysis involves the analyst gathering information about the duties of the incumbent, the nature and conditions of the work, and some basic qualifications.

The function of a job analyst is to perform this analysis and provide information to organizations that helps them determine which employees are best fit for specific jobs. The job analyst may use various methods to gather this information, such as observation, interviews, or performing the specific job or activity to know contextual factors and scenarios job holders face

What is a Political Analyst?

Political analysts (sometimes known as political scientists) research the origins, development, and operation of political systems. Governments, political ideas, policies, political trends, and international relationships are all investigated and analyzed. Their research on internal politics, foreign policy, and social concerns helps politicians make better decisions. Political analysts may also investigate the reasons, cultures, beliefs, history, and decision-making processes that underpin policies, as well as how they affect US objectives or public safety.
