Obesity is generally caused by eating too much and moving too little. If you consume high amounts of energy, particularly fat and sugars, but do not burn off the energy through exercise and physical activity, much of the surplus energy will be stored by the body as fat

Malnutrition is the condition that develops when the body is deprived of vitamins, minerals and other nutrients it needs to maintain healthy tissues and organ function. Malnutrition occurs in people who are either undernourished or overnourished.

28. A condition wherein a person is not getting enough or right food. A. Obesity B. Under nutrition C. Malnutrition D. Over nutrition


The answer is C. Malnutrition

Malnutrition is a health condition that one's body is not getting enough nutrients, vitamins and other necessary minerals that it needs to be healthy.  

Here are some symptoms of malnourishments: Yeast infection is visible by having white patches on cheeks. A person's mouth has a pale red color with canker sores. Blurred visions with swollen corneas. Not be able to think clearly. Unexplainable weight loss. Poor blood circulation

 

Types of Malnutrition Deficiencies - this happens when someone is not getting the right amount of nutrients that his/her body needs. Sometimes, not eating the right amount of food causes this.   Stunting - this goes to people who has a short height for his/her age. This is usually starts with children, in their growth development causes by infection or disease that they caught when they are still young.  Undernutrition - this case is for people who are not taking the right amount of calories. People who are underweight are the best example for this.  Wasting - this describes someone when he/she has a low weight compared to his/her height.
