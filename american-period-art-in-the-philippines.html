1. : Describe/Explain the Philippine art during PRE COLONIAL ART PERIOD,SPANISH COLONIAL ART PERIOD, AMERICAN COLONIAL ART PERIOD.and POST COLONIAL PERIOD.​


Colonial Art Period Philippines

The cultural achievements of pre-colonial Philippines include those covered by the prehistory and the early history (900–1521) of the Philippine archipelago's inhabitants, the indigenous forebears of today's Filipino people.

Among the cultural achievements of the native people's belief systems, and culture in general, that are notable in many ethnic societies, range from agriculture, societal and environmental concepts, spiritual beliefs, up to advances in technology, science, and the arts.

History of the Philippines from 1521 to 1898, also known as the Spanish colonial period, a period during which the Captain General of the Philippines located in a group of Islands in Southeast Asia was colonized by Spain known as "Las Islas Filipinos", under New Spain until independence Mexico which resulted in Madrid's direct control of the area.

This area was also known as the Spanish East Indies to the colonists. Starting with the 1521 arrival of the European explorer Ferdinand Magellan sailing for Spain, which marked the period when the Philippines was a colony of the Spanish Empire, and ending with the outbreak of the Philippine Revolution in 1898, which marked the beginning of the American colonial era in Philippine history.

5. What are the influences brought about by the American and Spanish period that are still evident with the art and culture of the Philippines?​


Answer:

The Spanish influence on Filipino culture has been profound, having originated from the Spanish East Indies. A variety of aspects of the customs and traditions in the Philippines today can be traced back to this influence.

Spanish settlement in the Philippines first took place in the 1500s, during the Spanish colonial period of the islands. The conquistador Miguel López de Legazpi founded the first Spanish settlement in Cebu in 1565 and later established Manila as the capital of the Spanish East Indies in 1571. The Philippine Islands are named after King Philip. Spaniards are referred to by Filipinos as "Kastila" (Castilian) named after the former Kingdom of Castile, now a region of Spain. The majority of the Filipinos of Spanish descent are of Spaniard origin, while a very small minority are Latin American. Another term for them is Spanish Filipino.

14. Learning Tasks 4: What are the Philippine Contemporary Art Forms? Define each in your own words based on the timeline presented. PHILIPPINE ART PERIOD TIMELINE Pre-Colonial Art Period Spanish Colonial Art Period Post War Colonial Art Period American Colonial Art Period Contemporary Art Period ​


Answer:

There are five Philippine contemporary art forms: Pre-Colonial, Spanish Colonial, Post War Colonial, American Colonial, and Contemporary. Each art form has its own unique features and characteristics.

Pre-Colonial art is the earliest form of Philippine art. It is characterized by simple designs and geometric shapes. The most common pre-colonial art forms are pottery and weaving.

Spanish Colonial art is more ornate and elaborate than pre-colonial art. It is often characterized by religious imagery and themes. Common Spanish Colonial art forms include painting, sculpture, and architecture.

Post War Colonial art emerged after the Philippines gained independence from Spain in 1898. This art form is a mix of pre-colonial, Spanish colonial, and American influences. The most common post war colonial art forms are painting and sculpture.

American Colonial art emerged after the United States acquired the Philippines from Spain in 1898. This art form is characterized by realistic depictions of everyday life. Common American Colonial art forms include painting, photography, and architecture.

Contemporary art is the most recent form of Philippine art. It is characterized by abstract or experimental designs. The most common contemporary art forms are installations, performance arts, and digital arts.PA BRAINLY THANKS

15. The determination of the Philippine Contemporary Art Period Is still being debated due to its loose usage even during the American Colonial Period. Some Philippine art historians/critics suggest that the Philippine Modern Art Period began during this time, but only fully developed after the war. paexplain po ako tagalog explain lang po salamat.


Explanation:

Ang terminong "Philippine Contemporary Art Period" ay ginamit nang maluwag kahit noong Panahon ng Kolonyal ng Amerika, kaya mahirap matukoy kung kailan talaga ito nagsimula. Gayunpaman, iminumungkahi ng ilang historyador/kritiko ng sining sa Pilipinas na nagsimula ang Panahon ng Makabagong Sining ng Pilipinas sa panahong ito, dahil sinusunod na nila ang mga istilo at uso ng sining sa Kanluran. Ito ay higit na sinuportahan ng paglikha ng Art Association of the Philippines (AAP), na may malakas na pagkahilig sa modernistang sining kaysa sa tradisyonal na sining (kilala rin bilang Amorsolo School). Gayunpaman, pagkatapos lamang ng Ikalawang Digmaang Pandaigdig na ang Makabagong Sining ng Pilipinas ay naging puspusan at lalo pang umunlad. Ang konteksto kung saan ginawa ang sining ay may mahalagang papel sa paghubog ng anyo at kahulugan nito, at ang kontemporaryong sining ay patuloy na nagbabago at nagbabago. Samakatuwid, mahalagang isaalang-alang ang parehong anyo at konteksto kapag pinag-aaralan ang kasaysayan ng sining ng Pilipinas.
