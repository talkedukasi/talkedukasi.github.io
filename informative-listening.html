10. explain Informative listening​


Answer:

Informative listening refers to listening for information and for the speaker's message. To understand the speaker's message, informative listeners will listen for key words, will concentrate to remember important concepts, and will log important information in their memories.


11. Information Listening​


Answer:

Informational listening is listening with the goal of learning, understanding, and grasping information. To understand the speaker's message, informative listeners will listen for key words, will concentrate to remember important concepts, and will log important information in their memories.  Informational listening is distinguished from several other forms of listening, such as relational, appreciative, critical, discriminative, and inspirational listening.

When we engage in listening we are doing so for many different reasons depending upon the goals in which we are trying to achieve. There are four different types of listening that are essential to know when deciding what your goal as the listener is. The four types of listening are appreciative, empathic, comprehensive, and critical. Familiarize yourself with these different types of listening so you can strengthen and improve your ability to critically think and evaluate what you have heard.
