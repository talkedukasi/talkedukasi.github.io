11. example name of community icon​


An example of a community icon.

A community, besides consisting of its members, also consist of its particular idea or goal that is shared between all of the members. For example, a community which is geared towards the goal of having a clean environment would center its activities regarding that topic.

A community also needs its particular icon. This icon serves as a logo or symbol that functions to represent that particular community. The making of this community icon is also very similar to making or designing a logo. The designer has to take many considerations before coming up with a logo sample.

An icon also must be able to represent the community correctly. For example, a community icon of an environmental organization or community would commonly use environment-related symbol, such as a tree, forest, or a mountain.

As for the name, it usually matches the community itself. A simple example would be "tree-saving community icon". 16. what are the famous community icon?


Answer:

A cultural icon is an artifact that is identified by members of a culture as representative of that culture. The process of identification is subjective, and "icons" are judged by the extent to which they can be seen as an authentic proxy of that culture. When individuals perceive a cultural icon, they relate it to their general perceptions of the cultural identity represented.[1] Cultural icons can also be identified as an authentic representation of the practices of one culture by another.[2]

Apple pie, baseball, and the flag of the United States are three American cultural icons.

In the media, many items and persons of popular culture have been called "iconic" despite their lack of durability, and the term "pop icon" is often now used synonymously. Some commentators believe that the word is overused or misused.[3]

The values, norms and ideals represented by a cultural icon vary both among people who subscribe to it, and more widely among other people who may interpret cultural icons as symbolising quite different values. Thus an apple pie is a cultural icon of the United States', but its significance varies among Americans.

National icons can become targets for those opposing or criticising a regime, for example, crowds destroying statues of Vladimir Lenin in Eastern Europe after the Revolutions of 1989[4] or burning the American flag to protest American actions abroad.[5]

Religious icons can also become cultural icons in societies where religion and culture are deeply entwined, such as representations of the Madonna in societies with a strong Catholic tradition
