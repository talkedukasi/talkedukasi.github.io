27. Understanding the James - Lange Theory on Emotions. What is the correct sequence? A. Physiological changes, feel emotion, interpretation of emotion, observable behavior. B. Physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, feel emotion, observable behavior. C. Feel emotion, physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, observable behavior. D. Brain interpretation, physiological change, observable way. E. Interpretation of stimuli, brain interpretation, physiological changes, observable behavior. Discuss your answer, and then submit.


Answer:

B. Physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, feel emotion, observable behavior.

Explanation:

The James-Lange Theory of Emotions states that events around us cause physiological changes or arousal. We then interpret these physiological changes or arousal and based on our interpretation, we feel the emotions associated with the interpretation. We then act in response to the felt emotion. These actions are the observable behaviors following the emotion. To illustrate the James-Lange Theory of Emotions, consider the following:

You see a man in a dark alley (stimulus)Your heart then suddenly beat faster and you feel sweaty (physiologic response)Your brain tried to interpret these emotions (interpretation)You feel fear based on the interpretation made by the brain (emotion)You turned around and ran back home (behavior)

30. what is physiological noise​


Answer:

Physiological noise is any distraction due to a physiological function that interferes with communication. Examples of physiological noise include hunger, fatigue, headaches, pain, and physiological effects from medicine that affect the way you think or feel.
