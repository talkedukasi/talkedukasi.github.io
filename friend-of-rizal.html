9. In not more than 3 sentences, describe Ferdinand blumentritt as Rizal's best friend. ​


Answer:

Blumentritt wrote extensively about the Philippines, although he never visited the islands, corresponding with the then Filipino student and writer José Rizal, who later became a national hero. Blumentritt's relationship with Rizal began as early as July 1886.[2] Blumentritt became one of Rizal's closest confidants although they met only once. He translated a chapter of the latter's first book, Noli Me Tangere, into German and wrote the preface to Rizal's second book, El filibusterismo, although he was against its publication as he believed that it would lead to Rizal's death. These two novels are commentaries disguised as fiction which angered both the Catholic Church and the Spanish colonial government, and which eventually led to Rizal's 1896 trial and execution. Before the execution in Manila, Rizal wrote his final letter for Blumentritt.[3][4] Blumentritt reportedly cried after receiving the letter.

12. A friend of rizal that offered financial aitance that led to the publication of el filibuterimo.


Jose Rizal's second novel, "El Filibusterismo," was published in 1891, after his first novel, "Noli Me Tangere," was published in 1887. "El Filibusterismo" is a sequel to "Noli Me Tangere" and continues the story of the Philippine Revolution and the struggles of the Philippine people against colonial oppression.

According to historical accounts, Rizal received financial assistance from several sources to fund the publication of "El Filibusterismo." One of these sources was his friend Ferdinand Blumentritt, a Czech-Austrian ethnographer and linguist who strongly supported Rizal and the Philippine Revolution. Blumentritt was instrumental in helping Rizal to publish both "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" and also played a crucial role in promoting Rizal's ideas and ideals to a broader international audience.

Other sources of financial assistance for the publication of "El Filibusterismo" included Rizal's funds, as well as contributions from other supporters and sympathizers of the Philippine Revolution. Despite his challenges and obstacles, Rizal successfully published both "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," two of the most important works of literature in Philippine history and had a significant impact on the country's struggle for independence.

17. in what way the family of rizal and his friends made contributions to his success. ​


Answer:

The family of Jose Rizal and his close friends played significant roles in his success as a national hero and a writer. Here are some ways they contributed to his success:

Encouragement and support: Rizal's family and friends supported and encouraged him in his pursuit of education, literature, and activism. They believed in his abilities and talents and provided him with the emotional and financial support he needed to succeed.

Intellectual stimulation: Rizal's family and friends were also intellectuals who engaged in lively discussions and debates about politics, culture, and society. This intellectual stimulation challenged Rizal to think critically and develop his own ideas and beliefs.

Inspiration for his characters: Rizal's family and friends also served as inspirations for the characters in his literary works. For example, his mother Dona Teodora inspired the character of Sisa in "Noli Me Tangere," while his friend Ferdinand Blumentritt was the basis for the character of Elias.

Financial assistance: Rizal's family and friends also provided him with financial assistance when he needed it. For example, his friend Valentin Ventura helped him pay for his studies in Europe, while his sister Saturnina provided him with funds for the printing of his books.

Protection and support during his exile: Rizal's family and friends also protected and supported him during his exile in Dapitan. His friend Maximo Viola brought him books and other materials, while his family visited him and provided him with the emotional support he needed to endure his exile.

In summary, the family of Jose Rizal and his friends made significant contributions to his success by providing him with encouragement, intellectual stimulation, inspiration for his literary works, financial assistance, and protection and support during his exile.
