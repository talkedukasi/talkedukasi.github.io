What is the differences in the taiga forest and deciduous forest?

Similar animal species can be found in both taiga and temperate forests, particularly in northern temperate forests where hard winters limit the populations of animals.

Trees in a Taiga forest have non-needle shaped leaves that fall off in the autumn. Trees in a temperate deciduous forest do the exact opposite! Their leaves have a waxy covering and remain on the branches all year! Temperatures in the Temperate Deciduous Biome, for example, fluctuate throughout the year.

What distinguishes tropical rainforests from temperate rainforests?

Tropical and temperate rainforests are the two types of rainforests. Closer to the equator, where it is warmer, tropical rainforests can be found. Temperate rainforests can be found near the equator's colder coastal regions to the north and south. The tropical rainforest is a hot, humid ecosystem with year-round rain.

26. how is a temperate forest different from a coniferous forest


Answer:

Oaks, elms, ash, and beeches are a few of the deciduous trees that can grow in temperate forests. Some temperate forests have trees with needles rather than leaves. Trees that have needles and cones, like pine cones, are called conifers. A forest with mostly conifers is called a coniferous forest.
