10. How does boat float in water and how does boat sink pahelp po at sana po with explination​


If the downward gravitational force is less than the upward buoyancy force then the object floats, otherwise it sinks. That is, if an object weighs less than the amount of water it displaces then it floats otherwise it sinks. A boat floats because it displaces water that weighs more than its own weight.

An object will float if it weighs less than the amount of water it displaces. So here the weight of the ship is less than that the amount of water it displaces. Hence it floats on water.

The air that is inside a ship is much less dense than water. That's what keeps it floating! The average density of the total volume of the ship and everything inside of it (including the air) must be less than the same volume of water.

16. Why does ship/boat float and a 10 centavos coin sink in water?​


Ship/boats float because they are made of materials that are less dense than water, such as wood or steel. The air inside the boat also helps it to stay afloat. A 10 centavos coin sinks in water because it is made of metal, which is denser than water and therefore heavier.

19. 2. A 214 kg boat is sinking in the ocean. The force of gravity that draws the boat down is partially offset by the buoyant force of the water, so that the net unbalanced force on the boat is 1310 N. What is the acceleration of the boat?​


ACCELERATION

Problem:

A 214 kg boat is sinking in the ocean. The force of gravity that draws the boat down is partially offset by the buoyant force of the water, so that the net unbalanced force on the boat is 1310 N. What is the acceleration of the boat?

Given:

f=1,310 N

m=214 kg

a=?

Solution:

a=f/m

a=1,310/214

a=6.121 m/s²

Hence, the acceleration of the boat is 6.121 m/s²

26. Imagine you are in a sinking boat sorrounded by sharks.How can you survive?​


Answer:

Assuming there is absolutely no way to save the lifeboat (i.e. plug the holes, etc) I would try to break part of the boat off. This would serve two purposes:

1. Give me something to hold onto and float. The boat as a whole will sink, but a single plank or piece of wood will float and provide you some support.

2. Possibly serve as a weapon if the sharks attack.

Once the boat was sunk I would remain as calm and still as possible. Sharks are attracted to movement and blood so hopefully I'm not bleeding and I can remain calm.

If worst came to worst, and the sharks attacked, I would attempt to hit/gouge their eyes and gills. Sharks are most vulnerable in those areas and they don't like to fight prey that will hurt them back.
