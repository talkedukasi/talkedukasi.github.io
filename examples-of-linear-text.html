What is Linear text?

It pertains to the traditional text that starts reading from beginning to end. And it is the most common text wherein the focus of this is the grammar, arrangement, and style. Also, it has an order or sequence that depends on the author or writer. Examples of linear text are on printed papers. It typically takes time to point out or determine the information needed.

Other examples of it are short stories, articles, newspapers.

What is Non-Linear text?

Compared with the linear text, the orderly sequence is not required here. It uses visuals to help readers to see and capture the true meaning. And it allows the readers to decide on making these choices and having non-linear text pinpoint the necessary specific details by skipping a lot of information and words that provide some background characters, figures, and context. And, there are a lot of multiple reading paths here.

Other examples are knowledge maps, digital text with hyperlinks, charts, concept maps, and graphs.
