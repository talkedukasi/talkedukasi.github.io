About Byzantine Art

Byzantine Architectural Art is a type of Architectural Art originating from the Byzantine Empire which flourished in the Medieval Period in the Ancient European Plain. Byzantine architectural art is a continuation and part of the journey of Roman architecture.

Byzantine Architectural Art is a symbol of the wealth of the Ancient Roman Architectural Art, where most of the people have embraced Christianity/Catholicism and built a famous Byzantine Empire in Constantinople.

History of Byzantine Architectural Art

The period of the development of Byzantine Architectural Art ranges from 395 - 1100 AD. The Byzantine period was a period of Roman rule that began to crumble in the Italian Peninsula and shifted power to Eastern Europe. The decision of Constantine the Great who wanted to control the plains of Europe and Asia, has made the decision to move the center of the Ancient Roman government from the city of Nicomedia. The center of the Roman government then moved to Eastern Europe and was centered on the City of Byzantium (before it was called the City of Constantinople), so it was called the Byzantine Empire.

When the Byzantine Empire turned to be led by Justinian the Great, the change in style and Art of Architecture, which was influenced by the culture of Ancient Greece and Ancient Rome and had developed with new styles and characteristics, became a hallmark of Byzantine Architectural Art. Although the shape of the building still has a tendency to the Early Christian Architectural Style, the Byzantine Architectural Art is a concept of architectural renewal towards modernization in the European region which was introduced through the culture of the Byzantine Empire. This Byzantine Art of Architecture later influenced the Art of Architecture in the Mediterranean region between Europe and Southwest Asia, and inspired the development of Renaissance Architecture and Ottoman Islamic Architectural Art after their collapse in 1453.

What is the specific sculpture of Byzantine?

Little sculpture was produced in the Byzantine Empire. The most frequent use of sculpture was in small relief carvings in ivory, used for book covers, reliquary boxes, and similar objects. Other miniature arts, embroidery, goldwork, and enamel work, flourished in the sophisticated and wealthy society of Constantinople.

What is the example of Byzantine sculpture?

Perhaps the best known example of Byzantine art is a tenth-century mosaic of the Virgin Mary in the Hagia Sophia in Istanbul that demonstrates the stylized forms, sharp contours, flat fields of color, and gold mosaic the period is known for.
