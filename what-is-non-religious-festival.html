7. What are the example of non religious festival in the philippines???


Here are some of the non-religious festivals in the Philippines:

1. Pangisdaan Festival in Navotas
Navotas, found on the north of Manila, is considered as the Fishing Capital of the Philippines. Pangisdaan Festival is one of the highlights of the Navotas Day Celebration every 16th of January. This is to pay tribute to their main source of income, which is fishing.

2. Panagbenga Festival in Baguio
Baguio holds its biggest festival, the Panagbenga Festival, every February. The event showcases floral floats and native dances. Panagbenga is a term which means "a season of blooming." The event also known as "Baguio Flower Festival."

3. Maskara Festival in Bacolod
Maskara came from two words: English word "mass" and Spanish word "kara" or "face." This started in early 1980s when Bacolod, the county's sugar capital, was hit by major crisis. To lift the people's spirit up, Maskara Festival was done. This celebration happens every third week of October.

11. 2. What are the differences of Religious and Non-religious Festivals/​


Answer:

1. Purpose: Religious festivals are typically celebrated to honor and commemorate religious events or figures, while non-religious festivals are often celebrated to mark cultural or secular occasions.

2. Observance: Religious festivals are usually observed by adherents of a particular religion or faith, while non-religious festivals are often observed by people of all faiths or backgrounds.

3. Rituals and Traditions: Religious festivals often involve specific rituals, prayers, or customs that are associated with that religion. For example, the Muslim festival of Eid al-Fitr involves special prayers, charity-giving, and the breaking of the fast at the end of Ramadan. In contrast, non-religious festivals may involve different customs and traditions that are not specific to any particular religion.

4. Dates: Religious festivals usually occur on specific dates that are determined by religious calendars. In contrast, non-religious festivals may be celebrated on specific dates or over a period of time that is determined by cultural or secular calendars.

5. Significance: Religious festivals often hold significant religious or spiritual significance for believers, while non-religious festivals may be more focused on social or cultural celebrations.
