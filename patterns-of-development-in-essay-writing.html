3. what are the different pattern of development in writing paragraphs or essays?


Answer:

In addition to the concept of the thesis indicating a general, logical order for the support, there are actually many different ways to think about and organize information in the body of an essay, using different patterns of development. These patterns, referred to academically as “rhetorical modes,” mirror the ways in which humans think about their worlds and organize their thoughts in order to communicate.

The concept of rhetorical modes actually goes back to ancient civilizations. Though it seems particularly pressing in our current social media, online-all-the-time culture, the idea of “information overload” has troubled humans for centuries. Despite these concerns, many of our ancestors found productive ways to manage information overload. And their strategies remain helpful today. Ancient rhetoricians, including Aristotle and Cicero, developed techniques that writers used to gather, categorize, and explore common features in sets of information.

They identified “topoi,” or patterns, which were those general features shared in any idea or argument regardless of the content of that argument, including definition, relationship, and/or division. For instance, ancient rhetoricians might ask “Is the argument about a definition?” If they discovered that a definition was, in fact, controversial, then they knew they could follow certain common patterns and use common strategies. Other common patterns included comparison and cause-and-effect.

Knowing that these common patterns of human thought exist, will help you as a writer to both develop and organize information in your essays. The following image identifies common patterns. Although it refers to “paragraph” patterns, understand that these are also common patterns for whole essays.

7. ESSAY WRITING. Min. of 3 por, Mers of 5 pr. - Differentiate the patterns of development in writing and state its uses.​


Answer:

Writing is an essential part of communication, and its effectiveness lies in its ability to convey information in a clear and coherent manner. To achieve this, writers use different patterns of development in their writing, which are techniques used to organize ideas and convey a specific message. These patterns of development are essential in creating a strong and effective piece of writing. In this essay, we will differentiate the patterns of development in writing and state their uses.

The patterns of development in writing are essential in constructing a well-organized piece of writing. They include narration, description, definition, process analysis, comparison and contrast, classification, cause and effect, argument and persuasion, and evaluation. Each pattern of development serves a unique purpose in writing, and the writer must determine which pattern to use based on the purpose of their writing.

Narration is a pattern of development that involves telling a story or describing a series of events. This pattern is commonly used in fiction writing or personal narratives, where the writer seeks to engage the reader and convey a sense of emotion or experience. Description, on the other hand, is a pattern of development that involves using sensory details to create a mental image of a person, place, or thing. This pattern is commonly used in descriptive essays or creative writing, where the writer seeks to create a vivid picture in the reader's mind.

Definition is a pattern of development that involves providing a clear and concise explanation of a concept or idea. This pattern is commonly used in academic writing or technical writing, where the writer seeks to provide a precise meaning of a term or concept. Process analysis is a pattern of development that involves breaking down a complex process into simple steps. This pattern is commonly used in instructional writing or technical writing, where the writer seeks to provide a clear and easy-to-follow guide.

Comparison and contrast is a pattern of development that involves examining the similarities and differences between two or more objects, concepts, or ideas. This pattern is commonly used in persuasive writing, where the writer seeks to convince the reader of a particular viewpoint. Classification is a pattern of development that involves grouping similar objects or ideas into categories. This pattern is commonly used in academic writing or technical writing, where the writer seeks to organize information in a logical and coherent manner.

Cause and effect is a pattern of development that involves examining the relationship between two or more events. This pattern is commonly used in academic writing or technical writing, where the writer seeks to explain the consequences of a particular action or event. Argument and persuasion are patterns of development that involve presenting a particular viewpoint and providing evidence to support it. This pattern is commonly used in persuasive writing or academic writing, where the writer seeks to convince the reader of a particular argument. Evaluation is a pattern of development that involves assessing the strengths and weaknesses of a particular object, concept, or idea. This pattern is commonly used in critical writing or academic writing, where the writer seeks to provide a balanced and objective assessment.

In conclusion, the patterns of development in writing serve a unique purpose in organizing ideas and conveying a specific message. Each pattern is essential in creating a well-organized and effective piece of writing. A writer must determine which pattern to use based on the purpose of their writing, the intended audience, and the type of information they are presenting. By understanding the patterns of development in writing, a writer can effectively convey their message and engage their readers.
