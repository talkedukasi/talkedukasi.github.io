3. What are the significance of nationalism and patriotism as a Criminology Student? ​


Answer:

Patriotism refers to a love for a country with a greater emphasis on principles and beliefs, whereas nationalism refers to giving more weight to unity through a cultural foundation, including language and legacy. Acts of patriotism and feelings of patriotism will help to build a better nation. Patriotism, both militarily and culturally, is by its very nature reactionary.

Not only will you be able to communicate to others in your shared passion for this nation, but the more people who have expressed their patriotism, the greater our nation as a whole should become and that is a significant trait as a criminology student.

answer :

What is the role of patriotism in our lives?

I think it depends on one’s definition of patriotism and discerning the subtle difference between the words patriotism and nationalism.

Patriotism is love and devotion one’s country and embodies such ideas as bravery, valor, duty and devotion. The role of patriotism, then, is to connect together to maintain the integrity of our values. It embodies the idea of unity.

Nationalism, on the other hand, exalts one’s country over all others. Its values are to challenge, belittle, and conquer other countries in order to prove we are bigger and better and to bend them to be like we are. It is exclusionary and promotes separation.

Increasing nationalism results in atrocities such as anti-Semitism, genocide, xenophobia, Fascism and war. True patriotism promotes cooperation and collaboration in order to live by loftier values.
