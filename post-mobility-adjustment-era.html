28. This is prepared after all adjustments have been recorded and posted.


In the accounting cycle, the last step being made is the post-closing trial balance where all closed entries are posted to the ledger. Part of that step is the adjusted trial balance where it is being prepared after adjusted entries are made and posted. This is to check the equality between debits and credits; to be the basis in preparing the financial statements. An adjusted trial balance has nominal accounts (can be found in income statements and withdrawals) and real accounts (can be found in the balance sheet).
