12. A short summary of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" by Antonio Pigafetta.​


Answer:

Antonio Pigafetta's account of "The First Voyage around the World of Ferdinand Magellan" is a journal of the famous Portuguese explorer's expedition to the Spice Islands. Pigafetta was one of the few crew members to survive the voyage and his journal provides a detailed account of the journey, including the crew's interactions with the native peoples they encountered.

The expedition left Spain in September 1519 and after a long journey, they arrived in Brazil in December. From there, they sailed down the coast of South America, discovering the Strait of Magellan and passing through it to reach the Pacific Ocean. They encountered various islands in the Pacific and made landfall in the Philippines, where Magellan was killed in a battle with local tribes.

Pigafetta describes the various cultures and customs of the people they met on their journey, including the native peoples of South America, the Pacific Islands, and the Philippines. He also documents the challenges the crew faced, including disease, hunger, and mutiny.

Despite the hardships, the expedition was a significant achievement in the history of exploration, as it was the first circumnavigation of the globe. Pigafetta's account provides a valuable historical record of the voyage and the cultures encountered along the way.
