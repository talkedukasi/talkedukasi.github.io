In addition, good people are forgiving. They do not hold grudges, and let go of anger that might lead them to hurt others.

Goodness begins with a belief and ends with behavior. If you believe that you are a good person then the hard work begins. You have to turn that belief into a behaviour. ... So becoming or learning to become a good person is not to get praised by others, it is for your own self first.

Please remember the following usages of these kinds of gerunds:

a. Subject

As the meaning of subject remains, it is used to identify who or what about the verb.  

b. Subjective Complement  

Usually acts as a verb to describe the noun next to it

c. Object of a preposition

These gerunds are usually placed before a preposition and is used to connect with the object

d. Direct Object

Gerunds here are usually the receiver of the action and answers to the question "what?"

Appositives is a word or group of words that identifies the noun before it. It is usually enclosed in commas.
