Answer:

Marco Polo was a Venetian merchant and explorer who is known for his extensive travels throughout Asia during the 13th century. He traveled along the Silk Road and visited many countries, including:

China: Marco Polo spent a significant amount of time in China, serving as a government official under Kublai Khan. He traveled throughout the country and wrote extensively about its culture, society, and political system.

India: Marco Polo also visited India, where he spent time in the court of the Sultan of Delhi. He wrote about the country's spices, jewels, and other treasures.

Persia: Marco Polo visited Persia, which was then ruled by the Ilkhanate dynasty. He wrote about the country's trade, architecture, and culture.

Central Asia: Marco Polo traveled through Central Asia, including areas that are now part of Uzbekistan, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. He wrote about the region's nomadic peoples and their customs.

Southeast Asia: Marco Polo traveled to Southeast Asia, including modern-day Vietnam and Cambodia. He wrote about the region's natural resources, including precious metals and gems.

During his travels, Marco Polo also worked as a diplomat, interpreter, and trader. He helped Kublai Khan to negotiate with neighboring kingdoms and tribes and was involved in several military campaigns. His writings, which were compiled into a book called "The Travels of Marco Polo," became an important source of information about the East for Europeans in the centuries that followed.

EVENT - WHAT HAPPENED IN MINDORO?

On December 18, 2020, a power barge operated by a subsidiary of San Miguel Corporation ran aground in the coastal waters off the island of Mindoro, Philippines. The barge was carrying 200,000 liters of fuel oil, which spilled into the sea as a result of the accident. The oil spill covered a vast area of coastal waters, affecting the towns of San Jose, Sablayan, and Mamburao in Occidental Mindoro, and Calapan City and Naujan in Oriental Mindoro.

EFFECTS - WHAT ARE THE OUTCOMES OF OIL SPILL?

The oil spill in Mindoro had severe environmental, social, and economic impacts on the affected areas. The oil contaminated the coastal waters, killing marine life and damaging mangrove forests and coral reefs. The oil spill also affected the livelihoods of thousands of fishermen and coastal communities who rely on fishing and tourism for their income. The spill disrupted the supply of clean water to the affected areas, leading to a water crisis in some communities.

The oil spill had significant health implications, as the toxic fumes and chemicals from the spilled oil can cause respiratory problems and other illnesses. The cleanup efforts also posed health risks to the workers involved in the operation.

WHAT MOVES DID THE GOVERNMENT DO TO SOLVE THE PROBLEM?

The Philippine government, through various agencies and local governments, responded to the oil spill by launching a massive cleanup operation. The government deployed resources such as oil booms, skimmers, and dispersants to contain and clean up the oil spill. The government also provided food assistance and financial aid to the affected communities and fishermen.

San Miguel Corporation, the company responsible for the oil spill, also took steps to address the situation. The company apologized for the accident and pledged to compensate the affected communities and fishermen. The company also offered to provide alternative livelihood programs and to fund the rehabilitation of the affected marine ecosystem.

However, some groups criticized the government and the company for their response to the oil spill, citing delays in the cleanup operations and insufficient compensation for the affected communities. Answer:

1. What is Art?

Art is generally understood as any activity or product done by people with a communicative or aesthetic purpose—something that expresses an idea, an emotion or, more generally, a world view. It is a component of culture, reflecting economic and social substrates in its design.

Arts- Subjects of study primarily concerned with HUMAN CREATIVITY and Social Life, such as LANGUAGES, LITERATURE, & HISTORY (as contrasted with scientific or technical subjects.)

2. What are the Elements of Arts?

1. Line

2. Shape

3. Form

4. Space

5. Value and Contrast

6. Color

7. Texture

3. What are the things that we do to the Elements of Arts?

-The Elements of Art are all important in what makes a work of art interesting. Line, Color, Shape, and Texture can be used individually, or combined together to create more impact. It is the artist's decision in how they want to use them according to their style of art.

-Elements of art are stylistic features that are included within an art piece to help the artist communicate. The seven most common elements include line, shape, texture, form, space, colour and value, with the additions of mark making, and materiality.

4. What are the Principles of design in Arts?

The fundamental principles of design are Emphasis, Balance and Alignment, Contrast, Repetition, Proportion, Movement and White Space. Design differs from art in that it has to have a purpose.

5. What are the core characteristics of the Arts and Crafts?

The core characteristics of the Arts and Crafts movement are a belief in craftsmanship which stresses the inherent beauty of the material, the importance of nature as inspiration, and the value of simplicity, utility, and beauty.
