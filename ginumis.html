17. Describe Special Products Square of a Binomial Square of a trinomial Sum & Difference of two Binomials Cube of a Binomial ​


Answer:

A special product is a product of two binomials. The binomials can be written in any order, and the product will be the same.

For example, the product of (x + 3) and (x - 2) is x^2 - 5x + 6.

The square of a binomial is a binomial raised to the second power.

For example, the square of (x + 3) is x^2 + 9x + 12.

The sum and difference of two binomials is a binomial raised to the first power.

For example, the sum of (x + 3) and (x - 2) is x + 1.

The cube of a binomial is a binomial raised to the third power.

For example, the cube of (x + 3) is x^3 + 27x^2 + 81x.


18. how to multiply binomial by binomial


Answer:

FOIL Method

Step-by-step explanation:

FOIL (First, Outer, Inner and Last)

Example

(2x+1)(3x-2)

First, multiply both first term.

2x * 3x = 6x^2

Outer, multiply the outer term, in this case that's 2x and -2 so..

2x * -2 = -4x

Inner, multiply the inner term, in this case that's 1 and 3x so...

1 * 3x = 3x

Last, multiply all last term, 1 and -2 from out example so...

1 * -2 = -2

Then add like terms

(2x^2) + 3x - 4x -2

(2x^2) - x - 2

22. multiplying binomial to binomial(2q-4)²


1. square the first term. → (2q)² = 4q²
2. multiply the first term with the second term then multiply their product by 2. → 2[2q × (-4)] = 2(-8q) = -16q
3. square the second term. → (-4)² = 16

final answer: 4q² - 16q + 16
