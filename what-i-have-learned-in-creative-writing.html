1. Finding Your Voice: In Creative Writing, it is important to find your own unique style and use it to convey your message.

2. Show Don’t Tell: Creative Writing emphasizes the importance of showing rather than telling. This encourages readers to come to their own conclusions and adds depth to the story.

3. Using Imagery: Creative Writing encourages the use of vivid imagery to bring the story to life and help readers connect with the characters and the setting.

4. Writing with Passion: Creative Writing requires you to use your own personal experiences and emotions to create an engaging story.

Friction is the resistance to motion of one object moving relative to another. It is not a fundamental force, like gravity or electromagnetism. Instead, scientists believe it is the result of the electromagnetic attraction between charged particles in two touching surfaces. Friction is used in car brakes, when we walk or climb a hill, making a fire, skiing down a hill, and more.

Examples:

Rubbing both hands together to create heat.A sled sliding across snow or ice.Skis sliding against snow.

Gravity is the force by which a planet or other body draws objects toward its center. The force of gravity keeps all of the planets in orbit around the sun.

ACTIVE VOICE

Active voice is a grammatical voice common in many of the world's languages. It is the unmarked voice for clauses featuring a transitive verb in nominative–accusative languages, including English and most other Indo-European languages.

PASSIVE VOICE

A passive voice construction is a grammatical voice construction that is found in many languages. In a clause with passive voice, the grammatical subject expresses the theme or patient of the main verb – that is, the person or thing that undergoes the action or has its state changed.
