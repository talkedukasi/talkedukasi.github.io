1. Claro m. recto biography and contribution


Claro M. Recto was born in Tiong, Tayabas on February 8, 1890. He was a Philippine nationalist leader and president of the 1934 constitutional convention. He was one of the most vocal advocates of the Philippine political and social autonomy.

15. what in short is nationalism according to claro m. recto?​


Claro M. Recto, a prominent Filipino nationalist, defined nationalism as "the complete devotion and loyalty of a people to their country, their willingness to make sacrifices for its welfare, and their determination to safeguard its sovereignty and integrity." Recto believed that nationalism is an essential element in the development of a strong and independent nation, and that it is necessary for the protection and advancement of the interests of the people of that nation. For Recto, nationalism was not just a political or economic ideology, but a deeply rooted cultural and social phenomenon that embodied the collective aspirations and values of a people.

Explanation:

It is of great importance that students understand the rationale behind having to take up a Rizal course in college.  For high school students, the Noli Me Tangere and the El Filibusterismo are injected into the Filipino subject as part of the overall curriculum.  In tertiary education, however, Rizal is a subject required of any course, in any college or university in the Philippines.

Answer:

Republic Act 1425, also known as the Rlzal Law

Explanation:

This act aims to include the teaching of Jose Rizal's life, works, and writings to both public and private schools colleges and universities nationwide.

Jose Rizal is one of the country's esteemed heroes — helming the frontier of the Propaganda Movement — as well as writing the two Filipino literature classics, Noli and El Fili.

According to the act, Rizal's life, works, and teachings are constant source of patriotism for the youth, serving as fortitude for them during their critical days at school.
