9. how presentations software/applicationd like microsoft powerpoint can be useful for your future or your childrens future?


Answer:

Presentation software like Microsoft PowerPoint can be extremely useful for both personal and professional development for individuals of all ages, including children. Here are some ways in which PowerPoint can be beneficial:

Academic Presentations: PowerPoint is an excellent tool for students of all ages to create academic presentations that can be used to communicate research, findings, and ideas to their peers, teachers, or professors. These presentations can help students develop important skills such as critical thinking, research, organization, and public speaking.

Professional Presentations: In the business world, PowerPoint is often used for professional presentations to clients, colleagues, and stakeholders. Knowing how to use this software can be a valuable skill for individuals in any industry, as it allows them to effectively communicate ideas, proposals, and results.

Creative Expression: PowerPoint is not just limited to academic and professional use. It can also be used as a creative outlet for individuals to express their ideas, stories, and art in a visual format. This can be especially beneficial for children who may find it easier to express themselves through images and graphics rather than words.

Collaboration: PowerPoint allows multiple users to collaborate on a single presentation, which can be useful in both academic and professional settings. Collaborative presentations can help individuals develop teamwork, communication, and leadership skills.

Technological Literacy: In today's digital age, being proficient in software applications like PowerPoint is becoming increasingly important. Knowing how to effectively use presentation software can help individuals stay current with technology and stand out in a job search or academic setting.

In summary, learning how to use presentation software like Microsoft PowerPoint can provide many benefits for personal and professional development, including academic and professional presentations, creative expression, collaboration, and technological literacy.

Because a career can be defined as a person's progress within an occupation or series of occupations.

However, a career is more than just a job, or working, or your occupation. It also includes your progress through life, your growth and development in vocational and avocational areas of life.

Many of us think that there is only one occupation that is best suited for us, but there are really several that may be good choices. The secret is to identify those occupations in which you have a high probability for success and happiness. As a college student, whether your career goals are accounting, theatre arts, or environmental sciences, there are general skills which will be required regardless of the career you pursue. These skills include the ability to read, write, compute, think critically, and communicate in an effective manner. For the most part, these skills are developed and/or sharpened in general education courses. These skills, along with effective career planning techniques, and the ability to cope with ambiguity in a changing environment, will enable you to overcome obstacles throughout your work life.

Deciding on your initial career may present a stressful and frightening prospect.
