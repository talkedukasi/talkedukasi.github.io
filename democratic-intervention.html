Answer:

A democratic intervention is a foreign intervention in a country's affairs with the aim of promoting democracy and human rights. Democratic intervention is justified based on the belief that every individual has a right to freedom, democracy, and respect for human rights. Democratic intervention can be carried out through diplomatic measures, sanctions, and military operations.

The processes of democratic intervention include:

1. Diplomatic pressure: Interventionists may pressure foreign governments through diplomatic channels and international organizations, such as the United Nations.

2. Economic sanctions: Interventionists may impose economic sanctions on a country with the aim of destabilizing its government and pressuring it to change its behavior.

3. Military intervention: In extreme cases, interventionists may resort to military intervention. This may involve air strikes, ground troops, or peacekeeping forces.

There are several reasons for democratic intervention, including:

1. Human rights abuses: Interventionists may intervene in a country with a history of human rights abuses to protect citizens from repression and violence.

2. Genocide or ethnic cleansing: Interventionists may intervene in cases of genocide or ethnic cleansing to deter these crimes against humanity and protect the targeted group.

3. Threats to international security: Interventionists may intervene in a country when its actions pose a threat to international peace and security. This may include cases involving the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction or terrorist activities.

25. Aano ba Ang Democratic Intervention?


Answer:

Democratic intervention means using of external military forces with the goal of assisting democratization of the country where the intervention is needed.  

Examples:  

Intervention in Afghanistan  Intervention in Iraq  

Explanation:

Democracy - means a form of government in which the common people hold political power and can rule either directly or through elected representatives. Example: United States, Philippines  

Intervention - is the act or fact of becoming involved intentionally in a difficult situation  

Military intervention is used as a means of building democracy in a particular country. The reason behind democratic intervention is because democratic countries rarely to war with each other. Also, stable democracies experience few civil wars. And if a civil war occurs in a stable democracy, it is less chaotic compared to an authoritarian country. Additionally, genocide is rare in democratic countries.
