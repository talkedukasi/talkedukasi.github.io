However, I can provide you with information about the main features and assumptions of the different teaching approaches you mentioned, as well as suggest which competencies from the AP Curriculum guide can be taught using the methods/techniques you listed.

Constructivist Approach:

Assumption: Learning is an active process in which learners construct their own understanding based on their experiences and interactions with the environment.

Features: Emphasis on student-centered learning, inquiry-based instruction, and collaborative learning.

Collaborative Learning:

Assumption Learning is a social process that involves interaction and collaboration among learners.

Features: Group work, peer tutoring, and cooperative learning activities.

Experiential/Contextual Learning:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is situated in real-life contexts and experiences.

Features: Hands-on learning, real-world problem-solving, and authentic assessment.

Thematic Approach:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is organized around a central theme or topic.

Features: Integration of different subjects around a common theme, emphasis on interdisciplinary learning, and exploration of real-world issues.

Conceptual Approach:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is organized around key concepts or principles.

Features: Emphasis on understanding big ideas, making connections between concepts, and applying concepts to real-world situations.

Integrative Approach:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is integrated across different subjects and disciplines.

Features: Emphasis on interdisciplinary learning, making connections between different subjects, and promoting critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Interdisciplinary Approach:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is viewed as a holistic process that integrates knowledge and skills from different subjects and disciplines.

Features: Emphasis on making connections between different subjects, exploring real-world issues, and promoting critical thinking and problem-solving skills.

Multidisciplinary Approach:

Assumption: Learning is most effective when it is viewed as separate and distinct subjects or disciplines.

Features: Emphasis on learning specific content and skills within defined subject areas, with little integration between subjects.

Competencies that can be taught by the use of the ff. methods/techniques:

a. Tri-question - critical thinking, problem-solving

b. Lesson indigenization - cultural awareness, communication

c. Multiple intelligences - creativity, communication, self-awareness

d. Survey/interview - research, communication, data analysis

e. Service learning - civic engagement, social responsibility, communication

As an artist, it is also important to express oneself. This means to convey or communicate something. Every work of art has a specific message to say, one way or another.
