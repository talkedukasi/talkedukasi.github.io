It is in the title of the Story:

The Peasant/Farmer

This character symbolizes wisdom. He subdued the Buffalo to help him in plowing the fields and outwitted the Tiger.

The Tiger

This character symbolizes strength. Proud of his strength and overconfident, the Tiger tried to try trick the Peasant/Farmer into giving his 'Wisdom in a Box'.

The Buffalo

This character symbolizes the lack of intelligence/wisdom. He had never figured out why he always obey his master (The Peasant).

21. What is the plot of The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo


Answer:

"The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo" is a popular folktale in Southeast Asia, particularly in the Philippines, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The story follows a poor peasant who comes across a tiger and a buffalo, both of whom are in need of his help. The peasant, being a kind and generous person, agrees to help both of them.

The tiger promises not to harm the peasant in exchange for his help in curing a wound on his paw. The peasant agrees and uses his knowledge of medicinal herbs to heal the tiger's paw. The buffalo, in turn, needs the peasant's help to untangle its horns from a tree. The peasant agrees to help the buffalo as well.

One day, the peasant overhears the tiger planning to eat him. The peasant, being resourceful, tricks the tiger into thinking that he is much larger than he really is by making his voice echo through a cave. The tiger, thinking he is facing a much larger foe, runs away in fear. Later, the buffalo saves the peasant from drowning in a river by using its horns to lift him to safety.

In the end, the peasant is rewarded for his kindness and resourcefulness when the tiger and buffalo both offer to help him in any way they can. The peasant becomes wealthy and lives a happy life, knowing that his good deeds have been rewarded. The story teaches the values of kindness, resourcefulness, and the importance of helping others in need

27. What is the problem of The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo


Answer:

"The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo" is a traditional folk tale from the Philippines, and it does not have a specific problem that needs to be solved. However, like many folk tales, it teaches a moral lesson and may contain a conflict or challenge that the characters must face.

In this story, the peasant is asked by the king to capture a tiger that has been terrorizing the village. The peasant sets out on his mission and encounters a buffalo that he befriends along the way. When he finally finds the tiger, he tricks it into a pit and brings it back to the king. The conflict in the story is the challenge that the peasant faces in capturing the dangerous tiger, and the moral lesson is the importance of wit and cleverness in overcoming obstacles.

30. What is the POV of The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo


Answer:

The Peasant, Tiger, and the Buffalo is told from a third-person omniscient point of view. This means that the narrator is not a character in the story but has access to all characters' thoughts and feelings.
