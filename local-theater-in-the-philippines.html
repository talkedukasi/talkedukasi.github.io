1. Local theaters and early philippine operas


The Local theaters and Early Philippine Operas

The emergence of the Filipino opera started to take shape during the middle part of the19th century.Foreign performers, including instrumental

virtuosi , as well asopera singers and Spanish  zarzuela  performers came to the country to perform forenthusiastic audiences .

1. Philippine Educational Theater Association (PETA) founded

by 1967 by Cecile Guidote-Alvarez whose theatre productions include classical

works, Philippine myths, legends, commentaries, and social and political

issues. Performed by the PETA are the Caredivas (2011), Haring Lear (2012), and

Pamana (2013).

2. Tanghalang Pilipino – the resident theatre company of the

Cultural Center of the Philippines founded in 1987. Among their theatre plays are

Himala (2004), Ibalong (2012), Noli (2011).

3. Repertory Philippines – organized by Zenaida Amador on

1967 that brought London’s West End and Broadway to Philippine Audiences. Among

their productions are A Portrait of the Artist as Filipino (2009), Little Women

(2010), and Alice in Wonderland (2013).

4. Trumpets – creates theatre productions that highlight

good characters and values of children and family that provide wholesome theatre

experiences for the youth.  

5. New Voice Company – established by Monique Wilson on 1994, this theatre group has earned reputation for staging though-provoking productions on “controversial” and profound topics.

6. Philippine Opera Company (POC) – founded in 1999 by a group of dedicated singers lead by soprano Karla Guttierez as artistic directress. Performed Harana,  Master Class, Mekado, etc.

7. Theater Down South – was founded with Philippine theatre mainstay Michael Williams as artistic director. Performed A Midsummer Night’s Dream, The Princess and the Red Carp and A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum.

8. Name a western theater and compare it to the theater of the Philippines.​


Answer:

One famous Western theater is the Broadway theater in New York City. It is well-known for its productions of musicals, plays, and other forms of live entertainment. Broadway productions are usually grand and lavish, with big sets, elaborate costumes, and impressive special effects.

On the other hand, the theater in the Philippines has a rich history and a unique character. Philippine theater is known for its rich and diverse cultural heritage, with influences from Spanish, American, and indigenous traditions. The most common form of theater in the Philippines is the "sarswela," which is a type of musical play that combines drama, music, and dance.

In terms of production value, Western theater productions are generally more expensive and elaborate than Philippine theater productions. However, Philippine theater has its own unique charm and appeal, with its focus on storytelling, music, and dance.

Another key difference is the audience. Western theater audiences tend to be more formal and reserved, while Philippine theater audiences are often more vocal and interactive, with cheering, clapping, and even singing along with the performers.

Overall, while there are differences between Western and Philippine theater, both are important and valuable forms of art that provide entertainment, education, and cultural enrichment to audiences around the world.

24. the local theater of ateneo de manila?​


Answer:

Tanghalang Ateneo is the longest-running theater company of the Loyola Schools, Ateneo de Manila University. The company weaves into its work the theatrical traditions of the University's sesquicentennial past: the devotion of the salon de actos at the Escuela Municipal, the eloquence of the Ateneo Dramatics Guild, the joie de vivre of the Ateneo Players Theater, and the innovative spirit of the Ateneo Experimental Theater. Like these companies, Tanghalang Ateneo uses theater to foster eloquentia, sapientia, and humanitas – the pillars of Jesuit pedagogy. It sees itself as a theater company in the service of student formation, and by extension – given the Jesuit ideal of magis – a theater in service of the nation.
