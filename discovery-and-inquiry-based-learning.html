6. essay about inquiry-based learning​


Answer:

Inquiry-based learning is an approach to learning that emphasizes the student’s role in the learning process. Rather than the teacher telling students what they need to know, students are encouraged to explore the material, ask questions, and share ideas.

Inquiry-based learning uses different approaches to learning, including small-group discussion and guided learning. Instead of memorizing facts and material, students learn by doing. This allows them to build knowledge through exploration, experience, and discussion.

19. Why inquiry thas be comes the key to a lot of discoveries


Inquiry, or the act of asking questions, is an essential part of the scientific process. By asking questions and seeking answers, scientists are able to explore the world around them and gain a deeper understanding of the natural world. This process of inquiry allows scientists to make new discoveries and advance our collective knowledge. Additionally, inquiry encourages critical thinking and helps us to develop new theories and hypotheses that can be tested through experimentation. In short, inquiry is the key to many scientific discoveries because it drives the scientific process and allows us to better understand the world around us.
