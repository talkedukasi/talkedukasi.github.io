29. 4 What is the attitude of the author in the poem Crossroads? ​


Answer:

The poem "Crossroads" does not contain any explicit statements about the author's attitude. However, the tone of the poem is reflective and contemplative, suggesting that the author is wrestling with a decision or important moment in their life. The repeated use of the phrase "two roads diverged" indicates a sense of being at a crossroads, and the final stanza's emphasis on the choice as "made all the difference" suggests that the author is reflecting on a pivotal moment in their life. Overall, the author's attitude seems to be one of introspection and reflection rather than one of judgment or emotion.
