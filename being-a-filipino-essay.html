11. Essay for science Metabolize :nourishing the Filipino youth towards national well being


Answer:

Nourishing the Filipino youth can appear so superficial when you consider a person’s basic needs. It can be about ensuring the availability of enough nutritious food in their home. A person cannot accomplish tasks mindfully and effectively when he lacks a basic need - food!

On the other hand, nourishing the Filipino youth can mean something else if seen in a deeper and thoughtful way. It can present a more holistic perspective and how this affects our nation’s well-being.

Explanation:

A Summary of PSYSC History

The Philippine Society of Youth Science Clubs (PSYSC) is a non-stock, non-profit, non-government, and non-sectarian organization. It is the national federation of youth science clubs in the Philippines. It is former called the National Science Club of the Philippines formed by a group of science fair winners. In order to have a more inclusive organization with a wider coverage, it later on named as Philippine Society of Youth Science Clubs, Inc.

The PSYSC collaborated with the Science Club Advisers Association of the Philippines (SCAAP) and the Philippine Association of Science and Math Supervisors (PASMS) and conducted activities that promote awareness and understanding of science, technology, and the environment. These activities were later on recognized and the National Science Club Month was declared.

The vision of the organization is to:

create a culture and mindset that learning science, technology, and environment is easy and fun;create a platform with a free exchange of concepts and ideas;create a way of life where science, technology, and environment is used for the development of families, communities, and the society.

In order to achieve these, the organization is dedicated to their mission:

to conduct activities that promote science education;to make science clubbing get known to promote awareness of the scientific culture among the youth;to organize and make connections among youth science clubs;to promote the understanding and application of science, technology, and the environment;to encourage the involvement of all sectors in st&e in the activities and advocacies.

The Three Main Events

National Youth Science, Technology, and Environment Summer Camp (NYSTESC) - a 4-day event filled with lectures, interactive workshops, team-building activities, contests, and socialization events for students and mentorsNational Science Club Month (NSCM) - a month long celebration packed with science-related activitiesChildren’s Science Interactive Workshop (CSIW) - a science camp for elementary students filled with fun and science related activities

National Science Club Month (NSCM) Activities

National Science Club Summit (SUMMIT) - a 2days-1night activity for high school students filled with lectures, workshops, team-building activities, and presentation of science clubsPSYSC Science Olympiad (PSO) - a nationwide quiz show that competed by the students across the PhilippinesI-Teach Science Seminar (ITSS) - an enrichment seminar for teachers on how to teach science in a fun an interactive wayMathematics, Science, at Kapaligiran (MATH-SCI-A-KA) - a Luzon-wide event allotted for workshops and experiments for high school and elementary students from public and private schools then present their engineering projects for the Engineering Science Challenge

PSYSC 2019

This year, the PSYSC celebrates the National Science Club Month with the theme: “METABOLIZE: Nourishing the Filipino Youth Towards National Well-Being.”

Nourishing the Filipino youth can appear so superficial when you consider a person’s basic needs. It can be about ensuring the availability of enough nutritious food in their home. A person cannot accomplish tasks mindfully and effectively when he lacks a basic need - food!

On the other hand, nourishing the Filipino youth can mean something else if seen in a deeper and thoughtful way. It can present a more holistic perspective and how this affects our nation’s well-being.

Creating an interactive and collaborative environment that includes healthy competitions will challenge the youth to be a better version of themselves. Competing with an approach to better themselves and learn new things about concepts, experiments, socialization, and interaction, will encourage the youth to always think of solutions. This will lead to innovations through science and technology. The involvement of the youth to these activities will not only develop their critical-thinking and science-related skills and abilities but will also create a collaborative environment among the Filipino youth.

By providing a safe and healthy environment, we allow the nourishing of the Filipino youth. Their involvement in these activities will then lead towards national well-being.
