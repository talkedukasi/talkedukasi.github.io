1. Reaction paper of san andreas fault


San Andreas Fault

San Andreas fault is situated along transform fault boundary in which two plates slides past each other. Most transform fault boundary are located under the ocean and only few were found on the continents and San Andreas Fault is one of them found in California, USA. Stronger earthquake will likely to form in transform fault boundaries. As the plate slide past each other, it creates friction which trigger the release of earthquake. You should be prepared for earthquake when you live near San Andreas fault.

To Understand more about Plate Boundary,

Here are the Three Types of Plate Boundaries

Convergent BoundaryDivergent BoundaryTransform Fault Boundary

Convergent Boundary

Occurs when two plates moves towards each other. It can be continental to continental convergence, oceanic to oceanic convergence, continental to oceanic convergence.

Divergent Boundary

Occurs when plates moves away from each other and new seafloor is created. Oceanic ridges, rift valleys are the common geological features can be found in this type of plate boundary.

Transform Fault Boundary

Occurs when the plate slide past each other. San Andreas Fault is an example to this and earthquake is one of the dangerous geological activity found in this type of plate boundary.

Since Earthquakes are common in San Andreas Fault, here are the things you should know about Earthquake.

Earthquake

Slippage or too much grinding between the two plates along the fault or the plate boundary which releases tremendous amount of energy which causes vibration or shaking on the surface of the Earth creating damages both properties and lives which is commonly known as Earthquake.

Types of Earthquake

Tectonic EarthquakeVolcanic Earthquake

Tectonic Earthquake

Tectonic earthquake is an earthquake cause by the movement of the plate which produces tremendous amount of energy that travels in all direction away from the epicenter causing vibration or shaking on the surface.

Volcanic Earthquake

Volcanic Earthquake are earthquake produce due to volcanic activity of erupting volcanoes. Volcanic activity due to the rising of magma on the surface of the earth causes vibration or shaking in surrounding areas known as volcanic earthquake.

Types of Seismic waves produce during Earthquake

Body waveSurface wave

Body waves can travel in the inner layer of the earth known as primary wave and secondary wave. Primary wave is said to be much faster to travel and it can travel in both solid and liquid medium. Secondary wave is a little bit slower compared to the primary wave and it can only travel in solid medium not in liquid. The information provided by body waves provide an important idea that some layer in the inner earth are not solid but instead liquid just like the outer core.

Surface wave on the other hand is composed of Rayleigh wave and love wave. Love wave is faster than Rayleigh wave but, it causes majority of the damage on the surface of the earthquake. Love wave moves the ground in a side to side destructive horizontal motion, like that of a snake's causing the ground to twist which is the main reason why it causes more damage compared to the rest of the waves released during earthquake. On the other hand, Rayleigh wave rolls along the ground just like an ocean wave.

Studying earthquake and its epicenter is one of the basis for the scientist in dividing the Earth's lithosphere into several segments called plates. Majority of the earthquake occur the plate boundary.

Locating the epicenter of an earthquake provides important data and details in identifying areas which are prone to it so that necessary precaution will be taken to prevent lost of life and other casualties.
