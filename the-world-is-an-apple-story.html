Answer:

In the story "The World is an Apple" by Alberto S. Florentino, there is no one to be solely blamed for the turnout of the story. Each character has contributed to the sad and tragic ending in their own way.

Explanation:

The story "The World is an Apple" portrays a situation where a family is struggling financially due to the father's decision to steal money from his employer. Mario's actions set the foundation for the tragic outcome, as they led to his loss of job and financial stability. His inability to find another job or take responsibility for his actions only worsened the situation.

Tondong, Mario's friend, also played a role in the unfortunate events. He encouraged Mario to spend the stolen money on alcohol and gambling, which further depleted their resources and prevented them from addressing their financial struggles. Tondong's actions made it difficult for Mario to see a way out of their situation, and instead, he turned to vices to cope with the stress.

Aling Atang, Mario's wife, chose to stay with him despite knowing about his actions and their family's struggles. She did not seek help or support from others, and instead, accepted their situation as inevitable. This lack of action and decision to remain passive contributed to the tragic ending.

In summary, each character's actions contributed to the sad and tragic ending of the story. Mario's decision to steal, Tondong's encouragement of vices, and Aling Atang's acceptance of their situation all contributed to the family's downfall. Therefore, no one character can be solely blamed for the turnout of the story.


29. How women portrayed in the story of The World is an Apple by Alberto Florentino?​


Women's portrayal in The World is an Apple

In "The World is an Apple" by Alberto Florentino, women are portrayed as victims of poverty and social inequality. The story revolves around a family of three: Mang Ambo, his wife, and their daughter, who are struggling to make ends meet. Mang Ambo is a former taxi driver who lost his job, while his wife takes care of their daughter and does laundry for a living.

In the story, Mang Ambo spends all their money on gambling and drinking, leaving his family with nothing to eat. His wife tries to reason with him, but he remains stubborn and selfish. The daughter, meanwhile, is forced to beg for food from their neighbor.

The portrayal of the wife and daughter in the story is one of helplessness and despair. They are powerless in the face of poverty and rely on the men in their lives to provide for them. The daughter is also shown as being innocent and vulnerable, having to beg for food due to circumstances beyond her control.

Overall, the story portrays women as victims of poverty and social inequality, highlighting the harsh realities faced by those living in marginalized communities.
