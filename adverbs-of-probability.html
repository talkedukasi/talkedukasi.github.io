24. Which is an example of a probability adverb?


Some examples of probability adverb are:

certainlydefinitelypossiblyprobably

Probability adverbs are used to indicate how sure a thing is. It gives you an idea how likely the "main verb" will happen. The given examples certainly, definitely, possibly and probably usually comes before the main verb in the sentence. There are also chances when we have an auxiliary verb and a main verb. In this cases, adverbs of probability are placed in the middle of the two verbs.

Certainly and definitely indicates a very high chance that the action is sure while possibly and probably tell us a lower chance.

Example of a probability adverb used in a sentence:

He certainly likes the girl from the neighborhood that is why he always comes out.You definitely deserve an explanation from her for breaking your heart.The team probably had a lot to discuss and finish after the weekend.Ellen possibly lost her phone on the party last night.
