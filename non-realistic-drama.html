15. What are the unique features of poetry,drama,fiction and non-fiction?


What are the unique features of poetry,drama,fiction and non-fiction?

Poetry

Poetry is a special language in that it incorporates and uses vocabulary to express meaning and articulate thoughts, emotions, sounds, gestures, signs, and symbols. It is a wisdom language since it applies human life experiences and insights to the world surrounding us. It is also a medium for thinking, emotion, and spirit wrapped in terms that are often nuanced, elusive, enigmatic, and have several layers of context. Poetry, in particular, has the potential to burst with significance. The more you read and reread a poem, the clearer this gets. Poetry can disclose certain things to a reader while still concealing certain details.

Drama

Drama, as opposed to epic (more than one narrator) and verse, is described by Aristotle as "imitation of an event by language without narrator" (one narrator). The concept of theatre is "the reproduction of an action in the medium of action." The stage directions are included in the script, so drama is often referred to as a "recipe for success." Drama, as opposed to a book or an epic poem, brings a plot or incident to life in a manner that the viewer can see rather than imagine. As a result, the term "theatre" is derived from the word "see." Drama helps the viewers to see as well as hear the drama.

Fiction

Fiction, in general, is a fictional genre of any medium that consists of fictitious characters, incidents, or places—that is, they are not based solely on history or fact. In the most restricted sense, fiction refers to written works in literature, most commonly novels, but also novellas and short stories. More generally, fantasy has come to include fictional fantasies presented in some medium, including live stage presentations, animations, television programmes, radio plays, comic books, role-playing games, and computer games.

 

Non-Fiction

Literary nonfiction is distinct in that it tells a compelling tale with a storyline, context, and characters based on true events. Rather than simply conveying details, this style of writing emphasizes tone and storytelling. Literary nonfiction is an enigmatic literary creature with many titles. Literary nonfiction is also known as narrative nonfiction or creative nonfiction. Literary nonfiction, regardless of its name, tells a tale in a unique manner. As a result, creative nonfiction authors use linguistic devices and writing conventions similar to those used in poetry and fiction, but their accounts are based on real evidence or observations.
