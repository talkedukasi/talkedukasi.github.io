6. what is sentences of grammatical signals​


Explanation:

Grammatical signals are words or phrases that indicate the relationship between different parts of a sentence. Here are some examples of sentences with grammatical signals:

1. Although it was raining, we decided to go for a walk.

2. In addition to studying for exams, I have to work part-time.

3. Despite her busy schedule, she always finds time for her hobbies.

4. Because he missed the bus, he was late for work.

5. Furthermore, the new software is much easier to use than the old version.

In each of these sentences, the grammatical signal (such as "although", "in addition to", "despite", "because", and "furthermore") helps to connect two ideas and show how they are related to each other. These signals are important for making sentences clear and easy to understand.
