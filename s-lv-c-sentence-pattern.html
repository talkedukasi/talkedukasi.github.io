1. Declarative Sentence: A declarative sentence is a statement that makes a declaration or assertion and ends with a period. Example: I am going to the store.

2. Interrogative Sentence: An interrogative sentence is a question that requires an answer and ends with a question mark. Example: Are you coming with me?

3. Imperative Sentence: An imperative sentence gives an order, command, or request and usually ends with an exclamation point or period. Example: Please bring me my coat!

4. Exclamatory Sentence: An exclamatory sentence expresses strong emotion and usually ends with an exclamation point. Example: What a beautiful day it is!

5. Optative Sentence: An optative sentence expresses wishes or hopes for the future and usually ends with either a period or an exclamation point depending on the intensity of emotion expressed in the wish/hope being expressed in the sentence.. Example: May you have many happy years ahead!

6 Compound-Complex Sentences : A compound-complex sentence contains two independent clauses joined by one of these conjunctions (for, nor, yet, so) plus one dependent clause .Example : She went to the store but she didn't buy anything because she had no money

S-LV-C stands for

S - Subject

LV -Linking Verb

C - Complement

Subject  

It is what we talk about in  sentence. It can be in a form of a nour or a pronoun. It is the doer of the action or the main verb but in the pattern S-LV-C, there is no action verb but only complement.

Linking Verb

Linking verb is a verb that links the subject and the other words in the sentence like another noun or an adjective. It does not tell an action word nor it precedes an action word. Linking verbs used are “be” verbs (am, is, are, was, were) or sensory verbs (appear, feel, look, grow) or what we call the auxilliary verbs because these are not action verbs.

Complement

It is the same thing as the subject or this is the description of the subject. Complement can also be called as modifier of the subject. In this case, the pattern asked is S-LV-C adjective so the complement in the given examples are adjectives.

In sentence number one in the given examples, “Mother is industrious”, Mother is the subject,' is' is the linking verb and industrious is the adjective complement. It is an adjective because it describes the subject. It tells something about mother so it is called a complement of the subject complement in the sentence.
