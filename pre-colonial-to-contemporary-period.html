11. Learning Tasks 4: What are the Philippine Contemporary Art Forms? Define each in your own words based on the timeline presented. PHILIPPINE ART PERIOD TIMELINE Pre-Colonial Art Period Spanish Colonial Art Period Post War Colonial Art Period American Colonial Art Period Contemporary Art Period ​


Answer:

There are five Philippine contemporary art forms: Pre-Colonial, Spanish Colonial, Post War Colonial, American Colonial, and Contemporary. Each art form has its own unique features and characteristics.

Pre-Colonial art is the earliest form of Philippine art. It is characterized by simple designs and geometric shapes. The most common pre-colonial art forms are pottery and weaving.

Spanish Colonial art is more ornate and elaborate than pre-colonial art. It is often characterized by religious imagery and themes. Common Spanish Colonial art forms include painting, sculpture, and architecture.

Post War Colonial art emerged after the Philippines gained independence from Spain in 1898. This art form is a mix of pre-colonial, Spanish colonial, and American influences. The most common post war colonial art forms are painting and sculpture.

American Colonial art emerged after the United States acquired the Philippines from Spain in 1898. This art form is characterized by realistic depictions of everyday life. Common American Colonial art forms include painting, photography, and architecture.

Contemporary art is the most recent form of Philippine art. It is characterized by abstract or experimental designs. The most common contemporary art forms are installations, performance arts, and digital arts.PA BRAINLY THANKS

What is the difference between pre-colonial literature and contemporary literature?

The Basics of the Stock Market.

The difference between pre-colonial and contemporary literature is that pre-colonial literature was written before the colonial period and contemporary literature was written in the current time.

Every piece of writing is influenced by the world in which it was written and the thoughts and beliefs of its writer. People who lived in pre-colonial times wrote their books against the background of a pre-colonial world and their thoughts and ideas were founded in that landscape.

I’m not going to make a list of absurdities about whether the writers used certain language or wrote in certain ways.

I don’t believe in the notion that writers from a certain period wrote the same way as each other and then someone flicked a switch and the writing was different.

Generalisations about literature such as this are not helpful.

Writers write about the world that they are writing in. The world changes, the topics change and writing progresses.
