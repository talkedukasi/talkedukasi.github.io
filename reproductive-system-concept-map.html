Reproductive System Concept Map

The Reproduction Concept Map is a formative assessment that aligns with Health Education Standard 6, Objective 1. The goal is for students to gain a deeper understanding of how the reproduction system works together in both male and female bodies.


3. Concept map. Create a concept map on the relationship of Reproductive, Digestive, Respiratory, and Circulatory system.


Answer:

The reproductive system is responsible for producing and delivering gametes, or sex cells, to enable fertilization and reproduction. The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients that can be absorbed and used by the body. The respiratory system brings oxygen into the body and removes carbon dioxide. The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, and hormones throughout the body and removes waste products.

The reproductive system relies on the circulatory system to deliver hormones and nutrients necessary for gamete production and transport. The digestive system provides nutrients necessary for the production of gametes and hormones. The respiratory system provides oxygen necessary for energy production and tissue repair, which is essential for the development and maintenance of reproductive organs.

The circulatory system also plays a crucial role in transporting hormones and nutrients necessary for digestion and respiration. The digestive system relies on the circulatory system to transport nutrients from the small intestine to the liver for processing and distribution. The respiratory system relies on the circulatory system to deliver oxygen to the body's tissues and remove carbon dioxide.

Overall, the reproductive, digestive, respiratory, and circulatory systems are interconnected and work together to maintain homeostasis in the body. Each system relies on the others to function properly, and any disruption in one system can affect the others.

The pituitary  gland is stimulated by estrogen to release (3) LH Luteinizing Hormone.  The Luteinizing Hormone  stimulates the ovary to produce (4)  progesterone. Increase in the amount of  progesterone (5) inhibit the production of Luteinizing Hormone.

This event happens during menstrual cycle. Note also that when LH Luteinizing hormone is at its peak, it will trigger the released of matured egg cell in the ovary going to the fallopian tube for fertilization.

27. Create a concept map on the relationship of Reproductive, Digestive, Respiratory, and Circulatory system.


Answer:

Explanation:

The reproductive system is connected to the other three systems through blood vessels and hormones. The digestive system is responsible for breaking down food and absorbing nutrients, which are transported through the circulatory system to other parts of the body, including the reproductive system. The respiratory system is responsible for bringing oxygen into the body, which is then transported by the circulatory system to the cells of the reproductive and digestive systems. The circulatory system, meanwhile, is responsible for transporting oxygen, nutrients, and hormones throughout the body, including to the reproductive and digestive systems.
