6. What is the theory of evolution


Also referred to as the Darwinian theory (as it was developed by Charles Darwin), it basically states that every single organism came from one common ancestor, and everything is related, from the birds to the bees, and the cats and the dogs. In a nutshell, as one common ancestor produced offspring, and that offspring produced more, every generation underwent some mutation, and as the beneficial mutations or properties were passed on (called "natural selection"), time went on and on until an entirely different organism was created.

7. What is theory of evolution? ​


The Theory of Evolution, which was first mentioned in Charles Darwin's book "On the Origin of Species" in 1859, holds the notion that all life forms are related and came from a common ancestor. This is the process in which organisms' physical and biological state are modified over time to allow the specie to adapt to its environment. These are the two main points of the theory. The characteristics that are altered in an organism can be a result of the expressions of genes that are passed down from parent to offspring, or can be the biological modification to suit the organism's lifestyle.

The theory of evolution by natural selection, first formulated in Darwin's book "On the Origin of Species" in 1859, is the process by which organisms change over time as a result of changes in heritable physical or behavioral traits. Changes that allow an organism to better adapt to its environment will help it survive and have more offspring.

13. what is the theory of evolution?


WHAT IS THEORY OF EVOLUTION?

    Theory of Evolution is a theory conceived by two scientists, namely Charles Darwin and Alfred Russell Wallace. The theory emphasizes the idea of Organic Evolution which is defined as the change in the genetic makeup of populations of organisms over a certain period of time.  

    It is supported by the idea of natural selection which is the idea that some individuals in a population possess variations that make them less able to survive and/or reproduce. These individuals die or fail to reproduce. Their genes are less likely to be passed into the next generation. Therefore, the genetic composition of the population in the next generation continuously changes.

MORE ABOUT THEORY OF EVOLUTION

    The idea of Evolution did not really originate with Charles Darwin. Some of the earliest references to evolutionary change are from the ancient Greeks. The philosophers Empedocles and Aristotle described concepts of change in living organisms over time. Georges-Louis Buffon spent many years studying anatomy and his observations of structural variations especially organs of related animals convinced him that change must have occurred during the history of life on earth.

  The Theory of Evolution was revived when Charles Darwin explored and spent 5 weeks on the Galapagos Island where some of his revolutionary ideas came from. By 1842, he developed the essence of his conclusions and made his famous book On the Origin of Species by Means of Natural Selection.  

    On his 5-year journey in the HMS Beagle Voyage, he was able to make his 4 more famous books tackling the mysteries and ideas of Evolution: Zoology of the Beagle Voyage, Fertilization of Orchids, The Variation of Plants and Animals under Domestication, and The Descent of Man.

18. Discribe theory of evolution


[tex]ANSWER:[/tex]

The theory of evolution, also known as Darwin's theory of evolution, is the scientific explanation of how species of living organisms change over time through natural selection. The theory is based on the idea that individuals with traits that are advantageous for survival and reproduction are more likely to pass those traits down to their offspring. Over many generations, these advantageous traits become more common in a population, leading to evolution and the development of new species. The theory of evolution was first proposed by Charles Darwin in his book "On the Origin of Species" in 1859. Since then, the theory has been extensively studied and supported by a wealth of scientific evidence from fields such as paleontology, biology, genetics, and comparative anatomy. Today, the theory of evolution is widely accepted as the explanation for the diversity of life on Earth.
