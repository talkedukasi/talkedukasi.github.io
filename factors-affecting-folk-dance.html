Answer:

1. Philippine Folk Dance is a traditional dance form that reflects the distinct cultural heritage and identity of the Filipino people. It encompasses various indigenous artistic expressions, including music, literature, and visual arts.

2. The primary objectives of folk dance are to preserve cultural heritage, promote social cohesion and solidarity, and provide entertainment for the community. Furthermore, it aims to foster national pride and identity, promote physical fitness and well-being, and enhance individuals' self-esteem.

3. The three values of folk dance are cultural identity, diversity, and creativity. It reflects the unique cultural identity of the people of the Philippines, celebrates the country's diversity and promotes creative expressions of the art form.

4. The four factors affecting folk dance are historical context, cultural diversity, socio-economic conditions, and technological advancements.

5. The four classifications of Philippine Folk Dance are tribal or ethnolinguistic dances, rural or country dances, court or palace dances, and religious or ceremonial dances.

6. The two geographical extents of origin are Luzon and Mindanao.

7. The eight nature of folk dance are communal, traditional, expressive, symbolic, celebratory, rhythmic, creative, and participatory. Examples of communal dances are Pandango sa Ilaw and Tinikling. Examples of celebratory dances are Ati-Atihan and Sinulog.

8. The four movements of folk dance are turning, hopping, leaping, and sliding.

9. The three formations of folk dance are circular, square, and straight line.

10. The three special classifications of folk dance are ancient or pre-colonial dances, fusion or modernized dances, and revival or recreation dances.

To empower and nurture traditional folk dance and ethnic dances, we need to promote and support the preservation of our cultural heritage. This can be done by organizing folk dance festivals and competitions and by providing opportunities for local dancers to showcase their talents.

Moreover, we must also integrate folk dance and ethnic dances in our school curricula and extra-curricular activities. This will expose younger generations to these traditional art forms and promote cultural awareness and understanding.

Finally, we should use technology to digitize traditional folk dance and ethnic dance performances and make them accessible to a broader audience, especially those who are unable to attend traditional performances in person. This can help promote the art form worldwide and create more opportunities for our traditional dancers and artists.
