10. Apparent Movement of the Stars though the Night Objectives Describe the apparent motion of the Stars at Night. Procedure 1. On a clear night sky,look at the stars from 7pm to 11pm. 2. Focus on one or two constellation like the Auriga and Orion which are the best seen in the month of December. 3. Look at the stars clearly every hour of the night from 7pm to 11pm. Guide Question: Q5. Compare the position of the Stars in the sky. What do you notice? Q6. Are you the stars visible at 7pm still visible at 11pm in their "Original position?" Q7. How does stars move? Describe the movement of the Stars in the night sky Q8. How is the motion of the Stars similar to the motion of the Sun?​


Answer:

When observing the stars in the night sky from 7pm to 11pm, it is noticeable that their position changes. One or two constellations, such as Auriga and Orion, can be focused on to better observe this movement. Comparing the position of the stars at 7pm to their position at 11pm, it can be seen that they are no longer in their original position, having moved across the sky. The stars appear to move in a circular motion around the North Star, with some stars even disappearing from view as they dip below the horizon. This movement is similar to the motion of the sun, which rises in the east and sets in the west due to the Earth's rotation on its axis. Therefore, the apparent movement of the stars through the night sky is a result of the Earth's rotation on its axis.

20. Identify wether the following is an effect of earth"s rotation or revolution. 1.Occurrence of the day and night. 2.Change of seasons. 3.Unequal length of day and night 4.Apparent movement of the sun. 5.Earth"s movement arounding the sun.


Answer:

1.We get day and night because the Earth spins (or rotates) on an imaginary line called its axis and different parts of the planet are facing towards the Sun or away from it. It takes 24 hours for the world to turn all the way around, and we call this a day.

2.People often think that the Earth is closer to the Sun during the summer. And it's farther away during the winter. ... The distance between the Earth and the Sun does not affect the seasons. Seasons change because of the tilt of the Earth and the planet's movement around the Sun.

3.Rotation of the Earth causes night and day to alternate. Since we learned that the Earth's axis is tilted and hence the equator is not facing the Sun directly, different places on Earth would experience unequal length of days and nights—not exactly 12 hours of day and 12 hours of night all the time.

4.The apparent motion of the sun, caused by the rotation of the Earth about its axis, changes the angle at which the direct component of light will strike the Earth. From a fixed location on Earth, the sun appears to move throughout the sky.

5.Earth spins around its axis, just as a top spins around its spindle. This spinning movement is called Earth's rotation. At the same time that the Earth spins on its axis, it also orbits, or revolves around the Sun. This movement is called revolution.
