8. Where did rizal hide his last poem?


At dawn,  of December 30, 1896, before his execution, Rizal’s mother and his sister’s visited him in his prison cell at Fort Santiago. Rizal hid the paper containing the poem in an alcohol stove which was later given to his sister Trinidad.  The poem started in a farewell statement and Rizal's pledge of a willful death.   At night before his execution, Rizal wrote his final poem on a single paper revealing his strong patriotism and untiring love for his country.  The poem was first written in Spanish and was not given any title.  

The message of the Poem

The author conveys that the Philippines is a  beautiful place and he wants Filipinos to learn from the past and see the truth about how the other country views us. Jose Rizal's "My Last Farewell" is a poem about his forthcoming death as he wanted to honor his homeland, the pre-Hispanic Philippines, for which he was sacrificing his life. Rizal also used the poem to say farewell to his friends, family and loved ones. This shows that Rizal is firm to his beliefs. The poem together with Rizal's execution flare up the Philippine Revolution.

My Last Farewell:  Reflection

Fighting for our country is like protecting for the things that are important to us love like freedom, family, friends, and peace. Dr. Jose Rizal as our national hero showed us that fighting is not only using a sword on a battlefield but we can use a pen to seek freedom. In his last poem, he shows that after the sufferings and sacrifices we can achieve freedom. These are the lessons that we can reflect in our daily life base on his poem:    

Heroism is innate in our hearts. Appreciate every moment in our life .Treasure all the people who love us and the people we love .

28. What is your reflection( introduction, body and conclusion) of the "The last Poem of Rizal"​


Answer:

Introduction: The last poem of Rizal, "Mi Ultimo Adios" or "My Last Farewell," is a poignant reflection of the life and struggles of Jose Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. This poem was written just before Rizal was executed by the Spanish colonial authorities, and it is considered one of the most powerful and moving pieces of literature in Philippine history.

Body: The poem begins with Rizal bidding farewell to his beloved homeland, the Philippines, which he calls "Pearl of the Orient." He expresses his gratitude for the beauty and richness of his country, but also acknowledges the pain and suffering of his people under the oppressive colonial rule of the Spanish. He urges his fellow Filipinos to continue the fight for freedom and independence, even in the face of great adversity and sacrifice.

Throughout the poem, Rizal shows his deep love for his country and his people, and his unwavering commitment to the cause of Philippine independence. He also expresses his hope that his death will inspire others to continue the struggle for freedom, and that his sacrifice will not be in vain.

Conclusion: "Mi Ultimo Adios" is a powerful testament to the life and legacy of Jose Rizal, and to the struggle for Philippine independence. It is a poignant reminder of the sacrifices made by Rizal and other Filipino heroes in the fight for freedom, and of the ongoing struggle for justice and equality in the Philippines and around the world. As we reflect on the life and legacy of Jose Rizal, we are reminded of the importance of standing up for what is right, even in the face of great adversity, and of the power of hope and courage in the face of oppression and injustice.
