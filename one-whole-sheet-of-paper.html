22. LEARNING TASK 2 (ONE WHOLE SHEET OF PAPER) Directions: Read the situations at the middle column. ​


1. After playing an outdoor game, you were tired and thirsty.

What I should do:

- Drink plenty of water to rehydrate.

- Take a rest to recover from fatigue.

- Stretch to avoid muscle cramps.

What I should not do:

- Drink sugary or alcoholic beverages that can dehydrate the body.

- Overexert myself and risk injury or exhaustion.

- Eat heavy meals immediately after playing.

2. Swimming class is over. You have changed your clothes and must deal with your hair.

What I should do:

- Rinse my hair with clean water to remove chlorine and other chemicals.

- Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to keep my hair healthy.

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle my hair gently.

What I should not do:

- Use harsh chemicals or hot water that can damage my hair.

- Brush my hair vigorously, which can cause breakage and split ends.

- Tie my hair tightly, which can cause hair loss and headaches.

3. You and your friends sat on a beach after a sweaty basketball game.

What I should do:

- Drink plenty of water to rehydrate.

- Take a shower or rinse off to remove sweat and sand.

- Apply sunscreen to protect my skin from the sun.

What I should not do:

- Stay too long under the sun, which can cause sunburn and skin damage.

- Leave trash or litter on the beach, which can harm the environment.

- Swim in the ocean if I am too tired or not a strong swimmer.

4. You and your brother preparing to job. Your brother is anxious because he can't find his favorite pair of socks you are about to use.

What I should do:

- Help my brother look for his socks to ease his anxiety.

- Offer to lend him another pair of socks if he cannot find his favorite ones.

- Encourage him to stay calm and positive.

What I should not do:

- Blame my brother for losing his socks, which can make him feel worse.

- Ignore his feelings and concerns, which can damage our relationship.

- Rush or pressure him, which can increase his stress and anxiety.

5. Your team won the game you shook hands with your opponents to show sportsmanship.

What I should do:

- Congratulate my opponents for playing well and showing good sportsmanship.

- Thank the referees and officials for their hard work and fair judgment.

- Celebrate with my team in a respectful and positive way.

What I should not do:

- Taunt or insult my opponents, which can create conflict and hostility.

- Argue or complain about the game's outcome, which can undermine the spirit of sportsmanship.

- Ignore the rules or show disrespect to the game and its participants.
