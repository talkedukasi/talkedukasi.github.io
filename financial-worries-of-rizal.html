30. A friend of rizal that offered financial aitance that led to the publication of el filibuterimo.


Jose Rizal's second novel, "El Filibusterismo," was published in 1891, after his first novel, "Noli Me Tangere," was published in 1887. "El Filibusterismo" is a sequel to "Noli Me Tangere" and continues the story of the Philippine Revolution and the struggles of the Philippine people against colonial oppression.

According to historical accounts, Rizal received financial assistance from several sources to fund the publication of "El Filibusterismo." One of these sources was his friend Ferdinand Blumentritt, a Czech-Austrian ethnographer and linguist who strongly supported Rizal and the Philippine Revolution. Blumentritt was instrumental in helping Rizal to publish both "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" and also played a crucial role in promoting Rizal's ideas and ideals to a broader international audience.

Other sources of financial assistance for the publication of "El Filibusterismo" included Rizal's funds, as well as contributions from other supporters and sympathizers of the Philippine Revolution. Despite his challenges and obstacles, Rizal successfully published both "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," two of the most important works of literature in Philippine history and had a significant impact on the country's struggle for independence.
