6. Differentiate the categories of computers, According to purpose, According to types of data handled and According to size or capacity. Explain Briefly (tagalog or english)​


Answer:

According to purpose:

-General purpose computers: These are computers that can be used for a variety of tasks. They can be used for word processing, spreadsheets, email, and browsing the internet, among other things.

-Special purpose computers: These are computers that are designed to perform a specific task. Examples include cash registers, industrial robots, and point-of-sale systems.

According to types of data handled:

-Analog computers: These computers process analog data, which is data that is continuous in nature. Analog data can include things like temperature, pressure, and sound.

-Digital computers: These computers process digital data, which is data that is discrete in nature. Digital data can include things like numbers, text, and images.

According to size or capacity:

-Mainframe computers: These are the largest and most powerful computers. They are typically used by large organizations for mission-critical applications.

-Midrange computers: These are smaller than mainframes but more powerful than personal computers. They are typically used by medium-sized organizations for applications such as database servers and email servers.

-Personal computers: These are the smallest and least powerful computers. They are typically used by individuals for tasks such as word processing and browsing the internet.

Ang Engkantada ng Makulot

Ang mga naninirahan sa munting bayan ng Cuenca ay maligaya, matahimik at matakutin sa diyos. Ang Cuenca ay tirahan ng mabait na prinsesang reyna ng Makulot. Siya ay mahiwaga. Ang kanyang kinalulugdang alaga ay Torong Ginto. Ang Torong Ginto, katulad din ng pangkaraniwang baka, ay malimit makitang nanginginain ng damo sa kaparangan.

Ang Torong ito ay siyang tulay ng pag-iibigan at pagmamagandang-loob ng mga mamamayan at ng prinsesa. Ang prinsesa ay napakabait at mapagkawanggawa sa mga taong dukha. Dahil sa Torong Ginto nabibigyan ng salapi ng prinsesa ang mga mahihirap na nangangailangan ng tulong upang ipagtawid-gutom.

Sila ay dapat may mabuting budhi’t malinis na asal. Ngunit kung ang mga tao’y mahilig sa pagkakasala wala silang hihintaying gantimpala sa prinsesa.

Lumakad ang mga araw at ang mga mamamaya’y nakalimot sa magandang halimbawa at malinis na pamumuhay. Dahil diya’y nawalang bigla ang Torong Ginto. Nawala rin ang prinsesa.

Sakali mang makita ang Torong Ginto, ito’y nangangahulugang magkakaroon ng gutom, salot, sakit at kung anu-anong sakuna, kaya ang mga tao ay nagprusisyon, nagdasal, nagpamisa at tumutupad ng sari-saring pangako sa kanilang mga anito.

Naging hampas na parusa ng mga anito sa mga tao ang paglabas ng Torong Ginto. Kung makita ang Torong Ginto, ito’y babalang matutuyo ang mga halaman o di kaya’y magkakaroon ng malaking baha o masamang ani.

Ang raha’t lakan ng magkakaratig na balangay ay nagkaisang tumawag ng pulong. Kanilang isinaalang-alang kung ano ang dapat gawin upang ang Torong Ginto ay huwag nang makita. Pinagkaisahan ng lahat na ang pinakamatapang at makisig na binata at subok na kawal ay ialay sa prinsesa upang maalis ang kanyang galit.

Ang kaawa-awang binata ay itinali sa puno ng kahoy upang sunugin. Di-umano’y ang usok nito ay isusubo sa Reyna ng Makulot. Anong pagpapakasakit!

Nang kakanin na ng apoy ang bagong taong ubod ng tapang ay siyang paglabas ng Torong Ginto sa yungib ng bundok. Sakay rito ang Engkantada ng Makulot. Iwinagayway ng prinsesa ang kanyang mahiwagang baston.

Ang binata ay tinangay ng hangin at naagaw sa nagngangalit na apoy. Sa isang iglap ay iniupo siya sa Torong Ginto. Ang prinsesa at binata na kapuwa sakay ng Torong Ginto ay pumasok sa yungib ng bundok. Ang Torong Ginto mula noon ay hindi na napakita.

Ang mga mamamayan naman, dahil sa takot na baka sumipot uli iyon, ay nagbago na rin at nanatiling mabubuting tao.

25. Territory is the second essential element of the state. State is a territorial unit. Define territory is it's essential component. A state cannot exist in the air at sea. It is a essentially a territorial state. The size of the territory of a state can be big or small, nevertheless, it has to be a definite well-marked portion of territory. paki explain po in Tagalog ​


Answer:

Territory, as an essential component of a state, refers to the defined and well-marked geographical area over which a state exercises its sovereignty and jurisdiction. It is a fundamental element that distinguishes a state from other forms of political entities. A state cannot exist solely in the air or at sea; it requires a physical space on land that it governs and controls.

The territory of a state provides the spatial framework within which the state's institutions, laws, and governance systems operate. It establishes the geographical boundaries within which the state exercises its authority, enforces laws, provides public services, and protects the rights and interests of its citizens. The territory represents the tangible manifestation of a state's sovereignty and defines its sphere of influence.

The size of a state's territory can vary significantly, ranging from vast nations with extensive land areas to small countries encompassing only a few square kilometers. Regardless of size, the territory must be clearly defined and demarcated through legal, historical, or geographical means. Well-defined borders, whether natural features like rivers or man-made boundaries like fences or walls, help establish and maintain the territorial integrity of a state.

In summary, territory is an essential component of a state, representing the defined and well-marked portion of land within which a state exercises its sovereignty and authority. It provides the physical space for a state's governance, jurisdiction, and the fulfillment of its obligations to its citizens. The concept of territory is crucial in understanding the nature and functioning of a state as a territorial unit.
