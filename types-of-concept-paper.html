2. what are the types of concept paper​


1. Research concept paper: A research concept paper is used to outline the research proposal for a specific project or study. It includes the research question, hypothesis, methodology, and expected outcomes.

2. Business concept paper: A business concept paper is used to outline the idea behind a new business venture. It includes information on the target market, competitive landscape, and the proposed business model.

3. Product concept paper: A product concept paper is used to outline the idea behind a new product or service. It includes information on the target market, features and benefits, and the proposed pricing strategy.

4. Event concept paper: An event concept paper is used to outline the idea behind a new event or program. It includes information on the target audience, event goals, and proposed activities.

5. Education concept paper: An education concept paper is used to outline the idea behind a new educational program or curriculum. It includes information on the target student population, learning objectives, and proposed teaching methods.

6. Social concept paper: A social concept paper is used to outline the idea behind a new social program or initiative. It includes information on the target population, program goals, and proposed activities.

Q: What is a concept paper?

Detailed Question -

I am a Masters student and for our final output for the Methods of Research subject, we are to submit a concept paper.

Asked on 30 Mar, 2019

Answer Follow this Question

1 Answer to this question

Answer:

An academic concept paper is a brief summary of a research project written by a university student who is about to conduct a certain research. It outlines the project in around 2-3 pages and its purpose is to give the department an idea of what the research is about, why the research is important, and how it will be carried out.

Typically, a concept paper contains the following elements:

1. A title which is usually in the form of a question.

2. A brief overview of the research topic, including a summary of what is already known about that topic.

3. A brief statement of the research question that the project will seek to answer. This is usally done in one or two senetences.

4. An explanation of why this research question is important and why this project is worth writing.

5. A description of how the methods that the researcher plans to use to answer the research question.
