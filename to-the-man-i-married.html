The first two quatrains mostly discuss various ways in which the physical beauty of the material world inevitably dims, fades, and/or falls short of ideal beauty at some point. Petrarchan and Shakespearean Sonnets

Shakespeare himself used the sonnet form in his own love poetry, following the guidelines established by English poets Wyatt and Surrey. Three quatrains and a couplet at the end make up this form, often known as the English or Shakespearean sonnet. The rhyming pattern follows a straightforward ABAB CDCD EFEF GG structure. The effect is similar to taking a short drive with a very fast driver: the first lines, including the first quatrain, are in low gear; the second and third quatrains accelerate sharply, and ideas and metaphors flash past; and then there is a sudden throttling-back, and one glides to a stop in the couplet. , and Shakespeare, like Petrarch, made use of structure to delve deeply into a theme in a brief work.
