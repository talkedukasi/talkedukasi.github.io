Answer:

Dyadic Communication is the form of verbal communication held face-to-face. This is the communication between two individuals related to their mutual ideas, behaviour, thoughts, perceptions, likes, dislikes etc. Dyadic communication is said to the the easiest form of verbal communication.

The different forms of dyadic communication are:  

1. Telephonic communications

2. Interviews

3. Instruction

4. Dictation

5. Face-to-Face Communication

There are three stages of dyadic communication, which are as stated below:

1. Transaction: It is when people start to communicate; it is when people tend to exchange facial expressions. They invite a talk based on mutual interests.  

2. Interaction: This stage is the core of the communication. In this, the subject matter of the talk is known/established.  

3. Termination: It is when the goal of the communication is achieved. It is the end of the communication.
