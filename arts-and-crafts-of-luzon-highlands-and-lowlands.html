1. Arts and Crafts of Luzon (Highlands and Lowlands)


The craftsmanship of people from a local area that shows their everyday life as they are handed down from one generation to another is called folk arts. The arts and crafts of the highlands and lowlands of Luzon is composed of weaving in which they used pinya cloth to be woven into tapestries, and other garments. It also includes pottery which refers to the ceramic ware made by potters or the material of which the pottery ware is made. Carving is one of the arts and crafts in Luzon too, the bulol or the "|fugao rice god" was among the carvings made by Filipinos. Our ancestors were known to wear elaborate jewelry made of gold. It is an expression of purity and simplicity, the creation of biased, subjective vision. Until today these arts and crafts are highly valued by most of the Filipinos.

29. What are the arts and crafts of luzon (highlands and lowlands)?


Folk arts refers to local craftsmen's work that depicts their way of life and is passed down from one generation to the next. The weaving used to create tapestries and other textiles, as well as other crafts, is a part of the highlands and lowlands of Luzon's arts and crafts. The term "pottery" can refer to both the ceramic items created by potters as well as the substance from which they are manufactured.

One of the arts and crafts practiced in Luzon is carving; among the carvings produced by Filipinos is the bul’ul, also known as the ""Ifugao rice god." Expensive gold jewelry was frequently worn by our forefathers. It is a creation of skewed, subjective vision, expressing simplicity and purity. The majority of Filipinos continue to place a high value on these arts and crafts.

Additionally, there are San Carlos bamboo crafts, bagoong, and delectable tapang bangus, in addition to the softly swaying Bolinao mats made of buri or raffia leaves. They are famous for their inabel or iloco cloth.
