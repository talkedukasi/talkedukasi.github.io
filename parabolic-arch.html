3. What is the height of the parabolic arch which has a span of 80 feet, if 40 feet from the center the arch is 15 feet high?​


Answer:

To solve this problem, we can use the formula for the equation of a parabolic arch:

y = a(x - h)^2 + k

where:

y is the height of the arch at a given distance x from the center

h is the horizontal distance from the center to the vertex of the parabola

k is the height of the arch at the vertex

a is a constant that determines the shape of the parabola

We can use the information given in the problem to find the values of h, k, and a, and then use the equation to find the height of the arch at the center.

First, we know that the arch has a span of 80 feet, so the distance from one end of the arch to the center is 40 feet. We also know that at a distance of 40 feet from the center, the arch is 15 feet high.

We can use this information to find k, the height of the arch at the vertex. Since the vertex is in the center of the arch, we know that at a distance of 0 feet from the center, the height of the arch is k. Therefore, we can set x = 0 and y = k, and solve for k:

15 = a(0 - h)^2 + k

k = 15 - a(h^2)

Next, we can use the fact that the arch has a span of 80 feet to find a, the constant that determines the shape of the parabola. At a distance of 40 feet from the center, the height of the arch is 15 feet, so we can set x = 40 and y = 15, and solve for a:

15 = a(40 - h)^2 + k

15 = a(40 - h)^2 + 15 - a(h^2)

0 = -a(h^2) + a(40 - h)^2

a = 15 / [(40 - h)^2 - h^2]

a = 15 / (1600 - 80h)

Now we can substitute the values we have found for k and a into the equation for the parabolic arch, and solve for the height of the arch at the center (where x = 0):

y = a(x - h)^2 + k

y = a(0 - h)^2 + k

y = ah^2 + k

y = 15h^2 + 15 - a(h^2)

y = 15h^2 + 15 - 15 / (1600 - 80h) * (h^2)

Simplifying this equation gives:

y = 15h^2 + 15 - (3h^2) / (320 - 16h)

To find the height of the arch at the center, we need to find h such that the span of the arch is 80 feet. We know that the distance from one end of the arch to the center is 40 feet, so we can set h = 40 and solve for y:

y = 15(40)^2 + 15 - (3(40)^2) / (320 - 16(40))

y = 15(1600) + 15 - (3(1600)) / (320 - 640)

y = 24 feet

Therefore, the height of the parabolic arch at the center is 24 feet.

22. precalA rectangular object 25 m wide is to pass under a parabolic arch that has a width of 32 m at the base and a height of 24 m at the center. If the vertex of the parabola is at the top of the arch, what maximum height should the rectangular object have?​


Answer:

The arch can be modeled by the vertex form of a parabola centered at the origin with the base being the x-axis.

y=a(x−h)2+k

where (h,k) is the vertex

Given the vertex (0,24) and the x-intercept (16,0) you can find the value of ‘a’

→a=y−k(x−h)2=0–24(16–0)2=−332

Now the max height is the point on the parabola where the x-value is half the width of the object.

x = 12.5

y=−332(12.5−0)2+24=9.35

The height of the object must be less than 9.35 meters
