2. What are thescientific attitudes?​


Scientific attitudes and values are exhibited by the greatest scientists of each generation and of the greatest minds in history. These attitudes and values are not, however, exclusive to those who are in the scientific field only. Even simple students and young minds can poses and exhibit these and it is a sign of one's great future ahead.

Here are just some the attitudes exhibited by fact- and research-driven individuals.

Curiosity

This is perhaps the main attitude that is present among almost all humans. We are all naturally  curious and that curiosity is what drives us to ask questions and seek satisfying answers.

Careful judgment

The patience to withhold judgment and conclusion until a proper investigation is made is what dictates the integrity of a scientist. A scientist will not jump to conclusions in his impatience to arrive at a solution.

Open-mindedness

The willingness to accept help, resources, and even an unexpected answer is something that each legitimate scientists have in common. Sometimes, wen things do not go your way, you must be open to changes.

Critical mindedness

Scientists do not look at a box from one side only. They are willing inspect all sides of the box, turn it upside-down, peek inside, and maybe even dismantle it or measure it. Leaving a possibility out without going through verification is not something that is approved of in the scientific field. One must be very critical when it comes to hypotheses and testing them out.

Objectivity

A scientist should be able to set emotions and biases aside and look at things without stereotyping or any preconceived notions. This will allow them to make balanced judgments and become credible sources of information.

Rationality

A good scientist thinks, observes, and compares. Instead of believing every word, accepting traditions and superstition, or letting emotion rule, he must look carefully into facts before arriving at a conclusion or making a decision.

Intellectual honesty

A true scientist acknowledges other people's works and gives credit where it is due. So many known scientist and inventors have been exposed throughout the years to have stolen ideas or taken credit fo things that were not originally theirs. If you are contributing to an idea, make sure to make people know who have helped or influenced you. This honesty is what gains proper respect from your peers and the people that will come after you.

12. what is the importance of scientific attitudes?


Belief based on evidence:

This would mean a person doesn’t accept/believe anything unless, backed by evidence, even when most of the world accept it.

e.g. -> The earth was Flat, it was widely accepted, without proper evidence and along came ‘radical’ thinkers who went to think otherwise.

Curious and Skeptic(to a good level):

Curious as in, an interest to know how things work and why they are that way.

e.g. Why we get waves at beaches and not at middle of sea?

Skeptic as in looking at existing belief / theories / phenomenon with an quest to find the truth and not to prove them wrong. e.g. Taking into account the existing works and measuring their merits.

Look at models of an Atom put forth by different scientists.

Different ways of arranging chemical elements, there were multiple methods proposed ultimately, Mendelev’s approach was accepted.

Patience and persistence:

More often than not, a scientific explanation is hard to find and sometimes one might go months or even years without much progress. Thus, patience and persistence become key. (Edison's Light bulb story)

Willing to explore:

Answer to an unknown, lies in the unknown, thus exploring all options and aspects becomes crucial in finding answers. Sometimes one has to look where no one ever has.

Being Open to postulates:

Something might sound crazy or even preposterous, But one has to trust in the process and results, not on how it sounds. E.g. Someone (Louis Pasteur) out of the blue said a Bacteria(anthrax bacilli) that kills other Bacteria, another man actually (Sir Alexander Fleming) synthesized antibiotics from fungi.
