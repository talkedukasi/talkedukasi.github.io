Difference between technical and tactical skillstechnical skills focus on the ability to perform specific tasks, while tactical skills focus on the ability to make strategic decisions and achieve goals in a given situation.

Technical skills refer to the ability to perform specific tasks or operate equipment using specialized knowledge, training, and experience. These skills are often hands-on and involve using tools or technology to perform tasks effectively and efficiently.

Tactical skills refer to the ability to plan, strategize, and make decisions in a particular situation to achieve a specific goal. These skills require a deep understanding of the situation and the ability to make decisions quickly based on available information. Tactical skills often involve critical thinking, problem-solving, and decision-making.

Which is more important Technical or Tactical skills and why?

It's not possible to say that one is more important than the other as the importance of technical and tactical skills depends on the specific context and goals. Technical skills are critical for performing specific tasks or operating equipment, while tactical skills are essential for making strategic decisions and achieving goals in a given situation. Both types of skills are important and complementary, and a well-rounded individual should strive to develop both to become more effective in their chosen field.

What happens when one skills is disregarded? is there's difference? Why?

In some fields, technical skills are more critical than tactical skills. For example, in a technical job such as software development, the ability to write clean, efficient code is essential for success. In this case, having strong technical skills is more important than having strong tactical skills.

However, in other fields, tactical skills may be more important than technical skills. For instance, in leadership roles, the ability to make strategic decisions, communicate effectively, and motivate teams are vital for success. In such cases, having strong tactical skills is more important than having strong technical skills.

The importance of technical and tactical skills depends on the specific context and the goals that need to be achieved. Both types of skills are important and complementary, and a well-rounded individual should strive to develop both to become more effective in their chosen field.

26. what is technical skills​


TECHNICAL SKILLS

Technical skills are abilities or knowledge that are utilized to complete practical activities in the fields of science, the arts, technology, engineering, and mathematics. Technical skills usually necessitate the use of certain tools as well as the technologies needed to employ such tools. Technical skills in finance related to traders and analysts who use charts, indicators, and patterns to generate buy and sell recommendations utilizing technical analysis processes.

Examples of technical skills

Finance

Technical skills in finance cover a wide range of areas such as computing, quantitative analysis, and numerous financial market forecasting approaches. To develop price chart analysis and model trends that look at historical data to anticipate future price movements, technical analysis necessitates a variety of mathematical skills, many of which are sophisticated.In this context, technical skills usually refer to someone who has the knowledge and expertise to fulfill the mathematical activities needed to collect historical data, create a data model set, and do directional analysis on the various outputs.

Accounting

Accounting, like finance, necessitates a set of specialized skills that go beyond fundamental math. Unlike in the past, when accounting professionals relied on paper and pen for documentation, today's accounting professionals use a range of software products, such as Microsoft Excel, to compute and analyze data. As a result, these professionals will require extensive working knowledge of a variety of software.Despite the fact that most accounting operations are computerized, accountants must be capable of comprehending and preparing financial statements (e.g., balance sheets, income statements, and statements of cash flows). These documents, which are essential to accounting, provide insight into the health of a company's or individual's portfolio.

Development of software

To expand computing capabilities, software developers must comprehend and know how to write and edit code, use complicated software, and manage hardware.Software developers, for example, must be skilled in coding, often known as software programming. There are various computer languages in which a developer can be fluent, just as there are many human languages. Coding is the process of converting human language into computer instructions for doing certain tasks.
