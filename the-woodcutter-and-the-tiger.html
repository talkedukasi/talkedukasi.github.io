3. character of woodcutter inn the tale of the woodcutter and the tiger​


Answer:

The characters in the Korean folklore "The Tale of the Woodcutter and the Tiger" are the Woodcutter, [Mamma] Tiger, Mother and the Three Baby Tigers.

The Woodcutter is a common villager who suddenly encounters a tiger. In his fear of being eaten by the tiger, he lied and made up a story that eventually made the tiger change his mind and spare his life instead.

Characteristics: Smart yet deceitful. Despite the life-threatening experience he had, he managed to think of a way to escape the danger. But although witty, lying to others is still a wrong thing to do because his actions affected the life and beliefs of the innocent baby tigers.

The [Mamma] Tiger is the wild animal whom the Woodcutter encountered. He thought that the Woodcutter's story was true and that he is his brother and they have a mother who has been looking for him for years. In the story, the Tiger begets three baby tigers.

Characteristics: Strong yet soft-hearted. The tiger is a ferocious beast and yet when told about [her] mother, her heart melted and she drew away from her true nature. She further portrays righteousness and ideals of respect as she passed on an important trait to her children: honoring their ancestors.

The Mother is the literal mother of the Woodcutter who already passed away. He is visited by his son, the Woodcutter, during Jesa, a Korean tradition where families and individuals pay honor to their ancestors in remembrance of their death anniversary.

Characteristics: Patient and loving. Her son remembered him to be someone who always prepared dinner (food) for them and patiently waited for their lost brother.

The Three Baby Tigers are the babies of the [Mamma] Tiger whom the Woodcutter has encountered. Despite the loss of their Mamma, these three baby tigers remembered the tradition their mother has taught them to do and was faithful in bringing offerings to their thought-to-be ancestor/grandmother, the mother of the Woodcutter.

Characteristics: Obedient and Respectful. Although unaware of the truth, these three baby tigers willfully obeyed the tradition their [Mamma] Tiger passed on them. 10. Plot of the tale of the woodcutter and the tiger


In this Korean folklore, a woodcutter encounters a tiger while he was walking in the woods. Afraid that he would be eaten by the wild animal, he decided to trick it. He exclaimed that the tiger must be his long lost brother and that their mother was heartbroken when the tiger supposedly left home. The mother, claimed the woodcutter, made sure that supper was waiting for the tiger in case he came home until the day she died. Touched and saddened by the story, the tiger left with tears in its eyes. From then on, on the anniversary of the death of the woodcutter's mother, he would find offerings on her grave. One year, no offering could be seen. However, three baby tigers approached the grave and gave the woodcutter their offerings, saying that their mother tiger has died. The woodcutter cries.

23. what is the lesson that you learn from the tiger in "the tale of the woodcutter and the tiger"?? ​


Answer:

The lesson that can be learned from this story is that wit and resourcefulness can sometimes be just as important as strength and power. In difficult situations, it's important to think creatively and come up with clever solutions that can help you outsmart your adversaries. Additionally, the story also illustrates the importance of honesty and integrity since the woodcutter's dishonesty almost led to a tragic outcome.

In "The Tale of the Woodcutter and the Tiger," the lesson that can be learned is the importance of honesty and keeping one's promises. The woodcutter, after being spared by the tiger, promised to offer his own flesh to the tiger in exchange for sparing his life. However, when the woodcutter was asked to fulfill his promise, he admitted that he had no intention of keeping it and was only trying to save his own life. The tiger, impressed by the woodcutter's honesty, let him go free.

This story teaches us that honesty and keeping one's promises are highly valued traits. It shows that honesty is the best policy and can earn one respect even in a difficult situation. The story also highlights the importance of being grateful and true to one's word, even in the face of adversity.
