21. Role of the governor-general in El-Filibusterismo


Explanation:

In "El Filibusterismo," the governor-general is portrayed as a corrupt and incompetent leader who is more interested in enriching himself and maintaining his power than in serving the interests of the Filipino people. His role in the novel is to represent the oppressive Spanish colonial regime that dominated the Philippines during the time period in which the story is set.

Throughout the novel, the governor-general is depicted as a puppet of the friars, who use their influence over him to suppress dissent and maintain their stranglehold on Filipino society. He is shown to be more concerned with maintaining his personal wealth and prestige than with the welfare of the people he governs, and he is willing to use violence and repression to maintain his grip on power.

Overall, the governor-general's role in "El Filibusterismo" is to symbolize the corruption and oppression of the Spanish colonial regime, and to highlight the injustices and abuses that the Filipino people were forced to endure under colonial rule.
