What truly makes a country unique? Is it the name, the flag, the currency or the territory? Or is it the people’s culture and national language?  For the Philippines, which is accurately comprised of 7,641 islands, cultures differ from one tribal group to another, as seen in their respective customs, traditions, cuisine and spoken word.

Of the 187 individual languages the country has, 183 are living—meaning they continue to be used—while fourare extinct and 175 indigenous. The figures are reflected on Ethnologue: Languages of the World, an annual reference publication that provides statistics and other information on the living languages of the world since 1951. The book further notes that while the number of living languages in the Phililppines is but a fraction of the global total 7,097, it is significant when compared by ratio to the total population.
