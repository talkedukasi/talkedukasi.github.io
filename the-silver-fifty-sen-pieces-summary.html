1. a piece of silver has a mass of 72 grams and a volume of 9cm^3 what is the density of the piece of silver​


The answer is [tex]8000 kg/m^3[/tex]


Introduction of density:

Density is a measurement of the mass of each unit volume of an object, or in short, density is density.Mathematically, density can be expressed in the formula for mass divided by volume. This formula applies to the thickness of liquids, gases, and solids. In SI units, the density of a liquid is expressed in kg/m3 or grams/liter. And the symbol most often used for density is (rho).The tools used to measure the density of solids that are regular in shape are enough to need a balance or scale (to measure the mass) and a ruler or length gauge (to measure or calculate the volume).Especially for liquids, the density can be measured using a hydrometer. A hydrometer is an instrument for measuring the thickness of a fluid directly. The hydrometer is a small long bottle with scale lines running along the bottle.

Information from the question:

Mass of object [tex]= 72[/tex] grams [tex]=72(10^-^3)[/tex] kg

Volume of object 9 [tex]cm^3[/tex] [tex]= 9 x 10^-^6[/tex] [tex]m^3[/tex]

Main question:

What is the density of the object?

How to work:

[tex]p(rho) = \frac{m}{V}[/tex]

[tex]p (rho) = \frac{72(10^-^3)}{9(10^-^6)}[/tex]

[tex]p(rho)=8,000 kg/m^3[/tex]

[tex]p(rho)=8 (10^3) kg/m^3[/tex]

The answer is [tex]8000kg/m^3[/tex]

5. Why are 24k gold, silver, and platinum pieces of jewelry not easily tarnished by sweat?


Answer

It is because oxygen does not have a strong chemical reaction against 24k silver, gold, platinum.

Explanation

A 24-karat precious metal means that the element is pure, meaning, the contents of the platinum, silver, or gold are at 100%.  Pure or 24k silver, gold, or platinum does not easily combine with oxygen, which is a very active element that causes elements like iron to rust. So, instead of rust, only a thin layer of the 24-karat metal develops a patina reaction.
