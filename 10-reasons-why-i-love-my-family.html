Explanation:

In literature, the point of view refers to the perspective from which a story is told. There are three main points of view: first person, second person, and third person.

First person point of view is when the narrator is telling the story from their own perspective using words such as "I" or "we." Example 7 uses first person point of view when the narrator says "I was amazed by its beauty, tranquillity and calmness."

Second person point of view is when the narrator is speaking directly to the reader using the pronoun "you." Example 6 uses second person point of view throughout the narrative, addressing the reader directly as "you" and describing their experience on a farm. Example 12 also uses second person point of view when the narrator addresses the reader as "you" and describes their experience of leaving their pencil case at school.

Third person point of view is when the narrator is not a character in the story and is telling the story from an outside perspective, using words like "he," "she," or "they." Examples 9, 10, and 11 all use third person point of view to describe the experiences of the characters.
