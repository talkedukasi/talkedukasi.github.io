1. "Summary the story of Servant Girl by Estrella D. Alfon"​


SUMMARY THE STORY OF SERVANT GIRL

Servant girl story tells about a housemaid. She is a beautiful and humble woman. Her fate in this story is to be physically and verbally abused by an obnoxious mistress.

Rosa fantasizes about a cochero which she names "Angel" who becomes known as Pedro. From her imagination, Pedro is a figure who is authoritative, gallant but gentle to herself and becomes her life savior from this slavery.

Her admirer, Sancho, on the other hand is rude and abusive towards her and violently hurts her. She ran from her employer's house and Sancho's rough arms. She is eager to find her "Angel" who will become known as Pedro. She imagined him to be different from other men, manlier, gentler, and her savior from her miserable life with mistress. Angel becomes the hope and embodiment of Rosa's desire to be free from slavery.

Rosa has an admirer named Sancho, one day she gets harsh and painful treatment from Sancho which makes her run away from her master's house. She hoped that a savior "angel" would come to save him.

But the angel never existed. It was just Rosa's imagination. Finally Rosa still had to accept the fate of being a miserable slave.

3. parable of the unforgiving servant, summary of the story​


SUMMARY

Answer:

One of the lord's servant owes him 10,000 on money. This man owes a truckload of money, as Jesus points out. To have this cash returned, the ruler arranges that this man be sold alongside his family and all that he claims. The worker begs the ruler, and the lord is forgiving. He really chooses to totally excuse this enormous obligation.

After that, the servant departs from the king's presence. The servant, however, runs across one of his own slaves, who owes him a hundred denarii. A hundred denarii would be equal to around 100 days of debt, which is a much lower amount than the king owed.

"Pay what you owe," the king's servant cries, grabbing his own servant by the throat. This second servant pleadings with the first, but the first is unmerciful and unforgiving. He imprisons his slave until he can repay the loan, which means that he will be imprisoned indefinitely because one cannot earn money while in prison and hence will never be able to repay the debt.

Those who witnessed the occurrence were deeply disturbed, so they went to their monarch and told him what had transpired. "You evil slave! I forgave you all that debt because you pleaded with me. Shouldn't you have had mercy on your fellow slave, as I had mercy on you?" the king asks to see the first servant.

The king then had the first servant tormented until he could repay his obligation, which he did. A person cannot earn money while being tormented, much as the second servant who was imprisoned, therefore this first servant would be tor tured indefinitely.

7. give me 1 example of the moralist of the story of servant girl by estrella alfon​


Answer:

The story "Servant Girl" by Estrella Alfon is about a young woman named Rosa who works as a servant in a wealthy household. She is mistreated by her employers and longs for a better life. She meets a man named Tony who promises to take her away from her miserable life and marry her.

However, Rosa's dreams are shattered when Tony abandons her, leaving her alone and pregnant. She is forced to return to her former employers and ask for their forgiveness and help. They offer her assistance, but the shame and humiliation of her situation weigh heavily on her.

The moral lesson of the story is that we should not be blinded by our desires and dreams to the point that we lose sight of what is truly important. Rosa's desire for a better life and escape from her circumstances led her to make choices that ultimately caused her great pain and suffering. The story serves as a cautionary tale about the consequences of pursuing our dreams without considering the possible outcomes and the people we may hurt along the way.

Story: The Servant Girl

Settings: The story is set in the early 1900s in a grand mansion in the countryside of England.

Characterization: The main character is a young servant girl named Lily. She is hardworking, kind, and has a love for reading books. Her employer, Mrs. Harrington, is a wealthy and demanding woman who treats her staff poorly. Mr. Harrington is a kind and gentle man who rarely intervenes in his wife's dealings with the staff. The other servants in the household are envious of Lily's close relationship with Mr. Harrington.

Conflict and Plot: Lily is mistreated by Mrs. Harrington, who often makes her work long hours and does not allow her to take breaks. One day, Lily discovers that Mrs. Harrington has been stealing money from Mr. Harrington's accounts. Lily is conflicted about what to do, as she is afraid of losing her job if she speaks out. However, her conscience gets the better of her, and she decides to confront Mrs. Harrington.

Emotional and Intellectual Payoff: The confrontation between Lily and Mrs. Harrington is emotional and intense. Lily stands up for herself and tells Mrs. Harrington that she knows about the theft. Mrs. Harrington is shocked and tries to deny it at first, but eventually confesses. Mr. Harrington is informed of the situation and is devastated by his wife's betrayal. He decides to let Mrs. Harrington go and offers to help Lily find a new job.

Theme: The story highlights the importance of standing up for oneself and doing what is right, even in difficult situations. It also portrays the negative consequences of greed and dishonesty.

Overall Treatment: The story is written in a simple and straightforward manner, with a focus on character development and emotional depth. The setting and time period are described vividly, adding to the overall atmosphere of the story. The conflict and resolution are satisfying, and the theme is conveyed effectively. The story provides a glimpse into the lives of servants in the early 1900s and the power dynamics that existed in households of the wealthy.

19. what is my indipendent critique of the story servant girl by estrella alfon​


Answer:

"Servant Girl" by Estrella Alfon.

One possible critique of the story is that it perpetuates gender inequality and reinforces the idea of women being subordinate to men. Rosa's character is portrayed as submissive and helpless, and her fate is determined by the men in her life. Tony, the man who promises to rescue her, ultimately betrays her, leaving her alone and vulnerable. Meanwhile, the male employers in her former household have the power to offer her forgiveness and assistance, highlighting the patriarchal nature of the society in which the story is set.

Another critique is that the story may be too simplistic and one-dimensional in its portrayal of social issues such as poverty and class differences. The characters are either rich or poor, and their interactions are primarily based on their economic status. The story does not delve deeper into the social and political structures that perpetuate poverty and inequality, nor does it provide a nuanced portrayal of the characters' motivations and struggles.

Overall, while "Servant Girl" is a compelling and emotionally charged story, it may be limited in its scope and portrayal of complex social issues.
