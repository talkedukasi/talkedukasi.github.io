Clear your cache and cookies

When your web browser loads the pages that you look at, it normally stores a copy of the files it uses to show these pages on your computer/phone. This helps speed up your browsing, because if you reload the page it doesn't have to download everything again. And also, if you go to a different page on the same website it doesn't have to re-download everything to make the page work - for example, the logo and the icons are already temporarily stored on your computer so it doesn't need to waste time and data re-downloading them.

17. patulong guys. editorial writing theme:school cafeteria food issues,food quality, and food price​


The school cafeteria is a crucial part of the school experience for many students. It serves as a place to refuel and socialize during the school day. However, in recent years, there have been growing concerns about the quality and price of the food served in school cafeterias.

One of the main issues is the quality of the food. Many students complain about the lack of healthy and fresh options, as well as the overreliance on processed and packaged foods. Students need healthy food to fuel their bodies and minds, but too often, they are given options that are high in fat, sugar, and salt. This not only affects their physical health but also their ability to concentrate and learn in the classroom.

Another issue is the price of the food. Many students come from low-income families and struggle to afford the cost of school meals. For some, the cafeteria is the only source of nutritious food they have access to during the day. However, the high cost of food can be a barrier for many students, leading to hunger and malnutrition.

It's time for schools to take action and address these food issues. The quality of food served in school cafeterias should be a top priority. Schools should provide fresh, healthy options that meet the nutritional needs of students. This can be achieved by partnering with local farmers and suppliers to source fresh produce and other healthy ingredients.

Additionally, schools should consider implementing programs that provide free or reduced-cost meals for low-income students. No student should have to go hungry or sacrifice their health because of their financial situation. By investing in programs that address food insecurity, schools can ensure that all students have access to nutritious meals.

In conclusion, the issues surrounding school cafeteria food quality and price must be addressed to ensure that students have access to healthy and affordable meals. Schools must take an active role in providing nutritious options and addressing food insecurity. It's time to prioritize the health and well-being of our students by providing them with the food they need to thrive.
