4. "Summary the story of Servant Girl by Estrella D. Alfon"​


SUMMARY THE STORY OF SERVANT GIRL

Servant girl story tells about a housemaid. She is a beautiful and humble woman. Her fate in this story is to be physically and verbally abused by an obnoxious mistress.

Rosa fantasizes about a cochero which she names "Angel" who becomes known as Pedro. From her imagination, Pedro is a figure who is authoritative, gallant but gentle to herself and becomes her life savior from this slavery.

Her admirer, Sancho, on the other hand is rude and abusive towards her and violently hurts her. She ran from her employer's house and Sancho's rough arms. She is eager to find her "Angel" who will become known as Pedro. She imagined him to be different from other men, manlier, gentler, and her savior from her miserable life with mistress. Angel becomes the hope and embodiment of Rosa's desire to be free from slavery.

Rosa has an admirer named Sancho, one day she gets harsh and painful treatment from Sancho which makes her run away from her master's house. She hoped that a savior "angel" would come to save him.

But the angel never existed. It was just Rosa's imagination. Finally Rosa still had to accept the fate of being a miserable slave.

28. Summary of greeks perspective. 750 sentence​


The ancient Greeks were a civilization that developed in Greece around the 9th century BCE. They are known for their contributions to art, philosophy, mathematics, and science. The Greeks also had a rich cultural and religious tradition, which was reflected in their literature, art, and architecture.

One of the defining features of Greek culture was their focus on reason and logic. The Greeks believed in the importance of critical thinking and the pursuit of knowledge. This approach was evident in their philosophy, which was characterized by a focus on understanding the world through logical argument and debate. The Greek philosophers, such as Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, are still considered to be some of the most influential figures in the history of Western philosophy.

The Greeks were also known for their love of art and beauty. They believed that art was an important way to express and understand the world around them. Greek art, which included sculpture, painting, and architecture, was characterized by its realism, balance, and harmony. The Greeks were also masters of storytelling and literature, and their works continue to be read and studied today.

In terms of science and mathematics, the Greeks made significant contributions to these fields as well. The Greek mathematician Pythagoras is credited with discovering the Pythagorean theorem, which is a fundamental principle of geometry. The Greek philosopher and mathematician Euclid is known for his work on geometry and his treatise "Elements," which is still considered to be a classic in the field.

The Greeks also had a rich cultural and religious tradition. They worshipped a pantheon of gods and goddesses, and their myths and legends were an important part of their culture. The Greeks also believed in the importance of physical fitness and the pursuit of excellence, which is reflected in the Olympic Games, which originated in ancient Greece.

Overall, the ancient Greeks were a civilization that valued reason, critical thinking, and the pursuit of knowledge. Their contributions to art, philosophy, mathematics, and science continue to be recognized and studied today.

30. literary devices used in the story green girl.


Answer:

If we assume, for the sake of argument, that my hypothesis of what the ending of green girl is will not turn out to be correct (that there is, indeed, an ending of green girl that doesn't turn out to be happy for one of the main characters), what literary devices might be at play to create such a powerful conclusion? A key device would be a change in structure.

The last chapter is written from a different person's perspective, giving us new information we never had before. Before that we have the entire story, so all that story is experienced by multiple people (even if they're different characters). Since the information we're seeing in green girl last chapter comes from a new person, they are a sort of 'newbie' or a younger version of one of the main characters in the book. The fact that the ending happens from their point of view as they enter into it, allows for a conclusion that doesn't fit with everything we know to have gone on in the book. The last chapter of green girl is a powerful experience, much like something in real life, as if someone were trying to tell us that even though we thought that person was okay, that they aren't really.
