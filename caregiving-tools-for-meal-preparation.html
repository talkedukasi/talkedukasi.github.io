Answer:

1. Caregiving refers to the act of providing care and support to someone who is ill, disabled, or otherwise in need of assistance. This can include activities such as providing personal care, administering medication, helping with daily activities, and providing companionship.

2. -Meal Preparation: kitchen tools such as a knife, cutting board, pots and pans, and utensils are needed.

-Cleaning, Washing, Ironing: cleaning tools such as a broom, mop, dustpan, and cleaning solutions, laundry detergent and iron.

-Taking Vital Signs: tools such as a thermometer, blood pressure cuff, stethoscope, and pulse oximeter are needed.

3. The importance of taking care of the tools, equipment, and paraphernalia in caregiving is to ensure their proper function and longevity. Proper cleaning, storage and maintenance of these tools and equipment will also help prevent the spread of infection and germs. It is also important to check for any damages or defects and to replace them promptly to avoid any hazards during care giving process.
