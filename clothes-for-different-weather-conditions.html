Answer: B. Weather is the day-to-day condition of the atmosphere while climate is the average of all-weather conditions.

Explanation: Weather refers to short-term atmospheric conditions like temperature, humidity, precipitation, and wind that occur on a day-to-day basis. Climate, on the other hand, refers to long-term patterns of temperature, precipitation, and other weather elements in a particular area.

2

Answer: C. The higher the altitude, the colder the climate.

Explanation: As we go higher in the atmosphere, the air becomes thinner, and the pressure and temperature decrease. This is known as the lapse rate, and it causes the temperature to decrease by about 6.5°C for every 1,000 meters of altitude gain. Therefore, the higher the altitude, the colder the temperature.

3

Answer: B. The amount of water vapor is not enough.

Explanation: Altitude affects climate because the higher up you go, the colder it gets due to the decrease in air pressure and temperature. However, altitude alone is not enough to determine the climate of a place. The amount of water vapor, prevailing winds, ocean currents, and other factors also play a crucial role in determining the climate of a particular area.

4

Answer: A. The air condenses as it moves to the leeward side of the mountains.

Explanation: When moist air rises over a mountain, it cools, and its water vapor condenses, forming clouds and precipitation. As the air moves down the other side of the mountain (leeward side), it warms up and dries out, causing the clouds to dissipate and resulting in a drier climate.

6

Answer: C. Global Warming.

Explanation: Global warming refers to the long-term increase in the Earth's average surface temperature due to human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes. This increase in temperature causes a series of other environmental problems, such as rising sea levels, melting of glaciers and ice caps, and more frequent and severe weather events. La Nina, on the other hand, is a climate pattern that occurs in the Pacific Ocean and results in cooler-than-average sea surface temperatures and changes in rainfall patterns.
