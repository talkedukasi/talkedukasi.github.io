6. Why is it important to use both local and foreign literature


Answer:

It is important to use both local and foreign literature in order to gain a broader understanding of a particular topic or issue. Local literature refers to works that are written by authors from the same country or region as the reader, while foreign literature refers to works written by authors from other countries or regions.

Using local literature provides insights into the cultural, social, and political context of a particular issue. It allows readers to gain a deeper understanding of the perspectives and experiences of people within their own community. Local literature can also provide valuable information about local laws, policies, and practices that may not be covered in foreign literature.

On the other hand, foreign literature can provide a different perspective on a particular issue. It can introduce readers to new ideas, theories, and practices that are not commonly known or practiced in their own community. Foreign literature can also provide a comparative analysis of the similarities and differences between different cultures and societies.

By using both local and foreign literature, readers can gain a more comprehensive understanding of a particular issue. They can also develop a more nuanced and informed perspective on different cultures and societies, which can lead to greater empathy, understanding, and collaboration.

Foreign literature in information technology includes scholarly books, articles, and journals on topics such as digital transformation, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, machine learning, and data science.
