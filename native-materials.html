Traditional Methods of Food Preservation

Food preservation is one of the most important inseparable parts of human life. To increase the shelf-life of the food products, application of various methods such as drying, storage in vinegar under acidic condition, canning, freezing, fermenting, dry salting, curing, smoking, and sealing have been suggested. Although the traditional methods of food preservation guarantee its safety, application of these methods in food systems promotes the loss of temperature sensitive compounds, denaturation of proteins, alteration of food structures, change of color and taste of the products, and formation of new undesirable substances. Thus today there is a growing interest in using nonthermal processing methods for preservation of food products.

21. Why is it important to consider a native material in making different product


Answer:

There are several reasons why it is important to consider native materials in making different products:

1. Sustainability:

Native materials are often more sustainable as they are sourced locally and do not require transportation from other areas. This reduces carbon emissions and helps to preserve natural resources.

2. Cultural significance:

Using native materials can help to preserve cultural traditions and heritage. It can also support local communities and economies by providing income and employment opportunities.

3. Environmental impact:

Using native materials can reduce the environmental impact of production as they are often biodegradable and require less energy to produce.

4. Quality and uniqueness:

Native materials can offer unique qualities and characteristics that cannot be found in other materials. They may also have a higher quality or durability, making them more desirable and valuable.

Overall, considering native materials in making different products can have a positive impact on the environment, culture, and economy.
