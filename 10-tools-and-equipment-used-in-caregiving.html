Here is a table listing ten caregiving tools, equipment, and paraphernalia along with their classification and uses:

| No. | Item | Classification | Uses |

| --- | ----------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |

| 1 | Thermometer | Diagnostic Tool | Measures body temperature to monitor health and detect fever |

| 2 | Stethoscope | Diagnostic Tool | Listens to heart, lungs, and other internal sounds for diagnosis |

| 3 | Blood pressure monitor | Diagnostic Tool | Measures blood pressure to monitor health and detect hypertension |

| 4 | Oxygen tank | Life Support | Provides supplemental oxygen to patients with respiratory distress |

| 5 | Wheelchair | Mobility Equipment | Helps patients with limited mobility to move around more easily |

| 6 | Walker | Mobility Equipment | Provides support and stability for patients with limited mobility |

| 7 | Bedpan | Hygiene Equipment | Collects bodily waste from patients who are unable to use a toilet |

| 8 | Urinal | Hygiene Equipment | Collects urine from male patients who are unable to use a toilet |

| 9 | Pill organizer | Medication Tool | Helps patients keep track of their medications and stay on schedule |

| 10 | Hoyer lift | Transfer Equipment | Transfers patients from one location to another, such as from bed to wheelchair |

Answer:

1. Some caregiving tools, equipment, or apparatus available at USIA Lost Home include walkers, mobility aids, wheelchairs, and assistance poles.

2. These caregiving tools, equipment, or apparatus can be used to provide support, stability, and safety for elderly individuals who require assistance when walking, standing, or rising from sitting positions. They can also provide assistance with transfers from bed to wheelchair and vice versa.

3. Advantages of using caregiving tools, equipment, or apparatus include improved balance, enhanced mobility, and lower risk of falls. Disadvantages may include the potential for misuse, incorrect setup, or injury if the tools are not used properly.

4. Some possible suggestions and solutions to ensure caregiving tools, equipment, or apparatus at home last longer include regular maintenance and inspection, proper storage and cleaning after each use, and observing any usage instructions that come with the tools.
