Answer:

A: Hi, how's it going? (Opening)

B: Not great, I'm really stressed out about this project. (Opening)

A: Oh no, what's wrong? (Clarifying)

B: I don't know where to start, I just feel overwhelmed. (Expressing feelings)

A: Have you tried breaking the project down into smaller tasks? (Advising)

B: No, I haven't. That's a good idea. (Accepting advice)

A: Yeah, try making a list of all the things you need to do and then prioritize them. (Advising)

B: Okay, I'll do that. Thanks for your help. (Accepting advice)

A: No problem, let me know if you need any more help. (Offering help)

B: I will, thanks. (Accepting offer)

1. Opening

2. Clarifying

3. Expressing feelings

4. Advising

5. Accepting advice

11. make a dialogue about nomination communicative strategy ​


Answer:

Sure! Here's an example dialogue about the nomination communicative strategy:

Person A: Hey, I noticed you're really good at leading team meetings. How do you manage to keep everyone engaged and on track?

Person B: Thank you for noticing! Well, one strategy I use is the nomination communicative strategy. It's quite effective in keeping the team members involved.

Person A: Oh, interesting! Could you explain how it works?

Person B: Of course! The nomination strategy involves acknowledging and recognizing individuals within the team by asking for their input or assigning them specific tasks. By doing so, it creates a sense of ownership and responsibility among team members.

Person A: That sounds great! How do you implement it during the meetings?

Person B: During our team meetings, I make it a point to ask for input from different team members by specifically nominating them to share their ideas or thoughts. This not only encourages active participation but also gives them an opportunity to contribute their expertise.

Person A: I see the benefit of that approach. Does it help in fostering collaboration among team members as well?

Person B: Absolutely! By using the nomination strategy, team members feel valued and recognized for their contributions. It promotes a collaborative environment where everyone's ideas are heard and respected. It also helps in building stronger relationships among team members.

Person A: That's fantastic! I think I'll try implementing the nomination strategy in our next team meeting. Thank you for sharing this valuable insight!

Person B: You're welcome! I'm glad I could help. I'm sure it will have a positive impact on your team dynamics. Let me know how it goes!

In this dialogue, Person B explains the nomination communicative strategy to Person A and shares how it can be applied during team meetings to enhance engagement, collaboration, and ownership among team members.
