Answer:

1. Curl-ups: The abdominal muscles (specifically the rectus abdominis) and hip flexors are being developed in curl-ups, which are a form of abdominal exercise that involves curling the upper body off the floor while keeping the lower back pressed against the ground.

2. Push-ups: The chest muscles (pectoralis major), shoulders (deltoids), and triceps are being developed in push-ups, which are a type of upper body exercise that involves pushing the body off the ground while maintaining a straight plank position.

3. Body twist: The oblique muscles (external and internal obliques) are being developed in body twists, which are exercises that involve rotating the upper body to one side or the other while keeping the lower body stable.

4. Pull-ups: The back muscles (latissimus dorsi), biceps, and forearms are being developed in pull-ups, which are an upper body exercise that involves pulling the body up towards a bar or other elevated surface using the arms and back muscles.

5. Leg-splits: The muscles of the inner thighs (adductors) and hamstrings are being stretched and developed in leg-splits, which are a flexibility exercise that involves spreading the legs apart as wide as possible while keeping the back straight.
