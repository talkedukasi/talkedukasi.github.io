1. applying 5s on your computer


Applying 5s on your computer :

5S is a system originated in Japan that teaches work or personal space organization and order for an individual to work efficiently with extreme safety precautions. By following this standard, people are become more productive in their works.

The 5S stands for: Sort Set in Order Shine Standardize Sustain

You can apply 5S on your computer area and here are some things you can do to apply this system on yourself and on your computer: Sort your files. Unused and unimportant files can be back up to reduce storage in your hard drive and make room for newer, important files. Older files can be put into external storages like CDs or USBs. Set in Order. You can order your files by creating folders for them. Segragating files in a folder make them easier to find  in the future. It is also much better to work knowing that all your files are complete and you are not misplacing any files among folders. Shine. Clean your computer parts regularly. Aside from hygiene and health reasons, cleaning your equipments prolong their lives. Standardize. To ensure that you won't leave any task or files behind, you can create a list or a schedule of when you are going to implement this again. Sustain - For other users of your desktop or laptops, make sure that you will also involve them, especially your family members and colleagues to ensure that nobody is left behind.

26. As a student, how can you apply the 5S principle to your computer?need ko po thankss​


Answer:

The 5S principle is a lean manufacturing methodology that aims to improve workplace organization and efficiency. It consists of five steps: Sort, Set in Order, Shine, Standardize, and Sustain. While it was originally designed for physical spaces, it can also be applied to digital environments like computers. Here are some ways that students can apply the 5S principle to their computer:

Sort: The first step is to sort through all the files and folders on your computer and get rid of anything that you don't need. This could include old assignments, duplicates, or files that are no longer relevant. Be sure to empty your trash or recycle bin afterward.

Set in Order: Once you have gotten rid of unnecessary files, organize the remaining files and folders in a logical way. Create a folder structure that makes sense to you and label folders with clear and concise names. This will make it easier to find what you need later on.

Shine: Keeping your computer clean and tidy is an important part of the 5S principle. This means regularly cleaning your computer of any dust or debris and making sure your files are organized and up-to-date.

Standardize: To maintain your organized computer, it's important to establish a set of standards or guidelines for file organization. This could include naming conventions, folder structures, or file types. Consistency is key to keeping your computer organized.

Sustain: Finally, it's important to sustain the 5S principle by regularly reviewing and maintaining your computer organization system. Make a habit of regularly deleting files that are no longer needed, and updating your folder structure as needed. This will help you maintain a clean and organized computer over the long term.

By applying the 5S principle to your computer, you can improve your productivity and make it easier to find the files and information you need.
