Answer:

Exposition: Once upon a time, in a land far away, there lived a girl named Cinderella. She lived with her evil step- mother and two evil step-sisters. Every day, the evil step-mother made Cinderella work all day long.

Rising Action: One day, an invitation to a ball was sent to all the young maidens of the kingdom, but the evil step- mother would not allow Cinderella to go to the ball and locked her in her room. Cinderella thought all hope was lost until her fairy godmother appeared. Her fairy godmother brought a very beautiful dress for Cinderella by magic. So she able to attended the ball. Cinderella meet Prince Charming, he fell in love with her.

Conflict: The rule of going to party is she have to go back home before 12 or else all dress will disappear. As the clock struck 12, Cinderella ran away as fast as she could. Although Prince Charming looked for her, all he found was a glass slipper.

Climax: Prince Charming decides to use this glass slipper to find his love. So after the party day Prince went out around the village and let every woman in the village fit this glass shoe. Finally he came to Cinderella’s house Cinderella held her breath as Prince Charming gently slipped the glass slipper onto her foot. As a destiny between the two soul mate, she fitted the shoe perfectly.

Resolution: Prince Charming took Cinderella by the hand and made her his bride. Cinderella and Prince Charming lived happily ever after.

Prince Charming

Settings:

Cinderella's home with her step-family

The royal palace where the ball is held

Conflict:

The conflict in Cinderella is primarily between Cinderella and her step-family. They treat her poorly and make her do all the chores in the house, which prevents her from living a happy life. This conflict is resolved when Cinderella goes to the ball and meets Prince Charming, who falls in love with her and saves her from her miserable life.

Plot:

The plot of Cinderella revolves around the life of Cinderella, a young woman who is mistreated by her step-family. She receives help from her fairy godmother, who transforms her into a beautiful princess so she can attend a royal ball. At the ball, Cinderella meets Prince Charming and falls in love with him. However, she must leave the ball before midnight, and the only thing she leaves behind is a glass slipper. The Prince searches for the owner of the glass slipper, and eventually, he finds Cinderella and they live happily ever after.

Point of view:

The point of view in Cinderella is third-person limited. The story is told from an outside perspective, but the narrator focuses mainly on Cinderella's thoughts and feelings.

Theme:

The theme of Cinderella is the triumph of good over evil, and the power of true love. The story teaches us that no matter how difficult life may seem, kindness, hard work, and a good heart will eventually lead to a happy ending. Additionally, the story highlights the importance of inner beauty, and how it can shine through even in the darkest of situations.

29. COMPARE THE STORY OF CINDERELLA AND SNOW WHITETHEIR THEMETHEIR CONFLICTTHEIR PLOTTHEIR SETTINGTHEIR CHARACTE​


The story of Cinderella and Snow White are both classic fairy tales that have been retold many times in different cultures. Both stories are about young women who are mistreated by their stepmothers and go on to find happiness and fulfillment with the help of magical beings.

The theme of both stories is similar in that they both revolve around the idea of inner beauty and the triumph of good over evil. Cinderella and Snow White are both kind, gentle, and pure of heart, and they are ultimately rewarded for their goodness.

The main conflict in both stories is between the young women and their stepmothers. In Cinderella, the stepmother is cruel and jealous, and she mistreats Cinderella and tries to keep her from attending the royal ball. In Snow White, the stepmother is also jealous and evil, and she plots to kill Snow White out of fear that she will be more beautiful than her.

The plot of both stories follows a similar pattern, with the young women facing hardships and challenges, but ultimately triumphing and finding happiness. In Cinderella, she is forced to do all the household chores and is prevented from going to the ball, but with the help of her fairy godmother, she is able to attend and catch the eye of the prince.
