24. NAME OF THE INVENTOR/ DEVELOPER 1. CHARLES BABBAGE 2. TOMAS HAROLD FLOWERS 3. JOHN ADAM PRESPER ECKERT JR. / JOHN WILLIAM MAUCHLY 4. HENRY EDWARD "ED ROBERTS 5. ADA LOVELACE 6. PER GEORGE 7. 8. KONRAD ZUSE 9. ATANASOFF AND CLIFFORD BERRY HERMAN HOLLERITH COUNTRY (DATE BORN-DATE DIED) INVENTIONS DESCRIPTION YEAR INVENTED ​


Answer:

CHARLES BABBAGE (26 December 1791 – 18 October 1871) - He was an English mathematician and inventor who is credited with designing the first mechanical computer called the "Difference Engine" and the analytical engine. He also developed the concept of a universal machine that could perform any computation that could be represented symbolically. However, Babbage's machines were not completed during his lifetime. - Year Invented: 1822 (Difference Engine).

TOMAS HAROLD FLOWERS (22 December 1905 – 28 October 1998) - He was an English engineer who is credited with inventing the world's first electronic and programmable computer called "Colossus" during World War II. Colossus was used to crack the German Lorenz cipher and was a crucial factor in the Allies' victory. - Year Invented: 1943-1944.

JOHN ADAM PRESPER ECKERT JR. (9 April 1919 – 3 June 1995) / JOHN WILLIAM MAUCHLY (30 August 1907 – 8 January 1980) - They were American engineers who are credited with developing the first general-purpose electronic computer called "ENIAC" (Electronic Numerical Integrator and Computer). ENIAC was used for ballistics calculations during World War II. - Year Invented: 1946.

HENRY EDWARD "ED" ROBERTS (13 September 1941 – 1 April 2010) - He was an American engineer and entrepreneur who is credited with developing the first commercially successful personal computer called "Altair 8800" in the mid-1970s. Altair 8800 was sold as a kit and was based on the Intel 8080 microprocessor. - Year Invented: 1974.

ADA LOVELACE (10 December 1815 – 27 November 1852) - She was an English mathematician and writer who is credited with being the world's first computer programmer. She wrote an algorithm for Charles Babbage's analytical engine that could calculate Bernoulli numbers. - Year Invented: 1843.

PER GEORGE (born 23 April 1926) - He was a Swedish engineer who is credited with inventing the computer mouse in the early 1960s while working at the Stanford Research Institute (SRI). The mouse was later popularized by Apple Inc. - Year Invented: 1963.

-

KONRAD ZUSE (22 June 1910 – 18 December 1995) - He was a German engineer who is credited with inventing the world's first programmable computer called the "Z3" in 1941. The Z3 was used for scientific calculations and was destroyed during World War II. Zuse also developed the first high-level programming language called "Plankalkül". - Year Invented: 1941.

JOHN VINCENT ATANASOFF (4 October 1903 – 15 June 1995) and CLIFFORD BERRY (18 April 1918 – 30 September 1963) - They were American inventors who developed the first electronic digital computer called the "Atanasoff-Berry Computer" (ABC) in the late 1930s and early 1940s. The ABC used binary digits and was designed to solve systems of simultaneous linear equations. - Year Invented: 1937-1942.

HERMAN HOLLERITH (29 February 1860 – 17 November 1929) - He was an American inventor who is credited with developing the first electromechanical tabulating machine

27. What are the elements of "Kipping ( Pahiyas ) "​


Answer:

Every May 15, the townsfolk of Lucban, Quezon, don their houses with fruits, vegetables and colorful kiping (a leaf-shaped wafer made of rice and dyed with food coloring) in celebration of the Pahiyas Festival.
