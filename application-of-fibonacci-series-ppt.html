23. Define the following 1. Sequence 2. Terms 3. General Term or nth term 4. Series 5. Arithmetic Sequence 6. Arithmetic Mean 7. Arithmetic Series 8. Geometric Sequence 9. Geometric Mean 10. Geometric Series 11. Harmonic Sequence 12. Fibonacci Sequence​


Answer:

1. A sequence is an ordered list of numbers (or other elements like geometric objects), that often follow a specific pattern or function. Sequences can be both finite and infinite.

2. A term is a single mathematical expression.

3. : a mathematical expression composed of variables and constants that yields the successive terms of a sequence or series when integers are substituted for one of the variables often denoted by k.

4. a series is the cumulative sum of a given sequence of terms.

5. An arithmetic sequence is a sequence where each term increases by adding/subtracting some constant k.

6. The arithmetic mean is the simplest and most widely used measure of a mean, or average.

7. An arithmetic series is the sum of a sequence.

8. A geometric sequence is an ordered list of numbers in which each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous one by a constant called r , the common ratio.

9. Geometric Mean That means you multiply a bunch of numbers together, and then take the nth root, where n is the number of values you just multiplied

10. Geometric series, in mathematics, an infinite series of the form a + ar + ar2 + ar3+⋯, where r is known as the common ratio.

11. Harmonic sequence, in mathematics, a sequence of numbers a1, a2, a3,… such that their reciprocals 1/a1, 1/a2, 1/a3,… form an arithmetic sequence (numbers separated by a common difference).

12. The Fibonacci sequence is a set of numbers that starts with a one or a zero, followed by a one, and proceeds based on the rule that each number (called a Fibonacci number) is equal to the sum of the preceding two numbers.

• Fibonacci series is such a series, in which the next term is sum of the previous two terms.

For example: 1,2,3,5,8,...
