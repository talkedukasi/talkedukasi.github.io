4. real life salary scheme of IT graduates in geometric sequence.


Example: 

Ten new graduates of IT are employed by the leading telecommunication company in January 2016.  Their employment contract has a provision that after 6 months if they meet the requirements and performance level expected from them, they will be become regular employees and enjoy the perks and benefits given to regular employees.  

Also after six months and every six succeeding months, their basic salary will be increased by 1.10 times. 

So if x is the basic salary upon employment, it is the first term, a₁.  What will be the salary after 6 months?  after 12 months?

Let's say the basic salary upon employment is Php 10,000.

a₁ =salary upon employment, Php10,000
a₂ = salary after six months
a₃ = salary after 12 months
d = 1.10

General Formula:  [tex] a_{n} [/tex] = a₁[tex] (r)^{n-1} [/tex]

After 6 months, n=2:
a₂ = 10,000 [tex](1.1)^{2-1} [/tex]
a₂ = 10,000 (1.10)¹
a₂ = 11,000  (Salary after 6 months)

After 12 months, n=3:
a₃ = 10,000 (1.1)³⁻¹
a₃ = 10,000 (1.10)²
a₃ = 10,000 (1.21)
a₃ =  12,100  (salary after 12 months)

To write the salaries in sequence:

10,000,   11,000,   12,100

28. What is the importance of patterns in making a salary scheme? Arithmetic Sequence or Geometric sequence ​


The importance of patterns arithmetic sequence or geometric sequence ​in making a salary scheme

Both arithmetic and geometric sequences are important in making a salary scheme. An arithmetic sequence can be used to determine how much someone will earn over time if they receive regular raises. A geometric sequence can be used to determine how much someone will earn if their salary doubles every year.

An arithmetic sequence is a list of numbers in which each number is the previous number plus a constant. For example, the sequences 4, 7, 10, 13, 16 is an arithmetic sequence because each number is 4 more than the previous number.

A geometric sequence is a list of numbers in which each number is the previous number multiplied by a constant. For example, the sequences 2, 6, 18, 54 is a geometric sequence because each number is 3 times the previous number.
