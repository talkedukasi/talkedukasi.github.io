18. as a student, describe how you communicate with your teachera, classmates, friends, and family members through social media.​


The Role of Social Media in Communication  

Social networks have dramatically altered human connectivity, serving as a commonplace means of creating and sharing information with the rest of the world in a clear, simultaneous manner. Despite the fact that social networks were not designed with academics in mind, they are increasingly being used as a medium of communicating between teachers and students, making them an incredibly valuable component of the teaching-learning process by providing new opportunities for communication and engagement as well as building new learning spaces.  

Before you create a social media profile, article, or subject, think about your goals.

You should ask yourself the following questions:  

What are your goals for using social media? On the page/post, what kind of details do you want to share or receive? Will the social media account be read and commented on by anyone? Who are you attempting to persuade? How are you going to find them and entice them to join your network?   How you should act and communicate in social mediaSensitive records, such as student identification numbers, staff identification numbers, Social Security numbers, personal addresses or phone numbers, or driver's license numbers, should not be shared on social media pages.  Social networking sites will share photos of public activities, but they must be appropriate.  Respect your audience, your coworkers, your coaches, and everybody else. Take caution not to partake in any behavior that may be considered inappropriate in the office or at school.  Be sure you're giving truthful facts to avoid misrepresenting the classroom, workplace, or district.  Remember that what you post will have ramifications, so think carefully about what you share. If you're going to write something that makes you feel a little uneasy, go over these instructions and decide whether or not to post it.
