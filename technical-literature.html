Appendix

Appendices contain material that is too detailed to include in the main report, such as long mathematical derivations or calculations, detailed technical drawings, or tables of raw data. The content should be summarised and referred to at the appropriate point in the the body of the report. The conventions for appendices are as follows:

•each appendix must be labelled with a number (or letter) and title

•the appendix numbers and titles must be listed on the Contents page under the heading Appendices (if more than one) or Appendix (if only one)

•each appendix must be referred to by number (or letter) at the relevant point in the text.

Depression
Technical definition: A mood disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest or pleasure in activities that were once enjoyed. It may also cause physical symptoms such as fatigue, insomnia, and changes in appetite and weight.
Source: American Psychiatric Association (APA)

Stress
Technical definition: A physiological and psychological response to a real or perceived threat, challenge, or change that requires an adaptive response. It can be acute or chronic and can manifest in physical, cognitive, emotional, and behavioral symptoms.
Source: National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH)
Operational definition: Measurable changes in heart rate, blood pressure, cortisol levels, and subjective reports of anxiety or tension in response to a stressor.

Quarantine
Technical definition: A public health measure to separate and restrict the movement of individuals who have been exposed to a contagious disease, to prevent the spread of the disease to others.
Source: World Health Organization (WHO)
Operational definition: A mandatory or voluntary period of isolation for a person who has been in close contact with an infected individual or has traveled from a high-risk area, to limit exposure and reduce the risk of transmission.

COVID-19
Technical definition: A highly contagious and potentially severe respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It primarily spreads through respiratory droplets and close contact, and can cause a range of symptoms from mild to severe, including fever, cough, and difficulty breathing.
Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)
Operational definition: Diagnosis of COVID-19 based on laboratory tests or clinical symptoms and signs, as defined by national or international public health authorities.

Blended Learning
Technical definition: A pedagogical approach that combines traditional face-to-face classroom instruction with online or digital learning methods, such as video lectures, interactive simulations, and virtual discussions. It seeks to provide a more flexible and personalized learning experience that enhances student engagement and achievement.
Source: International Association for K-12 Online Learning (iNACOL)
Operational definition: A course or program that incorporates both in-person and online learning activities, as specified by the course syllabus or institutional guidelines.

19. operational. technical definition definition source 1. research2. data3. treatment4.hypothesis5. method6. interview7. sample8. participants9. literature10. objectives​


1. Research:

- Operational Definition: The systematic investigation or inquiry into a particular subject or problem to gain knowledge or find answers.

- Technical Definition (source: Oxford Languages): The systematic investigation and study of materials, sources, etc., to establish facts and reach new conclusions.

2. Data:

- Operational Definition: Information or facts that are collected, analyzed, and used as evidence for research or analysis.

- Technical Definition (source: Merriam-Webster): Facts or information used usually to calculate, analyze, or plan something.

3. Treatment:

- Operational Definition: The application of specific procedures, therapies, or interventions to address a particular condition or problem.

- Technical Definition (source: Cambridge Dictionary): The use of drugs, exercises, etc., to cure a person of an illness or injury.

4. Hypothesis:

- Operational Definition: A proposed explanation or prediction based on limited evidence, which is further tested and analyzed in research.

- Technical Definition (source: Oxford Languages): A supposition or proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation.

5. Method:

- Operational Definition: The systematic approach or procedure used to conduct research, collect data, or achieve specific objectives.

- Technical Definition (source: Merriam-Webster): A procedure or process for attaining an object.

6. Interview:

- Operational Definition: A structured or guided conversation between an interviewer and interviewee to gather information, opinions, or insights.

- Technical Definition (source: Cambridge Dictionary): A meeting in which someone is asked questions to see if they are suitable for a particular job or course.

7. Sample:

- Operational Definition: A representative subset or group of individuals, items, or data selected from a larger population for research or analysis.

- Technical Definition (source: Oxford Languages): A small part or quantity intended to show what the whole is like.

8. Participants:

- Operational Definition: Individuals or subjects who voluntarily take part in research, experiments, or studies.

- Technical Definition (source: Merriam-Webster): One that participates, shares, or takes part in something.

9. Literature:

- Operational Definition: Written works, including books, articles, or scholarly publications, related to a specific topic or field of study.

- Technical Definition (source: Cambridge Dictionary): Written artistic works, especially those with a high and lasting artistic value.

10. Objectives:

- Operational Definition: Specific goals or outcomes that a researcher aims to achieve through research or a project.

- Technical Definition (source: Oxford Languages): A thing aimed at or sought; a goal.

26. is Technical Livehood Education and Technical Vocal Education the same?​


Answer:

No, Technical Livelihood Education and Technical Vocal Education are not the same.

Explanation:

Technical Livelihood Education typically refers to vocational or skills-based training that focuses on developing practical skills for a specific trade, profession, or occupation. It may include training in areas such as automotive repair, welding, plumbing, electrical work, culinary arts, and other trade-specific skills that prepare individuals for employment or self-employment in a particular industry.

On the other hand, Technical Vocal Education refers to training related to singing or vocal performance. It may include instruction on techniques for singing, vocal exercises, music theory, stage presence, and other aspects of vocal performance. This type of education is often geared towards aspiring singers, performers, or musicians who want to develop their vocal skills for performance, recording, or other music-related endeavors.

In summary, Technical Livelihood Education focuses on vocational skills for specific trades or professions, while Technical Vocal Education is specific to vocal performance and singing skills. They are distinct and separate areas of education with different focuses and goals.
