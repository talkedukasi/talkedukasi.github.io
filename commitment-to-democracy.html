3. ACTIVITY I: Define the following:Forms of Democracy1. Representative democracy2. Direct DemocracyTypes of Democracy3. Classical Democracy4. Liberal Democracy5. Social Democracy6. Radical DemocracyElements of Democracy7. Competition for Power8. Participation in Civic and Political Life9. Respect for Individual Rights10.Rule of Lawpa Sagot po Ng maayos ​


Answer:

Explanation:

Forms of Democracy:

1. Representative democracy - a system of government where citizens elect representatives to make decisions on their behalf.

2. Direct Democracy - a system of government where citizens directly participate in decision-making.

Types of Democracy:

3. Classical Democracy - a type of democracy that originated in ancient Greece, where all citizens have an equal say in government decisions.

4. Liberal Democracy - a form of democracy that emphasizes individual freedoms, human rights, and the rule of law.

5. Social Democracy - a form of democracy that aims to create a balance between individual freedoms and social welfare through government intervention.

6. Radical Democracy - a form of democracy that seeks to challenge and change the existing political and social structures.

Elements of Democracy:

7. Competition for Power - a system of government where political power is contested through free and fair elections.

8. Participation in Civic and Political Life - a system of government where citizens have the right to participate in decision-making and express their views freely.

9. Respect for Individual Rights - a system of government that protects individual rights such as freedom of speech, religion, and assembly.

10. Rule of Law - a system of government where laws are applied equally to all citizens, and nobody is above the law.

Participatory democracy or participative democracy is a model of democracy in which citizens are provided power to make political decisions. Etymological roots of democracy (Greek demos and kratos) imply that the people are in power, making all democracies participatory to some degree. However, participatory democracy tends to advocate greater citizen participation and more direct representation than traditional representative democracy. Direct Democracy:In a direct democracy people vote on policies directly such as the Brexit referendum where people get to directly influence the outcome of the result.

Representative Democracy:

Whomever gets the majority vote has a mandate to carry out his policies. In this system the voter does not have direct control over the outcome. e.g. in the UK we have Members of Parliament who represent a constituency in the government which is a area of the country and voices its issues and opinions on behalf to Parliament.
