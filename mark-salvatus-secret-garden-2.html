Answer:

Felix Bacolor's "Waiting" (2012) is a mixed media artwork that incorporates a variety of materials, including paper, acrylic paint, ink, and charcoal. The piece features a black and white portrait of a person, with the face and upper body depicted in intricate detail while the lower half is left unfinished, suggesting a sense of incompleteness or anticipation. The use of mixed media allows Bacolor to create a layered and textured effect, with subtle variations in color and texture that give the piece a sense of depth and complexity. The deliberate choice to leave the lower half of the portrait unfinished, combined with the title "Waiting," suggests a sense of longing or anticipation on the part of the subject, while also inviting the viewer to fill in the gaps and create their own interpretation of the piece. Overall, the use of mixed media in "Waiting" speaks to Bacolor's skill as an artist and his ability to create evocative and thought-provoking works that invite reflection and interpretation.

23. Acknowledgement of the secret garden​


Answer:

The Secret Garden is a novel by Frances Hodgson Burnett first published in book form in 1911, after serialization in The American Magazine (November 1910 – August 1911). Set in England, it is one of Burnett's most popular novels and seen as a classic of English children's literature. Several stage and film adaptations have been made. The American edition was published by the Frederick A. Stokes Company with illustrations by Maria Louise Kirk (signed as M. L.
