1. 19. There are some children in the botanical garden. 1/3 of them are from Makati City. 0.5 of them are from Quezon City and the rest are from Manila City. If there are 30 children from Manila City How many children are there altogether?​


Let's use the information given to determine the number of children from Makati City and Quezon City.

Let's assume that the total number of children is represented by the variable "x".

From the given information, we know that:

1/3 of the children are from Makati City, which means there are (1/3)x children from Makati.

0.5 of the children are from Quezon City, which means there are (0.5)x children from Quezon.

The rest of the children are from Manila City, which means there are 30 children from Manila.

To find the value of "x", we can set up an equation:

(1/3)x + (0.5)x + 30 = x

Simplifying the equation, we get:

(0.83)x = 30

Dividing both sides by 0.83, we get:

x = 36.14 (rounded to two decimal places)

Therefore, there are approximately 36 children altogether in the botanical garden.
