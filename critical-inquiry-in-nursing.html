21. Reflect _______ upon their own instructional practices. a. Analytical b. Inquiry c. Critically d. Research


The correct answer is C. critically as  teachers or aspiring teachers should be able to reflect critically upon their instructional practices. This means that they should be able to think for themselves of what went well and what could be done better. To a certain degree they must be able to find out how they can improve their teaching methods. If they are not able to identify what is wrong and what is right in their teaching methodologies, it could be that their critical thinking skills had not developed yet-this is an important skill for a teacher. In instances where a teacher thinks nothing is wrong, they can seek other means of feedback such as feedback from their students via a survey form or co-teachers.

Receiving feedback, thinking of how it applied to you situation, making the necessary changes and then applying them are all part of teacher development. As a teacher, being able to teach and pass on the skill and knowledge is important. Equally important is being able to give and receive feedback from others.
