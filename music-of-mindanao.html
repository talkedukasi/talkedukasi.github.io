22. musical content of the music of mindanao​


Answer:

Hello

Explanation:

Kulintang - This is a traditional ensemble that includes a set of small, horizontally laid gongs, played with wooden or bamboo beaters. The kulintang is commonly used in various ceremonies and events, such as weddings, funerals, and other important occasions.

Agung - The agung is a set of large, vertically hung gongs played with padded sticks. The agung is commonly used in indigenous music and is usually played as part of a kulintang ensemble.

Kudyapi - The kudyapi is a stringed instrument, similar to a guitar, that is played by plucking the strings. It is commonly used in the T'boli and Manobo communities.

Singkil - This is a dance that originated from the Maranao people of Mindanao. It involves the use of bamboo poles, where the dancers move gracefully through the poles, which are struck together rhythmically.

Dabakan - The dabakan is a large, single-headed drum that is played with the hands. It is commonly used in indigenous music and is often played alongside the kulintang and agung.

Diwdiw - The diwdiw is a bamboo zither that is played by plucking the strings. It is commonly used in the music of the Bagobo people.

Tagunggo - The tagunggo is a type of bamboo flute that is played by blowing into the instrument. It is commonly used in the music of the Subanen people.

Overall, the music of Mindanao is rich in tradition and cultural significance. Its diverse musical content reflects the island's unique cultural heritage and history.
