A Martian Sends a Postcard Home

As the title indicates, this poem is written from the perspective of a Martian that visits Earth. In the poem, the Martian tries to explain the phenomena it sees on Earth, such as books, toilets, and rain, all discussed in a postcard home to its planet. 13. Things that are describe in the Poem A martian send a postcard home


A Martian sends a postcard home

The poem  A Martian sends a postcard home described the following things:

Books Phones Cars Sleeping people Wristwatch Mist Rain Bathroom

The poem was about a Martian who visits the Earth and notices different things that are truly new to him and his senses. It is as if he was purely innocent of how things exist but knows how it works. He mostly described things that are both natural and some are technological inventions that we acquired for some time.

Books- the Martian called them Caxtons like the inventor of the printing press William Caxton or mechanical birds. It has a lot of wings which are the pages and he didn’t saw it even once fly as he expected a bird would do. Phones- he described it as a ghost or a haunted apparatus that needs soothing of our voice which actually represents us picking it up and answering a call when it rings. And we also use it or wake it up by calling someone else as the conversation starter.  Cars- he called it Model T which is locked on the inside and requires a key for it to starts moving across roads or places. Like when a car needs ignition, and the mirrors attached on the car was described as a film to watch whenever you miss something along the way. Sleeping People- when the night falls and the sky is darker people come to their beds and sleep. There he described it as going into pairs when the colors die. He also mentioned “us” reading ourselves since our eye moves even when we sleep as if we were reading. Wristwatch-he described it as time tied to our wrist, which makes it more accessible than carrying huge clocks to tell the time. Mist- he described it as when the sky gets tired it rest on the ground and makes the world less colorful and hazy. Rain-he described it as television with fewer colors present and displays grainy images much darker than usual. Bathroom- a punishment room where adults go to suffer alone, he also described that only the young are allowed to suffer out in the open. It is the same as doing business in the bathroom and locking the door to get some peace. He also said something about every people’s pain smells different.

About the Poet

It is wrtitten by Craig Raine a british poet that was born in the UK in Dec 3 1944. Unlike other famous poets, he is still living today at the age of 75.

About the Poem

A Martian Sends a Postcard Home is a 34 lined, un-rhymed couplets poem.

This is a poem that puts the reader in the perception of a martian about earth in all aspects (natural phenomenon, how the people lives on earth.

Moral of the Poem

The Poem Although full of Figures of Speech, is a really entertaining piece. As what was said above, It puts the readers in the point of view of the Martian.

We can compare it to us when we are unfamiliar or not used to the environment. We describe things based on how we see it. Example when we visit another country, there are different cultures and traditions that are interesting in our eyes. A lot of those things are new to us that's why we describe it in a way that we are familiar.  

In the poem, with us as the Martian we see things that we are used to but in a different perception. The Poem gives us a refreshing illustration of how life is on Earth in the perception of an alien from the planet Mars.
