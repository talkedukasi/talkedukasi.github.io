17. Why qualitative research is instrumental for positive societal changes?EXPLAIN​


Answer:

Qualitative research is instrumental for positive societal changes because it provides an in-depth understanding of people's experiences, perspectives, and behaviors. It aims to explore and interpret the subjective aspects of human life, including emotions, values, beliefs, and attitudes, that cannot be quantified or measured through numerical data. By understanding these subjective aspects, qualitative research can provide insights that can inform and guide interventions and policies that can create positive societal changes.

Qualitative research is particularly useful in addressing complex social issues such as poverty, inequality, discrimination, and social exclusion. These issues involve multiple factors that are often interrelated and context-dependent, and cannot be adequately understood through statistical data alone. Qualitative research can provide a deeper understanding of the lived experiences of marginalized and disadvantaged groups, and shed light on the social, economic, cultural, and political factors that contribute to their marginalization.

Moreover, qualitative research can promote social justice and empower marginalized communities by giving them a voice and a platform to express their views and opinions. It can highlight their unique perspectives and experiences, challenge stereotypes and misconceptions, and raise awareness about their struggles and aspirations. Qualitative research can also facilitate community participation and engagement, and foster collaboration between researchers, policymakers, and community stakeholders in identifying and addressing social issues.

In conclusion, qualitative research is instrumental for positive societal changes because it provides an in-depth understanding of human experiences, informs and guides interventions and policies, addresses complex social issues, promotes social justice and empowers marginalized communities, and facilitates community participation and engagement.
