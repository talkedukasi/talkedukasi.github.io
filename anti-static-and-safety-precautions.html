1. Give 10 Anti-static Safety Precautions​


Answer:

1. Keeping in contact with the metal frame of the computer is fine as long as it is earthed.

2. Handle circuit boards by the edges and avoid touching the contacts or components themselves.

3. Use a grounding wrist strap.

4. When possible, try to avoid working in carpeted areas. Carpeting greatly increases static buildup within your body.

5. Personal protective equipment are correctly used.

6. Always handle electronic components by a non-conducting (non-metallic) edge.

7. Do not eat, drink or smoke while assembling the computer.

8. Read and follow instructions on the manual carefully.

9. When working on a computer avoid places that are damp, subject to temperature extremes, dirty, or dusty.

10. The table should be clean and non-metallic to avoid short circuits.

7. Anti-static wrist strap


Answer:

An anti-static wrist strap is a key piece of safety gear that helps to prevent the buildup of static electricity near sensitive electronics or other projects where static charge could damage electronics or cause safety issues. The wrist strap is often used with an anti-static mat or other type of work space covering. Special, highly conductive threads on the wrist strap lead to a ground conductor in order to discharge static electricity safely.

14. What is a Anti static spray


Answer:

An anti-static spray is a liquid spray that can be used to eliminate static electricity and prevent static cling. It can help to repel dust and eliminates static build-up on optics, disc drives, photographic materials, lenses, metal rollers and other places where static may be present. When applied to surface, it is simply sprayed on and then wiped off, leaving behind nothing but a clean scent and no static buildup. It is not corrosive, but rather intensifies the contact resistance of the surface.
