13. In badminton, this term refers to loss of service. Answer Hand-Out


In badminton, this term refers to loss of service The answer is Service Over  If you've made a service and lost the rally, it'll be called SERVICE Done. It's now your opponent's turn to make a service for the next rally.

You just don't know badminton until you know the official terms.  

When you're playing badminton, besides knowing the actual game and the rules, it's also useful and  convenient to know the official words used during the game. This can give you a wellrounded understanding  of a game that will make you appreciate the game better and more profoundly.  

Those terms should be familiar to those who are interested not only in playing badminton, but who also love to watch badminton and follow it regularly. Understanding what these words are and what they mean will  help you understand what's being said in a badminton game.

Here is a list of the most widely used official terms:

During service:

1. Short– This indicates that the service did not hit the service line on the opponent's side. It was just short of the line.

2. Long– This indicates that the shuttle landed outside the grid. In singles, it means that it dropped beyond the second  

baseline, and in doubles, it means that it dropped outside the first baseline and the point was lost to server

 3. Wide– This indicates that the shuttle drops outside the courthouse. For men, this means the first sideline, and in pairs, the second sideline.

4. Let– The arbiter calls a let when the server does not know that the opponent was not ready or due to an  unexpected situation where the receiver or server was unexpectedly interrupted.

Scoring Terms:

1.  Love is 0. The game ends with the Love Number, which means 0-0.  

 

2. Deuce: In a general game of 21 points, when both players have hit 20-20, it is called deuce.  From here, the player who scores 2 straight points wins the game.  

3. Advantage: In the game, if there is a point where the players are at deuce, the player who wins the next point is said to have the advantage. There are two things that can happen, either a person with an advantage wins the next point and wins the game, or he can lose the point and he will again become a deuce for the  players.

Court Area:

1. Forecourt: This is applied to the front section of the court where the net shots are usually played.  

2. Mid-Court: This is the middle of the match, where it's easy to catch the winners.  

3. Rear court: applies to the back of the court. It is the only position you can drive your opponent to.  

Badminton shoots / Techniques:

1. Clear: clearing the shuttle means simply sending the shuttle back to get you out of trouble or to play it  safely.  

2. Follow through: The action / swing of the racquet must be completed after reaching the shuttle.  It is considered a follow-up.  

3. Half shattering: Half shattering means playing a shattering, but not with full force.
