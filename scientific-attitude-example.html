Scientific Attitudes

Scientific attitudes are the manner

of viewing things which are usually

manifested by curiosity in knowing how

and why things happen but are often practiced with an open mind which will enable identification of truths that will govern the newly found idea. Developing and possessing scientific attitudes are important since they lead to identification

of facts which have been carefully verified together and when these facts.

are merely accepted, the old theories will

displaced and discarded.

Ten Scientific Attitudes: . Communicative

.

Courage

. Creative

. Critical Thinker

. Curious

• Patience • Persistent

• Unbiased

Communicative is the ability to talk with

people and share information with them. This is often seen through working as

part of a group and collaborating with

others.

Courage is the decision to face agony, danger, intimidation, pain, and uncertainty. This is shown by the

attempts to find answers and aims for

success no matter how many times and

how long will it take.

Creative is the ability to make something

new. This is often manifested through

thinking out of the box and envisioning

things which are not easily seen by the

naked eye.

Critical Thinker is being objective

regarding an issue to be able to form

judgments. This is shown on how

information were being analyzed and

critical decisions were made in order to

solve the problem or address the issue.

Curious is having a deep interest in

knowing and learning something. This is manifested through curiosity towards the world people live in, why things happen,

and how they works. Detail - Oriented is ability to pay attention to details and can make keen

effort to understand causes and not

limited to effects only. This is manifested by people who stick with observations and collected data.

Open Minded is being receptive to new ideas. Listening to a

teammate during brainstorming sessions to see if the imparted information is relevant or needs improvement is a great example of being open-minded.

Patience is ability to overcome difficult

situations without complaining. This

is shown by the repeated attempts in

conducting experiments in order to verify

the results.

Persistent is the ability to stick with

something. For example, practicing

coming to school on time despite of

distance and heavy traffic.

Unbiased is the ability to stay away

from favoritism and all prejudice. This is

often observable on how a person treatanother whether that person is a family,

friend, colleague, or boss.
