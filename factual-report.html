20. 6. What are the examples of a factual text type?a. Factual recountb. Information reportc. Explanationd. All of the above​


The examples of a factual text type is (D) all of the above. Factual recount, information report, and explanation are categorized as factual text.

By providing facts and information, factual texts educate, instruct, or persuade. Literary works use words to conjure up images in the reader's mind that amuse or arouse emotion. One of these text types may be part of an assignment or project that students are frequently requested to present.

Several examples of factual texts:

Factual description

Describes a place or thing using facts.

Features:

Begins with an introduction, follows with a systematic description of the subject's various features, and may conclude with a statement.

Examples:

landscape descriptions

Factual recount

Gives instructions on how to make or do something.

Features:

Begins with a goal statement (could be the title)

Each instruction starts with a verb in the present tense and lists the materials needed in order of use before giving a series of steps (instructions).

Examples:

recipes

instructions

manual.

Information report

Classifies, describes and gives factual information about people, animals, things or phenomena.

Features:

Begins with a broad classification or definition lists a series of related facts about the subject and concludes with a final observation.

Examples:

facts about whales

Procedure

Gives instructions on how to make or do something.

Features:

Begins with a goal statement (could be the title)

Each instruction starts with a verb in the present tense and lists the materials needed in order of use before giving a series of steps (instructions).

Examples:

recipes

instruction

manual.

Procedural recount

Tells how something was made or done in time order and with accuracy.

Features:

Begins with a declaration of what was created or accomplished, detailing each step in the past tense.

Examples:

documentaries

retelling

a science experiment and its results.

Explanation

Explains how or why something happens.

Features:

Begins by introducing the subject, lists relevant elements in the proper order, discusses how the items relate to the subject and one another, and ends with a conclusion. The essay may also incorporate visual aids, such as flowcharts and diagrams, to further illustrate what is said in the present tense.

Examples:

the life cycle of a butterfly

how gears work

labelled diagrams

flowcharts.
