2. mangrove swamps living things and non living things​


Answer:

Living things:

Snails, barnacles, bryozoans, tunicates, mollusks, sponges, polychaete worms, isopods, amphipods, shrimps, crabs, and jellyfish all live either on or in close proximity to mangrove root systems. Some invertebrates thrive in the mangrove canopy, of which the most abundant are the crabs.

Non Living things:

Water, sand, mud, leaves, Branches, rocks and sunlight.

15. How do living and non-living things interact in a mangrove swamps?


Answer:

Bacteria create mutualistic relationships with the mangrove trees. The bacteria provide services such as N-fixation while the mangroves trees provide root exudates, stimulating microbial growth activity. Fungi show similar relationships with the mangrove trees. Plants also supply oxygen to these organisms
