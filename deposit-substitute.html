Types of non-cash payment

In summary, here are several types of non-cash payment instruments that you need to know:

Credit card

Is a card issued by a bank as a means of non-cash payment using a debt mechanism.

Debit card

Unlike a credit card, a debit card is a non-cash payment instrument based on the customer's balance. This card is issued by the bank where the customer saves his account. Depending on the type, debit cards usually have certain limits or limits for each transaction.

Check

A check is an order from a customer to a bank to withdraw funds in a certain amount under his name or a designated name. Checks are divided into three types, namely checks on behalf of, checks on show, and cross checks.

Transfer form

Is an order from the customer to the depository bank to transfer an amount of funds from the account concerned to the account of the holder whose name is mentioned.

Credit note

Credit notes are generally used to send or transfer non-cash funds to bank customers or other banks through clearing.

Debit note

It's a letter issued to collect customers of other banks or other banks through clearing.

E-money or electronic money

This type of non-cash payment instrument has become increasingly popular in recent years. Electronic money can be in the form of cards that can be used for payments based on the value of money or funds that have been deposited beforehand. This type of card can be used to pay for e-tolls, parking, and MRT tickets.

Lime juice

Lime juice is the best substitute for lemon juice, as it can be used as a one-to-one replacement and has a very similar taste and acidity level (5).

In fact, when canning or preserving food, it’s the ideal substitute for lemon juice because it has a similar pH level. Other substitutes, such as vinegar, are less acidic and may result in preserves that are unsafe for long-term storage (6).

In desserts in which lemon juice is a key ingredient, lime juice imparts a slightly different flavor. However, the result will still be tart and citrusy.
