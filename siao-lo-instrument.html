17. Give the functions of Sona, Ti-T's and Siao in MAPEH​


In the context of MAPEH (Music, Arts, Physical Education, and Health), here are the functions of Sona, Ti-T's, and Siao:

1. Sona (State of the Nation Address):

The Sona is an important function in the subject of MAPEH, particularly in the "P" or Physical Education component. Sona refers to the annual address delivered by the President of the Philippines to the nation. It serves as a platform for the President to inform the public about the achievements, plans, and priorities of the government in various areas, including physical education, sports development, and health initiatives. The Sona provides an opportunity to assess the current state of physical education programs, sports policies, and health promotion efforts in the country.

2. Ti-T's (Traditional Indigenous Filipino Games):

Ti-T's refers to traditional indigenous Filipino games. In the MAPEH curriculum, the study and inclusion of Ti-T's serve various functions. These traditional games promote cultural appreciation and understanding of the rich heritage of indigenous Filipino communities. They also provide opportunities for students to develop physical fitness, motor skills, teamwork, and strategic thinking. Ti-T's help preserve cultural traditions and foster a sense of pride and identity among Filipino learners.

3. Siao (Arts in the Vernacular):

Siao, in the context of MAPEH, refers to "Arts in the Vernacular." This component focuses on the exploration and appreciation of various artistic expressions deeply rooted in local cultural traditions and practices. The function of Siao is to promote cultural awareness, artistic creativity, and the preservation of indigenous art forms. Through Siao, students learn about indigenous visual arts, music, dance, literature, and theater, celebrating the diversity of Philippine culture and enhancing their artistic skills and appreciation.

Overall, the functions of Sona, Ti-T's, and Siao in MAPEH contribute to the holistic development of students, encompassing physical fitness, cultural appreciation, artistic expression, and awareness of national issues.

24. Which of the following musical instruments is NOT an aerophone?A. pipaB. siaoC. sonaD. ti-ts​


Answer:

A. Pipa

Explanation:

The pipa, pípá, or p'i-p'a (Chinese: 琵琶) is a four-stringed Chinese musical instrument, belonging to the plucked category of instruments. Sometimes called the "Chinese lute“, the instrument has a pear-shaped wooden body with a varying number of frets ranging from 12 to 31. Another Chinese four-string plucked lute is the liuqin, which looks like a smaller version of the pipa. The pear-shaped instrument may have existed in China as early as the Han dynasty, and although historically the term pipa was once used to refer to a variety of plucked chordophones, its usage since the Song dynasty refers exclusively to the pear-shaped instrument.
