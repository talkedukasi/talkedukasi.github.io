2. what is employee relations and movement?​


Answer:

Employee relations refers to the relationships and interactions between employers and employees in the workplace. It encompasses the various policies, practices, and procedures that employers use to manage their employees and create a positive work environment. Employee relations can include areas such as employee engagement, communication, performance management, compensation and benefits, employee development, and workplace culture.Employee movement refers to the various types of movement or transitions that employees can experience within a company. This can include promotions, transfers, job rotations, lateral moves, demotions, and terminations. Employee movement is often a key element of talent management and workforce planning, as it helps companies to develop and retain their employees, fill skill gaps, and adapt to changing business needs. Effective employee relations can play a crucial role in facilitating smooth and successful employee movements within an organization.

16. advantages and disadvantages of employee relations and movement​


Advantages:

Improved productivity: Good employee relations can lead to increased productivity, as employees who are satisfied with their work environment are more likely to be motivated to perform well.

Increased employee retention: A positive work environment and good employee relations can lead to increased loyalty and retention of employees.

Enhanced employer reputation: Companies that have positive employee relations are often viewed favorably by the public, which can lead to enhanced reputation and brand image.

Knowledge transfer: Employee movement, such as promotions or transfers, can lead to knowledge transfer across different areas of the company, which can increase innovation and efficiency.

Diversity and inclusion: Employee movement, such as hiring from diverse backgrounds and promoting employees from different departments, can help foster a diverse and inclusive workplace.

Disadvantages:

High turnover: Poor employee relations can lead to high turnover rates, which can be costly for companies and disrupt operations.

Conflict and disputes: Poor employee relations can also lead to conflicts and disputes between employees, which can be time-consuming and costly to resolve.

Training costs: Employee movement, such as transfers or promotions, may require additional training, which can be costly and time-consuming for the company.

Disruption of workflow: Employee movement, such as promotions or transfers, can disrupt the workflow of a team or department, which can affect productivity.

Loss of talent: When employees leave a company, it can result in a loss of talent and institutional knowledge, which can be detrimental to the company's performance.

20. give me one reason why the promotion of employee movements exists.​


Answer:The main reasons for promoting the employees are as follows:

Properly administered, transfers and promotion improve morale, stimulate efficiency and provide opportunity for advancement to loyal employees. Generally the following reasons for promoting employees are advanced:

(1) Promotion is the stepping up of an employee to a position in which he can render greater service to the company.

(2) Promotions from within the company are not so apt to be mistaken as are selections from without.

(3) The knowledge that deserved promotions are being made, increases the interest of other employees in the company and the desire to serve the interests of the company.

(4) Such knowledge causes other employees to believe that their turn will come and so they remain with the company and reduce the labour turnover.

(5) It creates a feeling of contentment with the present conditions and encourages ambition to succeed within the company.

(6) It increases interest in training and in self-development as necessary preparation for promotion.

Promotion from within fosters are inbreeding. It creates an inside market of talent and experience which is sheltered from competition and readily available as a resource to the company. However, selection of staff from inside candidate is not always good.
