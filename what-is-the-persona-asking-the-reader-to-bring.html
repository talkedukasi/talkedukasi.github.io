27. In Sonnet 307, what is common between the persona and the one spoken to? What does the persona want from the latter?


Answer:

In Petrarch's "Sonnet 307", the persona and the one spoken to are both grieving for their loss, most probably someone they love.

The persona wants the latter to come to him so that they could talk of the past.

A sonnet is a style of poetry written in the style of a song about a loved one. Traditionally, it is a poem of 14 lines written in iambic pentameter but with several rhyme schemes.

Considering the sonnet you're talking about is Petrarch's "Sonnet 307", then the persona referred to is the speaker of the poem while the one spoken to will be the bird.

The speaker is addressing a bird, "grieving for the past".

He expressed his desire to see the bird come to his "bosom, comfort you would bring" so that "[they] would weep together".

The common thing between them seems to be that they have both lost someone they love and that they are both "grieving for the past".

The speaker wanted the bird to come to him so that they can grieve together, "[to] talk of the past then, tenderlyPetrarch's sonnet presents the common pain and heartbreak of the speaker and a bird.
