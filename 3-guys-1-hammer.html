17. 1. molding of clay 2. . hammering of coin 3. pressing bread​


Answer:

1.Molding of clay- Probably the most ancient and easy way to mold clay is by pinching it with your fingers. Once your clay has been wedged, press and pull at it with your fingers to form it into a desired shape. 

2.Hammering of coin- Hammered coins were produced by placing a blank piece of metal (aplanchet or flan) of the correct weight between two dies, and then striking the upper die with a hammer to produce the required image on both sides. The planchet was usually cast from a mold.

3.Pressing bread- To squeeze, press, or roll with the hands, as in massaging. ... The definition of knead is to work or massage something with your hands, such as to work dough to make bread. When you work flour into bread dough with your hands to make bread, this is an example of knead.

- it depends on the kind of material for metals it could be flattened with strong hammering force, if the material is brittle like some rocks it will break, if the material is strong enough like a thick hard wood there will be deformation on the part that is being hammered.
