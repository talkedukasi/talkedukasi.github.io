5. at the same instant that you fire a bullet horizontal from a rifle, you drop a bullet from the height of the barrel, if there is no air resistance, which bullet hits the ground first, the bullet fired from the rifle or the bullet that is dropped?​


Answer:

Both bullets hit ground at the same time.

Explanation:

Remember that if two objects free-fall with the same initial vertical velocity, then they will fall at the same rate, then finally hit the ground at the same time.

The bullet fired may have a horizontal velocity, but that does not affect its vertical velocity; its intial velocity is 0 m/s. The bullet dropped started at rest and was dropped; its initial velocity is 0 m/s.

The two bullets will fall at the same rate (9.8 m/s² downwards), and will finally hit ground level at the same instance.

13. a bullet is fired at an angle 75° with an initial velocity of 150.0m/s how long os the bullet in the air? what is the maximum height reached by the bullet?​


Answer: PA BRAINLIEST
the bullet is in the air for approximately 30.9 seconds and reaches a maximum height of approximately 2,406 meters.

Explanation:

Assuming no air resistance, the time the bullet is in the air can be found using the following equation:

t = 2v₀sinθ/g

where v₀ is the initial velocity, θ is the angle of launch, and g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.8 m/s²).

Plugging in the given values, we get:

t = 2(150.0 m/s)sin(75°)/9.8 m/s²

t ≈ 30.9 seconds

To find the maximum height reached by the bullet, we can use the following equation:

h = v₀²sin²θ/2g

Plugging in the given values, we get:

h = (150.0 m/s)²sin²(75°)/(2 × 9.8 m/s²)

h ≈ 2,406 meters

Therefore, the bullet is in the air for approximately 30.9 seconds and reaches a maximum height of approximately 2,406 meters.

24. A bullet is fired at an angle of 45 degrees with an initial velocity of 200m/s.How long the bullet is in the air?What is the maximum height reached by the bullet?What is the horizontal displacement or range of the bullet?


Answer:

We can solve this problem using the kinematic equations of motion for projectile motion.

First, we can find the time the bullet is in the air. We know that the initial velocity can be resolved into horizontal and vertical components:

Vx = V * cos(45) = 200 m/s * cos(45) = 141.4 m/s

Vy = V * sin(45) = 200 m/s * sin(45) = 141.4 m/s

The time the bullet is in the air can be found using the vertical component:

Vy = Voy - gt

where Voy = 141.4 m/s and g = 9.8 m/s^2 (acceleration due to gravity)

At the maximum height, the vertical component of the velocity becomes zero. Therefore, we can find the time of flight by doubling the time it takes for the bullet to reach maximum height:

t = 2 * Voy / g = 2 * 141.4 / 9.8 = 28.8 s (approx.)

Therefore, the bullet is in the air for approximately 28.8 seconds.

To find the maximum height reached by the bullet, we can use the equation:

y = Voyt - 1/2g*t^2

At the maximum height, the vertical velocity is zero. Therefore, we can find the maximum height by substituting t = 14.4 s (half of the total time of flight):

y = Voyt - 1/2gt^2

y = 141.4 * 14.4 - 1/29.8*(14.4)^2

y = 1024.6 m (approx.)

Therefore, the maximum height reached by the bullet is approximately 1024.6 meters.

To find the horizontal displacement or range of the bullet, we can use the equation:

x = Vx * t

Substituting the values of Vx and t, we get:

x = 141.4 * 28.8 = 4070.7 m (approx.)

Therefore, the horizontal displacement or range of the bullet is approximately 4070.7 meters.

30. A bullet is fired at angle of 60 with an intial velocity of 200.0 m/s.How long is the bullet in the air?What is the maximum height reached by the bullet?


Answer:

1. Break the initial velocity into its horizontal and vertical components. The horizontal component is given by Vx = V*cos(θ), where V is the magnitude of the initial velocity and θ is the angle of elevation. The vertical component is given by Vy = V*sin(θ).

V = 200.0 m/s

θ = 60°

Vx = V*cos(θ) = 100.0 m/s

Vy = V*sin(θ) = 173.2 m/s

2. Find the time of flight. We can use the fact that the vertical component of the velocity is zero at the maximum height and also when the bullet hits the ground. Thus, we can use the following equation to find the time of flight:

t = 2*Vy/g

where g is the acceleration due to gravity, which is approximately 9.81 m/s^2.

t = 2*173.2/9.81 ≈ 35.3 s

Therefore, the bullet is in the air for about 35.3 seconds.

3. Find the maximum height reached by the bullet. We can use the fact that the vertical component of the velocity is zero at the maximum height. Thus, we can use the following equation to find the maximum height:

h = (Vy^2)/(2*g)

h = (173.2^2)/(2*9.81) ≈ 1.52 x 10^3 m

Therefore, the maximum height reached by the bullet is about 1.52 kilometers.
