26. why did Louis Armstrong wrote the song what a wonderful world?


The song "What a Wonderful World" is written by late Bob Thiele and George David Weiss, not by Louis Armstrong. These two writers firstly offered the song to Tony Bennett, but he turned it down. Until it is offered to Louis Armstrong.

George Weiss then wrote the song specially for Louis Armstrong. Weiss was inspired by Armstrong's ability to bring people of different races together.

The song recorded on August 16, 1967, and released on October 18, 1967 under ABC Records. It didn't made a popularity around the United States but the song made an instant hit in the United Kingdom earning #1 at the UK Singles Chart.

Last 2014, Louis Armstrong's "What a Wonderful World" 1967 record sold more than 2 Million downloads in the United States alone after it was digitally released.

29. The form/structure of the song " What a wonderful world" by Louis Armstrong?​


"What a Wonderful World" by Louis Armstrong:

"What a Wonderful World" is a classic song by Louis Armstrong, and this is its form/structure as follows:

The song is in a simple 4/4 time signature which is relevant.The song has an AABA form, which means that it has four sections: A, A, B, and A respectively.The first A section consists of the lyrics "I see trees of green, red roses too / I see them bloom for me and you / And I think to myself, what a wonderful world." which is more emphasized.The second A section is similar to the first line, with the same melody and lyrics, but with some different variations in the phrasing as the song comes along.The B section introduces a new melody and lyrics, which in contrast with the positivity of the A sections. The lyrics of the B section are "I see skies of blue and clouds of white / The bright blessed day, the dark sacred night / And I think to myself, what a wonderful world."Last but not the least, the final A section goes back to the melody and lyrics of the first two A sections.
