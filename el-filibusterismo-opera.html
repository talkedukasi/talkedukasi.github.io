2. The opera "Noli Me Tangere" was composed by Felipe de Leon.

3. The libretto of "Noli Me Tangere" was written by Guillermo Tolentino and Leandro Locsin.

4. The father of Crisostomo Ibarra, Don Rafael Ibarra, was accused of being a heretic and was imprisoned. He later died in prison, which led Crisostomo to seek revenge on those who caused his father's death.

5. The insanity of Sisa was caused by the abuse and mistreatment she received from her husband and the authorities.

6. The proposed wedding of Crisostomo Ibarra and Maria Clara did not succeed because Maria Clara's mother, Doña Pia Alba, did not want her daughter to marry Ibarra. She believed that Ibarra's father was responsible for the death of her husband.

7. The message of the opera "Noli Me Tangere" is the need for social reform and the eradication of the social cancer that afflicts Philippine society.

10. Make an essay that represent the culture if Filipino regarding on the Philippine Opera, LA LOBA NEGRA, LA LOBA NEGRA, and El Filibusterismo.​


Answer:

The culture of the Philippines is rich and diverse, with a long history of blending indigenous traditions with influences from Spain, America, and other cultures. This fusion of artistic and cultural elements is particularly evident in the country's celebration of music and theater, and three notable examples of how the Philippines celebrates theater in all its glory are the Philippine Opera, La Loba Negra, and El Filibusterismo.

The Philippine Opera aims to bring the rich tradition of Western classical music into Southeast Asia. With the aim to introduce the art form to Filipinos, the genre was introduced by the Spaniards during their colonization in the country, with the first performance being given in 1766. Since then, opera has been an integral part of the country's cultural heritage, a symbol of the cultural exchange between the Philippines and Western nations.

Another example is La Loba Negra, a play that is regarded as one of Philippines’ one-act masterpieces by Filipino literature experts. Written by Celestino Ulep (who is from Camalig, Albay), La Loba Negra is an insightful portrayal of the Filipino mindset before, during, and after the country was colonized by foreign powers. Through the play's story of Nena, a well-educated Filipina who has run away from her loveless marriage, La Loba Negra reveals the complexity of Filipino identity that emerged at the turn of the century.

Finally, El Filibusterismo, a novel by Filipino nationalist and author Jose Rizal, touched on the themes of social inequality, revolution, and social justice. A sequel to Noli Me Tangere, El Filibusterismo tells the story of Simoun, a wealthy and influential businessman in Manila, who is secretly a rebel seeking to overthrow the colonial government. The book is significant to the Filipino culture as it contains hidden meanings and clandestine messages that were vital in rallying the support of the Filipino people towards the revolution.

The Philippines also has other culture and traditions that are closely associated with music and theater. For example, the Bayanihan Cultural Troupe, which was established in 1954, showcases various Philippine dance forms that date back to precolonial times, including the tinikling (a bamboo dance), singkil, and maglalatik.

In conclusion, the Philippines’ rich culture is reflected through its celebration of music and theater arts. The Philippine Opera, La Loba Negra, and El Filibusterismo represent different aspects of the country's cultural heritage, highlighting how it has been shaped and molded by various influences throughout its history. These three artistic expressions showcase the complex and dynamic nature of the Filipino identity, making the Philippines an exceptional country, rich in culture and history.

14. explain briefly about noli me tangere and el filibusterismo as an example of an opera (ASAP)​


Explanation:

"Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo" are not operas, but rather novels written by Dr. Jose Rizal during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. These two novels are considered as two of the most important works of Philippine literature and played a significant role in Philippine history.

"Noli Me Tangere" was published in 1887 and is a story about the social and political issues experienced by Filipinos during the Spanish colonization in the Philippines. The novel exposes the injustices committed by the Spanish friars and highlights the struggles of the Filipino people during that time. The title "Noli Me Tangere" is a Latin phrase that means "Touch Me Not," which symbolizes the protagonist's warning to his fellow Filipinos to never touch the fragile social fabric of their society.

"El Filibusterismo," published in 1891, is the sequel to "Noli Me Tangere." It continues the story of the protagonist, Crisostomo Ibarra, now under the identity of Simoun, four years after the events of the first novel. It explores the themes of revolution and how Filipinos were pushed to rebellion due to the injustices they experienced. The title "El Filibusterismo" comes from the word "filibustero," which refers to a person who engages in rebellion or insurrection.

Although they are not operas, both novels have been adapted into various forms of media, including stage plays, movies, and musical productions. The themes of social injustices, rebellion, and the struggles of the Filipino people during the Spanish colonial period continue to resonate with Filipinos today.

Opera Review: Noli Me Tangere - A Captivating Journey into Philippine History

Noli Me Tangere, a Philippine opera based on Dr. Jose Rizal's renowned novel, is a captivating musical experience that delves into the country's tumultuous past. Composed by Felipe Padilla de Leon with libretto by Guillermo Tolentino, this opera offers a condensed yet powerful depiction of social injustice and colonial oppression during the Spanish era.

The opera follows the story of Crisostomo Ibarra, a young idealist who returns to the Philippines and seeks justice for his father's death. The music seamlessly blends Western operatic elements with traditional Filipino melodies, creating a harmonious fusion that resonates with the audience. The vocal performances are exceptional, conveying the emotional depth of the characters and intensifying the drama.

The production design transports viewers to 19th-century Philippines through intricate sets and costumes. The opulent Spanish colonial interiors and lush Filipino landscapes are visually stunning, adding authenticity and depth to the storytelling.
