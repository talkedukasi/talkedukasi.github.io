Francisca Reyes Aquino is known as the "Mother of Philippine Folk Dance." She is credited for her contributions to the preservation and promotion of traditional Philippine folk dances. She studied Philippine folk dances and music, documented and codified them, and taught them to generations of Filipinos.

In her book, "Philippine Folk Dances and Games," Aquino described the fundamental dance steps used in traditional Philippine folk dances. These steps include the basic step, hop, skip, and jump. The basic step is the foundation of most Philippine folk dances. It involves taking one step forward with the left foot, bringing the right foot beside the left, and then taking a step forward with the left foot again.

In addition to the fundamental dance steps, Aquino also emphasized the importance of music in traditional Philippine folk dances. She wrote that the music played an integral part in the dances and that the dance steps were often choreographed to match the rhythm and melody of the music.

Overall, Francisca Reyes Aquino's work in documenting, codifying, and teaching Philippine folk dances has helped to preserve and promote these traditional dances. In 1949, she traveled to various countries across Asia and Europe to study foreign folk dances and bring them back to the Philippines.

During her travels, Reyes Aquino observed and documented a wide range of folk dances from different cultures, including the Kung Fu dance from China, the Tinikling from Indonesia, the Saludo dance from Spain, and the Tarantella from Italy. She also learned about the traditional dances of Japan, India, and Greece, among others.

Upon her return to the Philippines, Reyes Aquino incorporated the elements and techniques she had learned from foreign folk dances into her own choreography, creating new and innovative styles that showcased the rich cultural diversity of the Philippines. Her work helped to revitalize traditional Philippine folk dances and inspire new generations to appreciate and embrace their cultural heritage.

Today, the legacy of Francisca Reyes Aquino lives on through the many dance companies and schools that continue to perform and teach her choreography, as well as through the ongoing efforts to preserve and promote traditional Philippine folk dances as a vital part of the country's cultural identity.
