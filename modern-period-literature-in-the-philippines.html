9. ACTIVITY 1 Directions: Revisit the history of Philippine literature and create a timeline of how literary forms in the country was developed up to the modern period by writing the most significant events in each period.‚Äã


Here is a timeline of the development of Philippine literature from pre-colonial period up to the modern period:

Pre-colonial Period

The indigenous people of the Philippines had their own form of literature, which included epic poems, riddles, proverbs, and songs.

The most famous examples of pre-colonial literature are the epics "Biag ni Lam-ang" and "Hinilawod".

Spanish Colonial Period (1565-1898)

During the Spanish colonization, literature in the Philippines was mainly written in Spanish.

The first known book to be published in the Philippines was "Doctrina Christiana" in 1593, which contained basic prayers and religious instructions in Tagalog and Spanish.

The most prominent literary figure during this period was Jose Rizal, who wrote the novels "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo".

American Colonial Period (1898-1946)

Literature in the Philippines during the American colonial period was largely influenced by American writers and themes.

The first English-language newspaper in the country, "The Manila Times", was established in 1898.

One of the most notable writers during this period was Nick Joaquin, who wrote short stories and essays that explored Filipino culture and society.

Japanese Occupation (1942-1945)

During the Japanese occupation, literature in the Philippines was heavily censored and controlled by the Japanese military government.

Resistance literature emerged during this period, with writers using allegory and symbolism to criticize the Japanese regime.

Prominent writers during this period include Jose Garcia Villa and Amado Hernandez.

Post-War Period (1946-1960s)
