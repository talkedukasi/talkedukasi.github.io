25. in 1897 Thomson discovered the electron and then went on to propose a model for the structure of the atom his work also led to the invention of the mass spectrograph explained?​


JJ Thomson's discovery of the electron in 1897 was a major breakthrough in the understanding of the atomic structure. He discovered that cathode rays, which were previously thought to be a type of wave, were actually made up of tiny, negatively charged particles called electrons. This led him to propose a new model for the structure of the atom, known as the plum pudding model, which suggested that the atom was composed of a positively charged substance with electrons embedded in it.

Thomson's work also led to the invention of the mass spectrograph, which is a device used to analyze the mass and abundance of different isotopes in a sample. The mass spectrograph works by ionizing the atoms in the sample, then separating them according to their mass-to-charge ratio, and finally detecting the resulting ion beams.

The invention of the mass spectrograph was significant because it allowed scientists to study the properties of atoms and molecules in more detail, including their mass, composition, and isotopic abundance. This, in turn, led to the development of new fields of study, such as nuclear physics and isotopic geochemistry, and has had important practical applications in areas such as medical research and environmental monitoring.
