7. Write and essay about the maglalatik and bulaklakan dance.


The Maglalatik and Bulaklakan dance is a traditional Filipino folk dance that is performed during festivals and special occasions. The dance is characterized by the use of coconut shells that are worn by the dancers on their hands, chest, and back. The dance is accompanied by lively music that is played on traditional instruments such as the bandurria and the tambourine.

The Maglalatik dance originated from the province of Laguna and is believed to have been performed during the Spanish colonial period. The dance tells the story of a battle between the Moros and the Christians over coconut meat, which was a valuable commodity at the time. The dancers use the coconut shells to represent the clash of the two groups.

The Bulaklakan dance, on the other hand, is a celebration of the blooming of flowers. The dancers wear colorful costumes adorned with flowers and perform graceful movements that mimic the swaying of the flowers in the breeze. The dance is a symbol of the beauty and abundance of nature in the Philippines.

In all parts, the Maglalatik and Bulaklakan dance are important cultural traditions in the Philippines that showcase the country's rich history and natural beauty. These dances are not only entertaining to watch but also serve as a reminder of the country's heritage and the importance of preserving it for future generations.

17. Describe the following: 1. Maglalatik 2. Itik-Itik 3. Bulaklakan 4. Creative Dance 5. Hiphop Dance


Answer:

1.Maglalatik-The Maglalatik (also known as Manlalatik or Magbabao) is a folk dance from the Philippines. Coconut shell halves are secured onto the dancers' hands and on vests upon which are hung four or six more coconut shell halves. ... The dance is intended to impress the viewer with the great skill of the dancer.

2.itik-itik-Itik-itik is a mimetic folk dance in The Philippines. It originated in the province of Surigao in Mindanao. In Itik-itik (from the Tagalog word for " duck"), the dance steps imitate the movements of ducks among rice paddies and swamplands, such as wading, flying, and short, choppy steps.

3.Bulaklakan-Bulaklakan, a lovely and attractive dance, is danced for this occasion. The girls in this dance each hold a garland of leaves and flowers attached to a wire, bamboo or rattan so that the garland will arch when held overhead and will gently sway these arcs of garlands to gracefully give honor to Virgin Mary.

4.Creative Dance-Creative Dance is a form of dance that combines movement and artistic expression, without the requirement of extensive training (Gilbert, 1992). ... Creative dance allows for variation amongst the skill level of its participants, affording opportunities for those of any age and ability

5.Hiphop Dance-Hip-hop dance is a vibrant form of dance that combines a variety of freestyle movements to create a cultural piece of art. Through its three main styles of popping, locking, and breaking, hip-hop dance has evolved into one of the most popular and influential styles of dance.

18. Learning Task 4: Define the folk dances below. Write the answer at the back. A. Bulaklakan B. Cariñosa C. Sayaw sa Bangko D. Dugso E. Tinikling​


Answer:

A. Bulaklakan

It is a dance that is seen as a very lovely and attractive dance that is showcased by 12 females.

B. Cariñosa

Philippine dance of colonial-era origin from the Maria Clara suite of Philippine folk dances, where the fan or handkerchief plays an instrumental role as it places the couple in a romance scenario

C. Sayaw sa Bangko

folk dance from the Pangasinan province of the Philippines. Performers dance on top of a bench that is normally six inches wide.

D. Dugso

a ceremonial dance among the Manobo people in Bukidnon, Agusan and Misamis Oriental. Performed during important occasions like kaliga (feasts) or kaamulan (tribal gatherings)

E. Tinikling

a traditional Philippine folk dance which originated during the Spanish colonial era. The dance involves two people beating, tapping, and sliding bamboo poles on the ground and against each other in coordination with one or more dancers who step over and in between the poles in a dance.
