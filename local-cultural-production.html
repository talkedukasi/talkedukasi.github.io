4. Explain the dynamic between local and global cultural production​


Answer:

What is the dynamic between local and global cultural production?

Cultures have always been a result of the encounter of local developments with outside influence. Adventurers, traders and warriors for example brought their cultures to the world but were also influenced by what they encountered.

Only post World War II we saw the spread of a kind of global culture. Increasingly competing with and replacing parts of local culture. This, because initially this influence was embraced and welcomed. Fashion, music, literature and the alien way of living was appealing to many and seen as exciting and enriching. Chewing gum, blue jeans, comics, rock ’n’ roll etc. Culture started to be mainly produced by actively adopting elements from abroad. At the same time, a global layer emerged - the amalgamation of local habits and customs from various places. Radio and TV were major accelerators of this development. Later came the internet.

Then, two factors brought a change of view and direction. First, the real and perceived (negative) impact of outside influence on local life, such as loss of identity and jobs. Second, politicians that started to emphasize the negative sides of internationalization and globalization on local systems. Combined with the human tendencies to largely filter out the negatives of the past and the preference for simple messages created a new populist trend and cultural tension between local and global protagonists.

The increasing rejection of foreign cultural elements by larger parts of the population drove a rebound of traditional local cultures. But little new local cultural production was occurring. It was the try of a remake

13. Write a 1 page insight on how local products would influence cultural tourism​


Answer:

Local products can have a significant impact on cultural tourism by providing visitors with an authentic and immersive experience of the local culture. These products can range from handicrafts and souvenirs to food and beverages, and they offer visitors a tangible connection to the history, traditions, and lifestyle of the community they are visiting.

One of the most obvious ways that local products influence cultural tourism is through the economic benefits they generate for the local community. When visitors purchase local products, they are directly supporting the livelihoods of local artisans, farmers, and entrepreneurs, helping to sustain and promote local culture and traditions. This, in turn, can create a ripple effect of economic growth and development, leading to job creation, infrastructure improvements, and increased investment in the local area.

Local products can also serve as a way of preserving and promoting cultural heritage. Many traditional crafts and products have been passed down through generations, and by purchasing them, visitors are helping to ensure that these skills and traditions are preserved for future generations. Local products can also serve as a way of educating visitors about the cultural significance of certain objects or practices, and can offer a deeper understanding of the customs and traditions of the local community.

In addition to economic and cultural benefits, local products can also enhance the overall tourism experience for visitors. Authentic local products can offer a unique and memorable souvenir of a trip, serving as a reminder of the sights, sounds, and tastes of a particular place. They can also help visitors to feel more connected to the local community, creating a sense of community and belonging that can be difficult to achieve through other means.

Overall, local products can play a vital role in cultural tourism, providing economic, cultural, and experiential benefits for visitors and local communities alike. By supporting and promoting local products, cultural tourism can help to preserve and promote cultural heritage, while also generating economic growth and enhancing the overall tourism experience.
