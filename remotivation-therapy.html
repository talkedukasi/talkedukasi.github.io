4. Which of the following statements about gene therapy is not true? OPTIONS A:Functioning genes are used to replace mutated genes. B:Gene therapy can cure any disease. C:Somatic gene therapy only works on individuals. D:Gene therapy is another form of artificial selection.


The statement that is not true is B: Gene therapy can cure any disease.

Why?

While gene therapy is a promising field of medicine, it cannot cure all diseases. Gene therapy works by introducing functional genes to replace or supplement non-functional or mutated genes in a patient's cells.

However, not all diseases are caused by a single gene mutation, and some diseases may have more complex causes that cannot be treated by gene therapy alone. Additionally, gene therapy is still an experimental approach, and its long-term effects and safety are still being studied.

Therefore, it is important to understand that gene therapy has limitations, and it is not a universal cure for all diseases.

17. Differentiate gene therapy from stem therapy ​


Answer:

Gene therapy consists of the introduction of genetic material into cells for a therapeutic purpose. Stem cells can be defined operationally as cells that can continuously self-renew and have the potential to generate intermediate and mature cells.


18. what is the difference between gene therapy and cell therapy​


Answer:

Gene therapy involves the transfer of genetic material, usually in a carrier or vector, and the uptake of the gene into the appropriate cells of the body. cell therapy involves the transfer of cells with the relevant function into the patient. Some protocols utilize both gene therapy and cell therapy.
