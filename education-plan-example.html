An annuity is a predetermined sum of money that you will receive every year for the rest of your life. It is a long-term investment offered by an insurance company that is intended to safeguard you against the possibility of outliving your income. Your purchase payments are turned into recurring payments that can last a lifetime through annuitization. An annuity is a contract between you and an insurance company in which the insurer agrees to pay you either immediately or in the future.

Annuities include regular savings account deposits, monthly house mortgage payments, monthly insurance payments, and pension payments. The frequency of payment dates distinguishes annuities. The frequency of payment dates distinguishes annuities. Payments (deposits) can be made on a weekly, monthly, quarterly, annually, or any other regular basis.

The key advantages of purchasing an annuity are principal protection, the possibility of guaranteed lifetime income, and the ability to leave money to your dependents. Annuities can be tailored for income or long-term growth, but they are not suitable for short-term investment. People who want long-term financial security, retirement income, diversification, and principal protection will be interested in these items.

Annuities can shield you from a variety of financial risks, but they come at a cost. You will pay annuity costs and will not have as much upside potential as you would with other investments. The biggest disadvantages include the long-term contract, losing control of your investment, earning little or no interest, and paying expensive fees.
