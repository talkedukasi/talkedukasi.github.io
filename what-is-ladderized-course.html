3. a ladder makes an angle of 52° with the floor. if the top of the ladder is 5 metres from the floor what is the length of the ladder?​

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

By this, we can represent the angles and the sides of a right triangle.

52° is the referred angle.5m height is opposite to the refered angle.The ladder's length is the hypotenuse.

Using trigonometry, we can solve for the hypotenuse by using the sine ratio. Let x be the length of the ladder

[tex] \rm \sin \theta = \frac{opposite}{hypotenuse} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sin 52\degree = \frac{5m}{x} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = \frac{5m}{\sin 52\degree} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x \approx 6.34m[/tex]

Therefore, the length of the ladder is approximately 6.34 meters

12. a ladder leans against a wall and makes 30 degree angle on the ground. If the ladder is 8.5 m from the base of the building. what is the length of the ladder?


A ladder leans against a wall and makes 30 degree angle on the ground. If the ladder is 8.5 meters from the base of the building. The length of the ladder is 9.8 meters.

To solve this problem, you can use the Pythagorean formula or the cosine formula.

The Pythagorean formula:

c² = a² + b² ⇒ c = √(a² + b²)

a² = c² - b² ⇒ a = √(c² - b²)

b² = c² - a² ⇒ b = √(c² - a²)

The cosine formula:

sin = [tex]\frac{y}{r}[/tex]

cos = [tex]\frac{x}{r}[/tex]

tan = [tex]\frac{y}{x}[/tex]

where r = √(x² + y²)

c² = a² + b² - 2 × a × b × cos C

a² = c² - b² + 2 × a × b × cos A

b² = c² - a² + 2 × a × b × cos B

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

A ladder leans against a wall and makes 30 degree angle on the ground. If the ladder is 8.5 m from the base of the building.

Question:

What is the length of the ladder?

Solution:

Step 1

Make a mathematical sentence from the statement of the problem.

The angle formed by the ladder and the ground is 30°.

The distance between the ladder and the base of the building is 8.5 meters.

Step 2

Cos of the angle formed by the ladder and the ground is the distance between the ladder and the base of the building divided by the length of the ladder.

Cos 30° = 8.5 meters ÷ the length of the ladder.

The length of the ladder = 8.5 meters ÷ Cos 30°

The length of the ladder = 8.5 meters ÷ [tex]\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{3}[/tex]

The length of the ladder = 8.5 meters × [tex]\frac{2}{\sqrt{3} }[/tex]

The length of the ladder = (8.5 meters × 2) ÷ √3

The length of the ladder = 17 meters ÷ 1.73

The length of the ladder = 9.8 meters

Step 3

You can also write:

The length of the ladder = [tex]\frac{8.5}{cos 30^o}[/tex]

The length of the ladder = [tex]\frac{8.5}{\frac{1}{2} \sqrt{3} }[/tex]

The length of the ladder = [tex]\frac{17}{\sqrt{3} }[/tex]

The length of the ladder = 9.8 meters

So the length of the ladder is 9.8 meters.
