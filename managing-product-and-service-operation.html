12. 1. Why do you need to learn about operations management? 2. Choose a product or service and illustrate its supply chain its process model. ​


Answer:

1. Why do you need to learn about operations management?

Operations management is an essential aspect of any business, regardless of the industry. It involves managing the processes and resources required to produce goods and services, including planning, organizing, and controlling the production and delivery of products or services. Learning about operations management is important because it can help you:

Improve efficiency: By streamlining operations, you can reduce costs, increase productivity, and improve customer satisfaction.

Increase profitability: Efficient operations can help you increase profit margins by reducing waste and improving resource utilization.

Enhance customer satisfaction: By delivering high-quality products or services on time and at a reasonable cost, you can enhance customer satisfaction and loyalty.

Identify and mitigate risks: By understanding your operations, you can identify potential risks and take steps to mitigate them.

Improve innovation: Operations management can help you identify areas for improvement and innovation in your products and services.

2. Choose a product or service and illustrate its supply chain and process model.

Let's consider a product like a smartphone and illustrate its supply chain and process model.

Supply Chain:

Raw Materials: The process begins with the acquisition of raw materials like metals, plastics, glass, and other components required for manufacturing a smartphone. These materials are sourced from suppliers globally.

Manufacturing: Once the raw materials are procured, the manufacturing process begins. The phone is assembled in multiple stages, including PCB (printed circuit board) assembly, battery and camera assembly, and final assembly. Different parts of the smartphone are assembled in various locations worldwide.

Logistics: Once the phone is assembled, it is packaged and shipped to warehouses worldwide. These warehouses then supply the smartphones to retailers or directly to customers who order online.

Retail: Smartphones are sold through various retail channels like online marketplaces, mobile phone carriers, and company-owned stores.

Process Model:

Design: The design team creates the blueprint for the smartphone, considering all the features, aesthetics, and user experience.

Planning: Once the design is approved, the planning team determines the resources required for manufacturing the phone, including raw materials, machinery, and labor.

Manufacturing: The manufacturing team follows the process of assembling various parts of the smartphone in different locations worldwide.

Quality Control: Once the smartphone is assembled, it undergoes a series of quality checks to ensure that it meets the required standards.

Packaging and Shipping: The phone is then packaged and shipped to warehouses worldwide.

Marketing and Sales: The marketing and sales team promotes the smartphone through various channels and sells it through different retail channels.
