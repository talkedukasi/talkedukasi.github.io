20. What is the denotation of a word? its etymology, its part of speech, its literal, dictionary definition, or its extra, implied meaning?


The denotation of a word is its literal, dictionary definition, or its primary meaning as found in a reference source such as a dictionary or encyclopedia. It is the objective, explicit, and precise meaning of a word, without any associated emotions, values, or attitudes. Denotation does not include any extra, implied meanings or cultural associations that may be attached to a word, but rather it focuses on the specific definition of the word itself. In contrast, the extra, implied meaning of a word is called connotation.
