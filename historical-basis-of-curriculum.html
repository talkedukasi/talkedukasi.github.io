2. Historical event curriculum development in the philippines


The historical event curriculum development in the Philippines :

Education before the coming of Spaniards.Education during the Spanish Occupation.Education during the American Occupation.Education during the Japanese Occupation.Education of the Martial Law.Education under the Philippine Republic.

Further Explanation :

Curriculum development refers to the process of selecting, organizing, executing about the learning experiences based on the needs, abilities of the learners. Based on the researches of the historical foundation of the Philippine curriculum, the development of the curriculum in Philippines history is based on the five motives which are religion, political, utilitarian, mass education and excellence in education.

The historical events of curriculum development in the Philippines :

Education before the coming of Spaniards : This period is also known as the pre Hispanic period, in the pre Hispanic period there were no formal schools. The education given was done by oral practical and hands-on, in this period the education was no formal parents mostly taught their children how to live and hunt for food, the sons are taught how to hunt, and the daughters were taught how to do household chores.Education during the Spanish Occupation : In the Spanish era the schools were managed by religious organizations as the main objective is to spread Christianity through teaching and education, the curriculum that was created was mostly religion based which were reading, writing, and religion. The subjects were; Languages (Latin, Spanish grammar and literature), History (Universal, Spanish), Mathematics, Philosophy, and Geography.Education during the American Occupation : During the American era, English became the main language in school, the soldiers became the first teachers and taught in school but later on the government hired proper teachers to teach and the American mostly focus on political education. The subjects were mathematics, language, science, history and government, social sciences and Physical Education.Education during the Japanese Occupation : In the Japanese era there were principles of the Japanese education which were; Diffusion of the Japanese language in the Philippines community, the promotion of vocational course, inspiration for people to love their neighbour, and foster a new Filipino culture based.Education of the Martial Law : The education system use of language were changed in school requiring the Filipino and English language to be used in schools. Science, math, and literature classes were taught in English while the rest were taught in Filipino.Education under the Philippine Republic : The curriculum that is established by the Philippine republic is K - 12 reform, which means 1 year of kindergarten education, six years of elementary education and six of secondary education, four years of junior high school and two years of senior high school.

8. What is the legal basis in the implementation of NSTP in the course curriculum?​


The legal basis for the implementation of NSTP in the course curriculum

Republic Act 9163 or the “National Service Training Program Act of 2001” serves as the legal basis for NSTP in the course curriculum. This republic act was signed by former president Gloria Macapagal Arroyo. This law also made the Reserve Officer’s Training Corps or ROTC an optional course in the curriculum and gave students 2 more choices to choose from.

The National Service Training Program or NSTP course was created to enhance the civic consciousness and defense preparedness of Filipino youth. The government believes that through NSTP, they can develop the values of services and patriotism among the youth. This program also aims to give chance to students to contribute to the public welfare of their society.

The National Service Training Program consists of three components:

Reserve Officers’ Training Corps or ROTCLiteracy Training Service or LTSCivic Welfare Training Service or CWTS

15. What are the strengths and weaknesses of each of the different types of curriculum operating in schools? taught curriculum supported curriculum recommended curriculum written curriculum learned curriculum assessed curriculum hidden curriculum I need it now. Nonsense report​


Answer:

Taught curriculum refers to the curriculum that is actively taught to students by teachers. The strengths of this type of curriculum include its ability to provide a clear structure and direction for teaching and learning, as well as the ability to ensure that key knowledge and skills are covered. However, a weakness of taught curriculum could be that it may be too rigid and not allow for flexibility or student-centered learning.

Supported curriculum refers to the resources and materials that are provided to teachers to assist them in delivering the taught curriculum. The strengths of this type of curriculum include the ability to provide teachers with the necessary resources to effectively teach the curriculum. A weakness may be that it might not be up to date with the latest research, or might not align with the needs and interests of the students.

Recommended curriculum refers to curriculum that is suggested or recommended by educational authorities or organizations, but is not necessarily mandatory. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide guidance and recommendations for teaching and learning, while also allowing for flexibility and local decision-making. A weakness may be that it might not be properly enforced or implemented.

Written curriculum refers to the official documents that outline the goals, objectives, and content of the curriculum. The strengths of this type of curriculum include the ability to provide a clear and measurable framework for teaching and learning, as well as the ability to ensure that key knowledge and skills are covered. A weakness may be that it might not be flexible enough to meet the needs of all students.

Learned curriculum refers to the curriculum that is actually learned by students, which may differ from the taught or written curriculum. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide insight into what students are actually learning and understanding. A weakness may be that it might not align with the goals and objectives of the taught or written curriculum.

Assessed curriculum refers to the curriculum that is evaluated or measured to determine student progress and achievement. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide a means of determining whether students are meeting the goals and objectives of the curriculum. A weakness may be that it might not be aligned with the goals of the taught or written curriculum or might not be fair or accurately measuring student learning.

Hidden curriculum refers to the implicit values, beliefs, and attitudes that are conveyed through the education system. The strength of this type of curriculum is that it can provide insight into the underlying values and beliefs of the education system. A weakness may be that it might be unconsciously conveying harmful or biased messages.
