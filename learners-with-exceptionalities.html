15. make a collection of teaching guidelines or tips on how to work with students exceptionalities .​


Answer:

1. Get to know your students: Take the time to get to know your students and their individual needs. Ask questions about their strengths, weaknesses, and any special needs they may have.

2. Create an inclusive environment: Make sure that all students feel welcome and accepted in your classroom. Provide a safe space for them to express themselves and be comfortable with who they are.

3. Utilize technology: Technology can be a great tool for helping students with exceptionalities learn more effectively. Consider using assistive technology such as voice recognition software or text-to-speech programs to help them access the curriculum.

4. Differentiate instruction: Differentiate instruction based on the individual needs of each student with exceptionalities in your classroom. This could include providing modified assignments, alternative assessments, or additional support as needed.

5. Collaborate with other professionals: Work closely with other professionals such as school psychologists, counselors, or special education teachers to ensure that you are providing the best possible support for your students with exceptionalities.

6. Advocate for your students: Advocate for your students by staying informed about their rights and making sure that they are receiving the services they need in order to succeed in school and beyond.
