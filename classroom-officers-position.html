3. The GPTA officers can paint a classroom in 3 hours, while Class officers can paint the same classroom in 5 hours. If the GPTA and Class officers work together, how long will i take them to paint the classroom?​


Answer:

1.875 hours

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the Working Together Formula:

1/T = 1/A + 1/B

where A = time of GPTA finishing the work

B = time of Class Officers finishing the work

T = total time of them working together

1/T = 1/3 + 1/5

1/T = (5+3)/15

1/T = 8/15

Cross multiply:

(8)(T) = (1)(15)

8T = 15

T = 15/8 or 1.875 hours

29. significances about The challenges that ABM students encounter in being a classroom officer or SSG officer while focusing on their study.


As a student myself, I understand the challenges that come with being a classroom officer or SSG officer while also focusing on our studies. One significant challenge is the added responsibility and workload that comes with the position. As an officer, we are expected to attend meetings, organize events, and communicate with our fellow students and faculty members. This can take up a significant amount of time and energy that could have been allocated towards studying and completing assignments.

Another challenge is the need to balance our priorities. As classroom or SSG officers, we are expected to fulfill our duties and responsibilities to the best of our abilities, but at the same time, we must also prioritize our academic performance. This means that we have to learn how to manage our time effectively, set realistic goals, and prioritize our tasks accordingly.

Furthermore, being a classroom or SSG officer also requires strong leadership skills, which can be challenging to develop and maintain while also focusing on our studies. It takes a lot of effort and dedication to be an effective leader and to ensure that our peers are motivated and engaged in the activities that we organize.

Despite these challenges, being a classroom or SSG officer can also be a rewarding experience. It allows us to develop our skills in communication, leadership, and organization, which can be valuable in our future careers. It also provides an opportunity to make a positive impact on our school community and leave a lasting legacy.

Overall, the challenges that ABM students encounter in being a classroom or SSG officer while focusing on their studies should not discourage us from taking on these roles. Instead, we should embrace the opportunity to grow and develop as individuals while making a positive impact on our school community
