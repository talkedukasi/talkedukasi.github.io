10. how did islam influence art before the coming of spanish colonizers?


Islam's Influence on Art Before The Coming of Spanish Colonizers

Even before the Spanish invaders arrived, Islam was well-known in the Southern Philippines, and it continues to be a strong and dominant cultural force. The people of the Philippines had created a semi-communal and semi-slave social structure in many areas before the advent of Spanish conquerors, as well as a feudal system in others, particularly in Mindanao and Sulu where a feudal faith known as Islam had already taken root.

Islamic culture is marked by geometric patterns that draw the believers' attention. Islam was highly established in the Southern Philippines even before the arrival of Spanish invaders. Muslims in the Philippines are aware that they are a part of a religious group called an ummah. The notion of God's unity is at the heart of Islam. This belief places an emphasis on the transient nature of things as well as the incredible majesty of the Divine Being.

When Sayyid Abubakar of Arabia landed in the Philippines in the 13th century, the Islamic Colonial Period officially began. By constructing schools so that people may learn and be able to utilize Arabic, he promoted Islam and Arabic reading and writing. People in Mindanao and Palawan, where they had previously been accepted, had previously adopted their religion. Due to the fact that 6% of the population of the Philippines is Muslim, their impact may still be seen today.

Islam was introduced to the Philippines in the 13th century by traders and missionaries. Islamic art and ethnic culture combined to create Filipino Muslim art that portrays the people's ethnic heritage and Islamic identity. Islam was primarily centered in the South during Spanish colonization and American occupation, but this did not stop Islamic art from blossoming.

17. how does Spanish artistry influence the arts and crafts of luzon​


Spanish Influence on Filipino Visual Arts. When the Spaniards arrived in the Philippines in 1521, the colonizers used art as a tool to propagate the Catholic faith through beautiful images.  They replaced the arts that were once done in a communal spirit and community setting for rituals.

Answer:

When the Spaniards arrived in the Philippines in 1521, the colonizers used art as a tool to propagate the Catholic faith through beautiful images. With communication as problem, the friars used images to explain the concepts behind Catholicism, and to tell the stories of Christ’s life and passion. Images of the Holy Family and the saints were introduced to the Filipino psyche through carved santos, the via crucis (Stations of the Cross), engravings on estampas and estampitas, and through paintings on church walls.

Though the ethnic art forms such as pottery, weaving and metalwork were retained, the Spanish friars and the Chinese, the colony’s primary trading partner, were slowly introducing newer art forms. Icons brought by the friars were used as models for sculpture. Filipino artisans were taught the Chinese brushwork technique in painting. Engraving was also introduced.

20. How did the Spanish influence the Philippines?


The Spanish influence on the Philippines was significant and lasting. Here are some ways that the Spanish influenced the Philippines:

Religion: The Spanish introduced Christianity to the Philippines, and today over 80% of the population are Roman Catholics. The Spanish also built many churches and religious schools throughout the country.Language: The Spanish language has had a lasting impact on the Philippines. Many Filipino words and phrases have Spanish origins, and Spanish loanwords are still used in the modern Filipino language.Government: The Spanish introduced a centralized form of government to the Philippines, with a governor-general appointed by the king of Spain. The Spanish also established a system of local government, with the creation of provinces and municipalities.Architecture: Spanish architecture can be seen in many old buildings in the Philippines, including churches, government buildings, and private homes.Cuisine: Spanish cuisine has also had an impact on Filipino food. Many Filipino dishes have Spanish roots, such as adobo, a popular meat dish, and paella, a rice dish.Arts and Culture: The Spanish influence can also be seen in Filipino arts and culture. Traditional Filipino dances, such as the tinikling, have Spanish influences, and many Filipino festivals, such as the Ati-Atihan and Sinulog, have Catholic origins.

Overall, the Spanish influence on the Philippines has been significant and enduring, shaping the country's history, culture, and identity.

23. what are some of the political policies made by the Spanish colonial administration that are still evident in the Philippines today?


Some of the political policies made by the Spanish colonial administration that are still evident in the Philippines today include the following :

1. The Spanish colonial administration established a centralized form of government in the Philippines, which is still evident in the country's current political system.

2. The Spanish colonial administration also implemented the policy of "divide and rule" in the Philippines, which has contributed to the country's current social and political divisions.

3. The Spanish colonial administration also implemented a policy of religious intolerance, which has resulted in the current situation in the Philippines where there is a high degree of religious intolerance and discrimination.

24. What are the influences brought about by the American and Spanish period that are still evident with the art and culture of the Philippines?​


Answer:

The Spanish influence on Filipino culture has been profound, having originated from the Spanish East Indies. A variety of aspects of the customs and traditions in the Philippines today can be traced back to this influence.

Spanish settlement in the Philippines first took place in the 1500s, during the Spanish colonial period of the islands. The conquistador Miguel López de Legazpi founded the first Spanish settlement in Cebu in 1565 and later established Manila as the capital of the Spanish East Indies in 1571. The Philippine Islands are named after King Philip. Spaniards are referred to by Filipinos as "Kastila" (Castilian) named after the former Kingdom of Castile, now a region of Spain. The majority of the Filipinos of Spanish descent are of Spaniard origin, while a very small minority are Latin American. Another term for them is Spanish Filipino.
