3. Use simple words and phrases. When you’re making word choices, pick the familiar or commonly used word over the unusual or obscure.

4. Use lots of concrete and specific words that stand for things you and your reader can see, hear, taste, touch, and smell. Concrete nouns help focus your reader’s attention. Always prefer the specific to the general, the definite to the vague, and the concrete to the abstract.
