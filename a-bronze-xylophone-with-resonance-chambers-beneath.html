4. Gong and Kempul: Hanging gongs that are struck with mallets. They produce a deep, booming sound and are used to punctuate the music.

8. Suling: A bamboo flute that is played with the fingers. It produces a high, piercing sound and is often used to play solo melodies.

9. Kendang: A horizontal drum that is played with the fingers on both ends. It provides the rhythmic backbone of the ensemble and is often used to signal changes in the music.

10. Tjelempung: A zither with thirteen double strings that is played with a plectrum. It produces a bright, ringing sound and is often used to play melodies.
