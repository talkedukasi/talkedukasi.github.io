27. identify the method of heat transfer by conduction convection or radiation1.ironing clothes​


Answer:

Heat transfers from high temperature to low temperature and is accomplished by 3 methods: conduction, convection, or radiation. Conduction occurs when moving particles in a hotter object collide with particles in a cooler object, thus causing these particles to move faster and increase in temperature. Convection occurs by utilizing the concept that hot gas or liquid will rise and move away from a heat source (and cooler gas/liquid will fall down towards the heat source), which creates currents to mix and transfer the heat throughout the sample. Radiation is heat energy transferred as infrared, which is energy that can travel through space without a medium


28. What makes it possible for clothes to become warm when pressed by a flat ironA. ConvectionB. Radiation and convectionC. ConductionD. Conduction and convection​


Answer:

C. Conduction

Explanation:

Conduction is the process by which heat energy is transmitted through collisions between neighboring atoms or molecules. Conduction occurs more readily in solids and liquids, where the particles are closer to together, than in gases, where particles are further apart.
