8. The World is an Apple Answer the questions about the play "The World is an Apple" by Alberto S. Florentino in the apple graphic organizer.lb. Who are the proletariat/ powerless people?la. Who are the bourgeoisie/ powerful people depicted in the text?( PA ANSWER PO NG MAAYOS, NEED LANG PO TALAGA TOMORROW THANKYOUUU PO!! )​


Answer:

lb. The proletariats/ powerless people in "The World is an Apple" are the poor urban dwellers, specifically the family of Mang Tomas who are struggling to make ends meet.

la. The bourgeoisie/ powerful people depicted in the text are the wealthy, represented by the character of Mario, who exploits the poor and takes advantage of their vulnerabilities for his own gain.

10 sentences answer

lb. Who are the proletariat/ powerless people?

1. The main character, Mario, is a poor and desperate man who can barely provide for his family.

2. His wife, Gloria, is also powerless and deeply concerned about their family's survival.

3. The couple's young son represents the next generation of powerless individuals who face the same challenges as their parents.

4. The street vendors who sell their wares in the same area as Mario are also part of the proletariat, struggling to make a living in a harsh environment.

5. The security guard, while employed, is also powerless in the sense that he is unable to stop the theft that occurs.

6. The landlord who owns the property where Mario and his family live is depicted as unsympathetic and indifferent to the plight of the proletariat.

7. The society at large is also complicit in perpetuating the powerlessness of the proletariat, as they do little to address the systemic issues that cause poverty and desperation.

8. Mario's friends and acquaintances, while also powerless, demonstrate the importance of community and support in times of need.

9. The vendors who sell their goods at the market are also part of the powerless proletariat, subject to the whims of a volatile customer base.

10. Ultimately, the majority of the characters in the play are part of the powerless proletariat, illustrating the prevalence and impact of systemic inequality.

la. Who are the bourgeoisie/ powerful people depicted in the text?

1. The thief who steals Mario's apples is depicted as a powerful antagonist who preys on the weak and vulnerable.

2. The character of Lola, who owns a sari-sari store in the same market, is depicted as relatively affluent and influential in the community.

3. The police officer who speaks to Mario after the theft occurs is part of a larger system of power that reinforces existing social hierarchies.

4. The wealthy and privileged members of society who ignore or dismiss the plight of the poor are also part of the bourgeoisie.

5. The contrast between the wealthy and poor is emphasized by the setting of the play, a bustling marketplace where the haves and have-nots coexist.

6. The landlord who owns the property where Mario and his family live is also part of the bourgeoisie, with power and control over the lives of his tenants.

7. The security guard, while powerless to stop the theft, is depicted as more powerful than the other characters given his employment status.

8. The juxtaposition of the thief's recklessness and the powerlessness of the proletariat highlights the imbalance of power in society.

9. Through the character of Lola, the play also illustrates the potential for upward social mobility for those who are able to access opportunities and resources.

10. Overall, the play depicts the powerful and the powerless as deeply intertwined, with the former exerting control over the latter at every turn.
