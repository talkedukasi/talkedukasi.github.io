Question:

What kind of propaganda device of McDonalds​?

Answer:

BandwagonBandwagon is a form of propaganda that exploits the desire of most people to join the crowd or be on the winning side, and avoid winding up the losing side. Few of us would want to wear nerdy cloths, smell differently from everyone else, or be unpopular. The popularity of a product is important to many people. Bandwagon argues that one must accept or reject an argument because of everyone else who accepts it or rejects it-similar to peer pressure. Examples of Bandwagon: 1. You believe that those who receive welfare should submit to a drug test, but your friends tell you that idea is crazy and they don't accept it.
