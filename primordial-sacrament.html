19. explain why jesus christ is considered the primordial sacrament cite accounts in the gospels to guide you in your explanation ​


Answer:

In Christian theology, Jesus Christ is considered the primordial sacrament because he is believed to be the ultimate sign and mediator of God's grace and presence to humanity. A sacrament is an outward sign or symbol that conveys an invisible, spiritual reality or grace. Jesus is seen as the perfect embodiment of this idea, as he is believed to be both fully human and fully divine, and through his life, death, and resurrection, he offers a visible, tangible expression of God's love and salvation.

The Gospel of John emphasizes this aspect of Jesus' identity as the primordial sacrament in several places. In John 1:14, it states that "the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth." Here, Jesus is identified as the "Word," or Logos, of God, who takes on human form to dwell among us and reveal God's glory and grace.

Similarly, in John 6:51, Jesus says, "I am the living bread that came down from heaven. If anyone eats of this bread, he will live forever. And the bread that I will give for the life of the world is my flesh." This statement emphasizes the idea that Jesus' physical body is a visible, tangible expression of God's love and grace, and that by partaking in the Eucharist (the sacrament of Communion), Christians can participate in that grace and experience eternal life.

The Gospel of Matthew also highlights Jesus' role as the primordial sacrament in the story of the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:1-9). In this account, Jesus takes three of his disciples up to a mountaintop, where he is suddenly transfigured before them, shining with divine glory. This event is seen as a manifestation of Jesus' true identity as the Son of God, and it serves as a visible sign of God's presence and power.
