16. What is counseling? Why do you think counseling is considered is discipline of the applied social science


What is counseling?

Counseling is the process of providing assistance by an expert to an individual who is experiencing a problem. The purpose of counseling, of course, is to solve the problem of the person being consulted. Counseling is a meeting between the counselor and his client that allows dialogue instead of providing therapy or treatment. Counseling also encourages problem solving by the client himself. Counseling can help solve problems experienced by clients. This effort can be done by reducing the intensity of the obstacles and or losses caused by the problem and eliminating the problem in question. With this counseling service, the hope is to lighten the client's burden and develop the client's potential. The goal of long-term counseling is for clients to find their own way so that they can rely more on themselves to deal with future life situations in a constructive way without constantly needing outside help.

The main characteristics of counseling are as follows:

Counseling is carried out by a counselor who has the ability professionally in dealing with problems related to personal, social, career and educational decisions and understands psychological processes and the dynamics of behavior in clients.Counseling involves interaction and communication between two people, namely the counselor and the client, either directly (verbal language) or indirectly (non-verbal).The purpose of the counseling relationship is the occurrence of behavioral changes in the client in accordance with the abilities and potential of the client. Counselors seek to facilitate and provide support, together with clients to make problem-solving alternatives for change for the better and in accordance with the goals to be achieved in counseling.Counseling is a dynamic process, in which individual clients are helped to develop themselves, develop their abilities in overcoming the problems at hand.Counseling is a learning process, especially for clients to develop new behaviors and make choices, decisions on their own (autonomous) towards the desired change.

Why do you think counseling is considered is discipline of the applied social science?

Counseling is considered is discipline of the applied social science because in the perspective of sociology, counseling should study how individuals interact in society. Counseling helps the process of providing assistance provided to realize a prosperous life order both for individuals, families and communities which include a sense of: safety, decency, security, order and peace both physically and mentally, this will be realized through various collaborations and shared responsibilities between the government and society.

Goal of counseling in social science is to guide:

Non-social individuals, are individuals who do not want to get along with their environment because there are barriers or reasons, these barriers can be in the form of inferiority, fear of ridicule, shame and so on.A social individual, is an individual who cannot adapt to the existing environmental conditions, or an individual who cannot fulfill his/her needs not because he is unable, but that the individual intentionally does not want or does not like to adapt to the surrounding environment. Usually the causes that become obstacles are not real, so that according to the size of the person, the individual can adjust Anti-social individuals, are individuals who are actually able to adapt or meet the demands that exist in society, but the individual is against or even against the surrounding environment.
