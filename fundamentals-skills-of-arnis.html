Answer:

1.Arnis, also known as Eskrima or Kali, is a martial art that originated in the Philippines. Here are five basic stances of Arnis:

Attention stance: This is the starting position in Arnis. Stand with your feet together, arms at your sides, and eyes focused straight ahead.

Ready stance: From the attention stance, step forward with your left foot and shift your weight to your left leg. Your right foot should be positioned diagonally behind your left foot, and your weapon hand (right hand for right-handed individuals) should be held up and ready to strike.

Offensive stance: From the ready stance, step forward with your left foot and lean forward slightly. Your weapon hand should be extended forward, ready to strike, while your other hand is held back for balance.

Defensive stance: From the ready stance, step back with your right foot and bend your knees slightly. Your weapon hand should be held back to block, while your other hand is ready to counterattack.

Back stance: This stance is used for evasion and defense. Step back with your right foot and turn it slightly outward. Your left foot should be forward, and your weight should be on your back leg. Your weapon hand should be held back for a counterattack, while your other hand is up for defense.

2.The skill-related components of Arnis include agility, balance, coordination, power, reaction time, speed, and timing. These components are essential in the execution of fundamental skills in Arnis.

Agility and coordination are important in Arnis as they enable the practitioner to move quickly and efficiently while striking and blocking with their weapons. Good balance is crucial for maintaining stability and power during strikes, especially when using one leg for support. Power, speed, and timing are critical for delivering effective strikes that can disable or disarm an opponent.

Reaction time is also important in Arnis, as practitioners need to be able to quickly respond to attacks and defend themselves. This skill is particularly crucial in sparring, where opponents are constantly trying to outmaneuver each other.

In summary, the skill-related components of Arnis are essential for the effective execution of fundamental skills in this martial art. Practitioners need to develop and maintain these skills to become proficient in Arnis and achieve success in sparring and self-defense situations.

9. What are the 5 fundamental forms of arnis​


Answer:

The first is solo baston. in this form, the arnisador fights with a single stick, called a baston. The second form is a doble baston. Here the arnisador uses

     two bastones of equal length. When the arnisador wields a long baston and a shorter one simultaneously, he is using the third form called espada. In

     those forms, the arnisador holds his stick with one hand. When the arnisador grips the stick with two hands, he would be using the fourth form, dos

     manos. In this form the stick is longer, from thirty-five to forty-five inches tip to tip. The last form is bare-hand fighting called mano-mano.
