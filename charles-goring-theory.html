I can tell you that Charles Darwin's theory of natural selection is widely accepted as the best explanation for the evidence of evolution. The theory of natural selection suggests that organisms that are better adapted to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing on their advantageous traits to their offspring. One of the key strengths of Darwin's theory is its ability to explain the wide range of observations made in the field of biology. For example, natural selection can account for the similarities and differences between different species, as well as the diversity of life on Earth.

Additionally, Darwin's theory is supported by a wealth of evidence from fields such as genetics, comparative anatomy, and biogeography. 8. Charles darwin has been published of what theory?


Charles Robert Darwin (February 12, 1809 to April 19, 1882) was a naturalist and biologist known for his theory of evolution and the process of natural selection. Born in Shrewsbury, England, in 1831 he embarked on a five-year survey voyage around the world on the HMS Beagle; his studies of specimens led him to formulate his theories. In 1859, he published On the Origin of Species. Darwin’s theory of evolution declared that species survived through a process called "natural selection," where those that successfully adapted, or evolved, to meet the changing requirements of their natural habitat thrived, while those that failed to evolve and reproduce died off. Through his observations and studies of birds, plants and fossils, Darwin noticed similarities among species all over the globe, along with variations based on specific locations, leading him to believe that the species we know today had gradually evolved from common ancestors. Darwin’s theory of evolution and the process of natural selection later became known simply as “Darwinism.”

At the time, other naturalists believed that all species either came into being at the start of the world or were created over the course of natural history. In either case, they believed species remained much the same throughout time.In 1858, after years of scientific investigation, Darwin publically introduced his revolutionary theory of evolution in a letter read at a meeting of the Linnean Society. On November 24, 1859, he published a detailed explanation of his theory in his best-known work, On the Origin of Species by Means of Natural Selection.

In the next century, DNA studies provided scientific evidence for Darwin’s theory of evolution. However controversy surrounding its conflict with Creationism — the religious view that all of nature was born of God — still abounds today.

11. Make a summary about Charles Darwin theory of evolution​


Who was Charles Darwin?

Born in 1809, Charles Robert Darwin was an English naturalist whose most important work was his book On the Origin of Species. It is in this book that Darwin brings a theory that revolutionized the way we look at the history of species, being considered the father of the “Theory of Evolution of Species”.

The life of Charles Darwin:

Initially, Darwin aimed to become a doctor, like his family members, but after an incident in class, he dropped out of the career and focused his studies on a Bachelor of Arts in Cambridge.

Darwin then met John Stevens Henslow, who invited him to travel around the world as a naturalist aboard the ship Beagle. When passing through the Galapagos Islands, Darwin noticed characteristics of the animals present there that instigated him to reflect, which culminated in his theory of natural selection.

According to the naturalist, at some point in history, a characteristic appears in the individual that is perfectly suited to the environment in which he lives. In this way, this characteristic ends up being transmitted to its offspring, which guarantees the survival of the species. On the other hand, those characteristics that are considered bad for the individual in the environment become less frequent and end up disappearing in the following generations.

Charles Darwin's Personal Life:

As a result of a heart attack, Charles Darwin died in 1882. His family was very religious, he married his first cousin Emma Wedgwood, with whom he had ten children.

15. 3 stages of the Theory of Evolution ni Charles Darwin


variation,heredity, and the struggle for existence

Explanation:

Beginning in 1837, Darwin proceeded to work on the now well-understood concept that evolution is essentially brought about by the interplay of three principles: (1) variation—a liberalizing factor, which Darwin did not attempt to explain, present in all forms of life; (2) heredity—the conservative force that transmits similar organic form from one generation to another; and (3) the struggle for existence—which determines the variations that will confer advantages in a given environment, thus altering species through a selective reproductive rate.
