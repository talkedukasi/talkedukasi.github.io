7. Programa ni Fidel V. Ramos​


Fidel V. Ramos was the 12th President of the Philippines and served from 1992 to 1998. During his presidency, he implemented several programs and initiatives to improve the country's economy, infrastructure, and peace and security. Here are some of the major programs and policies that he introduced:

Philippines 2000: This was a long-term development plan that aimed to make the Philippines a newly industrialized country by the year 2000. It focused on improving the country's infrastructure, agriculture, and industry, as well as attracting foreign investment.

Economic reforms: Ramos implemented several economic reforms, including the liberalization of trade and investment, deregulation of industries, and privatization of government assets. These policies helped to attract foreign investment and spur economic growth.

Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program (CARP): Ramos continued the implementation of the Comprehensive Agrarian Reform Program, which aimed to distribute land to farmers and promote rural development.

Peace and order initiatives: Ramos launched several peace and order initiatives, including the establishment of a national police force and the adoption of a comprehensive anti-crime plan. He also negotiated peace agreements with various rebel groups, including the Moro National Liberation Front and the National Democratic Front.

Education reforms: Ramos introduced several education reforms, including the creation of the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority (TESDA) and the K-12 basic education program.

Environmental initiatives: Ramos launched several initiatives to protect the environment, including the Clean Air Act and the National Integrated Protected Areas System Act.

Overall, Fidel V. Ramos' presidency was characterized by a strong focus on economic development, peace and security, and good governance.

20. Fidel V Ramos Fiscal policy


Answer:

Fidel V. Ramos, the 12th President of the Philippines, implemented various fiscal policies during his administration from 1992 to 1998. Here are some of his notable fiscal policies:

1. Fiscal Responsibility Law - In 1994, Ramos signed Republic Act No. 7354 or the Fiscal Responsibility Law, which aims to promote transparency and accountability in government finances. This law requires the government to submit to Congress a detailed report on its fiscal performance and financial position.

2. Comprehensive Tax Reform Program (CTRP) - Ramos also introduced the CTRP in 1995, which aimed to simplify the tax system, increase revenue, and improve tax compliance. The CTRP included the following measures:

• Reduction of income tax rates for individuals and corporations

•Expansion of the value-added tax (VAT) base and increase in the VAT rate

•Imposition of excise taxes on certain products such as gasoline, tobacco, and alcoholic beverages

3. Privatization - Ramos continued the privatization program started by his predecessor, Corazon Aquino. The privatization of state-owned enterprises generated revenue for the government, reduced the burden of subsidies, and improved the efficiency of public services.

4. Infrastructure development - Ramos also focused on infrastructure development, particularly in transportation and communication. He initiated the construction of various highways, bridges, airports, and seaports, as well as the expansion of telecommunication networks.

Overall, Ramos' fiscal policies aimed to promote fiscal discipline, increase government revenue, and improve the delivery of public services. His policies contributed to the country's economic growth during his presidency.

28. sino c Fidel V RAMOS,pls po​


Answer:

Fidel Valdez Ramos (Spanish: [fiˈðel βalˈdes ra.mos]; born Fidel Ramos y Valdez; March 18, 1928),[3] popularly known as FVR and Eddie, is a retired Filipino general and politician who served as the 12th president of the Philippines from 1992 to 1998. He is the only career military officer who reached the rank of five-star general/admiral de jure who rose from second lieutenant up to commander-in-chief of the armed forces. During his six years in office, Ramos was widely credited and admired by many for revitalizing and renewing international confidence in the Philippine economy. At age 93, he is currently the oldest living former Philippine president.

He ascended the ranks in the Philippine military early in his career and became Chief of the Philippine Constabulary and Vice Chief-of-Staff of the Armed Forces of the Philippines during the term of President Ferdinand Marcos.

During the 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution, Ramos was hailed as a hero by many Filipinos for his decision to break away from the administration of President Marcos and pledge allegiance and loyalty to the newly established government of President Corazon Aquino.

Prior to his election as president, Ramos served in the cabinet of President Corazón Aquino, first as chief-of-staff of the Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP), and later as Secretary of National Defense from 1986 to 1991.[4] He was credited for the creation of the Philippine Army's Special Forces and the Philippine National Police Special Action Force.
