5. 1.ano po ang price ceiling, price control , price support at floor price.2. Ipaliwanag kung paanong nakikialam ang pamahalaan sa pagtakda ng presyo sa pamilihan..?


1.) A price ceiling is a government-imposed price control or limit on how high a price is charged for a product. Price controls are governmental restrictions on the prices that can be charged for goods and services in a market .A price support may be either a subsidy or a price control, both with the intended effect of keeping the market price of a good higher than the competitive equilibrium level. A price floor is a government- or group-imposed price control or limit on how low a price can be charged for a product. A price floor must be higher than the equilibrium price in order to be effective.

6. Ano ang masasabi mo about sa price control? ​


PRICE CONTROL

WHAT IS PRICE CONTROL?

Ang Price Control ay ipinatupad ng pamahalaan o regulasyon ng gobyerno sa pagtatalaga ng pinakamataas na maaaring ipataw sa mga pangunahing produkto at serbisyo, sa gitna ng isang sakuna tulad ng kalamidad ay digmaan.

Kahalagahan

• Sa aking palagay ito ay isang magandang regulasyon upang hindi abusuhin ng mga negosyante at palengke ang pagtitinda ng mga produkto.

• Ito ay magandang paraan din upang patuloy na makabili ng mga murang produkto ang mga tao sa gitna ng krisis.

3. A.

      A price ceiling is a price limitation, or limit, established by the government or a group on how much a product, commodity, or service costs. Price limitations are reportedly used by governments to safeguard consumers against situations that may make items too expensive. Such conditions can develop during periods of excessive inflation, an investment bubble, or monopolistic ownership of a commodity, all of which can cause issues if applied over an extended length of time without managed rationing, resulting in shortages. Further issues might arise if a government imposes unreasonable pricing caps, resulting in corporate bankruptcies, stock market crashes, and even economic catastrophes. Price ceilings do not exist in unregulated market economies.

    While governments frequently impose price limitations, there are also price caps applied by non-governmental entities such as businesses, such as the practice of resale price maintenance. A manufacturer and its distributors agree to sell the manufacturer's goods at certain pricing (resale price maintenance), at or below a price ceiling (maximum resale price maintenance), or at or above a price floor.
