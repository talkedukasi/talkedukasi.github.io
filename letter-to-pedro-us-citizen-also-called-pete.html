11. the school also the nurseries of the citizens of the state. what are nurseries for? why are schools called the nurseries of the citizens of the state? ​


Yes, the school are considered nurseries of the state citizens. Usually, nurseries are places used to grow plants by nurturing them with enough light, water, and nutrients until plants mature that can be grown outside the nurseries. Schools are compared to nurseries because all children of any State enter school to achieve the right education and proper training to become a productive citizen in the future. Also, schools pass on beyond knowledge and literacy skills, it also teaches cultural norms and values to young generations. Therefore, school life is one of the most important periods in a man’s life that shape the totality of a person.  

Characteristics of a Good School

A good school adapts quickly to social change and possess the following qualities:

The learning outcome is clear and aligned with the school's mission and vision. Set high standards for all learners to maximize their full potential. Competent school administrators, teachers, and non-teaching personnel. Teamwork and communication are evident. The curriculum is aligned in the teaching-learning process. Regular monitoring of teaching-learning process vis-à-vis to the learning outcome. Continuous professional development among administrators, teachers, and non-teaching personnel. The school itself is conducive for learning and adhere to Child protection policy and related laws. Partnership programs between schools, parents, and the community are engaged in promoting quality education to all learners.

Components of a School

Every school has its unique components essential for the school operation. Here are some fundamental components of the school establishment to operate:

Spacious and well-ventilated classrooms .Offices for school heads, faculty room, and auxiliary offices such as registrar, clinic, guidance office, and accounting office to perform their duties effectively. Laboratories such as computer laboratories and science laboratories. Library with sufficient collections to support learning. Activity areas like a playground (for nursery and elementary) and gymnasium or court (for secondary and college). Well maintained school garden .Flag pole with a flag in good condition. Clean toilets and lavatories. School cafeteria that serves nutritious food and potable water.

19. Rewrite the following sentences using the adverbs in the parentheses. Pete feels terrible. (usually)


Task 6: Controlled Practice

Answer:

Adverb is a part of speech that has the same function as the adjective which describes a noun and pronouns. While adjective focuses on beings/situations, adverb focuses on the action word. It describes a verb, adjective and another adverb.  It is essential that these two exist to add information or description about a certain thing or event.

Adverb has 5 kinds, these are:

Adverbs of time- tells when the action was made

For example: during; later; yesterday; recently; soon

Adverbs of manner- describes how an action or activity is performed

For example: selfishly; quietly; suspiciously; successfully; courageously

Adverbs of degree-describes the intensity of an action

For example: very; extreme; enough; too; just

Adverbs of place- indicates location

For example: outside; somewhere; nowhere; here

Adverbs of frequency- describes how often an action is done

For example: regularly; seldom; often; frequently; generally

Adverbs should answer the questions:

When? Where? How? Why? To what extent?

Pete feels terrible. (usually)

Peter usually feels terrible.
