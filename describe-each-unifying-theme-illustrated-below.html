16. Directions: Describe each unifying theme illustrated below. Give details on how thesethemes serve as the foundation in the study of biology.1. growth and developmentcontinuity of life2. organization of life acquire and process energy​


UNIFYING THEME: FOUNDATION IN THE STUDY OF BIOLOGY

1. Growth and development continuity of Life

               Life is a continuous process, together with this, all living things will grow and develop continuously through time. Life ends, life starts, hence life is a cycle.

               Humans will grow in many aspects -- physically, mentally, emotionally, and spiritually. As we, humans are growing we are also developing. When humans mature, we develop lot of things, in physical aspect, the development involve changes in our body, motor skills, and senses. Mental skills or cognitive growth involves our ability to learn, our memories, thinking, and reasoning. As we are continuing to grow, there are also development or changes in our emotions, the way we act to our experiences, and on how we handle and interpret different feelings.

                 All living things, not only us humans are growing and developing. Plants and animals also, even bacteria and viruses are evolving. Because that's how life is -- through reproduction and death, life will come and go, will grow and develop and will eventually die, but the continuity of life will never end, as it is passed through one generation to another.

2. Organization of life acquire and process energy​

                 All processes require and process energy. A give and take relationship. Life needs energy to survive, and gives off energy to help others to live. These life processes are done and needed by different life organizations, from cells, organs, organisms, human, animals, plants, ecosystems, communities, and the whole biosphere.

                 The most obvious explanation or example on how life organization acquire and process energy is the process of respiration. Cellular respiration is the process by which organisms, for example, us humans, use oxygen to break down food molecules to get chemical energy for cell functions, carbon dioxide is released in this process and it is needed to process plant's food through photosynthesis. Photosynthesis acquires light energy from the sun and transform it to chemical energy. These two processes that involve different organization of life are working together in order to survive, thus, they acquire and process energy altogether.
