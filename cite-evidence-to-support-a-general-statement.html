16. what are the steps in citing evidence to support a general statement


Answer:

1. Identify the general statement you wish to support.

2. Gather evidence that supports the statement.

3. Cite the evidence using an appropriate citation style (e.g., MLA, APA).

4. Explain how the evidence supports the statement.

5. Repeat steps 2-4 for additional evidence, if necessary.

21. Present the importance of citing evidence to support a general statement


Answer:

Citing evidence is crucial when making a general statement because it allows you to back up your claim with reliable sources and facts. Without evidence, a general statement is just an opinion or an assumption, and it lacks the credibility and validity necessary to persuade or convince others.

When you cite evidence, you demonstrate that your statement is based on reliable sources, research, and data. This makes your argument stronger and more persuasive. Moreover, citing evidence allows others to verify the accuracy of your claims and to learn more about the topic at hand.

Citing evidence also helps to avoid plagiarism, which is the act of presenting someone else's work as your own. By citing your sources, you give credit to the original author or researcher and avoid any accusations of plagiarism.

In academic writing, citing evidence is particularly important because it demonstrates your ability to engage critically with the topic and to evaluate the quality of the sources you use. It also shows that you have done your research and have a deep understanding of the subject matter.

In conclusion, citing evidence is essential when making a general statement. It adds credibility and validity to your argument, helps to avoid plagiarism, and demonstrates your ability to engage critically with the topic.
