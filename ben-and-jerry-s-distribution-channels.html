21. how long does it take for Ben's car to change its velocity from 12 m/s to 25 m/s if the acceleration is 5 m/s²​


Calculating time with the change of velocity.

Given

Initial Velocity (Vi) = 12 m/sFinal Velocity (Vf) = 25 m/sAcceleration (a) = 5 m/s²Time (t) = ?

t = Vf - Vi / a

t = 25 - 12 / 5

t = 13 / 5

t = 2.6 s

25. Ben is running at a speed of 3m/s. How long will it take him travel 720 meters?​


Answer:

240 s or 4 minutes

Explanation:

To find the time it takes for Ben to travel 720 meters at a speed of 3m/s, we can use the formula:

time = distance / speed

Plugging in the given values, we get:

time = 720 meters / 3 m/s

Simplifying, we get:

time = 240 seconds

Therefore, it will take Ben 240 seconds or 4 minutes to travel 720 meters at a speed of 3m/s.

30. Ben Rushin is waiting at a stoplight. When it finally turns green, Ben accelerated from rest at a rate of a 6.00 m/s^2 for a time of 4.10 seconds. Determine the displacement of Ben's car during this time period.​


Given: Vi = 0m/s

a = 6.00 m/s²

t = 4.10 s

Unknown: d = ?

Formula: d = Vit + 1/2 at

where in, d = displacement

Vi = initial velocity

a = acceleration

t = time

Solution: d = Vit + 1/2 at

= (0m/s)(4.10s) + 1/2 (6.00m/s²)(4.10s)

= 0m + 24.6m

= 24.6m

Final Answer: 24.6m
