2. what is the meaning of Otjize​


Answer:

Otjize is a mixture of butterfat and ochre pigment used by the Himba people of Namibia to protect themselves from the harsh desert climate. The paste is often perfumed with the aromatic resin of Commiphora multijuga (omuzumba). The mixture lends the skin a deep orange or reddish tinge.

7. Meaning of GRIOT, UCU, OTJIZE, LOBOLA, ORI​


Answer:

GRIOT

    - a member of a class of traveling poets, musicians, and storytellers who maintain a tradition of oral history in parts of West Africa.

UCU

    -the word ucu means a single string. The context in which it is used as part refers to a single string of beads (ucu lobuhlalu). Literally translated ucu olumhlophe means a single string of white beads.

OTJIZE

    - is a mixture of butterfat and ochre pigment used by the Himba people of Namibia to protect themselves from the harsh desert climate.

LOBOLA

    - (among southern African peoples) a bride price, especially one paid with cattle.

    - the practice of paying a bride price.

ORI

- (known as Orí in Latin America) is a Yoruba metaphysical concept. Ori, literally meaning "head," refers to one's spiritual intuition and destiny.
