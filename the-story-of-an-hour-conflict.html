Answer:

1. BACKGROUND INFORMATION OF THE PLOT:

"The Story of an Hour" is a short story by Kate Chopin that was first published in 1894. The story is set in the late 19th century and revolves around the life of Louise Mallard, a woman with a heart condition, who is informed of her husband's death in a railroad accident. The plot of the story revolves around Louise's emotional journey, which leads her to realize her newfound sense of independence.

2. THREE MAJOR EVENTS THAT ADD SUSPENSE OR TENSION TO THE PLOT THAT LEAD TO THE CLIMAX:

a) Louise's initial reaction to the news of her husband's death, where she begins sobbing and goes upstairs to be alone in her room, creates suspense and tension in the plot.

b) Louise's realization of her newfound sense of freedom, as she gazes out the window and repeats the word "free" to herself, adds to the suspense and tension of the plot.

c) Brently's unexpected return at the end of the story, after Louise has already started to embrace her newfound independence, adds a twist to the plot and leads to the climax.

3.THE MOST SUSPENSEFUL PART OF THE PLOT:

The most suspenseful part of the plot is when Louise realizes her newfound sense of freedom and begins repeating the word "free" to herself. The buildup of emotions within her, her racing heart, and warm feelings add to the tension and suspense in the story.

4.TWO EVENTS THAT UNRAVEL THE CONFLICT BETWEEN THE PROTAGONIST AND ANTAGONIST THAT LEAD TO THE RESOLUTION:

a) Louise's realization that she has been oppressed in her marriage and her newfound sense of independence help to resolve the conflict between herself and her husband.

b) Brently's unexpected return and Louise's subsequent death help to resolve the conflict in a tragic way.

5.EVENT IN THE STORY THAT THE CONFLICT IS RESOLVED:
