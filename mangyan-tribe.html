24. The Mangyan tribe is in danger because of an unidentified disease that kills their people. They make smoke to ask for help from their neighboring tribe. What form of communication was used by the Mangyan tribe?​


Answer:

The form of communication used by the Mangyan tribe in this scenario is a form of nonverbal communication known as smoke signaling. Smoke signaling is a method of sending messages over long distances by creating smoke that can be seen from a distance. The Mangyan tribe in this scenario made smoke to ask for help from their neighboring tribe, by creating smoke they signal to the neighboring tribe that they need help, and it is a way to communicate their distress.

This type of communication is often used by indigenous people who live in remote areas and do not have access to modern communication technologies, such as phones or radios. Smoke signaling is a quick and effective way to send messages over long distances, and it can be used to communicate a variety of messages, such as distress calls, warning messages, and invitations to gather or trade.
