11. 1. why does a drafter need a T-square?​


Answer:

A drafter uses a T-square to help them draw straight, horizontal lines and to ensure the accuracy of their drawings. The T-square is a tool that consists of a long blade with a perpendicular handle at one end, creating a "T" shape. The blade of the T-square is usually made of wood, plastic, or metal, and has a straight edge that is used to guide the drawing instrument, such as a pencil or pen, along the paper. The handle of the T-square is held by the drafter to keep the blade perpendicular to the edge of the paper.

The T-square is particularly useful for drafting technical drawings and architectural plans that require precise measurements and straight lines. By using a T-square, drafters can ensure that their lines are straight and parallel to the edge of the paper, without the need for additional tools or measurements. This saves time and increases the accuracy of the drawing.
