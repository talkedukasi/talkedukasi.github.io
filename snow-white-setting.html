Snow White and the Huntsman is a 2012 American fantasy film based on the German fairy tale "Snow White" compiled by the Brothers Grimm. The film is the directorial debut of Rupert Sanders, with a screenplay by Evan Daugherty, John Lee Hancock and Hossein Amini, from a screen story by Daugherty. The cast includes Kristen Stewart, Charlize Theron, Chris Hemsworth, Sam Claflin, and Bob Hoskins in his final film performance. In the film's retelling of the tale, Snow White grows up imprisoned by her evil stepmother, Queen Ravenna, a powerful sorceress. After Snow White escapes into the forest, Ravenna tells Eric, the Huntsman, that she will bring back his dead wife if he captures Snow White.

Snow White and the Huntsman was theatrically released in the United Kingdom on May 30, 2012 and in the United States on June 1, 2012, by Universal Pictures. It was a success at the box office, earning $396 million worldwide against a $170 million budget. Although critics praised the production design and visual effects, Theron and Hemsworth's performances, and the musical score and action sequences, Stewart and Claflin's performances received mixed reviews, and the screenplay was heavily criticized. The film received two Oscar nominations for Best Visual Effects and Best Costume Design at the 85th Academy Awards.

A prequel/sequel, titled The Huntsman: Winter's War, directed by the first film's visual effects supervisor Cedric Nicolas-Troyan, was released on April 22, 2016. Hemsworth, Theron, Claflin and Nick Frost reprised their roles and new characters were played by Emily Blunt and Jessica Chastain. Stewart did not reprise her role, but appeared in archive footage.

The story of Cinderella and Snow White are both classic fairy tales that have been retold many times in different cultures. Both stories are about young women who are mistreated by their stepmothers and go on to find happiness and fulfillment with the help of magical beings.

The theme of both stories is similar in that they both revolve around the idea of inner beauty and the triumph of good over evil. Cinderella and Snow White are both kind, gentle, and pure of heart, and they are ultimately rewarded for their goodness.

The main conflict in both stories is between the young women and their stepmothers. In Cinderella, the stepmother is cruel and jealous, and she mistreats Cinderella and tries to keep her from attending the royal ball. In Snow White, the stepmother is also jealous and evil, and she plots to kill Snow White out of fear that she will be more beautiful than her.

The plot of both stories follows a similar pattern, with the young women facing hardships and challenges, but ultimately triumphing and finding happiness. In Cinderella, she is forced to do all the household chores and is prevented from going to the ball, but with the help of her fairy godmother, she is able to attend and catch the eye of the prince. In Snow White, she is forced to flee from her stepmother and takes refuge with a group of dwarves, but with the help of a prince, she is able to defeat her stepmother and live happily ever after.

The setting of both stories is a typical fairy tale kingdom, with a castle, a royal court, and a beautiful countryside. In Cinderella, the setting is a wealthy kingdom with a royal ball and a palace, while in Snow White, the setting is a forest filled with magical creatures like dwarves and talking animals.

The main characters in both stories are Cinderella and Snow White, as well as their stepmothers and the princes who help them. Cinderella and Snow White are both kind, gentle, and beautiful, and they are both mistreated by their stepmothers. The stepmothers are evil and jealous, and they both try to harm the young women in order to keep them from finding happiness. The princes in both stories are handsome and brave, and they help the young women to overcome their hardships and find their happily ever after.

24. Identify the intertextuality made between the films Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs and Snow White and the Huntsman. You can use the literary elements in providing at least 3 answers. 1. Setting 2. Plot 3. Characters


Answer:

Intertextuality refers to the relationship between texts, where one text refers to or is influenced by another text. In the case of Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs and Snow White and the Huntsman, here are three examples of intertextuality:

1. Setting: Both films share the same setting of a dark forest where Snow White seeks refuge from the Evil Queen. In both films, the forest is portrayed as a dangerous place where the protagonist is vulnerable and alone.

2. Characters: Both films feature the main character of Snow White, as well as the Evil Queen who wants to harm Snow White. In both films, the dwarfs are also present as loyal companions to Snow White.

3. Plot: Both films follow a similar plot in which Snow White is pursued by the Evil Queen and must go into hiding with the help of the dwarfs. In both films, the Huntsman is also a prominent character who helps Snow White escape from the Queen's clutches.

Overall, Snow White and the Huntsman can be seen as an intertextual adaptation of Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, as it borrows and transforms elements from the original story.

Answer:

Setting: Both films take place in a fantastical, medieval world with magical creatures and enchanted forests. However, Snow White and the Huntsman takes a darker and more Gothic approach to the setting, with a gritty and ominous atmosphere that reflects the villainous Queen's malevolence. This contrast to the bright, colorful and cheerful setting of the Disney classic, which has a more whimsical and fairy tale-like environment.

Plot: Both films revolve around Snow White, the titular character, and her battle against the wicked Queen. However, Snow White and the Huntsman features a more complex and mature plot, with a focus on themes such as power, revenge, and betrayal. The story also adds a twist by introducing the Huntsman, who is tasked with killing Snow White but ends up becoming her ally and protector. In contrast, Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs has a simpler plot that focuses on the heroine's journey to escape from the Queen's wrath and her eventual rescue by the Prince.

Characters: Both films feature the same cast of characters, including Snow White, the Queen, and the Seven Dwarfs. However, Snow White and the Huntsman presents a much darker and complex version of the characters. The Queen, for instance, is portrayed as a tragic figure who is consumed by her desire for power and beauty. The Huntsman, on the other hand, is a rough-edged and conflicted character who undergoes a redemptive arc as he helps Snow White defeat the Queen. In contrast, the characters in Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs are more one-dimensional, with the Queen being portrayed as pure evil, and the Dwarfs being presented as simple, lovable characters who help Snow White.
