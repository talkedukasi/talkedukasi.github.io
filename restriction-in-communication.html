15. 2 examples of Restriction communicative strategy


Two Examples of Restriction Communicative Strategy:

1. Constraints on the delivery process (process barrier) -> do not master the materials.

2. Psychosocial barriers -> not good in connotation.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

Restriction in communication usually occur at the time of delivery of messages from the communicator to the communicant, often there is no understanding as desired, in fact there is a misunderstanding. the message cannot be received perfectly due to the disparity of symbols or language between what is used and what is received. Or there are other technical obstacles that lead to the idea of a smooth communication system to both parties.

1. Constraints in the delivery process (process barrier)

This obstacle can come from the communicator (sender barrier) who receives difficulties in delivering his messages, does not master the message material, and does not yet have the ability as a reliable communicator. This barrier can also be the origin of the recipient of the message (receiver barrier) because of the difficulty of the communicant in understanding the message properly. This can be caused by the low level of mastery of language, education, intellectual and so on contained in the communicant. Communication failure can also occur due to factors, the feedback (which will not be achieved), the medium barrier (the media or tool is used inappropriately) and the decoding barrier (obstacles to understand the message perfectly).

2. Psychosocial barriers

The existence of a fairly wide disparity in aspects of culture, customs, norms, perceptions and values adopted as a result of similarities, needs and hopes of the two parties who communicate are also not the same. for example, a communicator (speaker) gives the word scourge which in the big Indonesian dictionary is correct. In fact, the term in Sundanese has a good coral connotation. If the word is uttered in a speech / welcoming speech in a formal program attended by officials, figures and elders of the Sundanese people, then the picture in question (communicator) can be dropped due to a misunderstanding of the language.
