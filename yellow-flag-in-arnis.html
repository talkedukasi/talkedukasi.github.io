19. how many ways can you display the five flags in witch three are blue,and two are yellow​


Assuming that all five flags are distinct and we only care about the colors of the flags, we can solve this problem using combinations.

We want to choose 3 blue flags out of the total 5 flags and 2 yellow flags out of the remaining 2 flags. We can do this in two steps:

Step 1: Choose 3 blue flags out of 5. This can be done in C(5,3) = 10 ways, where C(n,r) denotes the number of combinations of r items that can be chosen from a set of n items.

Step 2: Choose 2 yellow flags out of the remaining 2 flags. This can be done in C(2,2) = 1 way.

To get the total number of ways of displaying the flags, we multiply the results from step 1 and step 2, giving us:

10 x 1 = 10

Therefore, there are 10 ways to display the five flags in which three are blue and two are yellow.
