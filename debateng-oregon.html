28. What is the Oxford-Oregon Debate Format?


This is also known as the Cross-Examination Debate or the Forensic Debate.  It is a traditional debate format used in elementary, high schools, and colleges all over the country.

 

The two sides of this format is Affirmative, which proves the validity of proposition, and the Negative which disproves it.

 

This debate needs more than a superficial research to a specific topic, so this requires careful research to be able to use specific information in the form of evidence to support the arguments.

 

In this debate, there is an interpellation stage coming from the Negative side, which aims to disprove or expose an idea presented to be fallacious, hence, unacceptable.  After this, the Negative side will present their arguments, and the same format of interpellation takes place, so on and forth, until the final speaker (each has 4 members) presents their arguments.  In all case, a debate moderator ensures the smooth conduct and flow of the program.
