12. RESEARCH FOR GRADE 10 (review of related literature) titled “Attractiveness of Incentive Tools Influencing the Performance and Motivation of Teachers in St. Louse de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc., Sorsogon City”


Answer:

Introduction:

Incentive tools are often used by organizations to motivate their employees to perform better. In the context of educational institutions, teachers are the most important assets as they are responsible for imparting knowledge and skills to students. The purpose of this review of related literature is to explore the attractiveness of incentive tools that influence the performance and motivation of teachers in St. Louse de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc., Sorsogon City.

Incentive Tools:

Incentive tools refer to the rewards or benefits offered to employees for their performance. In the context of educational institutions, incentive tools can include monetary rewards, promotions, recognition, and professional development opportunities. These tools are designed to motivate employees to perform better, improve their productivity, and enhance their job satisfaction.

Performance and Motivation:

Performance and motivation are closely related concepts. A motivated teacher is more likely to perform better than an unmotivated one. Motivation can be intrinsic or extrinsic. Intrinsic motivation refers to the internal drive to perform well, while extrinsic motivation refers to external rewards that motivate an individual to perform better. Incentive tools can be used to enhance both intrinsic and extrinsic motivation.

Attractiveness of Incentive Tools:

The attractiveness of incentive tools is influenced by several factors. One of the most important factors is the perceived value of the incentive. If the incentive is perceived as valuable, it is more likely to motivate teachers to perform better. Another important factor is the fairness of the incentive. If the incentive is perceived as fair, it is more likely to be attractive to teachers.

Research on Incentive Tools:

Several studies have been conducted on the effectiveness of incentive tools in motivating teachers. According to a study by Rizvi and Naveed (2012), monetary incentives are the most effective tool for motivating teachers. Another study by Kim et al. (2016) found that non-monetary incentives, such as recognition and professional development opportunities, are also effective in motivating teachers.

Conclusion:

Incentive tools are important in motivating teachers to perform better. The attractiveness of these tools is influenced by several factors, including the perceived value and fairness of the incentive. Monetary incentives are the most effective tool for motivating teachers, but non-monetary incentives can also be effective. Educational institutions should carefully design and implement incentive programs to ensure that they are attractive and effective in enhancing the performance and motivation of teachers.

Answer:

Introduction:

The education sector is a vital part of any country's development, and teachers play a crucial role in shaping the future of society. Teachers' performance and motivation depend on various factors, such as job security, incentives, and contract duration. This research aims to synthesize the state of the art about the duration of teachers' contracts and the attractiveness of incentive tools that influence the performance and motivation of teachers in St. Louise de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc. in Sorsogon City.

Duration of Teachers' Contracts:

Teachers' job security is a vital aspect that can affect their motivation and performance. The length of teachers' contracts is one of the most critical factors that influence their job security. Research has shown that longer contracts have a positive impact on teachers' performance and motivation. Longer contracts provide teachers with job security, which gives them a sense of stability and reduces their stress levels. This leads to better job satisfaction, increased productivity, and improved student outcomes.

Attractiveness of Incentive Tools:

Incentives are another critical factor that can influence teachers' motivation and performance. Incentive tools can come in various forms, such as financial rewards, professional development opportunities, and recognition programs. Research has shown that financial incentives can have a positive impact on teachers' motivation and performance. Financial incentives provide teachers with tangible rewards for their hard work and can increase their job satisfaction. Professional development opportunities and recognition programs can also be attractive incentive tools that can enhance teachers' motivation and performance.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the duration of teachers' contracts and the attractiveness of incentive tools are crucial factors that can influence the performance and motivation of teachers in St. Louise de Marillac College of Sorsogon, Inc. in Sorsogon City. Longer contracts provide teachers with job security, which can reduce their stress levels and increase their job satisfaction. Incentive tools such as financial rewards, professional development opportunities, and recognition programs can also be attractive incentives that can enhance teachers' motivation and performance. Therefore, it is essential for educational institutions to provide teachers with longer contracts and attractive incentive tools to improve their performance and motivation, ultimately leading to better student outcomes.
