22. What are the different water forms found in or near the Philippines? Describe them. ​


Answer:

The Philippines is an archipelagic country located in Southeast Asia, and it is home to various water forms such as oceans, seas, lakes, rivers, and waterfalls. Here are some descriptions of the different water forms found in or near the Philippines:

Oceans: The Philippines is surrounded by several oceans, including the Pacific Ocean, the South China Sea, and the Celebes Sea. These oceans provide the country with an abundant supply of fish and other seafood, making fishing a significant industry in the country.

Seas: The Philippines is also home to several seas, including the Sulu Sea, the Visayan Sea, and the Philippine Sea. These seas provide habitats for various marine species, including coral reefs, fish, and other aquatic animals.

Lakes: The Philippines has several lakes, including Lake Lanao, Lake Taal, and Lake Mainit. These lakes provide freshwater to surrounding communities and are also popular tourist destinations.

Rivers: The Philippines has numerous rivers, including the Cagayan River, the Agusan River, and the Pampanga River. These rivers provide freshwater for irrigation and are also used for transportation and recreation.

Waterfalls: The Philippines is home to several beautiful waterfalls, including the Pagsanjan Falls, the Tinago Falls, and the Kawasan Falls. These waterfalls attract tourists who enjoy hiking, swimming, and taking in the natural scenery.

Overall, the various water forms found in or near the Philippines provide the country with natural resources, economic opportunities, and beautiful scenery for both locals and tourists to enjoy.
