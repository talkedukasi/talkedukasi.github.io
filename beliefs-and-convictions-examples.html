16. example of 5 beliefs and 5 convictions ​


BELIEFS AND CONVICTIONS:

Beliefs - A person's thoughts, views, or convictions are referred to as beliefs. These convictions are frequently derived from past experiences, cultural background, or religious convictions.

Many things, including family values, social conventions, education, and religious teachings, might have an impact on them. Broad philosophical or spiritual concepts to particular attitudes and perceptions about oneself, other people, and the world are all examples of beliefs.

They have the power to influence one's behavior, choices, and worldview as well as how they interact with others and society at large.

EXAMPLES:

Beliefs:

I believe that honesty is always the best policy.I believe that everyone deserves to be treated with respect and dignity.I believe that hard work and dedication can lead to success.I believe that love and kindness can make the world a better place.I believe that education is important for personal and societal growth.

Convictions - Strong, firmly held convictions or principles are what an individual considers to be true and which direct their behavior and choices.

Convictions, in contrast to beliefs, are typically strongly held and challenging to change. Beliefs, on the other hand, might be more malleable and amenable to change depending on new knowledge or experiences.

Personal values, moral or ethical principles, political or religious beliefs, or a feeling of justice or fairness can all serve as its foundations. Even in the face of hardship or criticism, convictions may motivate individuals to act, defend their values, and make sacrifices for them.

In difficult circumstances, they may also provide as a source of resiliency and strength, enabling people to uphold their morals and convictions.

EXAMPLES:

Convictions:

I am convinced that climate change is a pressing issue that requires immediate action.I am convinced that everyone should have access to affordable healthcare.I am convinced that racial and social justice are essential for a fair and equitable society.I am convinced that democratic institutions and processes are crucial for upholding the rule of law and protecting individual rights.I am convinced that violence and hate have no place in our communities and should be condemned and actively opposed.

23. give me the example what is the between belief and conviction ​


Answer:

Belief and conviction are two related but distinct concepts.

A belief is something that you hold to be true, either based on evidence, personal experience, or simply because you were told it was true. For example, you may believe that the Earth is round, that climate change is real, or that a particular political party is the best choice for the country.

A conviction, on the other hand, is a strong belief that you hold so firmly that you are willing to act on it, even if it goes against popular opinion or societal norms. Convictions are often deeply ingrained in a person's values, and they are typically not easily shaken or changed.

For example, you may believe that it is important to stand up for what you believe in, even if it means facing criticism or opposition. This belief may become a conviction if you are willing to take actions that demonstrate your commitment to this principle, such as participating in protests or speaking out against injustice, even if it may be risky or unpopular.

In summary, belief is a general acceptance of something as true, while conviction is a deep and unwavering commitment to a belief, often involving action or sacrifice.

Answer:

Conviction & Belief

Although the words conviction and belief appear similar in meaning at times, there is a difference between the two words, conviction and belief. First, let us define these two words. A belief or else to believe is to consider that something is correct and true. For example, a person can believe in various things such as fate, destiny, alien life, etc. A belief is an opinion. A conviction, however, is a bit different to a belief. A conviction is something that an individual is convinced of. To be convinced the individual needs to obtain all the information. It is based on this information that the conviction is built. Hence, unlike a belief that changes with time and new experiences, a conviction remains the same. Through this article, let us examine the difference between a belief and a conviction.

What is Belief?

Simply, belief is something that an individual considers as the truth. As human beings, we all have our very own belief systems. For example, let us take religion. In each religion, there are various beliefs. These beliefs are different from one religion to another. Followers of various religions consider these sets of beliefs as their reality. They acknowledge these beliefs and make it a part of them.

What is Conviction?

A conviction is a firm belief that an individual has on a particular subject. Conviction is usually a bit different to a belief because an individual becomes convinced of something after he has a thorough knowledge and understanding of all the available information. It is based on the information that the conviction is created. Once a conviction has been formed, it is difficult for the individual to see the world in a different light. He only sees things in the light of his conviction.
