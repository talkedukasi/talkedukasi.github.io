11. B. Write five-sentence paragraph using the graphic organizer (concept map) indicating the factual description information about Dr Jose P. Rizal. ​


Answer:

1. José Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda was a Filipino nationalist, writer, and polymath during the tail end of the Spanish colonial period of the Philippines.

2. Dr. Jose Rizal is considered as the national hero (pambansang bayani) of the Philippines.[8][9] An ophthalmologist by profession, Rizal became a writer and a key member of the Filipino Propaganda Movement, which advocated political reforms for the colony under Spain.

3. He was executed by the Spanish colonial government for the crime of rebellion after the Philippine Revolution, inspired in part by his writings, broke out. Though he was not actively involved in its planning or conduct, he ultimately approved of its goals which eventually led to Philippine independence.

4. He is widely considered one of the greatest heroes of the Philippines and has been recommended to be so honored by an officially empaneled National Heroes Committee. However, no law, executive order or proclamation has been enacted or issued officially proclaiming any Filipino historical figure as a national hero.

5. He was the author of the novels Noli Me Tángere and El filibusterismo, and a number of poems and essays.

Explanation:

He expressed the growing national consciousness of many Filipinos who opposed Spanish colonial tyranny and aspired to attain democratic rights

23. Directions: Write 5 qualities of Jose Rizal based on the text that you have read. Write your answer inside the graphic organizer.​


Answer:

Explanation:

1. open-minded

Jose Rizal does not agree with violent methods and that is why he refused Andres Bonifacio’s proposal of a revolution that mimics the French Revolution. Although they are different in that path, he still heeded what Bonifacio said and treated him as an equal.

2. Intelligent

Everyone knows that Jose Rizal is hyperintelligent because he studied different fields or topics. He is also fluent in more than 10 languages. He is a perpetual learner and never stops being curious about things and life.  

3. Hardworking

Even during his exile and imprisonment, he was very productive. He built a water pipe system for a farm that still exists today in Zamboanga and completed El Filibusterismo during his imprisonment.  

4. Respect for women

Given his reputation of having paramours, his respect for women is not limited only for romantic purposes but he generally respects women during a time that they are oppressed. From his mother, sisters, lovers and to women in general, he showed respect for the existence of their gender.  

5. Patriotism

Jose Rizal is well-educated and multi-talented but he chose to offer that to our country who almost has nothing. He had nothing but vision and mission for the Philippines and Filipinos
