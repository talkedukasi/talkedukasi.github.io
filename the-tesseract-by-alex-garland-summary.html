3. What is a tesseract?​


Answer:

Definition of Tesseract

The tesseract, also known as a hypercube, is a four-dimensional cube, or, alternatively, it is the extension of the idea of ​​a square to a four-dimensional space in the same way that a cube is the extension of the idea of ​​a square to a three-dimensional space

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In geometry, the tesseract is the four-dimensional analogue of the cube; the tesseract is to the cube as the cube is to the square.

Just as the surface of the cube consists of six square faces, the hypersurface of the tesseract consists of eight cubical cells. The tesseract is one of the six convex regular 4-polytopes.

translation;

Sa geometry, ang tesseract ay ang four-dimensional analogue ng cube; ang tesseract ay sa kubo bilang ang kubo ay sa parisukat.

Kung paanong ang ibabaw ng kubo ay binubuo ng anim na parisukat na mukha, ang hypersurface ng tesseract ay binubuo ng walong cubical cell. Ang tesseract ay isa sa anim na convex na regular na 4-polytopes.

Simply put, a tesseract is a cube in 4-dimensional space. You could also say that it is the 4D analog of a cube. It is a 4D shape where each face is a cube. The tesseract can be unfolded into eight cubes into 3D space, just as the cube can be unfolded into six squares into 2D space.

11. THE TESSERACT. what does the narrator mean that he is on top of two archipelago?​


Answer:

The narrator's statement about being on top of two archipelagos is a metaphorical description of his position in the fourth dimension while holding the tesseract. An archipelago is a group of islands, and the narrator is suggesting that there are two separate groups of islands (which represent the three-dimensional worlds) that he is now able to see and move between because he has access to the fourth dimension through the tesseract. Essentially, he is describing his ability to perceive and move through space and time in a way that is not possible for someone limited to three dimensions.

22. How is defamiliarization used in the story "The Tesseract​"?


Answer:  Defamiliarization is used in The Tesseract to challenge the reader's assumptions about time and reality, as the story shifts between different perspectives and timelines. This technique creates a sense of disorientation that mirrors the characters' experiences and adds to the overall tension of the narrative.
