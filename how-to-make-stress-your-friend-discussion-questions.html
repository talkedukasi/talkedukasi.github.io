7. 1. What is Stress 2 Give 10 examples of Stress 3. Explain each number on how you can manage istress your 4. Is stress all bad? Why? 5. What Strasses you out?​


1. Stress is a physical, mental, or emotional response to a demand, threat, or challenge that exceeds a person's ability to cope with it. It is a natural reaction that prepares the body to respond to danger or pressure, also known as the "fight or flight" response.

2. Examples of stress include:

Financial problems

Work deadlines

Family conflicts

Relationship issues

Health problems

Traffic jams

Academic pressure

Social anxiety

Performance anxiety

Life transitions (moving, divorce, etc.)

3. Here are some ways to manage stress:

Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or yoga

Exercise regularly to release tension and boost endorphins

Get enough sleep and establish a regular sleep routine

Eat a healthy diet and avoid excessive caffeine, alcohol, or drugs

Set realistic goals and prioritize tasks to avoid feeling overwhelmed

Seek social support from friends, family, or a therapist

Practice time management and avoid procrastination

Engage in hobbies or activities that bring joy and relaxation

Learn to say "no" to commitments that cause unnecessary stress

Maintain a positive attitude and practice gratitude and self-compassion

4. Stress is not all bad. It can motivate us to take action, achieve goals, and adapt to new situations. However, chronic or excessive stress can have negative effects on our physical and mental health, including increased risk of heart disease, anxiety, depression, and other illnesses.

5. work or school-related stress, financial stress, relationship problems, and health issues

It could seem like there's nothing you can do about your stress level. Bills will never stop coming, there will  never be enough hours a day for all your duties, and your job or family commitments will always be  overwhelming. But you've got a lot more influence than you would expect. In reality, the simple knowledge that you are in control of your life is the cornerstone of stress management.

Identify the sources of stress in your life

Tension management is beginning to recognise the causes of stress in your life. It's not as easy as it sounds.  

Your true causes of stress are not always apparent, so it's all too easy to ignore your own stress-inducing thoughts, emotions, so habits. Yeah, you should know that you're still worried about job deadlines.  But it may be your procrastination, rather than the actual demands of your work, that contributes to the stress of the deadline.

.

 

Unless you take responsibility for the role you play in creating or sustaining it, your stress level will remain  beyond your control.
