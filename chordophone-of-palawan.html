Explanation:

Idiophone

-is any musical instrument that creates sound primarily by the vibration of the instrument itself, without the use of air flow, strings or membranes.

Chordophone

-is a musical instrument that makes sound by way of a vibrating string or strings stretched between two points. It is a stringed instrument.

Aerophone

-is a musical instrument that produces sound primarily by causing a body of air to vibrate. It is a a wind instrument.

Membranophone

-is a musical instruments whose sound is generated by striking, rubbing, or singing into a stretched membrane.

Kalutang

Musical Mangyan (Mindoro) sticks played in pairs producing harmonies of seconds, thirds, or fourths; idiophones

Subing

Mangyan bamboo jaws harp, used for imitating nature sounds and communicating messages, as well as for courtship; aerophones

Bangsi

Mangyan bamboo duct flute which has a chip glued on; aerophones

Agong

Bossed Mangyan gong; idiophones

Gitgit

Three-stringed Mangyan violin with human hair strings; chordophones

Aruding

Bamboo jaws harp from Palawan; idiophones

Babarak

Ring flute from Palawan; aerophones

Suling

Banded flute from Palawan; aerophones

Kusyapi

Two-stringed lute from Palawan; has drone and melody strings; chordophones

Pagang

Polychordal bamboo tube zither from Palawan; chordophones
