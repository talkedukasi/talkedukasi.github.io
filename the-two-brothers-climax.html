30. Five brothers, one mother l. SettingsA. Exposition B. Conflict C. Climax D. Resolution E. Ending ​


Here's a possible breakdown of the story "Five Brothers, One Mother" into the elements of exposition, conflict, climax, resolution, and ending:

A. Exposition: The story introduces the five brothers and their mother, but does not provide much detail about them. We know that there are five brothers and one mother, but we do not know much about their background, personalities, or relationships.

B. Conflict: The conflict of the story arises when the mother becomes ill and there is only one apple left in the house. Each of the five brothers wants the apple for themselves, but they do not want to harm their mother by taking it from her.

C. Climax: The climax of the story occurs when the brothers come up with a solution to the conflict. They decide to cut the apple into five pieces and each brother will eat one piece. This shows their love and concern for their mother, as they are willing to share the apple and make a sacrifice for her well-being.

D. Resolution: The resolution of the story is when the mother recovers from her illness and is grateful to her sons for their love and care. She is proud of them for their kindness and willingness to share, and they are happy to have helped her.

E. Ending: The story ends with the mother and her five sons living happily together, united by their love for each other and their willingness to help each other in difficult times.

Overall, the story of "Five Brothers, One Mother" is a simple but powerful tale about the importance of family, love, and sacrifice. It shows how even in times of conflict and scarcity, people can come together and find a way to overcome their challenges by working together and showing kindness and generosity to one another.
