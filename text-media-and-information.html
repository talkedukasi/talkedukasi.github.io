22. comprehends how text information and media is are formally and informally produced organized and disseminated​


Comprehending how text information and media (such as newspapers) are formally and informally produced organized and disseminated helps us understand how we should interpret what we see on the news. For example, one may learn that there was an accident at a certain place but did not know where this happened. One could then use his/her own resources to find out more details about the accident. In addition, one may also need to consider other factors which affect our interpretation, such as whether the source of the information is credible.
