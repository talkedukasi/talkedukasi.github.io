16. What is the difference between people as media and people in media?


Answer:

Explanation:

People in Media and People as Media comprise a bigger term known as People Media, wherein there is an assembly of people with a common interest where they are considered as the main means of mass communication. In other words, People Media refers to the persons are involved in the analysis, evaluation, production, and use of media and information.

Media can be in the form of radio, television, social media platforms, print, web, or instructional media. To differentiate the two:

a. People as Media - These are the people who are well-versed in media sources and messages, making them as the messenger who relay reliable and accurate data and information. These people are the media itself, making them as the direct medium to learn and acquire new knowledge.

Examples: Opinion Leaders, Citizen Journalists, Social (Media) Journalists, Crowdsourcing Contributors (in platforms like TripAdvisor, Waze, Wikipedia, Yelp), YouTubers, Independent Bloggers, Paid Vloggersm or Even a simple Facebook User or Your Classmate

b. People in Media - These people are media practitioners who provide data and information coming from their first-hand experience of an event or their expert knowledge.

Examples: Teachers, Authors, Disc Jockeys, TV hosts, Authors, Actors, Directors, News Columnists, Visual Artists, Screenwriters

The evolution of People in Media:

1.Pre-spanish Era

• Knowledge was passed on through folk media and indigenous forms of media.

Examples : Town criers, messenger, community scribe.

2. Spanish Era

• In this education was limited to elite, publication of books such as Noli Me tangere and El Filibusterismo) and newspaper (La solidaridad) advanced the propaganda movement which led to the peoples revolution against Spanish rule.

3. American and Japanese Era

• During this era major newspaper such as  Manila times and Manila bulletin , radio broadcasting and movies become available.  Media was used to propaganda by government and other groups. This includes book writers,editor,publisher, copyreader, artist, cartoonist, reporter,producer, broadcaster.

4. Post-war era

• Known to be the golden age of the Philippine Journalism. Advertising, communication education,and press freedom flourished in this era.

5.Martial law era  

• The government take over the media and the press.

6.Post-EDSA Era

• People power brought greater freedom for Philippine media.

7.Modern Era

• The birth of new careers such as  archivist, web designer, online instructors, digital producers, bloggers, animators, programmer, archivist, metadata analyst, data miner and many others.
