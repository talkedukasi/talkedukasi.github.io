14. What do we call the type of blog that is in video format.?​


Vlog

A video blog, often known as a video log or simply a vlog, is a type of blog that uses video as its primary medium. Vlog postings frequently include accompanying text, photos, and other information along with an embedded video (or a video link). Entries may be recorded in a single take or in a number of takes. The Vlog genre on the video-sharing website YouTube is well-liked.

Further explanation

One of the most well-liked types of digital entertainment in recent years, "vlogging" has given rise to a sizable online community. The general consensus is that, in addition to being entertaining, vlogs may convey complex context through images more effectively than written blogs.

Web syndication, which enables the dissemination of video over the Internet using either the RSS or Atom syndication formats, is another technique that video logs (vlogs) frequently use to enable automated aggregation and playing on mobile devices and personal computers (see video podcast).

The inaugural posting on what would eventually grow to be the longest-running video blog in history was made on January 2, 2000 by Adam Kontras, who posted a video along with a blog entry to tell his friends and family of his cross-country migration to Los Angeles in pursuit of a career in show business. Adrian Miles created the word "vog" to describe his video blog when he published a film in November of the same year in which text was changed on a still image.
