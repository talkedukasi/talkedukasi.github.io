5. NAME 10 PHYSICISTS SCIENTISTS WITH CONTRIBUTION WRONG ANSWER AUTO REPORT


Answer:

Isaac Newton

- He invented calculus and provided a clear understanding of optics. But his most significant work had to do with forces, and specifically with the development of a universal law of gravity.

Niels Bohr

- Niels Bohr proposed a model of the atom in which the electron was able to occupy only certain orbits around the nucleus. This atomic model was the first to use quantum theory, in that the electrons were limited to specific orbits around the nucleus.

Galileo Galilei

- Galileo's greatest contribution to physics was his studies of the motions of objects. He rolled balls down an inclined plane to "slow down" their falling and study it.

Albert Einstein

- Einstein is also known for his contributions to the development of quantum theory. He postulated (1905) light quanta (photons), upon which he based his explanation of the photoelectric effect, and he developed the quantum theory of specific heat.

James Clerk Maxwell

- He contributed to eight other scientific spheres: geometrical optics, kinetic theory, thermodynamics, viscoelasticity, bridge structures, control theory, dimensional analysis, and the theory of Saturn's rings.

Michael Faraday

- His discovery of electromagnetic induction, his contributions to electrical engineering and electrochemistry, or due to the fact that he was responsible for introducing the concept of field in physics to describe electromagnetic interaction.

Marie Curie

- Curie discovered and isolated polonium and radium, and established the nature of radiation and beta rays.

Richard Feynman

- He revolutionized the field of quantum mechanics and formulated the theory of quantum electrodynamics.

Ernest Rutherford

- He discovered alpha and beta rays, set forth the laws of radioactive decay, and identified alpha particles as helium nuclei.

Paul Dirac

- Paul Dirac made fundamental contributions to the development of quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, and quantum electrodynamics, and is particularly known for his attempts to unify the theories of quantum mechanics and relativity theory.
