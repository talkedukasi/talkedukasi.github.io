30. Gregorian Chants shows how western culture influences the Philippine culture by​


Gregorian Chants demonstrates how Western culture influences Philippine culture through Roman Catholicism's music, which influenced what was to be the mainstream, "lowland-Christian" Filipino society.

Further explanation:

Filipino folklore that has been influenced by the West dates back to the nearly three-hundred-year spans of Spanish colonial rule from 1521 to 1898. What became dominant, "lowland-Christian" Filipino civilization was influenced by the development of a colonial state, an economic system based on Roman Catholicism, and a culture.

The people's cultural experience was heavily influenced by religious or Christian themes. The Spanish friars who taught Gregorian Chant for masses and other Christian ceremonies were the first to introduce Western music. Additionally, the Manila Cathedral founded a singing school in 1742. Around this time, baroque pipe organs were built, two of which are still in use today: the San Agustin Church organ in Manila (which was rebuilt in 1998) and the renowned Bamboo Organ of Las Pias.

As Christian components were incorporated into indigenous traditions, folk rites and para-liturgical rituals arose. The music changed as these rites progressed, dialectically combining indigenous/native styles with Westernized patterns. The most well-known of them is perhaps the Pasyon, a chanted epic-like song of the life of Christ that is performed during Lent.
