5. what is the difference of katipunan from other group that revolts against Spanish colonization?​


The Katipunan was a revolutionary organization in the Philippines that was founded in 1892. It played a crucial role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonization. While there were other groups and uprisings against Spanish rule, the Katipunan stood out in several ways:

1. Secret Society: The Katipunan operated as a secret society, with its members organized into cells or chapters. It employed a system of initiation rites, secret codes, and passwords to maintain secrecy and security. This clandestine nature allowed the Katipunan to plan and carry out its activities discreetly, making it more difficult for the Spanish authorities to suppress.

2. Nationalist Ideology: The Katipunan was driven by a strong sense of Filipino nationalism. Its members, led by prominent figures such as Andres Bonifacio, sought to achieve independence and self-rule for the Philippines. The organization aimed to unite Filipinos across different social classes and regions to fight for their common goal of liberation.

3. Mass Membership: Unlike some other uprisings that were led by specific individuals or factions, the Katipunan aimed to mobilize the masses. It attracted members from various backgrounds, including workers, farmers, intellectuals, and even women. This broad-based membership gave the organization a wide reach and allowed it to tap into the grievances and aspirations of the Filipino people.

4. Armed Rebellion: The Katipunan actively prepared for armed rebellion against Spanish rule. It organized its members into military units and developed a plan for a nationwide uprising. In 1896, the organization launched the Philippine Revolution, marking a significant turning point in the struggle for independence.

Overall, the Katipunan distinguished itself through its covert operations, nationalist ideology, mass membership, and armed rebellion. It became a symbol of Filipino resistance against Spanish colonization and laid the foundation for the eventual establishment of an independent Philippine republic.

The Lakandula and Sulayman Revolt, also known as the Tagalog Revolt, was an uprising in 1574 by Lakandula and Rajah Sulayman in Tondo, Manila. The revolt occurred in the same year as the Chinese pirate Limahong attacked the palisaded yet poorly defended enclosure of Intramuros.
