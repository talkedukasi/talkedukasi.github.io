3. fantasy work of cyan-abad jugo?​


Answer:

Cyan Abad-Jugo

Explanation:

Having finished her final lecture of the day, Cyan Abad-Jugo, PhD (AB Literature ‘91), who teaches literature at the Loyola Schools, exits her last class and begins the short but tiresome journey back to Horacio de la Costa Hall. Curiously, she does not look like someone who had just come from her fifth class of the day. In fact, she is all smiles.

To the people who know her, this is nothing out of the ordinary. Long known for her gleeful and gentle demeanor, Abad-Jugo is usually described as being incapable of hurting a fly. As Martin Villanueva of the Fine Arts Department says, “[She’s the] kindest person you’ll ever meet. And she doesn’t get outwardly rattled. [She’s a] calming, playful presence.”

True enough, her warm and inviting nature manifests itself as she jubilantly brings up the little things that made her day: funny anecdotes from class, a multimedia message from her husband containing a picture of a billboard promoting The Hobbit, and her children.

Gentleness, however, is only one side of Abad-Jugo’s multifaceted character—just like her critically acclaimed novels, there’s more to her than meets the eye.

5. White - Yellow White - Red - Cyan White - Green - Blue White - Magenta - Green


Answer:

These color combinations appear to be the order of colors in a standard RGB color wheel, where:

White is the combination of all colors

Red, green, and blue (RGB) are the primary colors of light

Cyan, magenta, and yellow (CMY) are the primary colors of pigments

The combinations in the given sequence are:

White and yellow: These are complementary colors, meaning they are opposite each other on the color wheel. Yellow is a primary color of light and is created by mixing red and green.

White, red, and cyan: Red and cyan are complementary colors, and white is the combination of all colors. Red is a primary color of light and is created by mixing magenta and yellow. Cyan is created by mixing blue and green.

White, green, and blue: Green and magenta are complementary colors, and blue is a primary color of light. Green is created by mixing yellow and cyan. Magenta is a primary color of pigments and is created by mixing red and blue.

White, magenta, and green: Magenta and green are complementary colors, and white is the combination of all colors. Green is created by mixing yellow and cyan. Magenta is a primary color of pigments and is created by mixing red and blue.
