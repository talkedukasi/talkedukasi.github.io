12. Goldilocks and the three bears retell story​


A family of three bears once lived in a small cottage in the middle of a forest: Baby Bear, Papa Bear, and the Bears' Mama. Mama Bear made a pot of porridge for breakfast one sunny day. The bears decided to take a walk while the porridge cooled because it was too hot to eat.

Goldilocks, a curious young girl, stumbled upon their cottage while they were out. She walked right in after knocking, but no one answered. She decided to try the porridge when she saw the pot on the table inside. She exclaimed, "This porridge is too hot!" after tasting the first bowl. She exclaimed, "This porridge is too cold!" after tasting the second bowl. After giving the third bowl a try, she declared, "Ah, this porridge is just right."

Goldilocks looked for a place to rest after her long walk because she was exhausted. The living room contained three chairs for her: three chairs: one large, one medium, and one small. She attempted the principal seat and shouted, "This seat is too enormous!" She gave the second chair a try, but she sighed and said, "This chair is too small." Then she sat on the third seat and said, "Ah, this seat is perfect."

Inevitably, Goldilocks became drowsy and chosen to track down a spot to rest. She went up and discovered three beds: three beds: a large, a medium, and a small one. She put the first bed to the test and complained, "This bed is too hard!" She put the second bed to the test and complained, "This bed is too soft!" After that, she fell asleep on the third bed and exclaimed, "Ah, this bed is just right."

But Goldilocks had no idea that she had broken into the bears' house. They knew someone had been there when they returned from their walk to find their cottage in disarray. They were taken aback when they discovered Goldilocks asleep in Baby Bear's bed and woke her up. She rushed out of the cottage and back into the forest, terrified, and never came back.

The lesson here: Before entering someone else's home, always ask for permission.

Ang kaisipan o ideyang taglay ng isang akda ay ang pangunahing mensahe o konseptong ipinapahayag ng may-akda sa pamamagitan ng kanilang pagsulat. Ito ay naglalarawan ng mga ideya, pananaw, at damdamin na nais ipahayag ng may-akda sa kanilang mga mambabasa.

Ang estilo ng pagkakasulat ay ang paraan o teknikang ginagamit ng isang may-akda sa pagsulat ng kanilang mga obra. 25. goldilocks the three bear's rissing action ​


The rising action of the story of Goldilocks and the Three Bears includes the events that lead up to the climax or the turning point of the story. Here are the rising action events:

1. The Three Bears leave their house to take a walk in the forest.

2. Goldilocks discovers the empty house of the Three Bears and enters it.

3. Goldilocks sees the three bowls of porridge on the table and decides to taste them.

4. Goldilocks finds the first bowl too hot, the second bowl too cold, and the third bowl just right, so she eats it all up.

5. Goldilocks tries the chairs and finds the first chair too hard, the second chair too soft, and the third chair just right, but it breaks under her weight.

6. Goldilocks goes upstairs and finds the three beds. She tries the first bed, which is too hard, the second bed, which is too soft, and the third bed, which is just right, so she falls asleep.

7. The Three Bears come back from their walk and discover that someone has been in their house.

8. The Three Bears find Goldilocks sleeping in the baby bear's bed.

These events build up the tension and lead to the climax of the story, where the Three Bears confront Goldilocks.
