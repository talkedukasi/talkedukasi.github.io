Answer:

The title of the story is "The Woodcutter and the Wood Gnome." The main idea of the story is that honesty is always rewarded. The woodcutter is rewarded with two golden axes for being honest about losing his axe in the river. The dishonest man is not rewarded and is left without his axe. The story also teaches that it is better to be honest and have nothing than to be dishonest and have something.

The story begins with a woodcutter chopping down a tree and stacking the branches in two piles. He accidentally drops his axe into the river and sits down on the bank, feeling upset. Suddenly, a strange little man dressed all in green appears and asks the woodcutter why he is upset. The woodcutter explains that he lost his axe in the river. The little man, who is actually a wood gnome, dives into the river and comes up with a golden axe. He asks the woodcutter if it is his axe, but the woodcutter says no. The wood gnome dives back into the river and comes up with a silver axe. Again, he asks the woodcutter if it is his axe, but the woodcutter says no. The wood gnome dives into the river a third time and comes up with the woodcutter's own axe. The woodcutter is overjoyed and thanks the wood gnome. The wood gnome tells the woodcutter that he must keep the two golden axes as a reward for his honesty.

The woodcutter goes home and tells his friends what happened. The next day, one of his friends goes to the river and pretends to accidentally drop his axe into the river. He sits down on the bank and cries, and the wood gnome appears. The man says that he lost his axe in the river and the wood gnome dives in and comes up with a golden axe. The man grabs the axe and runs away, without even thanking the wood gnome. The wood gnome does not give chase and simply lets the man keep the golden axe.

The moral of the story is that honesty is always rewarded. The woodcutter is an honest man and is rewarded with two golden axes. The dishonest man is not rewarded and is left without his axe.

This story also teaches that it is better to be honest and have nothing than to be dishonest and have something. The woodcutter is honest and loses his axe, but he is still better off than the dishonest man who pretends to lose his axe. The dishonest man gets a golden axe, but he is left without his original axe and he does not have the respect of the wood gnome.

The story ends with the woodcutter being rewarded for his honesty and the dishonest man being punished for his dishonesty.

Kinetic Energy:

[tex]\mathsf{KE=\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = velocity

[tex]\\[/tex]

Work Done:

[tex]\mathsf{W=Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work done

F = applied force

d = distance

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{GIVEN:}[/tex]

m = 2.5 kg

F = 80 kN = 80,000 N

d = 18 mm = 0.018 m

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of the head of the axe is equal to the work done on the tree trunk.

[tex]\mathsf{KE=W}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{1}{2}mv^2=Fd}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{v=\sqrt{\dfrac{2Fd}{m}}}[/tex]

Substituting the given values,

[tex]\mathsf{v=\sqrt{\dfrac{(2)(80000)(0.018)}{2.5}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{v=24\sqrt{2} \ \approx33.94 \ m/s}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]
