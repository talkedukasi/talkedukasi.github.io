7. list down all human activities that may affect the quality and quantity of soil​


Soil is very important for human life and other living things with functions that cannot be replaced by others. Almost all human activities are carried out on land, ranging from housing, agriculture, industry and other activities. However, these activities can affect the quality and quantity of soil.

What are the human activities that affect the quality and quantity of soil?

1. Deforestation and soil erosion

Deforestation is one of the causes of the decline in soil quality and quantity. Land that has been converted to dry or barren land can never be fertile again, no matter what action is taken to overcome it. Land conversion means the change or modification of the original properties of the land so that it is suitable for use for certain purposes. Land conversion is the main cause of land degradation. When forests are cleared for development and to meet the demand for wood supply, the soil will become loose. Without tree protection, the soil becomes barren over time and begins to erode. Vacant land that has not been used for years becomes infertile and unusable.

2. Agricultural Activities

Agricultural processes often involve the use of harmful pesticides and insecticides. Farmers often use toxic fertilizers and pesticides to remove insects, fungi, and bacteria from crops. These chemicals cause the soil to become polluted. Soil that was once fertile becomes more susceptible to environmental elements. Overuse of chemicals actually has the effect of causing contamination and poisoning the soil.

3. Mining activities

Mining creates large, cave-like open spaces beneath the earth's surface. The existence of these caves or cavities endangers the integrity of the soil. Naturally, the soil will collapse to fill the space left after mining or extraction activities. Mining also produces hazardous chemicals such as uranium, which can pollute the environment.

4. Industrialization

Industrialization is one of the main contributors to the pollution problem. To meet the demands of a growing population, more and more industries are being developed. An increase in the population results in an increase in the demand for food, shelter, housing and the production of more goods. The Industrial Revolution may have resulted in significant positive changes to the economy and society, but it also caused significant soil pollution through inefficient waste disposal practices, the use of unsafe chemicals in manufacturing, poor regulation, and the many industries and factories that pollute the soil every day.

5. Waste management activities

As the population grows, each household produces tons of waste every year. The number of landfills is getting full and growing. Garbage in landfills is filled with toxins that eventually seep into the earth. When it rains, the toxins are carried to other areas, and the pollution spreads.

Those are some examples of human activities that can affect the quality and quantity of soil
