7. To support a tree damaged in a storm, a 12-foot wire is secured from the ground to the tree at a point 10 feet off the ground. The tree meets the ground at a right angle. At approximately what angle does the wire meet the ground?



Wire ⇒  hypotenuse = 12 ft

Height from a point on the tree from the ground ⇒ opposite side = 10 ft

The angle between the wire and the ground: Sin⁻¹ θ


Sin⁻¹ θ = opposite/hypotenuse


Sin⁻¹ θ = 10 ft/12 ft


Sin⁻¹ θ = 0.833


θ = 56.40°  :  56°  ⇒ the angle where the wire meets the ground.

Forward Swing Teachnique – power is generating from the  speed and velocity of the forward and upward  movements of the arms, legs and body.
