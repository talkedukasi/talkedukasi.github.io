24. ACTIVITY 2 Direction: Compare and contrast the different social sciences disciplines by completing the table below. Use a separate bond paper. Disciplines Anthropology Demography Economics Geography Linguistics History Political Science Psychology Sociology Etymologies Definition​


Answer:

Anthropology:

Studies human societies and cultures, past and present

Focuses on understanding the diversity and complexity of human societies

Employs a range of research methods, including fieldwork and participant observation

Examines topics such as kinship, religion, language, and social organization

Demography:

Studies the characteristics of human populations

Focuses on statistical analysis of population data

Examines topics such as population size, density, migration, and fertility

Economics:

Studies the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services

Focuses on the behavior of individuals, firms, and markets

Examines topics such as supply and demand, economic growth, and international trade

Geography:

Studies the physical and human features of the Earth's surface

Focuses on the spatial relationships between people and their environments

Examines topics such as land use, urbanization, and environmental issues

Linguistics:

Studies the structure and use of language

Focuses on understanding the nature of language and how it is used in communication

Examines topics such as syntax, phonology, and language acquisition

History:

Studies past events and the development of human societies over time

Focuses on understanding the causes and effects of historical events and movements

Examines topics such as political, social, and cultural changes over time

Political Science:

Studies the organization and operation of governments and political systems

Focuses on understanding power relations and decision-making processes within political systems

Examines topics such as democracy, authoritarianism, and international relations

Psychology:

Studies the human mind and behavior

Focuses on understanding the cognitive, emotional, and social processes that influence behavior

Examines topics such as perception, memory, personality, and social influence

Sociology:

Studies human societies and social relationships

Focuses on understanding the patterns and structures of social behavior

Examines topics such as social stratification, inequality, and cultural norms and values

Etymology:

Studies the origin and evolution of words and languages

Focuses on understanding the historical development of language and its relationship to culture and society

Examines topics such as linguistic borrowing, language change, and the relationship between language and identity.

29. etymology of economic​


Answer:

Late Middle English: via Old French and Latin from Greek oikonomikos, from oikonomia (see economy). Originally a noun, the word denoted household management or a person skilled in this, hence the early sense of the adjective (late 16th century) ‘relating to household management’. Modern senses date from the mid 19th century.
