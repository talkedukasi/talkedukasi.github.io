Philippine Administrative System (DPA 102)

PAS applies to a network of organisations with unique rules and objectives, processes, personnel and activities. It involves internal mechanisms and communications between and between public entities that are intended to enforce, help create, track or review public policies.

Public PA Socio-political and economic environment

This system includes the interaction between the PA and the immediate public in communication, as well as the responses of the PA to or how it is influenced by the wider socio-political and economic environment in which it operates.

23. 2. What are the Problems of administrative law in the system of public administration? ​


Administrative law is a branch of law that deals with the legal principles and procedures governing the actions of administrative agencies, such as government bodies and regulatory agencies. While administrative law is important in ensuring accountability and transparency in the functioning of public administration, it is not without its problems. Here are some of the problems of administrative law in the system of public administration:

Lack of transparency and accountability: One of the key problems of administrative law is the lack of transparency and accountability in the decision-making processes of administrative agencies. This can lead to arbitrary and inconsistent decision-making, as well as a lack of public trust in the fairness and impartiality of the administrative system.

Over-centralization: Administrative law can also be hindered by the over-centralization of decision-making power in administrative agencies. This can limit the ability of lower-level officials to make decisions and can also lead to delays in decision-making and an inefficient bureaucracy.

Limited access to justice: Administrative law can also be problematic in terms of access to justice. Administrative agencies may have limited resources and may not always be able to provide adequate remedies or appeals for those who have been adversely affected by their decisions.

Inflexibility: Administrative law can be inflexible and may not be able to keep pace with changes in society and technology. This can lead to outdated regulations that are not effective in addressing new challenges and issues.

Lack of expertise: Administrative agencies may lack the necessary expertise to make informed decisions, particularly in technical areas such as environmental or health and safety regulations. This can lead to decisions that are based on incomplete or inaccurate information.

Corruption: Administrative law can also be undermined by corruption and other forms of misconduct within administrative agencies. This can lead to decisions that are made for personal gain rather than in the public interest.

Overall, these problems highlight the need for ongoing reforms and improvements in the field of administrative law, to ensure that it remains effective and relevant in the evolving landscape of public administration.
