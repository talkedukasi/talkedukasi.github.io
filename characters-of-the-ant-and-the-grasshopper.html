1. What is the event in the ant and the grasshopper?


There is an event in the ant and the grass-hopper. We call it the tale of the "Ant and The grass-hopper"

THE STORY / EVENT EXPLANATION:

Once upon a time in a fertile field, lived a family of diligent ant and the a lazy  grass-hopper. Even so, grass-hoppers are very friendly with the ant family. They often greet each other when they meet. In the field, the grass-hopper is known as a idle animal figure. He loves to spend his time relaxing, singing, dancing, or just lying on the soft green grass. The grass-hopper enjoys his days happily without ever having any plans for the future. "Life flows like water," he said.

Unlike the grass-hoppers, the ant family is very diligent. They work hard to find and collect food every day. The ant family did all this without getting tired. Ants always work hard while grass-hoppers are always lazy. When summer comes, the ants continue to work even though the sun is very hot. But the grass-hoppers haven't started working yet. The heat of the sun makes it even more idle to work.

It had been several hours since the grass-hopper had been out looking for food, when he suddenly remembered the ants who had worked so hard from the spring. Feeling unable to find a way out, the grass-hopper decided to go to the ant family. Since then, the grass-hopper has learned a very valuable lesson. From the following spring he was eager to help the ant family work and gather food.

30. Which food chain is in the correct order? grasshopper → grass → snake → frog → hawk grasshopper → frog → hawk → snake → grass grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk grass → grasshopper → snake → frog → hawk


The food chain that is in the correct order is: grass → grasshopper → frog → snake → hawk

Remember that no food chain can start from a consumer since there will be no source of energy for the primary consumers. In this case, the producer is the grass, it produces its own food via photosynthesis; the grasshopper is the primary consumer, it consumes the producer; the secondary consumer is the frog which in turn eats the primary consumer; and finally the tertiary consumer is the hawk, which eats the secondary consumer.

Do note though that energy received per level in the food chain and energy pyramid is decreasing as you ascend. This is because some of the energy is lost by the organisms to the environment as heat while some is used by the organism.
