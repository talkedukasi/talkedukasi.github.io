12. what are the differences between drama and theatre?​


What are the differences between drama and theatre?

Drama and Theatre are two terms that we frequently use conversely as the two of them are identified with performing arts. However, there is a difference between these two terms.

Drama refers to the script of a play. In a drama, there is direct communication between the writer and the peruser. The term 'drama' additionally refers to a scene of life, or fiction that includes feeling or struggle. In contemporary society, this word is utilized to refer to kinds of movies and TV representation too. Drama can be addressed as a part of theatre that helps illustrate a scene through acting. Drama has numerous structures and should be interpreted as a generic term taking on numerous structures, one of which is theater. The demonstration or cycle of playing out a play before a crowd is Dramatization. Drama can be a scene of life, a segment in a DVD library or a library of plays, or it tends to be a fiction brimming with feelings and disagreements.

Theatre is the actual act as a whole and not just a text or script. This requires actors, content, stage/platform, lighting, audio cues, foundation and a group of people. These are the required areas and what makes theatre. There are different individuals that add to rejuvenating theatre, from directors to actors, technicians and fashion designers. Theater is a vital performing art, and within a period of time, it has taken numerous new structures, for example, TV dramas and even films, where there are practices and takes though, in the theater, there is no such room for takes.

Additionally, in most dramas there is an interaction between the audience and the creator, whereas theatre is a one way communication. Theatre has an advantage as it is a physical entity and on the other side Drama is an abstract entity.
