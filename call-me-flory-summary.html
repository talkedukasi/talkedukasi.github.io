1. Differences between Flory and Matilde in the story (Call me flory) G7 English​


Answer:

In the story "Call Me Flory" , the characters Flory and Matilde are two very different individuals with distinct personalities and backgrounds.

Flory is a young girl who is struggling to find her identity. She is a quiet and reserved individual who is often teased by her classmates for her name. She is also unsure of her place in the world and feels out of place in her community.

Matilde, on the other hand, is a confident and outgoing young girl who is not afraid to speak her mind. She is outgoing and sociable, and is well-liked by her classmates. She is also very independent and is not afraid to stand up for herself and what she believes in.

In terms of their backgrounds, Flory comes from a poor family, while Matilde comes from a middle-class family. This also plays a role in how they view the world and how they interact with others. Flory's background has made her more reserved and introverted, while Matilde's background has allowed her to be more outgoing and confident.

Overall, Flory and Matilde are two very different characters with distinct personalities and backgrounds. While Flory is struggling to find her place in the world, Matilde is confident and independent. Their background also plays a key role in their personalities and how they interact with the world around them.

"Footnote to Youth" by Jose Garcia Villa is a story about a young man named Dodong who falls in love and marries a girl named Teang. The characters Flory and Matilde are two of the key players in the story and are contrasted against each other to highlight the differences between the two women.

Flory is the first love interest of Dodong. She is depicted as a carefree and flirtatious young woman who is not committed to any one man. Flory represents the idea of love as a fleeting and ephemeral experience that is not meant to last. On the other hand, Matilde is depicted as a more responsible and mature young woman who is more interested in settling down and building a life with someone she loves. Matilde represents the idea of love as a commitment that requires effort and sacrifice.

The contrast between Flory and Matilde highlights the differences between two types of love experiences and serves as a commentary on the consequences of impulsive decisions. Through the story, Villa shows that choosing the right person to spend one's life with is a serious decision that requires careful consideration and a realistic understanding of the responsibilities that come with marriage.
