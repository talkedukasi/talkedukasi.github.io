21. inductive or deductive reasoning1. Mayumi wears a band shirt every time you see her, so Mayumi’s wardrobe consists of only band shirts.2. The cost of goods was Php150, and the labor cost of a particular item was Php50. If the selling price of the itemwas Php250, thenthe stores selling that item always earn a good profit.3.Succulentsare plants and all plants perform photosynthesis; therefore, succulents perform photosynthesis.4.Smuggled goods are illegal. If a medicine is smuggled, then it is illegal.


Answers:

1. Mayumi wears a band shirt every time you see her, so Mayumi’s wardrobe consists of only band shirts.

Answer: Inductive reasoning

Explanation: Mayumi wearing band shirts every time she is seen is a specific observation about her taste in clothing. Since the assumption that her wardrobe consists of only band shirts is based on that specific observation, this reasoning is then inductive.

2. The cost of goods was Php150, and the labor cost of a particular item was Php50. If the selling price of the item was Php250, then the stores selling that item always earn a good profit.

Answer: Inductive reasoning

Explanation: The assumption on the overall profit of stores selling a particular item is based off on the computation of profits from selling a particular item. This makes the reasoning inductive.

3. Succulents are plants and all plants perform photosynthesis; therefore, succulents perform photosynthesis.

Answer: Deductive reasoning

Explanation: In this reasoning, an assumption is made that since succulents are plants and since plants perform photosynthesis, succulents then do photosynthesis themselves. Photosynthesis is generally attributed to plants, and the general (plants doing photosynthesis) down to specific (succulents doing photosynthesis) approach is characteristic of deductive reasoning.  

4.Smuggled goods are illegal. If a medicine is smuggled, then it is illegal.

Answer: Deductive reasoning

Explanation: In this case, a generalization is made that smuggled goods are illegal. From that generalization, a specific argument is made that smuggled medicine is illegal. This is another case of creating a generalization (smuggled good are illegal) then making a specific argument (smuggled medicine is illegal), which characterizes deductive reasoning.

Additional Explanation:

Inductive reasoning is making generalized conclusions based on a specific scenario. In this type of reasoning, specific observations are made and then a general conclusion if formulated based on the observations noted. An example is the argument that the Earth is round. This is a reasoning made after thousands of years of observing specific phenomena, such as that boats appear to sink until it disappears altogether as it travels toward the horizon, shadow that Earth casts on the moon during an eclipse, and observations from astronauts, to name a few.

In deductive reasoning, on the other hand, reasoning flows from general to specific. A specific conclusion is made based on a general statement. For example, we can state that space observations found out that planets are round. And since Jupiter is a planets, then Jupiter must also be round.
