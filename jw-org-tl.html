10. what is the difference between org. and management?


Organization= samahan
Management= pamamahalaIt depends on how you're using each - management as a noun is a person or group of people in supervisory role(s) within an organization, usually responsible for the “management” of resources, be it people, budget, programs, etc (often all of the above). Top management is really not that different, beyond the level of their respective purview.

Organization is the way in which the component parts of an enterprise are designed to fit together so that the net result is greater than the sum of its parts. By extension, organization design is the ways in which one structures an enterprise to most effectively leverage it’s resources to achieve the overall business strategy, and operating objectives.
