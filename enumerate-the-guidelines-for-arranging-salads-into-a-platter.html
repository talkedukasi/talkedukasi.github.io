2. enumerate the guidelines for arranging salad into a platter


Rules for arranging salads on a plate

1. Choose a Ceramic Plate

Set Ceramic plates such as Mono Coupe from the Fun Dining Java Tableware collection can make your salad look even more delicious. It is very important for you to consider the aspect, shape and size of the ceramic plate that you use to serve various foods and adjust the portion of the salad that will be made so that the visual composition is balanced. Generally a salad plate has a diameter of about 18cm.

2. Combine Salad and Plate Colors

Don't be afraid to play with a variety of natural colors from the ingredients, vegetables and salad dressings you use. Use a white salad plate to create a harmonious composition unit between the ingredients, vegetables and ceramic plates you use.

3. Position of Salad Plates at Official Banquets

When at a banquet together, usually the position of the salad plate is next to the dinner plate. Enjoying the salad first can prevent overeating the next time. That's why in some formal dinners the position of the salad/soup plate will be served first. Then followed by appetizer, main course, then dessert.

Salad dishes not only add color to the overall appearance of a meal, but also help you control your portions. For salad dishes that will be served at a formal dinner, place the salad plate position next to the main dinner plate. Salads should be served first, followed by an appetizer, main course, then dessert.

Explanation:

Salad is a delicious snack which is of course nutritious because the snack or snack we usually call, consists of various fruits that have high nutrition. This healthy snack is good for people who have a history of poor health or it could be you who have an excess but still want to eat but do not want to gain weight. It can also be consumed by those of you who are on a diet or want to maintain an ideal weight but want to enjoy a delicious but healthy snack. Serving vegetable-based dishes requires special attention and techniques in plating in order to make the whole dish look fresh and attractive. Plating salad usually consists of fresh green vegetables eaten with salad dressings as well as a mix of spices and other healthy foods. Plating using a good and appropriate plate is very important because the use and placement of vegetables randomly and randomly can reduce appetite.

With a good and appropriate visual appearance of a salad dish, all elements of the dish will look even more tempting. Salad dishes themselves usually already use colorful vegetables, therefore, you are strongly advised to serve salads with white tableware because this white color not only helps the natural colors of these vegetables stand out but also helps you to improve the visual appearance and appearance. artistic that really determines people's perception of the taste of the food served. This applies to all dishes, especially vegetable and fruit salads that were originally less tempting to taste.

15. enumerate the guidelines for arranging salad​


Enumerate the guidelines for arranging salad

Guidelines for arranging salads Take a peek at the plate or bowl as a frame. Choose the best size of the dish. Hold the salad off the edge. Maintain strong color balance. Three colors are typically nice enough. The height of the salad makes it more appealing. Cut the ingredients neatly and evenly Make sure all the products can be identified Keep plans simple;

The Precautions should be taken before preparing a simple salad

1. Wash all ingredients including fruit and vegetables thoroughly (as it can be a good source of contamination) pat dry as it may result in the finished product becoming watery.

2. Have all ingredients ready before the salad is cooked, since this guarantees freshness and crunchiness in the salad.

3. Use the freshest ingredients, particularly seasonal ones, as this will keep the nutrients intact.

4. Light leafy vegetables should be broken into bits of bite size and never cut the greens carefully tossed in a dressing right before serving to prevent wilting the salad.

5. Add enough dressing to the seasoning; do not drown the main ingredient as it will wilt the salad and overwhelm the taste.

6. Using the appropriate jar to present the salad garnish with the edible ingredient to make it an eye-catching touch.

7. Never overcrowd the salad dish.

8. Within the dish and not on or around the lip of the salad.

Guidelines for preparation of simple salads

Following activities are involved in preparing simple salad

1 .Wash the vegetables/fruit in cold water. Keep the roots in the bath to push the water to core, thereby eliminating the soil and the gravel.

2. Cut the vegetables as needed. (The cubes, threads, strips, etc.)

3. Keep in a cool position until needed. Refrigerate greens in a colander covered with a damp towel or in a perforated storage container to allow air circulation and full drainage. This helps keep the greens crisp.

4. When appropriate, combine all ingredients with a dressing

5. Used neatly in china  glass or wooden bowls. Garnish if needed. Serve dressing aside. Keep it still cool.
