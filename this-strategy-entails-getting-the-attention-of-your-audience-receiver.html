8. 2) This strategy entails identification of the basic structure of a text and the main ideas of the text. A. Contextualizing B. Evaluating C. Outlining D. Summarizing nyan​


Answer:

The correct answer is D. Summarizing.

Summarizing is the strategy of identifying the main ideas and central points in a text and expressing them concisely in one's own words. This approach is helpful in retaining key information and understanding the big picture of a text. It is also useful for evaluating the relevance and significance of the information presented in the text.

Contextualizing involves understanding the background and context of a text, such as the author's intentions, historical or cultural influences, and audience. Evaluating involves critically analyzing the quality, credibility, and biases of a text. Outlining involves organizing the main ideas and supporting details of a text into a logical structure. While these strategies are also important in comprehending and analyzing a text, they do not necessarily focus on identifying the main ideas and central points in a concise manner, which is the primary goal of summarizing.

18. this strategy entails starting with the end result and reversing the steps you need to get those result?


Answer:

It sounds like you may be referring to the concept of "backward planning," which is a strategy that involves starting with the desired end result and then working backward to identify the steps needed to achieve that result. This approach can be used in a variety of contexts, including education, project management, and strategic planning.

Step-by-step explanation:

The idea behind backward planning is to focus on the end goal first, and then work backward to determine what needs to be done in order to reach that goal. This can be an effective approach because it helps to ensure that all of the steps along the way are aligned with the ultimate goal and that resources are being used effectively to achieve it.
