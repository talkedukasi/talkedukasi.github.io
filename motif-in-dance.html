5. motif of the articraft​


Answer:

A motif may be an element in the iconography of a particular subject or type of subject that is seen in other works, or may form the main subject, as the Master of Animals motif in ancient art typically does. The related motif of confronted animals is often seen alone, but may also be repeated, for example in Byzantine silk and other ancient textiles. Where the main subject of an artistic work such as a painting is a specific person, group, or moment in a narrative, that should be referred to as the "subject" of the work, not a motif, though the same thing may be a "motif" when part of another subject, or part of a work of decorative art such as a painting on a vase.

13. Differentiate the motifs and design of Mindanao from the motifs and design of the Visayas​


Answer:

The motifs and designs of Mindanao and Visayas are distinct from each other, reflecting the diversity of culture and traditions within the Philippines.

Motifs and designs of Mindanao:

Geometric patterns - Mindanao designs often use geometric shapes, such as triangles, diamonds, and squares.Bold colors - The use of bright, bold colors is common in Mindanao design, often with contrasting colors to create a vibrant, eye-catching effect.Nature-inspired - Mindanao motifs often incorporate natural elements such as plants, animals, and landscapes.Arabic influence - Some Mindanao designs have an Arabic influence, particularly in Muslim-influenced areas such as the Sulu Archipelago.

Motifs and designs of Visayas:

Floral patterns - The Visayas region is known for its intricate floral patterns, often using flowers and leaves in complex, detailed designs.Pastel colors - Visayan designs tend to use softer, pastel colors, often in shades of pink, blue, and green.Sea-inspired - Given the region's proximity to the sea, Visayan designs often incorporate aquatic elements such as fish, shells, and seaweed.Spanish influence - The Visayas region has a strong Spanish influence, particularly in its architecture and design. Some Visayan designs incorporate elements of Spanish Baroque and Rococo styles.

Both Mindanao and Visayas use intricate designs and motifs, they differ in their use of colors, shapes, and cultural influences. Mindanao designs tend to be bolder and more geometric, with Arabic influences, while Visayan designs are softer and more floral, with Spanish influences.
