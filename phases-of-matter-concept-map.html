1) Phase changes:

a) Melting: It is the process of changing a solid into a liquid when the substance is heated above its melting point.

b) Freezing: It is the process of changing a liquid into a solid when the substance is cooled below its freezing point.

c) Vaporization: It is the process of changing a liquid into a gas by adding heat, which can be further divided into two types:

i) Evaporation: It is the process of vaporization at the surface of a liquid at a temperature below its boiling point.

ii) Boiling: It is the process of vaporization throughout the liquid, occurring at its boiling point.

d) Condensation: It is the process of changing a gas into a liquid by removing heat.

e) Sublimation: It is the process of changing a solid directly into a gas without passing through the liquid phase.

f) Deposition: It is the process of changing a gas directly into a solid without passing through the liquid phase.

2) Reflection:

a) I learned that phase changes are physical changes that involve a substance changing its state or phase, and they are caused by the addition or removal of heat or pressure.

b) I realized that understanding phase changes is essential in many scientific fields, including chemistry, physics, and materials science, as it helps to explain how different substances behave under various conditions.
