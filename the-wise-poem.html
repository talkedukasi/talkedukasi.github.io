4. English Read the poem “When I was one and twenty” Questions: 1. What specific advice was given by the wise man to the young man? 2. Did the young man listen to the advice of the wise man? Explain. 3. What is the theme for traid by poem? What is it about?​


"When I was one and twenty" is a poem by A.E. Housman. The poem is about a young man who receives advice from a wise man but does not listen to it.

1. The specific advice given by the wise man to the young man is to "beware" of love and to not make any promises or give away his heart too easily. The wise man advises the young man to wait until he is older and wiser before committing to love.

2. The young man did not listen to the advice of the wise man. He disregards the warning and makes a rash decision to give his heart away, only to regret it later.

3. The theme of the poem is the consequences of not listening to the wisdom of others and acting impulsively. The poem is about the young man's regrets after not following the advice of the wise man and making a mistake in love.

16. 1. wise, the, man, old A. the old wise man B. the wise old man C. wise old the man order of adjectives


B. The Wise, Old Man

Answer:

The correct order of adjectives in this case would be "the wise old man," so option B is the correct answer.

Explanation:                                                                                

in English, the general order of adjectives that describe a noun is as follows:

Determiner (e.g., the, a, an)

Opinion/observation (e.g., wise, beautiful, interesting)

Size (e.g., large, small, tall)

Shape (e.g., round, square, rectangular)

Age (e.g., old, new, young)

Color (e.g., red, blue, green)

Origin (e.g., American, Chinese, African)

Material (e.g., wooden, metal, plastic)

Purpose (e.g., cooking, cleaning, gardening)

However, it's important to note that this order can be flexible, and adjectives can be rearranged for emphasis or style.
