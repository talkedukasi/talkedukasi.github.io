15. Why are invasive species considered a threat to native wildlife? They can breed and spread quickly. They consume native species’ food supply. They evolve, and native species don’t. They all have higher genetic diversity than native species. They don’t need resources from the environment as native species do.


In your question, there are two possible answers. Let me discuss them below

They can breed and spread quickly - Invasive species have a tremendous ability to increase their population as compared to native species .This can increase the competition in the resources, which brings us to the other reason;They consume native species' food supply - Invasive species compete for the same resources as the native species. Combined with the rapidly increasing population of the native species, it's only a matter of time before the invasive species will take over the habitat of the native organism. This can either push them to extinction by loss of supply or drive them away from their natural habitat.
