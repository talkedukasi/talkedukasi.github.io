5. how do you feel while reading the poem that padre faura


Answer:

Padre Faura's poem

While reading the poem, I felt a variety of emotions but what stood out was sadness. Imagine having someone close to you, someone you consider like a child, be executed in front of you. There could the feeling of guilt for not being able to do anything, there could be the feeling of anger towards those who want your loved one executed but sadness really prevails. The sadness of not being able to see them or hear them talk anymore.

More about the poem:

The poem began by expressing Padre Faura's thoughts and sentiments on the morning of Rizal's execution, which occurred early in the morning. Padre Faura was shivering in his robes. Afterward, he related an incident that occurred between him and Jose Rizal in the Manila Obeservatory, during which Jose Rizal inquired about Purgatory.

After that, he described Rizal's mental condition and mood just before his execution. He stated that Rizal's cheeks were still red despite everything that he had been through at the time of the incident. This just serves to demonstrate that Rizal was not frightened to die because he was convinced that he was on the right side of history. He was prepared to die in the service of his country. Finally, he stated that even if Rizal were hanged, he would still be remembered fondly because he had made a significant contribution to the country. He was elevated to the status of national hero as a result of his actions against the colonists.

Even though he struggled silently while writing his novels, he was successful in conveying his thoughts to the Filipino people, encouraging them to stand up for what is right.

The poem ‘Padre Faura witnesses the execution of Rizal’ talks about the perspective of Padre Faura on the day of the execution of his friend Pepe where it is clearly stated that Padre Faura witnessed the execution on the roof of Ateneo Municipal. In the poem, the author give the audience a flashback about the moment when Padre Faura and Pepe had a conversation months ago, in the same room where he get executed. They were talking about stars in the sky, but months later, Padre Faura find himself in the same room, looking at the same man, but the man he had a conversation with are ready to be executed by some soldiers. There’s so much emotion found in this poem.
