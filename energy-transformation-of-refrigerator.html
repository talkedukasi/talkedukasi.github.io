10. 18. The energy that runs a refrigerator. C. Electrical Energy C. Light Energy D. Sound Energy D. Thermal Energy​


Energy

a. Electrical Energy

What is electrical energy?

Electrical energy, when used informally, refers to power transformed from electric powered capacity power. The motion of the electrical rate generates electric energy. The ability to carry out work or use pressure to transport an object is called energy. Electrical attraction or repulsion among charged debris is the pressure in the case of electrical energy. Negatively and definitely charged molecules entice or repel one other, generating electric power. Both capacity and kinetic energy exist in electric energy.

What is light energy?

Electromagnetic radiation is a type of light energy. The only kind of energy visible to the human eye is light, which moves in waves. It is made up of electromagnetic radiation and follows a straight course. How fast does light move? We witness many examples of light energy in our daily lives, such as a lit candle, flashlight, fire, electric bulb, kerosene lamp, stars, and other luminous bodies, among others. Each one provides illumination. A candle that is burning is an example of light energy.

What is sound energy?

Vibrations passing through anything generate sound energy. Energy waves are transmitted by solids, liquids, and gases. Humans can only hear waves with frequencies ranging from 16 Hz to 20 kHz. This range, however, is typical and may vary significantly from person to person.

What is thermal energy?

In physics and engineering, the phrase "thermal energy" is used loosely in numerous situations. Thermal energy is more difficult to convert into usable work than energy from systems that are not in thermodynamic equilibrium. Thermal electricity refers to the electricity possessed through an item because of particle motion inside a system. Take a closed gas cylinder as an example. The gas particles are in a continual state of random movement and therefore have thermal electricity.

11. how we can save on electrical energy consumption of refrigerator?​


Answer:

1) Do not stuff the refrigerator full as it will hinder the flow of cold air thereby decreasing its efficiency. Adequate space will increase the cooling efficiency of the refrigerator.

2) Make sure that the items being kept in the refrigerator are cooled to room temperature before keeping them in it.

3) Do not open refrigerator door unnecessarily as every time it is opened, some heat enters which decreases its efficiency. Decide what you want before opening the door so that the time for which it is open is less.

4) Set the temperature to medium cool for optimum cooling and lesser electricity bill. High cool will increase electricity consumption significantly (by up to 25%).

5) Always cover any liquids kept in the refrigerator as moisture lost from the liquids can impact condenser performance.

6) Do not let ice accumulate in your refrigerator as it acts as unwanted insulation and decreases the efficiency of the condenser. Defrost the refrigerator regularly to maintain the efficiency.

7) Clean up the refrigerator regularly & make sure that dust does not accumulate on coils (back or bottom) and air intake grill (below the doors). Dust build up can make the motors work harder thereby needing lot more electricity.

8) Make sure that the refrigerator remains tightly closed when not open. Any open space will cause heat to get into the refrigerator causing motor to work harder thereby consuming more electricity. You can make sure that rubber door seals are tight by slipping a paper through it. If the paper slips easily then it needs to be fixed. You can also try keeping a flashlight inside the refrigerator and making sure that the light is not visible from outside. Else seals need to be replaced.

21. 7. A 75kg refrigerator is located on the 70th floor of a skyscraper (300m above ground). What is the potential energy of the refrigerator?​


Answer:

Potential energy = (mass) x (gravity) x (height above the reference)

Relative to the 70th floor, on which the refrigerator is standing,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (0) = 0 joules .

Relative to the 35th floor, halfway up to where the fridge is standing,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (150 m) = 110,250 joules .

Relative to the ground,

PE = (75 kg) x (9.8 m/s²) x (300 m) = 220,500 joules .
