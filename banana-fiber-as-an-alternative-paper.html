1. what's the problem of converting banana leaves fiber to pulp paper​


Answer:

The main problem with converting banana leaves into pulp for paper production is that banana leaves are difficult to process due to their high lignin content. Lignin is a natural polymer that gives plant cell walls their strength and rigidity. It is also resistant to degradation by enzymes and chemicals, making it difficult to break down the fibers in the leaves to create a usable pulp. Additionally, banana leaves are often scarce, meaning it would be difficult to produce large amounts of paper from the leaves. Furthermore, the leaves are usually only available in certain regions, which would limit the potential for widespread commercial production.

Making paper out of banana fiber can be a tricky process, as it involves overcoming various complex problems.

• Firstly, finding suitable resources for the banana fiber can be a challenge; from sourcing an appropriate type of banana peel to processing and preparing it for paper-making takes specialised know-how.

• Secondly, obtaining other natural ingredients for binding and coating the sheets is also difficult and requires considerable effort.

• Thirdly, since bananas are not native to all parts of the world, transportation costs can become a major factor.

• Fourthly, in order to produce a dye-resistant product, one needs either special dyes or an appropriate coloring process.

• Fifthly, some byproducts from the paper industry such as lignin can stick to the fibers during production (causing discoloration) without proper treatment prior to use.

28. local study of effectiveness of banana peel as an alternative floorwax.​


Answer:

The effectiveness of banana peel as an alternative floorwax has not been extensively studied. However, anecdotal evidence suggests that the wax is just as effective as commercial floor wax products.

In one study, banana peels were boiled in a pot of water and the resulting liquid was used to wax a wooden floor. The floor was left to dry and, after a few days, it was observed that the floor was left with a glossy, smooth finish.

Other anecdotal evidence suggests that banana peels are also effective at removing dirt and cleaning floors. In one study, banana peels were placed on a floor and left to sit for a few hours. After this time, the peels were removed and the floor was found to be clean and shiny.

Overall, the effectiveness of banana peel as an alternative floorwax appears to be positive. While further research is needed to definitively prove its efficacy, the anecdotal evidence suggests that it is just as effective as commercial floor wax products.
