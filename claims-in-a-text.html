A thesis is the central claim or main argument of an essay. Because it provides a unifying theme for the rest of the essay, it typically appears early on—in shorter papers, most often within the first paragraph or two. The thesis should be analytic or interpretive rather than merely descriptive or factual.

An Explication on the Use of 'Explicit' and 'Implicit'

What to Know?

Explicit describes something that is very clear and without vagueness or ambiguity. Implicit often functions as the opposite, referring to something that is understood, but not described clearly or directly, and often using implication or assumption. To help remember, explicit things are explained, implicit things are implied.


'Explicit' Meaning

To be explicitly clear, explicit and implicit are different words and, in some contexts, they are truly true antonyms. Explicit denotes being very clear and complete without vagueness, implication, or ambiguity. When something is said to be explicit, there is no question as to what is being expressed or conveyed—nothing is implied or assumed.


Implicit' Meaning

Implicit, on the other hand, denotes that something is understood although not clearly or directly expressed or conveyed—there is implication, assumption, or question. It often precedes a preposition, usually in and less frequently from, with, or within.

30. 3. Identify claims explicitly or implicitly made in a written text a. Claim of fact b. Claim of policy c. Claim of value​


Answer:

Explanation:

a. Claim of fact: This type of claim asserts that something is true or false. It can be supported by evidence, data, or logical reasoning. For example, "The Earth revolves around the sun" is a claim of fact.

b. Claim of policy: This type of claim asserts that something should or should not be done. It often proposes a course of action and is backed by evidence or data. For example, "The government should increase funding for education" is a claim of policy.

c. Claim of value: This type of claim asserts that something is good or bad, right or wrong, desirable or undesirable. It is often based on personal or cultural beliefs and can be subjective. For example, "Eating meat is morally wrong" is a claim of value.
