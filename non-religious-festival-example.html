5. give atleast 5 example of non religious festival


What is a non-religious festival?

It is a festival or feast that is not celebrated to commemorate any of Gods and deities.

Here are some of the example:

Bangus Festival

The Bangus Festival is an annual celebration in the city of Dagupan. It highlights the city's rich milkfish aquaculture industry in the province of Pangasinan and in the country.

Manggahan Festival

Manggahan Festival is a month-long celebration of Guimaras’ rich culture and their feast of thanksgiving for having bestowed a rich land where delicious mangoes are being grown.  

Buyogan Festival

The Buyogan Festival of Abuyog, Leyte represented the Philippines in WCF. The Buyogan Festival is dedicated to celebrating bees or “buyog” where the town’s name was derived from.

Kadayawan Festival

It is an annual festival in the city of Davao. Its name derives from the friendly greeting "Madayaw", from the Dabawenyo word meaning good, valuable, superior or beautiful.

Masskara Festival

 The purpose to start the festival is to throw away all the sadness and the grief that was popping in the air of the Bacolod City during the early '80s.

30. What are the example of non religious festival in the philippines???


Here are some of the non-religious festivals in the Philippines:

1. Pangisdaan Festival in Navotas
Navotas, found on the north of Manila, is considered as the Fishing Capital of the Philippines. Pangisdaan Festival is one of the highlights of the Navotas Day Celebration every 16th of January. This is to pay tribute to their main source of income, which is fishing.

2. Panagbenga Festival in Baguio
Baguio holds its biggest festival, the Panagbenga Festival, every February. The event showcases floral floats and native dances. Panagbenga is a term which means "a season of blooming." The event also known as "Baguio Flower Festival."

3. Maskara Festival in Bacolod
Maskara came from two words: English word "mass" and Spanish word "kara" or "face." This started in early 1980s when Bacolod, the county's sugar capital, was hit by major crisis. To lift the people's spirit up, Maskara Festival was done. This celebration happens every third week of October.
