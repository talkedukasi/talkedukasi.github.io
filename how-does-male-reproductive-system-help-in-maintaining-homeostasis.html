1. Digestive System: The digestive system helps maintain homeostasis by breaking down food into nutrients that can be absorbed into the body, providing energy and materials for growth and repair. It regulates the intake of nutrients and water, and eliminates waste products from the body through the process of digestion, absorption, and excretion. It also maintains the pH balance of the body by regulating the levels of acids and bases in the digestive tract.

2. Skeletal System: The skeletal system provides support and protection for the body, as well as facilitating movement and the storage of minerals. It helps maintain homeostasis by regulating calcium levels in the blood through the processes of bone resorption and deposition. 3. Male Reproductive System: The male reproductive system helps maintain homeostasis by producing and delivering sperm to the female reproductive system for fertilization. It regulates the levels of hormones such as testosterone, which are responsible for the development and maintenance of male characteristics. It also plays a role in regulating the body's fluid balance through the production of semen.

It also plays a role in regulating the body's fluid balance through the menstrual cycle and lactation.


5. “How Does the Nervous System Maintain Homeostasis.”


Our brain is like a thermostat, It regulates by detecting if we have the right amount of hormones, temperature, etc. When it is too low or high, the homeostasis comes in. When we are feeling hot: the brain senses it and activates sweat glands and dilates blood vessels; thus, maintaining our body temperature. When we are cold: the brain senses it and makes us shiver and constrict blood vessels; thus, maintaining body temperature. When it comes to hormones. When we have a little amount of hormones: the brain powers our endocrine glands to secrete more hormones. When it becomes too high, it shutdowns temporarily and powers it on when we only have a little amount of hormones. This is called a negative feedback loop. So basically homeostasis regulates our body to function normally. :)
