13. When entrepreneurs don't stop to think about their responsibilities and accountabilities to the general public or the community, what do you think will happen to the business?​


Answer:

When entrepreneurs don't consider their responsibilities and obligations to the wider society or community, it can have negative consequences for their business in the long term.

Firstly, such behavior can lead to a loss of reputation and trust among customers and stakeholders. If a business is seen as neglecting its responsibilities towards society, it may be perceived as unethical, and people may choose not to do business with it. This can result in a decrease in sales and revenue, ultimately impacting the profitability and sustainability of the business.

Secondly, neglecting social responsibilities can also result in legal and regulatory issues. In many jurisdictions, businesses are required to comply with certain laws and regulations related to environmental protection, consumer protection, labor standards, and other social responsibilities. Failure to comply with these regulations can lead to fines, legal action, and damage to the reputation of the business.

Finally, not considering social responsibilities can also lead to negative impacts on the broader community or environment. This can result in public backlash, protests, and calls for boycotts or other actions that can harm the business.

Therefore, it is important for entrepreneurs to consider their responsibilities and obligations towards society and the community as a whole, not just for ethical reasons, but also for the long-term sustainability and success of their business

18. Situational Analysis Instruction: Identify the specific stakeholders involved in this scenario. Determine what is the responsibility and/or accountability of the entrepreneur to them. Write your answers concisely. Situation 1 Carlo wants to expand his business operation in Polomolok. However, he needs additional capital to push through with the plan. He considers applying for a loan in the bank or invite potential investors to join him in the business.


Stakeholders:

1. Carlo himself - responsible for ensuring that his business remains profitable and sustainable.

2. Bank - responsible for evaluating Carlo's loan application and ensuring that the loan will not pose significant risks to their operations.

3. Potential investors - responsible for investing their money wisely and expecting good returns from Carlo's business.

4. Customers in Polomolok - responsible for patronizing Carlo's products or services once the business expansion is realized.

5. Government - responsible for regulating businesses and ensuring that they comply with legal requirements.

Responsibilities and/or accountability of the entrepreneur:

1. Carlo himself - responsible for ensuring the success of his business expansion plan through effective planning and decision-making.

2. Bank - accountable for the soundness of their lending practices and ensuring that the loan will not lead to defaults and losses.

3. Potential investors - accountable for the risks they take in investing in Carlo's business but also expect a fair return on their investment.

4. Customers in Polomolok - expect value for their money from Carlo's products and services.

5. Government - responsible for ensuring that Carlo's business complies with laws and regulations, and contribute to the growth of the local economy.
