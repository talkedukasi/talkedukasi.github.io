2. ano ang batas Friar land


Ang Batas para sa mga Lupain

Hindi popular kung ano ang batas Friar land. Ito ay isang probisyong ibinigay para sa mga Pilipino. Matagal nang isyu ang maling pamamahala sa mga lupain. Apektado nito ang kawalan ng maayos na tirahan. At maging ng hanap-buhay para sa pamilya.


Ang terminong friar (https://brainly.ph/question/1187856) ay tumutukoy sa mga lider ng relihiyong Katoliko.  

History of friar lands in the Philippines: Nang sakupin ang bansang Pilipinas ng Espanya sa loob ng 333 taon (https://brainly.ph/question/192947), ang pagkontrol ng pulitika, relihiyon, ekonomiya at kalakalan ay napasakamay ng mismong mga prayle na binigyan ng kapangyarihan ng Espanya na humawak ng responsibilidad kapwa sa pulitika at relihiyon. Maraming mga hacienda at kalat-kalat na mga lupain sa buong arkipelago ang matatawag na friar lands o sa diwa ay pagmamay-ari ng Simabahan. Wala itong binabayarang buwis at malaki ang kalugihan ng ating bansa mula dito. Karamihan din ay masasabing tiwangwang at hindi napagkukunan ng produkto upang maging kita ng mga indibidwal at ng bansa. May ilan dito na maaaring nagagawan ng negosyo o agrikultural na gawain, ngunit bilang pagrerenta lamang ng mga karaniwang mga Pilipino noon.  

Nang matapos ang pananako na ito, isa sa isyung panlipunan ang Friar Lands. Nais ng pamahalaan na tugunan ang paghihirap at kawalan ng tirahan at trabaho ang mga Pilipino. Kung kaya sa pamamagitan ng mga batas at lakas ng Amerika-Pilipinas, nabawi ng ating gobyerno ang ilan sa mga ito. May ilan na nagamit bilang lugar ng serbisyo sa irigasyon (https://brainly.ph/question/513333) katulad ng sa Laguna at Cavite. Pero sa paglulunsad ng isang batas- ang RA 1120, mabibigyan na ng pagkakataon ang mga pangkaraniwang mamamayang Pilipino na magkaroon ng sariling lupain. Ibinigay sa Land Management Bureau ang pamamahalang administratibo ukol dito. Ang mga interesado na bumili ay kinakailangang abutin ang mga kahilingan ng batas upang makamit ang titulo katunayan na nabili mo ang lupa. Ito ay makikita din sa kanilang website page. Sa bersyong English, mababasa natin ang mga kahilingang ito ukol sa friar lands definition.  

Requirements on the issuance of Deed of Sale Under Friar Lands Act:

• Friar lands application duly accomplished by the applicant/s and acknowledged/ratified before an authorized officer/official;  

• Preliminary investigation report (Information Sheet);  

• Recent appraised value of the land (Appraisal Report) DAO 98-20, S. 1998; latest assessed value of the land from Assessor's Office and BIR Zonal Valuation;  

• Categorical findings as to whether or not the land is already covered by Transfer Certificate of Title in favor of private person;  

• Two copies of approved plan; Original and/or certified copy of the technical descriptions of the land and a certification that it overlaps/does not overlap an old survey/claim when projected in the projection map; certified copy of projection map;  

• Proof of payment of the 10% and/or full purchase price of the land;  

• Proof of publication by bandillo and proof of posting of notice made before auction sale;  

• Report of Auction Sale;  

• Sales Contract/Certificate and a certified xerox copy of the sale register page of the lot;  

• Investigation report that will ascertain the complete names and addresses offal the actual occupants, kind of improvements thereon and introduced by whom; status of the land whether it is free from claims and conflicts and other material information’s pertaining to ownership;  

• Enclosed form of affidavit to be duly accomplished by the applicant/s and submitted to this Office;  

• A joint affidavit stating that neither of the registered purchaser during his/her lifetime nor any of the legal heirs have/has received the title to the land;  

• The exact residence and mailing address of the applicant;

Friar Lands Question, problem confronting the U.S. government after the takeover of the Philippines from Spain in 1898, concerning the disposition of large landed estates owned by Spanish monastic orders on the islands.

For more than 300 years the Roman Catholic Church had been intimately involved with Spanish colonial government in the Philippines. During that time three religious orders—Dominicans, Augustinians, and Recollects—had acquired about one-tenth of all the improved land in the islands. The discontent of the Filipino peasants with this situation had been a contributing factor in the Philippine Revolution of 1896–98.For more than 300 years the Roman Catholic Church had been intimately involved with Spanish colonial government in the Philippines. During that time three religious orders—Dominicans, Augustinians, and Recollects—had acquired about one-tenth of all the improved land in the islands. The discontent of the Filipino peasants with this situation had been a contributing factor in the Philippine Revolution of 1896–98.In the Treaty of Paris (1898), which settled the Spanish–American War, the U.S. government agreed to protect the friars’ property rights, but it was clearly dangerous to allow them to return to their lands. An agreement was eventually reached under which the U.S. purchased 410,000 acres (about 170,000 hectares) for a sum of $7,000,000. The land was then resold to tenant farmers on an installment basis. This solution was not entirely satisfactory; numerous disputes arose over the accuracy of surveys and terms of repayment. Continued monastic possession of certain parcels of land has been a source of contention ever since, as has the issue of land reform in general.

22. ano Ang Friar Land Act of 1904​


Answer:

AN ACT PROVIDING FOR THE ADMINISTRATION AND TEMPORARY LEASING AND SALE OF CERTAIN HACIENDAS AND PARCELS OF LAND, COMMONLY KNOWN AS FRIAR LANDS, FOR THE PURCHASE OF WHICH GOVERNMENT OF THE PHILIPPINE ISLANDS HAS RECENTLY CONTRACTED, PURSUANT TO THE PROVISSIONS OF SECTIONS SIXTY-THREE, SIXTY-FOUR, AND SIXTY-FIVE OF AN ACT OF THE CONGRESS OF THE UNITED STATES, ENTITLED "AN ACT TEMPORARILY TO PROVIDE FOR THE ADMINISTRATION OF THE AFFAIRS OF CIVIL GOVERNMENT IN THE PHILIPPINE ISLANDS, AND FOR OTHER PURPOSES," APPROVED ON THE FIRST DAY OF JULY, NINETEEN HUNDRED AND TWO.

The Spanish Friars were the crucial elements in the Westernizing of the Philippine Archipelago, and in spreading the Christian faith in that part of the world. Journeying with the first European explorers to these islands in the Far East, they came with the intention of establishing Catholicism under the Patronato real of the Kings of Spain.

After the conquistadores brought the Filipinos under the rule of the Spanish crown, either by peaceful means of treaties and pacts or, alternatively, by war, Spain did not send large standing armies to maintain its empire in the East. The apostolic zeal of the missionaries followed the efforts of men such as Miguel López de Legazpi, and aided to consolidate the enterprise of Hispanizing the Philippines. The Spanish missionaries acted as de facto conquerors; they gained the goodwill of the islanders, presented Spanish culture positively, and in so doing won approximately 2 million converts.[1]
