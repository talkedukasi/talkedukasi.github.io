THE EARTH'S HEAT FURNACE

THE EARTH'S HEAT FURNACEThe Earth's internal heat source provides the energy for our dynamic planet, supplying it with the driving force for plate-tectonic motion, and for on-going catastrophic events such as earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. This internal heat energy was much greater in the early stages of the Earth than it is today, having accumulated rapidly by heat conversion associated with three separate processes, all of which were most intense during the first few hundred thousand years of the Earth's history: (1) extraterrestrial impacts, (2) gravitational contraction of the Earth's interior, and (3) the radioactive decay of unstable isotopes.

21. three main sources of earth internal heat ​


In the deep earth, there are three main sources of heat:  

(1) the heat from when the planet formed and accreted, which has not been lost;  

(2) frictional heating, caused by denser core material sinking to the planet's center; and  

(3) the heat from radioactive element decay.  

It takes a long time for heat to escape from the planet. This is accomplished through both "convective" heat transport within the earth's liquid outer core and solid mantle, as well as slower "conductive" heat transport through nonconvective boundary layers, such as the earth's plates at the surface. As a result, much of the planet's primordial heat, dating back to when the earth first accreted and evolved its atmosphere, has been lost.
