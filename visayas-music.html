FIRST CIRCLE OF VENN DIAGRAM

Most Visayan songs or music are written in duple or triple meter with a simple melody that is easy to sing. One of the most popular musical styles in the Visayan region is the balitaw. Folk songs like Si Pilemon, Pakitong-kitong, Ili-ili Tulog Anay, Rosas Padan and Matud Nila originated from this region.

SECOND CIRCLE OF VENN DIAGRAM

Mindanao folk music includes the ancient Muslim folk song and dance called estijaro, and a Mindanao folk song called uruyan. These are usually accompanied by drums, gongs, or other percussion instruments like the subing, a gong.

MUSIC OF VISAYAS

Karamihan sa mga kantang Bisaya ay nakasulat sa doble o triple meter, na may simpleng melody na madaling kantahin. Ang duple (double) meter ay isang ritmo na batay sa dalawang malalaking beats bawat sukat, habang ang Triple meter ay batay sa tatlong malalaking beats bawat sukat.

MUSIKA NG MINDANAO

Kasama sa musikang katutubong Mindanao ang sinaunang awitin at sayaw ng mga Muslim na tinatawag na estijaro, at isang awiting bayan ng Mindanao na tinatawag na uruyan. Ang mga ito ay kadalasang sinasaliwan ng mga tambol, gong, o iba pang mga instrumentong percussion tulad ng subing, isang gong. Ang mga musikal na gawi ng mga tao sa baybayin at mababang lugar ng Kanlurang Mindanao ay mahahalagang elemento sa kanilang buhay panlipunan at ang pangunahing pinagmumulan ng kanilang mga etnikong pagkakakilanlan. Ang mga musikal na kasanayan ng mga katutubo na ito ay pinaghalong mga ideyang Kanluranin at hindi kanluranin.

PAGKAKATULAD

May pagkakatulad sila sa mga tuntunin ng tungkulin ng musika sa kanilang pang-araw-araw na buhay. Ang musika ay kadalasang ginagamit sa mga ritwal at seremonya. Karamihan sa kanilang mga instrumentong pangmusika ay gawa sa mga materyales na matatagpuan sa kanilang lokalidad.

Ang Visayas ay isa sa 3 pangunahing grupo ng isla ng Pilipinas. Sa Central Visayas, kasama sa kolonyal na arkitekturang Espanyol ng Cebu City ang tatsulok na Fort San Pedro at ang Basilica del Santo Niño. Ang Chocolate Hills ng Bohol Island ay isang grupo ng higit sa 1,000 conical, natatakpan ng damo na bunton na nagiging kayumanggi sa tag-araw. Sa Corella, ang mga bihirang bug-eyed primate na tinatawag na tarsier ay protektado sa Philippine Tarsier Sanctuary.

Sinasaklaw ng Mindanao ang malaking isla ng Mindanao kasama ang maliliit na isla sa katimugang Pilipinas. Sa pinakamalaking lungsod nito, Davao, ipinapakita ng Philippine Eagle Center at Davao Crocodile Park ang mga lokal na wildlife. Nagtatampok ang urban People's Park ng mga estatwa ng mga katutubo at ang Durian Dome, na ipinangalan sa matinik at mabahong prutas na saganang tumutubo sa Mindanao. Music of Mindoro, Visayas and Mindanao

1. Unit IV Lesson 19 Music of Mindoro

2. Hanunuo-Mangyans Mangyan – is the generic name for all the indigenous groups that live in the island of Mindoro. Hanunuo – is used to describe the Mangyans who have remained faithful to their traditions and have kept their customs free from foreign influences.

3. Ambahan – is a poem and a song chanted by the Hanunuo-Mangyans. It came from the word amba meaning “invocation to the spirit.”

4. Ambahan: Childhood Sweet are the memories of our childhood. For the Mangyan child, it is a time of unconcern and carefreeness, even if the child has to take his share of the family duties to the measure of his capacities. It is with feelings of sentimentality and homesickness that a young man recalls the happy years of his youth that passed away too fast. It is also with pride that he remembers the love and kindness shown to him by his parents.

5. That time, when I was still young, (I was just a baby still) when I sat on mother's lap, when she rocked me in a crib, in a cradle newly made. 6. Luka – is a portion of the bamboo pole where then Mangyans carve or engrave their ambahans.

7. Musical Instruments of Mindoro

8. Chordophones or String Instruments Gitgit – it is a three stringed fiddle played with a bow made from bamboo and human hair.

9. Kudlung – is a zither made from bamboo that has parallel strings. Kudyapi – is a six- stringed zither that is carved out from soft wood. It has six nylon threads that can be plucked or strummed.

10. Aerophones or Wind Instruments Cantuy – it is a nose flute.

11. Bangsi – is an external pipe flute. Tangkap – is a bamboo whistle.

12. Idiophones or Instruments that are Struck or Shaken Agong – is a bossed bronze gong that is struck with bamboo sticks. This instrument is used in social gatherings to celebrate good harvest and burial ceremonies.

13. Kalutang – are percussion sticks cut from softwood. They are played by striking each other. Barimbaw – is a bamboo slit gong that is played with a stick.
